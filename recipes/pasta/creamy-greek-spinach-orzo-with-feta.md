# Creamy Greek Spinach Orzo with Feta

## Description
A creamy, Greek-inspired one-pan orzo where feta dissolves into scallion-infused orzo and wilted spinach for a nutritious, comforting vegetarian meal. Serve it as a main dish, or as a side with chicken, beef, pork, lamb or seafood.

## Ingredients
_(for 4 servings)_

- 2 tablespoons unsalted butter
- 5 large scallions, whites and greens thinly sliced, divided
- 3 large garlic cloves, minced
- 9 ounces baby spinach (about 8 cups)
- 1 teaspoon kosher salt, or to taste
- 1 3/4 cups low-sodium vegetable broth
- 1 cup orzo
- 1 teaspoon lemon zest, finely grated (from 1 large lemon)
- 3/4 cup feta cheese, crumbled, plus more for garnish
- 1/2 cup frozen peas, thawed, optional
- 1 cup fresh dill, parsley or cilantro, chopped

## Instructions
1. Heat a large oven-safe skillet on the stove over medium heat. Add the butter and melt (if using salted butter, omit any additional salt in the recipe). Add the garlic and 3/4 of the scallions, reserving some greens for topping the orzo later. Stir and cook for about 3 minutes.
2. Add the spinach, in batches if needed. Stir until it's wilted, about 4 to 5 minutes.
3. Pour in the broth and bring to a slow boil. Reduce heat to low. (Chicken broth can be used instead of vegetable broth.)
4. Add the orzo and lemon zest and stir. Cover and let simmer until all the liquid is absorbed, about 10 to 13 minutes. Stir once or twice to avoid the orzo sticking to the bottom of the pan.
5. When the liquid is absorbed, add the feta and herbs and stir for another minute.
6. Serve sprinkled with a bit more cheese, if desired, and the reserved scallion greens. Store any leftovers in an airtight container in the fridge; reheat in the microwave.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
De-stemming the spinach first is optional and not necessary. Frozen peas are an optional add-in. Leftovers keep in an airtight container in the fridge and reheat well in the microwave.
