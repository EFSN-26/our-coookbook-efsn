# Cherry Tomato & Feta Pasta

## Description
The star of this simple pasta recipe is the feta — it turns the pasta into a creamy, almost mac-and-cheese-like dish, with juicy roasted tomatoes and pesto making it so much more. Inspired by New York Times Cooking and adapted from a popular recipe from a Finnish blogger. Most of the time is hands-off, spent in the oven.

## Ingredients
_(for 4 servings — see §8 for scaling)_

- 1 1/2 - 2 cups cherry tomatoes
- 3 teaspoons garlic, minced
- 1 teaspoon dried thyme
- 1 teaspoon dried rosemary
- 1 teaspoon sea salt
- 4 tablespoons extra virgin olive oil
- 6 ounces (block) feta
- pinch of black pepper
- pinch of ground red pepper
- 9 ounces short pasta, such as farfalle, rotini or cavatappi
- 3/4 cup reserved pasta water
- 3 teaspoons jarred pesto

## Instructions
1. In a casserole dish, combine tomatoes, garlic, thyme, rosemary and 1/2 teaspoon of the salt. Toss with half of the olive oil to coat. Place the feta in the middle of the dish, and top with the remaining olive oil. Sprinkle the black pepper and red pepper over everything. Bake for about 35 minutes in a preheated 400-degree F oven, or until the tomatoes have burst their skins.
2. Meanwhile, cook pasta until it's al dente. Drain, reserving about 3/4 cup of the pasta water. Add the pasta to the pan in an even layer and sprinkle with the remaining salt. Add the pesto and mix everything together. Pour the pasta water on top of this mixture. Cover the dish tightly with foil and bake for about 15 minutes.
3. Remove from the oven. Keeping the dish covered, let it stand for about 5 minutes or until the liquid is absorbed.
4. Serve topped with more black pepper, salt and oil, if desired.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
- Add meatballs or sausage for extra protein.
- Fresh herbs can be substituted for dried, but reduce the quantity.
- To make this gluten-free, use gluten-free pasta.
