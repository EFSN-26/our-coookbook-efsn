# Spinach with Fettuccine

## Description
A fantastic and easy side dish or light main course that's ready in no time, combining fettuccine and fresh spinach with the classic flavor combo of olive oil, garlic, and Parmesan.

## Ingredients
_(for 4 servings)_

- 8 ounces fettuccine
- 5 ounces fresh spinach (1 package)
- 1 tablespoon olive oil
- 1 tablespoon chunky garlic paste (or use finely minced garlic)
- ½ teaspoon salt
- grated Parmesan cheese (optional)

## Instructions
1. Boil salted water in a large pot and cook the pasta to al dente based on the directions on the box.
2. Right before draining the pasta, stir in the spinach until wilted. Drain the pasta/spinach mixture and set aside.
3. Heat the olive oil over medium-high heat in a large skillet. Add the garlic paste and salt and cook until fragrant, about 2-3 minutes.
4. Add the pasta/spinach mixture to the skillet and toss to combine.
5. Reduce heat to low to keep warm until ready to serve, tossing occasionally.
6. Serve topped with Parmesan cheese, if desired.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
- Any kind of pasta works well in this dish, but fettuccine gives a nice bite.
- Substitute frozen spinach for fresh by thawing and draining it first, then adding it to the skillet with the olive oil and garlic right before adding the cooked pasta.
- Use finely minced fresh garlic instead of garlic paste for even more garlic flavor, or jarred minced garlic for convenience (use one fresh garlic clove per tablespoon of garlic paste).
- Season with salt and pepper to taste, or add other seasonings like garlic powder or red pepper flakes for heat.
- Top with grilled chicken or pan-fried tofu to add more protein.
- For a thicker, creamier result, add heavy cream, cream cheese, or a store-bought Alfredo sauce.
- Garnish with fresh chopped herbs like parsley or basil for color and freshness.
- Storage: cool completely, then refrigerate in an airtight container for 3-4 days, or freeze for several months; thaw before reheating. Reheat in the microwave or in a skillet over medium heat with a little extra olive oil.
