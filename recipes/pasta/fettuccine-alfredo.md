# Fettuccine Alfredo (Roman-Style)

## Description
The original "Fettuccine all'Alfredo," created in Rome in the early 20th century by Alfredo di Lelio, made with just three ingredients — butter, pasta water, and Parmigiano-Reggiano. No cream, no garlic — the magic comes from emulsifying butter and cheese into a silky sauce that coats the noodles.

## Ingredients
_(for UNVERIFIED servings — see §8 for scaling)_

- 1 pound (450 g) fettuccine
- 1/2 cup (115 g) unsalted butter
- 2 cups (about 180 g) freshly grated Parmigiano-Reggiano
- Salt
- Freshly ground black pepper

## Instructions
1. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Add the fettuccine and cook until al dente, about 8-10 minutes.
2. While the pasta cooks, melt the butter in a large skillet or pan over medium heat — just melted and warm, not sizzling or browning.
3. Drain the pasta, reserving about 1/2 cup of the pasta water. Add the drained pasta directly to the skillet with the melted butter. Sprinkle grated Parmesan over the pasta and toss gently with tongs. Add a few tablespoons of the reserved pasta water to help create a smooth, creamy sauce; adjust consistency with more pasta water if needed.
4. Season with salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste (the cheese is salty, so add salt cautiously). Serve immediately — the sauce can thicken and clump as it cools.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
Use the best quality butter and Parmigiano-Reggiano you can find. Grate the cheese finely so it melts smoothly. Don't overheat the butter or the sauce can separate and become oily. The starchy pasta water is what emulsifies the sauce, so don't skip it.
