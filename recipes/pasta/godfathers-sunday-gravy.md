# The Godfather's Sunday Gravy

## Description
A rich Italian Sunday gravy inspired by Clemenza's spaghetti sauce scene in "The Godfather" — diced tomatoes, tomato sauce and tomato paste simmered with Italian sausage, meatballs, red wine and a touch of sugar. [Source](https://30seconds.com/food/tip/66562/The-Godfathers-Sunday-Gravy-Recipe-How-to-Make-Clemenzas-Spaghetti-Sauce-9-Ingredients)

## Ingredients
_(for 10 servings)_

- 2 - 3 tablespoons extra virgin olive oil
- 2 - 4 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 can (28 ounces) diced tomatoes
- 1 can (28 ounces) tomato sauce
- 1 can (6 ounces) tomato paste
- 1 pound (16 ounces) Italian sausage, cooked and crumbled
- 1 pound (16 ounces) meatballs, cooked
- 1/4 cup granulated sugar
- splash of red wine (quantity not specified by source)

## Instructions
1. Heat a large soup pot or Dutch oven over medium heat. Add the olive oil. When hot, add the garlic and cook, stirring, until golden brown, about 30 seconds (do not burn).
2. Add the diced tomatoes, tomato sauce and tomato paste. Stir to combine and bring to a boil.
3. Add the meatballs, Italian sausage, splash of red wine and sugar. Stir. Simmer for as long as desired, from 30 minutes to 8 hours. Serve as desired.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
- Sunday gravies are typically simmered for hours on the stovetop; simmer for as long as you have time for. If doing a long simmer, add the meat later so the sauce doesn't dry out.
- The source notes none of the versions it found called for fresh basil or other Italian herbs — dried Italian seasoning can be added if desired.
- Homemade Italian meatballs are best, but frozen meatballs can be used if needed.
- Store any leftover sauce in airtight containers in the fridge.
- Serve over pasta, in pasta casseroles, lasagna, or for a Sunday supper.
