# BLT Carbonara

## Description
A summer twist on classic pasta carbonara, inspired by the BLT sandwich — smoky thick-cut bacon and a shower of fresh basil stand in for lettuce, while juicy heirloom tomatoes replace the usual peas. [Source](https://www.simplyrecipes.com/blt-carbonara-recipe-12015101)

## Ingredients
_(for 4 servings)_

- 4 large eggs
- 1 cup (about 4 ounces) finely grated Pecorino Romano cheese, divided
- 1/2 cup (about 2 ounces) finely grated Parmesan cheese
- 2 cloves garlic, pressed through a garlic press or minced
- 1/2 teaspoon freshly ground black pepper, or more to taste
- 6 slices thick-cut bacon, cut crosswise into 1/4-inch strips
- 12 ounces fettuccine (or other favorite pasta)
- 1 1/2 pounds (4 to 5 medium) firm-ripe tomatoes, preferably heirloom and a combination of colors, cut into 3/4-inch pieces (about 4 cups)
- salt, to taste
- 1/2 cup chopped or torn basil leaves

## Instructions
1. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil over high heat. Go a little easier on the salt than normal, because the saltiness of the bacon and cheeses can vary.
2. In a medium bowl, whisk the eggs, 3/4 cup of the Pecorino Romano, the Parmesan, garlic and pepper. Set aside.
3. While the water heats, cook the bacon in a medium skillet over medium heat, stirring occasionally, until crisp, about 5 minutes. Remove the pan from the heat and set aside.
4. When the water boils, add the pasta and cook according to package instructions, 10 to 12 minutes. Drain, reserving 1 cup of the cooking liquid. Quickly transfer the pasta back to the pot or a mixing bowl.
5. Working quickly, whisk 1/4 cup of the reserved pasta water into the egg mixture to temper the eggs (this helps prevent curdling). Immediately pour the egg mixture over the pasta, tossing constantly with tongs. Add the bacon (with drippings) and tomatoes, and toss to combine, adding more pasta water if desired to loosen the sauce. Add salt and more pepper to taste.
6. Transfer to bowls and sprinkle with the remaining 1/4 cup Pecorino and the basil.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
- Use real Parmigiano Reggiano and Pecorino Romano if possible, and grate it yourself — it melts into the sauce more easily than pre-grated cheese.
- Thick-cut bacon adds heft and the smokiness that makes this a "BLT" carbonara; pancetta is more traditional but isn't smoky, so it moves away from that BLT flavor.
- Any pasta shape can be used in place of fettuccine.
- Use the best, most flavorful tomatoes available — a mix of colors is ideal, but one or two colors is fine if that's what's freshest.
- It's important to have everything ready before draining the pasta: eggs and cheese whisked, bacon crisped, tomatoes diced, basil chopped.
- If the tomatoes are very ripe and juicy, the sauce likely won't need extra pasta water to loosen it.
- Refrigerate leftovers in an airtight container for up to 5 days.
