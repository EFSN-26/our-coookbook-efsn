# Creamy Lemon Pasta (Pasta al Limone)

## Description
A creamy, buttery lemon pasta sauce with the zesty tang of fresh lemon, salty Parmesan cheese, and a little red pepper flakes for a spicy kick. Made from scratch in about 30 minutes, it's an easy weeknight take on pasta al limone using spaghetti, garlic, and heavy cream.

## Ingredients
_(for 4 servings)_

- 12 ounces spaghetti
- 4 tablespoons butter
- 1 tablespoon olive oil
- 2 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 cup heavy whipping cream
- zest from 1 lemon, plus more for garnish
- juice from 1 lemon
- 1/4 teaspoon red pepper flakes, plus more for garnish
- 1/2 teaspoon kosher salt, plus more to taste
- 1/2 teaspoon black pepper, freshly ground, plus more to taste
- 1/2 cup reserved pasta water
- 1/2 cup parmesan cheese, grated, plus more for garnish
- fresh parsley, chopped, for garnish

## Instructions
1. Boil water in a large pot over high heat and then cook the spaghetti according to the package directions.
2. Drain the pasta and save 1 cup of the starchy pasta water.
3. Melt the butter and olive oil in a large skillet over a medium heat.
4. Add the garlic and sauté for 1 minute, or until fragrant.
5. Whisk in the heavy cream, lemon zest, lemon juice, red pepper flakes, salt, pepper, and pasta water.
6. Cook for 5 to 7 minutes, or until the sauce has thickened.
7. Add the parmesan cheese and stir to combine.
8. Toss the cooked pasta in the lemon sauce.
9. Garnish with red pepper flakes, lemon zest, parmesan cheese, and parsley.
10. Serve immediately.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
- Zest the lemons before juicing them — it's easier that way. Start with less lemon juice and zest if you don't want a strong lemon flavor, adding more to taste.
- Once the pasta water comes to a boil, add salt to make the pasta tastier.
- Try adding capers or thinly sliced mushrooms as add-ins.
- Any pasta shape works — linguine, fettuccine, farfalle, tagliatelle, or penne can be substituted for spaghetti.
- Storage: keep leftovers in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 4 days. This dish will freeze, but the cream sauce may separate and thaw slightly grainy; thaw in the refrigerator overnight and add more cream, lemon juice, and butter to thin the sauce when reheating.
