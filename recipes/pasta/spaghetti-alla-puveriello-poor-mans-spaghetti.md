# Spaghetti alla Puveriello (Poor Man's Spaghetti)

## Description
A four-ingredient Neapolitan pasta that came about after World War II, when ingredients were scarce. Fried eggs and cheese are stirred vigorously into hot spaghetti to form a creamy, carbonara-esque sauce — simpler and quicker than classic carbonara. [Source](https://www.simplyrecipes.com/spaghetti-alla-puveriello-recipe-11964068)

## Ingredients
_(for 2 servings)_

- 8 ounces spaghetti
- 3 tablespoons unsalted butter
- 4 large eggs
- 1/2 teaspoon kosher salt, plus more for pasta cooking water
- 1/4 teaspoon freshly ground black pepper, plus more for garnish
- 1 cup (2 ounces) grated Pecorino Romano cheese or Parmigiano-Reggiano cheese, plus more for garnish

## Instructions
1. **Boil the pasta:** Bring a medium pot of salted water to a boil over high heat. Add the spaghetti and cook according to package directions until al dente, stirring occasionally. Reserve 1 cup of the pasta cooking water, then drain the pasta.
2. **Cook the eggs:** While the pasta cooks, melt the butter in a large nonstick skillet over medium heat. Crack the eggs into the skillet and sprinkle evenly with the salt and pepper. Cover the skillet, reduce the heat to medium-low, and cook without disturbing until the whites are set and the yolks are still runny, 2 to 4 minutes. Remove from heat. Transfer 2 of the eggs to a plate and set aside.
3. **Make the sauce:** Add the cooked pasta, cheese, and 1/2 cup of the reserved pasta cooking water to the eggs remaining in the skillet. Stir vigorously, breaking up the egg whites into small pieces and adding more pasta cooking water as needed, until a creamy sauce forms, 1 to 2 minutes.
4. Divide the pasta evenly between 2 bowls and top each with one of the reserved cooked eggs. Garnish with more black pepper and cheese, and serve immediately.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
- If you don't have a lid big enough for the skillet, use a pizza pan or baking sheet to cover it.
- Stir like you mean it — vigorous stirring is what makes the sauce creamy.
- To serve 4, double the recipe and use a larger skillet; fry the eggs in two batches (4 eggs per batch), reserving the first batch for topping the spaghetti and the second for the sauce.
- Use high-quality, preferably bronze-cut, spaghetti, which the sauce clings to better.
- Use real Pecorino Romano or Parmigiano-Reggiano, not generic parmesan.
- Origin: Spaghetti alla puveriello ("poor man's spaghetti") originated in Naples; the story goes that after World War II, ingredients were hard to come by and this pasta arose out of necessity.
- Recipe developed by Isabelle Easter.
- Refrigerate leftovers in an airtight container for up to 2 days.
