# Short Rib Pappardelle

## Description
A mostly hands-off, cozy pasta dish built on a rich short rib ragù — bone-in beef short ribs braised low and slow with carrots, celery, onion, red wine, and tomatoes, finished with cream and Parmesan, and tossed with wide pappardelle noodles. [Source](https://www.thekitchn.com/short-rib-pappardelle-23768996)

## Ingredients
_(for 6-8 servings)_

- 4 pounds English-cut bone-in beef short ribs, cut crosswise into pieces no larger than 4 inches
- 2 teaspoons kosher salt
- 1 teaspoon freshly ground black pepper
- 1 tablespoon olive oil
- 4 medium carrots, peeled and diced (about 2 1/2 cups)
- 4 medium stalks celery, diced (about 2 cups)
- 1 large yellow onion, diced (about 2 cups)
- 4 cloves garlic, minced
- 2 tablespoons tomato paste
- 1 1/2 cups dry red wine
- 28 ounces passata or crushed tomatoes (about 3 cups)
- 2 cups low-sodium beef broth
- 2 tablespoons soy sauce
- 2 sprigs fresh thyme
- 1 dried bay leaf
- 1/2 cup heavy cream
- 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese (about 2 ounces), plus more for serving
- 1 pound dried pappardelle pasta
- Chopped fresh parsley leaves, for garnish (optional)

## Instructions
1. Pat the short ribs dry with paper towels. Season all over with the kosher salt and black pepper.
2. Heat the olive oil in a Dutch oven or large heavy-bottomed pot over medium-high heat until shimmering. Working in 2 batches, add the short ribs and sear until all 4 sides are browned, 2 to 3 minutes per side. Transfer all the short ribs to a large plate.
3. Reduce the heat to medium. Add the carrots, celery, and onion to the pot. Sauté until the vegetables are softened, about 7 minutes. Add the garlic and tomato paste. Cook, stirring frequently, until fragrant and the tomato paste darkens in color, about 1 minute.
4. Add the red wine and scrape up any browned bits from the bottom of the pot. Vigorously simmer until reduced slightly, about 5 minutes.
5. Stir in the passata, beef broth, soy sauce, thyme sprigs, and bay leaf. Return the short ribs and their accumulated juices to the pot, arranging the ribs in an even layer. Bring to a boil.
6. Reduce the heat to maintain a simmer. Partially cover and cook, stirring once or twice, until the short ribs are very tender, about 3 hours.
7. Using tongs, transfer the short ribs to a rimmed baking sheet. Remove and discard the thyme stems and bay leaf. Remove the meat from the bones and shred into large pieces with two forks. Discard the bones and return the meat to the pot.
8. If serving immediately, skim off the excess fat from the surface, then stir in the heavy cream and Parmesan cheese. If not serving immediately, let cool completely, then cover and refrigerate overnight. Scrape off and discard the hardened layer of fat from the surface before reheating over medium heat; stir in the cream and Parmesan cheese before serving.
9. When ready to serve, bring a large pot of heavily salted water to a boil. Add the pappardelle and cook according to package directions until al dente. Drain.
10. Add the pasta to the pot with the short ribs and sauce and toss to combine. Serve garnished with chopped parsley and more Parmesan cheese if desired.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
- Make ahead: the short rib ragù can be cooked up to 1 day ahead. Let cool completely, then cover and refrigerate. Scrape off and discard the hardened fat from the surface before reheating.
- Storage: it's best to only toss the amount of pasta you plan to eat in the sauce. Leftover short ribs in sauce keep refrigerated in an airtight container for up to 4 days, or frozen for up to 3 months.
- Look for English-cut (rather than flanken) short ribs — they benefit from low and slow cooking, which renders the meat extremely tender.
- Use a dry red wine such as Pinot Noir, Merlot, or Cabernet Sauvignon.
- The wide, toothy pappardelle holds up extremely well to the thick, rich ragù.
- Recipe tester's note: "The braised short ribs were fall-off-the-bone tender... The sauce was rich and flavorful, and the soy sauce worked well here for a boost of umami. I thought the ratio of ragù to pasta was perfect."
- Nutrition per serving (per source, based on 8 servings): 1307 calories, 92.4g fat (40.8g saturated), 63.3g carbs, 6.3g fiber, 11.3g sugar, 47.4g protein, 1262.6mg sodium.
