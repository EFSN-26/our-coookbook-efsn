# Sizzling Spaghetti

## Description
A viral variation on spaghetti aglio e olio that originated with Instagram user @ohsweetbasil: instead of cooking the garlic, a hefty pour of hot olive oil is poured directly over spaghetti tossed with raw garlic, fresh herbs and red pepper flakes, lightly cooking the garlic and blooming the herbs on contact. [Source](https://www.aol.com/meet-sizzling-spaghetti-simple-dinner-000000971.html)

## Ingredients
_(servings not specified by source)_

- 1 pound spaghetti, cooked al dente
- 8 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 tablespoon finely chopped fresh basil
- 1 tablespoon finely chopped fresh parsley, divided
- 2 teaspoons finely chopped fresh oregano
- 1 1/2 teaspoons finely chopped fresh rosemary
- 1 teaspoon red pepper flakes
- 2/3 cup extra-virgin olive oil
- 1 teaspoon salt, or to taste
- 1 teaspoon ground black pepper, or to taste
- 1 to 2 tablespoons finely grated Parmesan cheese

## Instructions
1. Place the cooked spaghetti into a large bowl; sprinkle with the garlic, basil, 2 teaspoons of the parsley, oregano, rosemary and red pepper flakes.
2. Warm the olive oil in a small saucepan over medium heat until it shimmers.
3. Carefully pour the hot oil over the spaghetti, herbs and pepper flakes; season with salt and black pepper. Toss until well combined.
4. Garnish with Parmesan and the remaining 1 teaspoon of parsley. Serve immediately.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
- Traditional spaghetti aglio e olio toasts the garlic in the oil until golden brown; this variation nixes cooking the garlic and instead pours extra-hot oil directly over raw garlic and herbs, similar to how chili oil or "Wet-Wet Sauce" is made.
- The Allrecipes test kitchen described it as "the grown-up version of buttered noodles" — as easy as a box of mac and cheese but feels like a 5-star restaurant dish.
- It's not the fastest pasta sauce to make since the oil isn't jarred, but the payoff in flavor and presentation is worth it.
