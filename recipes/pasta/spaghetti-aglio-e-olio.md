# Spaghetti Aglio e Olio

## Description
The classic pantry pasta every Italian nonna knows by heart: just garlic, olive oil and chili flakes tossed with spaghetti. No sauce, no cheese, no trip to the store — humble, fast, and deeply comforting. [Source](https://www.gamintraveler.com/2026/07/08/the-15-minute-pantry-recipe-italian-nonnas-swear-by-that-saves-dinner-every-time/)

## Ingredients
_(for 2 servings)_

- 200 g (about 7 oz) spaghetti
- 3 to 4 cloves garlic, thinly sliced
- 4 tablespoons extra virgin olive oil (good quality matters)
- 1/2 teaspoon dried red chili flakes (adjust to taste)
- salt, for the pasta water
- small handful fresh flat-leaf parsley, chopped (optional)
- a spoonful of reserved pasta water, for texture (optional)

## Instructions
1. Fill a large pot with water, salt it generously (like the sea), and bring it to a boil.
2. Slice the garlic thinly — not minced, not crushed — so it can toast evenly without burning.
3. Chop the parsley (if using), and measure out the chili flakes.
4. Add the pasta to the boiling water and cook until al dente, usually one minute less than the package suggests. Save about 1/4 cup of pasta water before draining.
5. While the pasta cooks, in a wide skillet add the olive oil and garlic. Place over medium-low heat so the garlic slowly infuses the oil rather than frying. Stir gently.
6. Just as the garlic starts to turn golden at the edges, sprinkle in the chili flakes; they will bloom in the oil and release their heat.
7. Once the pasta is drained, transfer it directly to the pan with the garlic oil using tongs. Toss thoroughly to coat.
8. Add a spoonful or two of reserved pasta water if needed to help the oil cling to the pasta for a silkier finish.
9. Toss in the chopped parsley just before serving, if using.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
No cheese, cream or butter is added — that's the point of the dish. Traditionally no cheese is added at the table either. Serve immediately while hot and glossy, paired with a small green salad and a glass of dry white wine or sparkling water.
