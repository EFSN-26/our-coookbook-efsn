# Sheet-Pan Garlic-Butter Gnocchi With Summer Vegetables

## Description
An easy, veggie-packed vegetarian meal that comes together on a single sheet pan — pillowy gnocchi roasts alongside zucchini, corn and tomatoes, then gets tossed with garlic butter and a bright hit of lemon. [Source](https://www.eatingwell.com/sheet-pan-garlic-butter-gnocchi-with-summer-vegetables-11974732)

## Ingredients
_(for 4 servings)_

- 2 medium zucchini, cut into 1-inch-thick half-moons
- 8 ounces shelf-stable potato gnocchi
- 4 tablespoons extra-virgin olive oil, divided
- 1/4 teaspoon salt, divided
- 1 pint cherry or grape tomatoes
- 1 1/2 cups fresh corn kernels
- 6 medium cloves garlic, peeled
- 4 tablespoons unsalted butter
- 1/2 teaspoon crushed red pepper
- 1/2 teaspoon grated lemon zest
- 1 1/2 teaspoons lemon juice
- 2/3 cup grated Parmesan cheese
- 1/4 cup torn fresh basil

## Instructions
1. Place a large rimmed baking sheet on the lowest oven rack and preheat the oven to 450 degrees F. Toss the zucchini, gnocchi, 3 tablespoons of the oil and 1/8 teaspoon of the salt in a large bowl until evenly coated. Carefully remove the hot baking sheet from the oven, add the gnocchi mixture and spread it in an even layer (reserve the bowl). Roast on the lowest rack until browned on the underside, about 10 minutes.
2. Meanwhile, toss the tomatoes, corn, garlic and the remaining 1 tablespoon of oil in the reserved bowl until coated. Remove the baking sheet from the oven and stir the gnocchi mixture, then spread it in an even layer. Sprinkle the tomato mixture evenly over the gnocchi mixture. Return to the lowest rack and roast until the gnocchi and zucchini are tender and the tomatoes have burst, about 15 minutes.
3. Remove from the oven and carefully smash the garlic with a fork. Add the butter, crushed red pepper, lemon zest and lemon juice, and gently toss until the butter is mostly melted. Return to the oven and roast until the butter is fully melted, about 2 minutes. Sprinkle with the remaining 1/8 teaspoon salt and toss to coat. Sprinkle with the Parmesan and basil before serving.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
- Use shelf-stable gnocchi rather than refrigerated fresh gnocchi — it has less moisture, holds its shape and roasts up crispy on the outside and tender inside instead of steaming and turning gummy.
- Eggplant, yellow squash or any summer squash can be substituted for the zucchini.
- If fresh corn isn't in season, thawed frozen corn works too.
- Always preheat the sheet pan before adding ingredients — it encourages better browning. A dark-colored baking sheet browns more evenly and reduces sticking.
- Best enjoyed the day it's made, since the tomatoes and zucchini can turn mushy over time. Leftovers keep in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 5 days; reheat with a splash of water in the microwave in 60-second intervals, or covered with foil in a 375-degree F oven for 10 to 15 minutes.
