# Creamy Cajun Sausage Spaghetti

## Description
This creamy Cajun sausage spaghetti makes two generous servings of cheesy, creamy, Cajun-spiced pasta. The sausage brings so much flavor, and it's quite simple to make. [Source](https://www.allrecipes.com/creamy-cajun-sausage-spaghetti-recipe-11892234)

## Ingredients
_(for 2 servings)_

- 1 tablespoon olive oil
- 1/2 (13 ounce) package smoked sausage or andouille
- 4 ounces spaghetti
- 2 tablespoons butter
- 1 tablespoon minced shallot
- 1 tablespoon minced garlic
- 1 tablespoon Cajun seasoning, such as Penzey's
- 1 tablespoon lemon juice
- 2/3 cup heavy cream
- 1/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese
- chopped green onion and parsley, for garnish

## Instructions
1. Heat oil in a medium-sized skillet to medium heat. Slice sausage coins in half, then add to the pan. Cook sausage, turning occasionally, until brown on all sides and fully cooked. Remove to a bowl lined with paper towels; set aside.
2. Bring a pot of salted water to a boil. Add pasta and cook until tender with a bite, about 9 minutes. Drain and hold in the strainer.
3. Bring a heavy-bottomed sauce pan to medium-low heat and add butter. Once melted, add shallots, garlic and Cajun seasoning. Stir to combine. Stir in lemon juice. Slowly add cream, stirring continually. Reduce heat; let the sauce simmer to thicken for about 3 minutes.
4. Remove from heat and stir in Parmesan. Add sausage to the sauce, then stir in noodles. Serve in shallow bowls with green onion or parsley garnish.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
One reviewer doubled the sausage and tripled the sauce (using a full 12 oz box of gluten-free spaghetti) and found the extra Cajun seasoning was not too much; the lemon flavor still came through. Another reviewer added a pinch each of sage, allspice, thyme and paprika for a more complex, "vintage New Orleans" seasoning blend, and suggested trying it with jumbo shrimp.
