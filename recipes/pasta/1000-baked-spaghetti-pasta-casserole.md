# $1000 Baked Spaghetti Pasta Casserole

## Description
A spaghetti casserole that can be put together ahead of time, layering cooked spaghetti with a Parmesan-mozzarella-sour cream mixture and a simmered meatball sauce, then baking until bubbly. The recipe is vegetarian thanks to plant-based meatballs, though regular meatballs can be substituted.

## Ingredients
_(for 6 servings)_

- 8 ounces spaghetti, cooked, drained and returned to pan
- 3/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese (divided)
- 3/4 cup grated mozzarella cheese (divided)
- 8 ounces sour cream
- 2 eggs, beaten
- 1 1/2 teaspoons Italian seasoning
- 20 ounces spaghetti sauce
- 1 ounce (1 packet) McCormick's Thick and Zesty Spaghetti Seasoning
- 1 1/2 packages (12.7 ounces) Gardein plant-based meatballs (or your favorite meatballs)
- 1 cup mozzarella cheese, for topping

## Instructions
1. In a large skillet, add the spaghetti seasoning to the sauce. Heat the sauce until simmering. Add meatballs. Simmer for 10 minutes. Cut the meatballs into small pieces. Reserve some sauce in case your casserole is a little dry, especially when reheating.
2. To your pan with cooked and drained spaghetti noodles, add 1/4 cup Parmesan cheese, 1/4 cup mozzarella cheese and stir.
3. In a medium bowl, mix sour cream, eggs, 1/2 cup mozzarella cheese, 1/2 cup Parmesan cheese and Italian seasoning.
4. Layer the cooked spaghetti in the bottom of a lightly greased 8x8-inch baking pan. Spread sour cream mixture on top with spatula. Cover with meatball sauce mixture. (You could double this recipe and cook it in a 13x9-inch baking pan.)
5. Bake uncovered in a preheated 350-degree F oven for 20 minutes. Add 1 cup mozzarella cheese and bake an additional 10 minutes.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
This recipe was originally intended to be cooked in a deep-dish pie pan; the author used an 8x8-inch baking pan instead. If you have a favorite homemade spaghetti sauce, substitute that for the jarred sauce and spaghetti seasoning. You could easily substitute ground beef meatballs or your favorite meatballs instead of the plant-based ones. Serve with a garden salad and fresh bread.
