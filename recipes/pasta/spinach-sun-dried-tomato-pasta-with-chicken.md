# Spinach-And-Sun-Dried Tomato Pasta

## Description
A pasta dinner studded with sweet sun-dried tomatoes, bright green spinach, and chunks of juicy roast chicken. The sauce starts with some of the reserved sun-dried tomato oil, adding color and savory flavor while cutting down on food waste; the pasta water poured over raw spinach in a colander is just enough heat to wilt it without overcooking.

## Ingredients
_(for 6 to 8 servings)_

- 5 cups chopped fresh spinach
- 1 lb. uncooked penne pasta
- 1 cup sliced oil-packed sun-dried tomatoes, plus 1 Tbsp. oil from the jar
- 1 cup chopped yellow onion
- 2 tsp. sliced garlic
- 2 Tbsp. leftover roasted garlic (from a Roasted Lemon-Pepper Chicken recipe, or see Cook's Notes to roast your own)
- 1 tsp. kosher salt
- 1/4 tsp. crushed red pepper
- 1 1/2 cups heavy whipping cream
- 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese, plus more to serve
- 2 cups leftover shredded Roasted Lemon-Pepper Chicken or rotisserie chicken

## Instructions
1. **Cook pasta and wilt spinach:** Place chopped fresh spinach in a colander in the sink. Cook penne pasta in boiling salted water according to package directions for al dente. Reserve 1 cup of the cooking water; drain the pasta over the spinach in the colander. Set aside.
2. **Cook tomatoes and onion:** Heat the sun-dried tomato oil in a stockpot over medium-high. Add the sliced sun-dried tomatoes and chopped yellow onion; cook, stirring often, until tender, about 4 minutes. Add the sliced garlic, leftover roasted garlic, kosher salt, and crushed red pepper; cook 1 minute.
3. **Finish sauce:** Add the heavy whipping cream; cook until reduced slightly, about 3 minutes.
4. **Combine ingredients:** Stir in the pasta, spinach, grated Parmesan cheese, and shredded chicken. Stir to combine; add the reserved pasta water gradually until the pasta is saucy.
5. **Season and serve:** Season to taste with kosher salt; serve with additional Parmesan.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
- Use a stockpot or a large Dutch oven — this recipe makes a very large quantity and needs a big enough vessel with tall sides to toss everything together without making a mess.
- The spinach is wilted by pouring the hot pasta and pasta water over it in a colander, rather than cooking it directly — just enough heat to wilt it without becoming overcooked, so the leaves stay vibrant.
- **How to roast garlic** (if not starting with leftover roasted garlic): Preheat oven to 450°F. Trim the top of a garlic head so the tops of the cloves are exposed. Place on a large piece of aluminum foil; drizzle with 2-3 tsp. olive oil. Sprinkle with a pinch of salt; wrap tightly in foil and seal. Place on a baking sheet and bake until the garlic is soft, about 45 minutes. Remove from the oven, open the foil, and let stand 5 minutes, then squeeze the cloves from the heads into a small bowl.
- Any leftover sun-dried tomato oil can be saved for a vinaigrette or drizzled over grilled chicken.
- The source recipe calls for leftover roasted garlic/chicken from its own "Roasted Lemon-Pepper Chicken" recipe, but explicitly lists plain rotisserie chicken as the substitute — this file uses that substitution, so no external recipe is required to cook it.
