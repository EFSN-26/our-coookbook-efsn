# Easy Gnocchi Alla Vodka With Burrata

## Description
Tender gnocchi simmered in a creamy homemade vodka tomato sauce and topped with burrata — a restaurant-quality dish that's rich, flavorful and comforting, ready in about 41 minutes. [Source](https://www.tastingtable.com/1794159/gnocchi-alla-vodka-burrata-recipe/)

## Ingredients
_(for 4 servings)_

- 4 tablespoons unsalted butter
- 1 small white onion, finely chopped
- 1/3 cup vodka
- 1 1/2 cups tomato puree
- 1/2 teaspoon salt
- 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
- 1/2 cup heavy cream
- 24 ounces gnocchi
- 1 (4-ounce) ball of burrata
- chopped parsley, for serving
- grated Parmesan, for serving (optional)

## Instructions
1. Heat the butter in a large, heavy skillet on medium.
2. Add the onion and saute until softened, about 5-6 minutes.
3. Pour in the vodka and let it cook for about 2 minutes while the alcohol evaporates.
4. Add the tomato puree, salt, and pepper, and bring the liquid back to a simmer. Reduce the heat to low and let simmer gently for 10-15 minutes, stirring occasionally, until reduced.
5. Pour in the cream and simmer for 5 more minutes, stirring well until the cream is evenly blended into the sauce.
6. While the sauce cooks, bring a large pot of salted water to a boil.
7. When the sauce is almost done, add the gnocchi to the water and let them cook until they float to the top, about 2-4 minutes. Try to time it so the gnocchi and the sauce will finish cooking at the same time.
8. Remove the gnocchi from the pot with a strainer and place them in the skillet with the sauce while it is still on the heat. Stir well to coat the gnocchi with sauce and then remove from the heat.
9. Break the burrata into pieces and place them on top of the gnocchi, stirring the sauce slightly to distribute and melt the cheese.
10. Spoon the gnocchi onto 4 individual pasta dishes and top with chopped parsley and grated Parmesan, if desired.
11. Serve immediately.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
- Not all supermarkets carry burrata; a simple substitute is pieces torn from a ball of mozzarella plus a little additional cream. Stracciatella can also be used.
- Other substitutes for burrata include cottage cheese, ricotta cheese, feta cheese and queso fresco (texture and flavor will differ). Vegans could use cashew cheese and make the sauce with olive oil and dairy-free cream instead of butter and heavy cream.
- Variations: some versions include meat or fish (smoked pancetta, crumbled sausage, smoked salmon, baked ham or shrimp); vegetarians could add smoked tofu. The white onion can be substituted with shallot, garlic or a different onion. Dried penne pasta can be used instead of gnocchi (adjust cooking time, as fresh gnocchi cooks much faster).
- To lower the fat content, reduce the amount of cream, substitute milk for part or all of the cream, or use low-fat/non-dairy substitutes like blended white beans, blended silken tofu, or onion cream.
- All the alcohol evaporates during cooking, so the dish won't get you drunk. White wine can substitute for vodka; non-alcoholic alternatives include broth, stock or white grape juice for flavor.
