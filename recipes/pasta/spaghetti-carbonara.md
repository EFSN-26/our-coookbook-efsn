# Authentic Spaghetti Carbonara

## Description
A classic Roman pasta made from just five pantry staples — spaghetti, guanciale, eggs, Pecorino Romano and black pepper — with no cream, emulsified into a silky sauce using the residual heat of the pasta. [Source](https://www.gamintraveler.com/2026/01/28/how-to-make-spaghetti-carbonara/)

## Ingredients
_(servings not specified by source)_

- 400 g (about 14 oz) spaghetti
- 150 g (about 5.3 oz) guanciale (cured pork cheek or jowl; pancetta can be used as a substitute)
- 3 large eggs
- 1 cup grated Pecorino Romano cheese (a mix of Pecorino and Parmesan can also be used)
- freshly ground black pepper (quantity not specified by source)
- salt, for the pasta water (quantity not specified by source)

## Instructions
1. Slice the guanciale into small pieces or strips. The fat will render out during cooking, adding flavor to the dish.
2. In a bowl, beat the eggs and mix in the grated Pecorino Romano cheese along with a generous amount of freshly ground black pepper. Set aside.
3. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Add the spaghetti and cook until al dente, usually about 1 minute less than the package instructions suggest.
4. While the pasta is cooking, place the guanciale in a cold skillet. Turn the heat to medium and cook until the fat renders out and the guanciale becomes crispy and golden. Remove from the heat and set aside.
5. Once the spaghetti is cooked, reserve 1 cup of pasta water and then drain the pasta. Add the hot, drained spaghetti to the skillet with the guanciale. Toss well to coat the pasta in the rendered fat.
6. Remove the skillet from the heat. Quickly pour the egg and cheese mixture over the hot pasta, stirring vigorously. The heat from the pasta will gently cook the eggs, creating a creamy sauce. If the sauce is too thick, add a little reserved pasta water to loosen it.
7. Serve immediately, with extra Pecorino Romano and freshly ground black pepper on top.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
- Traditional carbonara does not include cream — the creaminess comes entirely from the emulsion of eggs, cheese and starchy pasta water.
- It's crucial to mix the pasta and egg mixture off the heat to avoid scrambling the eggs.
- Guanciale (cured pork cheek/jowl) gives the dish its signature richness; pancetta works in a pinch but is not a traditional substitute.
- The source states a standard serving of spaghetti carbonara might range from 650 to 750 calories, but does not specify how this maps to the ingredient quantities above.
- The source states the dish "can be completed in under thirty minutes" with proper preparation, without breaking this down into separate prep/cook figures.
- Best served and eaten immediately — the source notes carbonara doesn't hold up well for reheating.
