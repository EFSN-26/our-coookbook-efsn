# Creamy Boursin Orzo with Spinach

## Description
An ultra-creamy orzo side dish made with garlic-herb Boursin cheese, evaporated milk, and spinach — just six ingredients and about 15 minutes from start to finish. [Source](https://30seconds.com/food/tip/97425/Creamy-Boursin-Orzo-Recipe-With-Spinach-Is-a-Satisfying-Side-Dish-6-Ingredients-15-Minutes)

## Ingredients
_(for 6 servings)_

- 1 tablespoon butter
- 1 cup orzo, uncooked
- 14.5 ounces chicken broth (1 can)
- 5.3 ounces Boursin cheese (1 box)
- 1/2 cup evaporated milk (or heavy cream)
- 10 ounces frozen spinach, thawed and squeezed dry (or fresh baby spinach)
- Salt and black pepper, to taste

## Instructions
1. Melt the butter in a skillet. Add the orzo and cook, stirring, for 1 minute.
2. Pour in the chicken broth. Bring to a simmer, reduce the heat, and cook for 8 to 10 minutes, or until the orzo is tender.
3. Add the Boursin cheese and evaporated milk.
4. Stir until the cheese melts.
5. Add the spinach.
6. Stir until everything is incorporated and creamy. Season with salt and black pepper.
7. Serve immediately.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
- Boursin is a spreadable French cheese created in 1963 by cheesemaker François Boursin, inspired by fromage frais blended with garlic, cream, herbs, salt, and pepper.
- Variation: stir in sun-dried tomatoes.
- Add crushed red pepper flakes for a little heat.
- Pairs well as a side with chicken, beef, pork, lamb, venison, turkey, or seafood.
- Store leftovers in an airtight container in the fridge; add a splash more milk when reheating to bring back the creaminess.
