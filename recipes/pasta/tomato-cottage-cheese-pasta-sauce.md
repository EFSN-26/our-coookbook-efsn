# Tomato Cottage Cheese Pasta Sauce

## Description
A silky, high-protein tomato pasta sauce that gets its creaminess from blended cottage cheese instead of heavy cream — ready in under 30 minutes with pantry staples. [Source](https://lentillovingfamily.com/tomato-cottage-cheese-pasta-sauce/)

## Ingredients
_(for 4 servings, about 2-2.5 cups of sauce)_

#### For the sauce:
- 1 tablespoon olive oil
- 3 cloves garlic, minced
- 1/2 teaspoon red chili flakes, optional
- 1 can (14.5 oz) crushed tomatoes (or fire-roasted tomatoes)
- 2 tablespoons tomato paste
- 1 teaspoon dried oregano
- Salt and pepper, to taste
- 1 cup full-fat cottage cheese
- 1/4 cup Parmesan cheese, grated, optional
- Fresh basil leaves, for garnish

#### For the pasta:
- 8 oz rigatoni or pasta of choice
- 1/2 cup reserved pasta water, as needed for consistency

## Instructions
1. Cook the pasta in salted water until al dente. Reserve 1/2 cup of pasta water before draining.
2. In a skillet, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the garlic and red chili flakes and sauté for about 1 minute until fragrant.
3. Add the crushed tomatoes, tomato paste, oregano, salt, and pepper. Stir and simmer for 8-10 minutes until slightly thickened.
4. Let the tomato mixture cool slightly, then transfer it to a high-speed blender. Add the cottage cheese and Parmesan, and blend until completely smooth and creamy.
5. Return the sauce to the skillet over low heat. Add pasta water as needed to adjust the consistency.
6. Toss in the cooked pasta and stir until coated. Let it sit off the heat for 1-2 minutes before serving. Top with fresh basil leaves and more Parmesan just before serving.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
- Use full-fat cottage cheese for the creamiest texture; low-fat or non-fat will work but the sauce will be thinner and less rich.
- Blend while the tomato mixture is still warm (not hot) — cold cheese lumps don't blend as well.
- Add a pinch of sugar or a teaspoon of balsamic vinegar if your tomatoes taste too acidic.
- Store leftover sauce (without pasta) in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 4 days, or freeze for up to 3 months; reheat gently with a splash of pasta water or broth.
- Once blended, the sauce does not taste like cottage cheese — the curdy texture disappears entirely.
- Approximate nutrition per the source (per serving): 362 kcal, 18 g protein, 55 g carbohydrates, 9 g fat.
