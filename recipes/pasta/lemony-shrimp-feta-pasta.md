# Lemony Shrimp Feta Pasta

## Description
A lemony, cheesy shrimp and pasta dish, lighter and brighter than typical heavy tomato or pesto-based pastas, with a kick from crushed red pepper. Inspired by Real Simple, it comes together in just 20 minutes.

## Ingredients
_(for 4 servings)_

- 1 pound shrimp, peeled, deveined, tail removed
- 8 ounces angel hair pasta
- 3 tablespoons olive oil
- ½ cup black olives, sliced
- 3 cloves garlic, finely chopped
- ¼ teaspoon kosher salt
- ¼ teaspoon crushed red pepper flakes, plus more for serving
- ¼ teaspoon freshly ground black pepper
- 2 teaspoons lemon zest, divided
- ⅛ cup fresh lemon juice
- ¼ cup grated Parmesan cheese
- ¼ cup crumbled feta cheese

## Instructions
1. Cook pasta according to the al dente directions on the package. Drain, reserving ¾ cup of the pasta water. (Don't forget to reserve some pasta water, as the dish will become too dry without it.)
2. Heat oil over medium heat in a large, high-sided skillet and add the shrimp. About 2 minutes into cooking, add the olives, garlic, salt, crushed red pepper flakes, black pepper, and 1 teaspoon lemon zest. Cook the shrimp mixture until the shrimp is no longer pink, about 5 minutes total, stirring often. Mix in the lemon juice and remove the skillet from heat.
3. Add the pasta and ½ cup of the reserved pasta water to the shrimp mixture in the skillet. Gradually stir in the Parmesan cheese. The Parmesan and water will create a light sauce (add more pasta water as needed).
4. Serve the dish sprinkled with feta and the remaining 1 teaspoon lemon zest. Top with more crushed red pepper flakes before serving, if desired.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
- This recipe is easy to double or triple for dinner parties.
- Store the pasta in an airtight container in the fridge and reheat in the microwave.
- Great served solo or with a salad and a chunk of bread.
