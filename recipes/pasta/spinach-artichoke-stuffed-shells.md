# Spinach Artichoke Stuffed Shells

## Description
These Spinach Artichoke Stuffed Shells are filled with creamy spinach artichoke goodness and topped with a homemade Alfredo sauce and melty mozzarella. It's a comforting dish perfect for casual weeknights, but impressive enough for special occasions.

## Ingredients
_(for 8 servings)_

### For the Alfredo Sauce
- 1 1/2 cups parmesan cheese, grated
- 1 1/2 cups heavy cream
- 1 cup whole milk
- 2 cloves garlic, minced
- 2 tablespoons butter
- 1/4 teaspoon salt
- 1/4 teaspoon ground black pepper

### For the Stuffed Shells
- 20 large shell pasta
- 15 ounces whole milk ricotta
- 2 cups shredded mozzarella cheese, divided
- 9 ounces frozen spinach, thawed and drained
- 1 cup parmesan cheese, grated
- 1/2 cup artichoke hearts, chopped
- 3 cloves garlic, grated
- 1/2 teaspoon salt
- 1/4 teaspoon ground black pepper

## Instructions
1. Heat a large pot of salted water to boil, then boil the shells according to the package instructions. Cook them just before al dente so they don't overcook in the oven.
2. While the shells are cooking, add the butter to a saucepan over medium heat, and once melted, add the garlic. Sauté for 20 seconds, making sure not to brown the garlic, or it will become bitter.
3. Pour in the milk, cream, grated parmesan cheese, salt and ground black pepper. Simmer over medium heat for 5 minutes or until thickened enough to coat the back of a spoon, then set aside to cool slightly.
4. Preheat oven to 375°F.
5. Add the ricotta cheese, 1 cup of the shredded mozzarella cheese, parmesan cheese, thawed and well-drained spinach, chopped artichoke hearts, grated garlic, salt and ground black pepper to a large mixing bowl. Mix until well combined.
6. Once the shells are cool enough to handle, fill each shell with the mixture, making sure not to overstuff them.
7. Add most of the Alfredo sauce to the bottom of a greased 9x13 casserole baking dish. Arrange the stuffed shells over the sauce, placing them close together. Drizzle the remaining sauce over the tops of the shells.
8. Cover with aluminum foil and bake for 25 minutes.
9. Remove from the oven and uncover the casserole. Increase the temperature to 425°F and add the remaining 1 cup of shredded mozzarella cheese on top. Bake uncovered for another 10-12 minutes or until the cheese is golden and bubbly.
10. Cool slightly before serving, as the filling in the shells will be piping hot.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
- Cook the shells al dente — slightly undercooking the pasta helps it hold its shape when baked.
- Thoroughly drain the spinach — excess water can make the filling soggy.
- Use freshly grated parmesan cheese — it melts more smoothly and adds better flavor than pre-shredded varieties.
- Stuff evenly and avoid overstuffing the shells to prevent splitting during baking.
- Bake covered first — this helps the shells stay moist while the cheese melts beautifully.
- The Alfredo Sauce recipe makes about 2 1/2 cups and can be used in other recipes or served over pasta.
- Storage: fridge leftovers keep up to 3 days, reheat in the oven or microwave. Freeze unbaked stuffed shells tightly wrapped for up to 2 months; bake from frozen, adding 15-20 minutes to the cook time.
- Can be assembled a day ahead, covered, and refrigerated; bake as directed when ready.
- If the sauce thickens too much, add a splash of milk or cream to loosen it.
- Jarred Alfredo sauce can be substituted, though homemade adds richer flavor.
