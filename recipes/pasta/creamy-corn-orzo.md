# Creamy Corn Orzo

## Description
A one-pot, risotto-style orzo highlighting peak summer corn, cooked lazy-risotto style with broth added gradually, then finished with mascarpone and Pecorino Romano. Excerpted from Hailee Catalano's cookbook "By Heart." [Source](https://www.thekitchn.com/creamy-corn-orzo-recipe-review-23736100)

## Ingredients
_(for 4 servings)_

- 3 tablespoons unsalted butter
- 2 large shallots, minced
- 2 cloves garlic, minced
- 2 ears fresh sweet corn, kernels removed (about 2 cups)
- Kosher salt and freshly cracked black pepper, to taste
- 8 ounces (1 cup) dry orzo
- 3 cups unsalted chicken broth, homemade or store-bought, at room temperature
- 3 ounces mascarpone cheese
- 1/3 cup finely grated Pecorino Romano cheese, plus more for serving
- Mild chile flakes or powder, to taste
- Extra-virgin olive oil, for drizzling

## Instructions
1. Heat the butter in a large pot over medium-low heat until melted. Add the shallots and garlic, and cook until softened, 3 or 4 minutes.
2. Add the corn, and cook until it starts to soften, 2 or 3 minutes. Season lightly with salt and pepper.
3. Add the orzo, and stir to coat it in the butter. Add about 1 cup chicken broth, and cook, stirring frequently while scraping the bottom of the pan, until most of the liquid is absorbed, about 5 minutes.
4. Add another 1 cup broth, and continue to cook, stirring frequently while scraping the bottom, until most of the liquid is absorbed, about 5 minutes.
5. Repeat with the last 1 cup broth, and cook until the orzo is al dente, most of the broth has been absorbed, and the mixture has a loose, stew-y risotto texture, 15 to 20 minutes. Maintain a simmer (not a full boil) throughout so the pasta cooks gently and the liquid doesn't cook off before the pasta absorbs it.
6. Reduce the heat to low, and stir in the mascarpone and Pecorino Romano. Season with salt and pepper.
7. Garnish with more Pecorino Romano, a pinch of chile flakes, and a drizzle of extra-virgin olive oil, and serve.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
- The reviewer used Guntur Sannam Chilli from Diaspora Co. for garnish, but Aleppo or Espelette pepper would also work well.
- Riffable base recipe: add tender herbs like basil, top with diced tomatoes, or serve alongside grilled steak, fish, or chicken.
