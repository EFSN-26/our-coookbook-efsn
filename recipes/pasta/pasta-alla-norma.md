# Pasta alla Norma

## Description
Pasta alla Norma is one of Sicily's classic pasta dishes, combining sauteed eggplant with a rich tomato sauce, tossed with pasta and topped with grated ricotta salata cheese and fresh basil. [Source](https://www.gamintraveler.com/2026/05/01/how-to-make-pasta-alla-norma/)

## Ingredients
_(for servings not specified by source)_

- 12 oz (340g) pasta (spaghetti, rigatoni, or penne work well)
- 1 large eggplant, diced into 1-inch cubes
- 3 tablespoons olive oil
- 3 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 can (14 oz) crushed tomatoes or 4 fresh tomatoes, diced
- Salt and black pepper, to taste
- Fresh basil leaves, chopped (about 1/4 cup, plus extra for garnish)
- 1/2 teaspoon red pepper flakes (optional for a spicy kick)
- 1/2 cup grated ricotta salata or Pecorino Romano cheese

## Instructions
1. Place diced eggplant in a colander, sprinkle with salt, and let it sit for 20-30 minutes to draw out excess moisture and reduce bitterness. Rinse thoroughly under cold water to remove the salt, then pat dry with a paper towel.
2. In a large skillet, heat 2 tablespoons of olive oil over medium-high heat. Add the eggplant and cook until golden brown and tender, about 8-10 minutes. Remove the eggplant from the skillet and set aside.
3. In the same skillet, add the remaining tablespoon of olive oil. Add minced garlic and red pepper flakes (if using), and saute for 1-2 minutes until fragrant.
4. Add the crushed tomatoes to the skillet, stirring well. Season with salt and black pepper. Let the sauce simmer for 10-15 minutes, until it thickens slightly and flavors combine.
5. While the sauce is simmering, cook the pasta in a large pot of salted boiling water according to package instructions until al dente. Reserve a cup of pasta water, then drain the pasta.
6. Stir the cooked eggplant and chopped basil into the tomato sauce. Add the drained pasta to the skillet with the sauce, tossing to combine. If the sauce is too thick, add a splash of the reserved pasta water to reach desired consistency.
7. Divide the pasta among plates and sprinkle with grated ricotta salata or Pecorino Romano. Garnish with extra basil leaves and a drizzle of olive oil if desired.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
- Fresh, firm eggplant holds its shape and texture better; salting it helps remove excess moisture and improves flavor.
- Ricotta salata adds a nice salty contrast; if unavailable, Pecorino Romano or feta can be used as substitutes.
- A pinch of red pepper flakes is optional but adds a slight complementary heat.
- Adding a splash of reserved pasta water to the sauce helps bind it to the pasta for a silkier texture.
- Variation: after combining pasta and sauce, transfer to a baking dish, top with cheese, and bake at 375°F (190°C) for 10-15 minutes for a casserole-style version. Capers or black olives can be added to the sauce for an extra Mediterranean twist.
