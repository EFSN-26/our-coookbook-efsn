# Prisoner's Pasta (Pasta alla Carcerata)

## Description
A lesser-known Roman rigatoni dish — *Pasta alla Carcerata* — built around Italian pork sausage in a creamy tomato and brandy sauce, finished with Parmesan and fresh parsley. [Source](https://www.masalaherb.com/prisoners-pasta-is-a-forgotten-roman-dish-making-a-quiet-comeback)

## Ingredients
_(for 3 servings)_

**To boil pasta**
- 3 cups rigatoni (or another tubular pasta such as tortiglioni, penne, or mezzi rigatoni)
- dash of salt
- water

**For the meat sauce**
- 3/4 pound Italian sausage (salsiccia), with or without fennel or chili pepper
- 1 tablespoon olive oil
- 1 medium onion, sliced or diced
- 1/2 tablespoon sweet paprika (or cayenne pepper, for heat)
- 1/8 teaspoon black pepper
- 1/3 cup brandy
- 1 1/2 cup tomato sauce or crushed tomatoes, unflavored
- 1/2 cup heavy cream or table cream
- salt, to taste
- Parmesan, shaved or grated, to top
- fresh Italian parsley, chopped, to top

## Instructions
1. Cook the pasta as per package instructions in boiling salted water. Strain when done and set aside.
2. While the pasta is boiling, prepare the meat sauce. If the sausage is in a casing, remove it and break it into smaller chunks.
3. Heat a skillet with olive oil and sauté the sliced/diced onion over medium heat until a bit soft.
4. Stir in the sausage pieces and season with paprika (or cayenne) and black pepper. Combine and cook for a minute or two.
5. Reduce the heat if it's too intense, and deglaze with the brandy.
6. Add the tomato sauce (or crushed tomatoes) and cream. Mix well and increase heat to medium if needed.
7. Season with salt to taste and let simmer for at least 3 to 5 minutes so the flavors develop.
8. Take the sauce off the heat. Toss the cooked pasta in the sauce so it's fully coated.
9. Top with freshly chopped parsley and shaved or grated Parmesan. Serve hot.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
- Makes about 2-3 servings depending on serving size.
- Prep time is about 5 minutes, but 10 minutes if the sausage needs to be removed from its casing.
- Prepare the sauce in a pan while the pasta cooks to save time — about 10 minutes for the pasta and 12 minutes to prep the sauce.
- 7 ounces of pasta = 3 cups = 200 grams, per the source.
- The source's nutrition estimate (per serving, one of an assumed 3): 776 calories, 45 g fat, 57 g carbohydrates, 22 g protein — provided by the source's recipe card and not independently verified.
- This dish is a full meal on its own; the source suggests pairing with only a small tangy side salad.
- Can be meal-prepped and stored in the refrigerator or frozen; don't top with parsley or Parmesan until ready to serve. Reheat gradually in the oven or microwave.
