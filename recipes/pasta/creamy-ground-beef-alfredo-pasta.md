# Creamy Ground Beef Alfredo Pasta

## Description
A rich, creamy twist on chicken alfredo that uses ground beef instead — tender beef in a sea of alfredo sauce that one taster likened to beef stroganoff. It's on the table in about 20 minutes with just 8 ingredients.

## Ingredients
_(for 6 servings)_

- 8 ounces spaghetti noodles
- 1 pound ground beef
- 4 cloves garlic, minced
- 1/2 teaspoon Italian seasoning
- 1 cup heavy cream
- 1/2 cup Parmesan cheese, grated
- 1/2 cup Italian cheese blend or mozzarella, grated
- 2 tablespoons butter

## Instructions
1. Cook the pasta according to package directions for al dente. Drain and set aside. (Any pasta shape works, such as fettuccine, linguine, rotini, fusilli, bowties or shells.)
2. While the pasta cooks, brown the ground beef in a large skillet, about 4 to 5 minutes. Drain any fat.
3. Add the garlic and Italian seasoning. Cook, stirring, about 1 minute. Season with salt and black pepper.
4. Turn the heat to medium-low. Add the heavy cream, butter and both cheeses. (Evaporated milk can be used instead of heavy cream; for a creamier pasta use 1 1/4 to 1 1/2 cups of heavy cream.)
5. Stir and cook until the cheese has melted and the sauce is creamy.
6. Add the pasta to the sauce. (For some spice, add crushed red pepper flakes.)
7. Stir to combine. Serve hot with more Parmesan cheese, if desired, and a sprinkle of parsley for color.
8. Store leftovers in an airtight container in the fridge.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
Any pasta shape can be used in place of spaghetti. Evaporated milk can substitute for heavy cream. Adjust the amount of heavy cream (1 to 1 1/2 cups) for desired creaminess. Crushed red pepper flakes can be added for heat.
