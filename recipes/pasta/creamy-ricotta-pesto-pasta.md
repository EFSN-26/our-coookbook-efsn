# Creamy Ricotta Pesto Pasta

## Description
A wonderfully creamy Italian pasta dinner where ricotta meets a fresh nutty basil pesto for a dish that's simple, filling, and on the table in about 20 minutes.

## Ingredients
_(for 6 servings — see §8 for scaling)_

- 3 cups fresh basil leaves
- ⅓ cup extra virgin olive oil, plus more if needed
- 2 cloves garlic
- ⅓ cup grated Romano or Parmesan cheese
- ¼ cup roasted pecans, macadamia nuts, or pine nuts
- ½ – 1 teaspoon salt (or to taste)
- 16 ounces pasta, cooked to al dente according to package directions (reserve 1 cup pasta water)
- 15 ounces ricotta cheese

## Instructions
1. Put the fresh basil, cheese, nuts, and salt into a food processor or blender. Pulse to combine. Drizzle in the olive oil until the pesto is thinned and blended together. Taste and add more salt and black pepper as needed. (For more flavor, toast the nuts in a dry skillet until fragrant, being careful not to burn them. In a pinch, store-bought basil pesto can be used instead.)
2. Add the ricotta and pesto to the cooked pasta in a bowl and toss to combine.
3. Use a bit of the reserved pasta water to thin the mixture if desired, adding only a little at a time. Serve with crusty bread and a sprinkle of more Parmesan.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
- Any pasta shape or type works — the source used a gluten-free pasta, but regular pasta is fine too.
- Don't overcook the pasta or it becomes mushy and loses the texture layered into the dish.
- Serve with a simple salad or a side dish like broccolini for a complete meal.
- Store in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 4 days.
