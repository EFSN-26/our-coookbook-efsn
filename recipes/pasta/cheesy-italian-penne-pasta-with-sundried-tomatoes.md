# Cheesy Italian Penne Pasta with Sundried Tomatoes

## Description
A quick, creamy penne pasta with sundried tomatoes and a cheesy sauce made from evaporated milk and an Italian cheese blend - five ingredients, ready in 15 minutes.

## Ingredients
_(for 4 servings — see §8 for scaling)_

- 8 ounces penne pasta
- 1/3 cup sundried tomatoes, cut up (drained if in oil)
- 12 ounces (1 can) evaporated milk
- 8 ounces Italian cheese blend, or feta cheese crumbles for a Greek-inspired variation
- 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder
- grated Parmesan cheese, for serving, optional
- fresh parsley, for serving, optional

## Instructions
1. Cook the pasta according to package directions. Drain. (Any short pasta works.)
2. While the pasta cooks, put the sundried tomatoes, milk, cheese, and garlic powder into a large saucepan. Cook over low heat until the cheese has melted. Season with salt and pepper. (If you don't have evaporated milk, you can substitute heavy cream.)
3. Add the pasta. Toss to combine. Serve with freshly grated Parmesan cheese and garnish with fresh parsley, if desired. Store leftovers in an airtight container in the fridge and reheat in the microwave.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
- For some crunch, add pine nuts or walnuts.
- Fresh chopped tomatoes can be used instead of sundried tomatoes.
- Swap the Italian cheese blend for feta cheese crumbles for a Greek-inspired twist.
- Serve as a high-protein Italian dinner alongside a salad and garlic bread.
