# Sausage-Broccoli Pasta

## Description
A weeknight staple built from just four ingredients — pasta, Italian sausage, broccoli, and garlic — that the author's husband requests on repeat. All the flavor comes from generous garlic, good olive oil, and spicy Italian sausage rather than a sauce. [Source](https://www.southernliving.com/sausage-broccoli-4-ingredient-pasta-11849223)

## Ingredients
_(for servings not specified by source)_

- 1/2 box pasta (any shape — the author has used angel hair, linguine, spaghetti, and orecchiette), (exact weight not specified by source)
- 1 head broccoli, cut into small, thin florets
- 8-10 cloves garlic (or a whole head), thinly sliced
- Italian sausage, removed from casing (quantity not specified by source; author prefers a hot variety, e.g. Aldi Hot Italian Sausage Links)
- Olive oil, generously, at each cooking stage (quantity not specified by source — use a high-quality oil)
- Salt, to taste (does not count toward the 4-ingredient total per source)
- Parmesan cheese, for topping (quantity not specified by source)

## Instructions
1. Boil about half a box of pasta. Don't overboil — aim for al dente or slightly under, since it will cook a little more with the other ingredients at the end.
2. Cut one head of broccoli into small, thin florets (about 1/2 to 1/3 the size of typical bite-sized pieces) so it has no crunch and cooks quickly.
3. Peel and thinly slice 8 to 10 cloves of garlic.
4. Add the garlic and a generous amount of olive oil to a pan. Cook on medium heat for a couple of minutes.
5. Add the broccoli and more olive oil. Cook until the broccoli starts to soften.
6. Remove the sausage from its casing and add it to the pan, breaking it into small crumbles with a wooden spatula. Cook until both broccoli and sausage are done.
7. Add the cooked pasta and more olive oil. Toss everything together.
8. Top with Parmesan cheese and serve.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
- Any pasta shape works fine.
- Use an olive oil that's high enough quality that you'd want to dip crusty bread in it — it and the garlic carry the dish since there's no separate sauce.
- If you can only find mild Italian sausage, add a couple dashes of red pepper flakes to make up for the lost spice.
