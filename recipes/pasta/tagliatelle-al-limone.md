# Tagliatelle al Limone (Lemon Sauce)

## Description
A classic Amalfi Coast pasta dish: fresh tagliatelle tossed in a creamy lemon sauce that balances bright citrus with rich Parmesan and butter (or cream) for silkiness. Comes together quickly with just a handful of high-quality ingredients. [Source](https://www.gamintraveler.com/2025/07/05/how-to-make-tagliatelle-al-limone-lemon-sauce/)

## Ingredients
_(servings not specified by source)_

For the Tagliatelle:
- 400 g (14 oz) fresh or dried tagliatelle pasta
- Salt, for the pasta water

For the Lemon Sauce:
- 2 medium lemons (zest and juice)
- 1/2 cup heavy cream (or light cream for a lighter version)
- 3 tablespoons unsalted butter
- 1/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese, plus extra for garnish
- Salt and freshly ground black pepper, to taste
- 2 tablespoons olive oil (optional, for added richness)
- 2 tablespoons chopped fresh parsley or basil (optional, for garnish)

## Instructions
1. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Add the tagliatelle and cook until al dente, following package instructions. Reserve 1/2 cup of pasta water before draining.
2. In a large skillet over medium heat, melt the butter. If using olive oil, add it to the butter for extra flavor. Stir in the lemon zest and juice, letting it simmer for 1–2 minutes to infuse the butter.
3. Reduce the heat to low and stir in the heavy cream. Simmer gently for 2–3 minutes, allowing the sauce to thicken slightly.
4. Add salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste. Stir in the Parmesan cheese, which will further thicken the sauce.
5. Add the cooked tagliatelle to the skillet with the lemon sauce. Toss gently to coat the pasta evenly. Add a splash of reserved pasta water if the sauce is too thick. Heat for 1–2 minutes to ensure the pasta absorbs the flavors.
6. Divide the pasta among plates. Garnish with additional Parmesan cheese, fresh parsley or basil, and a sprinkle of lemon zest. Serve immediately.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
- Use fresh lemon juice and zest, not bottled, for the brightest flavor.
- Cream sauces can separate if overheated, so keep the heat low.
- Adjust lemon juice to taste; add more Parmesan for a creamier sauce.
- For added substance, serve with grilled chicken, shrimp, or seared scallops.
- Some purists make this without cream, using only butter, Parmesan, and lemon; the version above includes cream for a modern, richer take.
- Source's approximate calorie estimate: 400–450 calories total (tagliatelle ~200 calories, lemon sauce ~200–250 calories, depending on butter/cream/Parmesan amounts).
