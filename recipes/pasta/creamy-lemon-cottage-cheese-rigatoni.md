# This Creamy Lemon Rigatoni Is All I'm Making This Week for Dinner

## Description
A Kitchn writer's take on Giada De Laurentiis's "Pasta alla Giadzy" — a creamy, lemony rigatoni that's part pesto, part alfredo, made in a food processor with toasted almonds, a whole chopped lemon, and Parmesan. The Kitchn's version swaps in cottage cheese for the ricotta in Giada's original recipe, a substitution the writer says worked so well that "once blended, the sauce does not taste like cottage cheese."

## Ingredients
_(for 6 servings, per the original "Pasta alla Giadzy" recipe this article is based on, adapted with a cottage cheese swap)_

- 1 cup slivered almonds
- 2 - 3 cloves garlic, smashed and peeled (the reviewer used 3 instead of the original 2 for more garlic flavor)
- 1 whole lemon (Meyer lemon in the original recipe), scrubbed clean, ends trimmed, roughly chopped, skin and seeds included
- 1 cup cottage cheese (full-fat; substituted 1:1 for the ricotta in Giada's original recipe)
- 1/3 cup heavy cream
- 1/2 teaspoon chili flakes
- 1 1/2 cups grated Parmesan cheese, divided, plus more for garnish
- 1/3 cup extra virgin olive oil
- 1 teaspoon kosher salt
- 1 pound rigatoni (mezzi rigatoni in the original)
- 1/2 cup fresh basil, torn, plus more for garnish
- Reserved pasta water, as needed

## Instructions
1. Add the slivered almonds to a medium skillet and toast over medium heat, stirring occasionally, until fragrant and lightly toasted, about 5 minutes. Remove to a plate to cool slightly.
2. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil.
3. Add the cooled almonds and garlic to a food processor and pulse until finely chopped. Add the chopped lemon and pulse again until finely chopped.
4. Add the cottage cheese, heavy cream, chili flakes, 1 cup of the Parmesan, olive oil, and salt to the food processor. Blend until completely smooth and combined. Transfer the sauce to a large mixing bowl.
5. Cook the rigatoni in the boiling water until al dente, according to package directions. Before draining, reserve at least 1 cup of the pasta water.
6. Add the drained hot pasta directly to the bowl with the sauce, along with the remaining 1/2 cup of Parmesan. Stir until every noodle is coated and glossy, adding reserved pasta water as needed to loosen the sauce.
7. Top with torn basil and extra grated Parmesan before serving.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
- The Kitchn's reviewer substituted cottage cheese for the ricotta called for in Giada's original recipe and "highly recommend[s] the substitution" — once blended, the sauce doesn't taste like cottage cheese at all.
- Reserving pasta water is essential: the blended sauce is very thick, and pasta water loosens it to a glossy consistency that coats the noodles.
- Rigatoni is ideal because its ridges and center tube trap the sauce well.
- The reviewer used 3 cloves of garlic instead of 2 for a stronger garlic flavor — adjust to taste.
- No stovetop sauce-cooking is required beyond toasting the almonds; the sauce comes together entirely in a food processor.
