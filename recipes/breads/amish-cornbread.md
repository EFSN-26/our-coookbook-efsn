# Amish Cornbread

## Description
A sweet, moist cornbread recipe sourced from a community cookbook — simple pantry ingredients mixed together and baked until it melts in your mouth. [Source](https://30seconds.com/food/tip/55586/Easy-Amish-Cornbread-Recipe-Melts-In-Your-Mouth-8-Ingredients-30-Minutes)

## Ingredients
_(for 9 servings)_

- 3/4 cup yellow cornmeal
- 1 cup flour (all-purpose or gluten-free)
- 1/2 cup granulated sugar
- 1 teaspoon salt
- 3 1/2 teaspoons baking powder
- 2 tablespoons melted butter
- 2 eggs
- 1 cup milk

## Instructions
1. Whisk together the cornmeal, flour, sugar, salt and baking powder in a mixing bowl.
2. In another bowl, whisk together the eggs, butter and milk. (Cheese or fresh jalapeños can be stirred in for extra flavor.)
3. Add the wet ingredients to the dry ingredients until just combined — do not overmix. Pour into a greased 9x9-inch baking pan.
4. Bake in a preheated 400-degree F oven for about 20 to 25 minutes. Store the cornbread in an airtight container.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
- This is a sweet cornbread; reduce the sugar if you prefer it less sweet, or try an unsweetened skillet cornbread instead.
- For cornbread muffins, fill 12 greased or lined muffin cups about 3/4 full and bake for about 19 to 20 minutes.
- Do not overbake or overmix for the moistest results.
- Great as a side dish with chili, soup or stew, or even served as a dessert with honey.
- Use gluten-free flour to make this recipe gluten-free.
