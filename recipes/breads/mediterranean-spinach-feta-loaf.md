# Savory Mediterranean Spinach Feta Loaf

## Description
A moist, high-protein savory bread loaf packed with spinach, bell pepper, feta and mozzarella, with Greek yogurt mixed into the batter for extra protein — hearty enough to feel like a meal rather than just a snack. [Source](https://30seconds.com/food/tip/73996/Savory-Mediterranean-Spinach-Feta-Loaf-Recipe-Is-Almost-a-Meal-In-Itself)

## Ingredients
_(for 10 servings — see §8 for scaling)_

- 1 tablespoon plus 1/4 cup extra virgin olive oil (divided)
- 8 ounces fresh baby spinach
- 3/4 cup onions, diced
- 3/4 cup yellow bell peppers, diced
- 1 1/2 teaspoons minced garlic
- 2 eggs, lightly beaten
- 3/4 cup low-fat plain Greek yogurt
- 1 1/2 cups flour (all-purpose or gluten-free)
- 2 teaspoons baking powder
- 1/2 teaspoon salt
- 8 ounces feta cheese, crumbled
- 1/2 cup shredded mozzarella cheese
- 2 tablespoons fresh dill, diced

## Instructions
1. In a large skillet or sauté pan with sides, heat 1 tablespoon of the olive oil on medium-high heat. Add the onions, peppers and garlic. Sauté until crisp-tender, about 4 minutes.
2. Add the spinach and sauté until wilted, making sure it's mixed in with the other vegetables. Remove from heat and place the mixture onto a plate lined with paper towels to remove excess moisture. (You can use 10 ounces of frozen spinach instead of fresh — thaw and squeeze out the moisture first.)
3. In a large mixing bowl, add the eggs, remaining 1/4 cup olive oil and the Greek yogurt. Whisk to blend.
4. Add the flour, baking powder and salt (and black pepper, to taste, if desired). Mix well to combine. (Use gluten-free flour to make this bread gluten-free.)
5. Add the feta, mozzarella and dill. Stir to combine.
6. Press any remaining moisture out of the vegetable mixture and add it to the dough. Mix well to combine.
7. Evenly spread the batter into an 8x4-inch loaf pan lined with parchment paper.
8. Bake at 350°F for about 50 to 60 minutes, or until it passes the toothpick test. (Cover with aluminum foil if the top browns too quickly.)
9. Allow the loaf to cool to room temperature before slicing and serving. Store in an airtight container in the refrigerator; leftovers are great toasted or warmed in the oven.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
- Serve as a vegetable side dish with dinner, a savory snack, or even for breakfast or brunch.
- The source enjoyed it toasted with butter, and even served with chicken salad in place of regular bread.
- Greek yogurt and the two cheeses make this loaf extra dense and moist.
