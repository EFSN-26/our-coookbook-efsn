# Whole-Loaf Cheesy Garlic Bread

## Description
An entire loaf of country-style bread is crosshatched, basted with garlic-herb butter, and stuffed with cheese, served alongside its own head of roasted garlic for spreading. The recipe comes from Tartine bakery co-founder Elisabeth Prueitt's cookbook *Tartine All Day: Modern Recipes for the Home Cook*, as featured in a Gear Patrol article by Emily Singer. [Source](https://gearpatrol.com/2017/04/07/cheesy-garlic-bread-recipe/)

## Ingredients
_(for 4-8 servings)_

- 1 oval or round loaf of country-style bread
- 1–2 cups grated semi-firm or firm cheese (such as raclette, fontina, cheddar, or gruyère)
- 3/4 cup unsalted butter (plus more as needed)
- 2 full heads garlic, plus 4 cloves chopped
- 2 tablespoons olive oil
- 1 teaspoon plus 2 pinches sea salt
- 1/4 teaspoon ground black pepper
- 2 tablespoons chopped flat-leaf parsley
- 1 teaspoon chopped fresh thyme
- 1/4 teaspoon chopped fresh rosemary

## Instructions
1. Preheat the oven to 375°F. Cut the top quarter off of the 2 garlic heads and rub with the olive oil, making sure some seeps into the top of each. Season with 2 pinches of salt. Wrap in aluminum foil and bake for 30 minutes.
2. Lower the oven to 350°F. Slice the upper crust of the loaf in a crosshatch pattern, cutting down through the upper crust and the middle of the loaf but not all the way through to the bottom crust. Place the roasted garlic heads, cut-side down, in the center of a baking dish and set the loaf on top of them, gently pressing the ends of the bread so it curves into a convex shape that reveals the interior cubes and crevices.
3. Melt the butter in a small saucepan over medium heat. Reduce the heat to low, add the chopped garlic, and swirl the pot to gently cook the garlic without browning it, about 1 minute. Add the remaining salt, pepper, parsley, thyme, and rosemary, swirling to mix well.
4. Brush the flavored butter into all the crevices and on top of the bread. If more butter is needed, melt a few more tablespoons and keep basting until the bread is well covered.
5. Sprinkle the cheese over the loaf, making sure some settles between the cuts as well as on top. (The loaf can be made up to this point, then wrapped well and refrigerated for up to 3 days.)
6. Bake for about 20 minutes, until the cheese has melted and is crisp on top. Serve straight out of the oven, along with the roasted garlic for scooping out and spreading on the bread.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
- The recipe appears in *Tartine All Day: Modern Recipes for the Home Cook* by Elisabeth Prueitt (Ten Speed Press, an imprint of Penguin Random House); the loaf can be assembled up to the point of adding cheese and refrigerated up to 3 days ahead.
- Article recommends starting with the best country-style bread available, ideally from Tartine bakery (San Francisco) or a similar high-quality bakery.
- Suggested pairings: game-night snack, accompaniment to grilled steaks or roast chicken.
