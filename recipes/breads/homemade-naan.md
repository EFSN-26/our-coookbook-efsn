# Homemade Naan

## Description
Soft, fluffy Indian flatbread with a slightly charred, buttery finish, cooked stovetop in a hot skillet. Yogurt in the dough keeps it tender, while brushing the naan with melted butter or ghee after cooking adds flavor and aroma.

## Ingredients
_(for 8 naan — see §8 for scaling)_

- 2¼ teaspoons (1 packet) active dry yeast
- 1 teaspoon sugar
- ½ cup warm water (110°F/45°C)
- 2½ to 3 cups all-purpose flour
- ½ teaspoon salt
- ¼ cup plain yogurt
- ¼ cup vegetable oil
- 1 large egg
- ¼ cup warm milk
- 2 tablespoons melted butter or ghee (for brushing)
- Optional toppings: minced garlic, chopped cilantro, sesame seeds, nigella seeds

## Instructions
1. **Activate the yeast:** In a small bowl, dissolve the sugar in the warm water. Sprinkle the yeast over the water and let it sit for about 10 minutes until it becomes frothy.
2. **Prepare the dough:** In a large mixing bowl, combine 2½ cups of flour and salt. In a separate bowl, whisk together the yogurt, vegetable oil, egg, and warm milk.
3. Pour the yeast mixture and yogurt mixture into the flour. Mix until a dough forms. Turn the dough out onto a floured surface and knead for about 8–10 minutes, adding more flour as needed until the dough is smooth and elastic.
4. **Let the dough rise:** Place the dough in a lightly oiled bowl, cover with a damp cloth, and let it rise in a warm place for about 1–2 hours, or until it has doubled in size.
5. **Shape the naan:** Punch down the risen dough and divide it into 8 equal pieces. Roll each piece into a ball and flatten it into an oval or teardrop shape using a rolling pin.
6. **Cook the naan:** Heat a large cast-iron skillet or non-stick pan over medium-high heat. Place one piece of rolled dough into the hot skillet. Cook for 1–2 minutes, or until bubbles form on the surface and the bottom is golden brown.
7. Flip the naan and cook for another 1–2 minutes until the other side is golden brown and cooked through.
8. Remove the naan from the skillet and brush with melted butter or ghee. Repeat with the remaining pieces of dough.
9. **Optional toppings:** Sprinkle minced garlic, chopped cilantro, sesame seeds, or nigella seeds on the naan while it's cooking, if desired.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
- Ensure the water is warm, not hot, to properly activate the yeast without killing it.
- The dough should be soft and slightly sticky — avoid adding too much flour during kneading.
- Allowing the dough to rise properly is crucial for achieving the right texture.
- Cook the naan on a hot skillet to get the characteristic bubbles and charred spots.
- Pair with classic Indian curries like butter chicken, tikka masala, chana masala, or palak paneer, or use it as a wrap base for grilled chicken and vegetables.
