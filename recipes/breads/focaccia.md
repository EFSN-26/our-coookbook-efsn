# Focaccia

## Description
Focaccia is one of Italy's most beloved breads, cherished for its golden, crisp crust, airy yet chewy interior, and fragrant toppings of olive oil, herbs, and sea salt. Originating from Liguria, this versatile flatbread has been a staple of Italian cuisine for centuries. [Source](https://www.gamintraveler.com/2026/05/29/how-to-make-focaccia/)

## Ingredients
_(for 8 servings)_

- 500g (about 4 cups) all-purpose flour
- 2 teaspoons salt
- 2 teaspoons instant yeast
- 2 tablespoons olive oil, plus extra for drizzling
- 1½ cups warm water
- Extra toppings: coarse sea salt, rosemary, cherry tomatoes, olives, etc.

## Instructions
1. **Prepare the dough:** In a large bowl, whisk together the flour, instant yeast, and salt. Make a well in the center of the dry ingredients and add the olive oil and warm water. Stir with a wooden spoon until a sticky dough forms.
2. **First rise:** Cover the bowl with plastic wrap or a damp kitchen towel. Let the dough rise in a warm place until doubled in size, about 1 to 2 hours.
3. **Shape and second rise:** Line a baking sheet with parchment paper and drizzle it with olive oil. Turn the risen dough out onto the prepared baking sheet. Spread the dough out with your hands to roughly fill the sheet. If the dough springs back, let it rest for a few minutes before continuing. Dimple the surface of the dough with your fingertips, creating small indentations, and let the dough rise uncovered for about 30 minutes, or until noticeably puffy.
4. **Add toppings and bake:** Preheat your oven to 425°F (220°C). Drizzle additional olive oil over the top of the dough. Sprinkle coarse sea salt and any other toppings like rosemary, cherry tomatoes, or olives. Bake in the preheated oven for 20-25 minutes, or until golden brown on top and the edges are crisp.
5. **Serve:** Let the focaccia cool slightly on a wire rack before cutting into pieces. Serve warm or at room temperature.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
- Focaccia dough is quite wet and sticky compared to other bread doughs; this moisture helps create a light, airy bread with a crisp exterior.
- Generously oiling the pan and the top of the dough ensures the characteristic crisp, flavorful crust.
- Experiment with various herbs and toppings like sliced onions, minced garlic, or different cheeses for added flavor.
- Focaccia is best enjoyed the day it is made but can be stored in an airtight container for up to 2 days.
- Most of the ~4-hour total time is hands-off resting: about 30 minutes is active hands-on work, the rest is the first rise (1-2 hours), second rise (~30 minutes), and baking (20-25 minutes).
- The source estimates about 985 calories for the whole batch (largely from the olive oil used in the dough and for drizzling, about 480 calories from ¼ cup), or roughly 123 calories per piece if cut into 8.
- Also serve as a sandwich base: slice horizontally and fill with prosciutto, mozzarella, arugula, and a drizzle of olive oil.
