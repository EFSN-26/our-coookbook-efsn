# Cheese Bread (Pão de Queijo style)

## Description
A warm, golden, gooey cheese bread made with tapioca flour (in the Brazilian pão de queijo tradition), giving it a naturally gluten-free, chewy texture packed with melted Parmesan and mozzarella. [Source](https://www.gamintraveler.com/2025/10/03/how-to-make-cheese-bread/)

## Ingredients
_(servings not specified by source)_

- 2 cups tapioca flour
- 1 cup milk
- 1/2 cup butter
- 1 teaspoon salt
- 1 1/2 cups grated Parmesan cheese
- 1 cup mozzarella cheese, shredded
- 2 large eggs

## Instructions
1. Preheat your oven to 375°F (190°C). Line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
2. In a saucepan, combine the milk, butter, and salt. Heat over medium until the butter is fully melted and the mixture starts to boil. Remove from heat immediately.
3. Add the tapioca flour to the milk mixture and stir until well combined; this will create a gelatinous dough. Let the mixture rest for about 10-15 minutes to cool slightly.
4. After the mixture has cooled, add the eggs one at a time, thoroughly mixing after each addition until the batter is smooth.
5. Stir in the grated Parmesan and mozzarella until the mixture is well combined and sticky.
6. Using an ice cream scoop or two spoons, scoop uniform balls of dough onto the prepared baking sheet. Bake for 20-25 minutes, or until the cheese breads are puffy and lightly golden.
7. Serve warm for the best texture and flavor.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
- Parmesan and mozzarella are traditional, but cheddar or Asiago can be used for different flavors.
- Stir the dough vigorously after adding the tapioca flour to ensure it becomes elastic and smooth.
- Once the eggs are added, mix just enough to incorporate them — overmixing can make the dough tough.
- Can be frozen either before or after baking; for pre-baked dough, pop straight in the oven to warm through for a fresh taste.
- Source's calorie estimate: each cheese bread (about the size of a small muffin) is roughly 100-150 calories, depending on size and cheese used.
