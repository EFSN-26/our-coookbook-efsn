# Lemon-Blueberry Trifle

## Description
A stunning yet simple layered dessert of homemade lemon pastry cream, store-bought angel food cake and fresh blueberries. A clear glass trifle bowl shows off the layers best. Any seasonal berry can stand in for the blueberries.

## Ingredients
_(for 12 servings)_

- 1/2 cup granulated sugar plus 2 tablespoons, divided
- 3 tablespoons cornstarch
- 1/8 teaspoon salt
- 5 cold large egg yolks
- 2 cups whole milk
- 1 tablespoon grated lemon zest, plus more for garnish
- 5 tablespoons lemon juice (from 2 lemons), divided
- 2 tablespoons unsalted butter, cut into 1/2-inch pieces
- 3 (6-ounce) containers fresh blueberries (about 4 cups)
- 6 cups (1-inch) cubes angel food cake
- 2 tablespoons simple syrup (see Cook's Notes)

## Instructions
1. Half-fill a large bowl with ice water and set aside. In a medium heatproof bowl, whisk together 1/2 cup sugar, the cornstarch and salt. Whisk in the egg yolks until pale, smooth and fluffy, about 1 minute.
2. Combine the milk and lemon zest in a medium saucepan and bring to a gentle simmer over medium heat, stirring occasionally. Remove from heat. Slowly whisk the warm milk into the yolk mixture until fully incorporated, then return everything to the saucepan.
3. Cook over medium heat, whisking constantly, until the mixture bubbles and thickens, about 3 minutes. Off the heat, whisk in the butter until combined. Pour through a fine-mesh sieve into a clean bowl. Press plastic wrap or buttered parchment directly onto the surface to prevent a skin. Set the bowl in the ice bath for 30 minutes, then refrigerate until chilled, about 2 hours.
4. Stir 2 tablespoons of the lemon juice into the chilled pastry cream; set aside. In a large bowl, stir the blueberries with the remaining 2 tablespoons sugar, mashing some to release juices; let stand at room temperature until glossy, about 5 minutes.
5. In another large bowl, gently toss the cake cubes with the simple syrup and remaining 3 tablespoons lemon juice until coated. Layer half the cake in the bottom of a small (2-quart) trifle bowl. Spoon over half the pastry cream in an even layer, then half the blueberries with their juices. Repeat the layers with the remaining cake, pastry cream and blueberries. Garnish with lemon zest if desired.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
Pastry cream can be made up to 3 days ahead (keep plastic wrap on the surface); the assembled trifle keeps up to 24 hours, refrigerated. If you like the texture of zest, you don't need to strain the cream. No bottled simple syrup? Microwave 1 1/2 tablespoons sugar with 3 tablespoons water until bubbling and dissolved (about 1 minute); measure out 2 tablespoons. Lime zest and juice can replace the lemon, and any berry works instead of blueberries.
