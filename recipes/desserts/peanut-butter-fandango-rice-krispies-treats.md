# Peanut Butter Fandango Rice Krispies Treats

## Description
Peanut Butter Rice Krispies Treats (aka Peanut Fandangos) are a delicious variation of the classic Rice Krispies Treats, made with marshmallows, peanut butter, peanuts, chocolate chips and butterscotch chips, and finished with a poured chocolate topping. This microwave-only recipe has minimal prep and cook time, and can be chilling in the fridge in about 15 minutes.

## Ingredients
_(for 12 servings)_

- 1/4 cup (1/2 stick) butter or margarine
- 10 ounces (1 bag) large marshmallows (about 40)
- 1/2 cup smooth peanut butter
- 5 cups crisp rice cereal (such as Rice Krispies)
- 1 cup unsalted peanuts
- 1 cup (6 ounces) semisweet chocolate chips
- 1 cup (6 ounces) butterscotch chips

## Instructions
1. Measure rice cereal and peanuts. Combine together in a mixing bowl. Set aside. Lightly grease a 13x9-inch baking pan.
2. In a large, microwave-safe mixing bowl, microwave butter on high for 45 seconds or until melted.
3. Remove bowl from the microwave. Add marshmallows and toss to coat. Return bowl to the microwave. Microwave on high for 1 1/2 minutes, stirring halfway.
4. Remove bowl from the microwave. Stir marshmallows, blending in peanut butter until mixture is smooth.
5. Pour marshmallow mixture into rice cereal mixture. Mix thoroughly to coat cereal and peanuts. Press mixture into the prepared baking pan. (You can use the butter wrapper to press the cereal mixture into the pan.)
6. In a separate microwave-safe bowl, combine the semi-sweet chips and butterscotch chips. Cook on high 1 to 1 1/2 minutes until melted, stirring every 30 seconds. (You can use only chocolate chips, if preferred.)
7. Spoon melted chocolate over bars. Use a knife or a spatula to spread chocolate evenly over bars.
8. Chill in the refrigerator 15 to 30 minutes (or longer, if needed). Cut bars into 1 1/2-inch squares. Remove bars from the pan. Place waxed paper in between layers to store them. Store bars in an airtight container.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
You can use only chocolate chips instead of both chocolate and butterscotch chips, if preferred. Store bars in an airtight container with waxed paper between layers.
