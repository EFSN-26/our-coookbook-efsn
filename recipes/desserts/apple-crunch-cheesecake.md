# Apple Crunch Cheesecake

## Description
A creamy, classic cheesecake filling sits on a cinnamon-spiced graham cracker crust and is topped with cinnamon-sugar apples and a crispy oat streusel topping. A warm, fall-flavored dessert with four distinct layers.

## Ingredients
_(for 8 slices)_

### For the crust
- 3 cups graham crackers, crushed into crumbs
- 3 tablespoons brown sugar
- ½ teaspoon cinnamon
- ½ cup unsalted butter, melted

### For the cinnamon apples
- 2 large Granny Smith apples, peeled, cored, and sliced
- 2 tablespoons brown sugar
- 1 ½ teaspoons cinnamon

### For the crisp
- ½ cup flour
- ½ cup brown sugar
- 1 teaspoon cinnamon
- ½ cup quick oats
- ¼ cup unsalted butter, melted

### For the cheesecake batter
- 3 (8 oz) boxes cream cheese, softened
- ¾ cup granulated sugar
- ¼ cup brown sugar
- ½ teaspoon cinnamon
- 2 tablespoons flour
- 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
- ½ cup sour cream
- 3 whole eggs
- water, for water bath

## Instructions
1. Preheat oven to 350°F.
2. Line a 9-inch springform pan with two layers of foil on the inside, making sure they overlap.
3. Line the outside of the springform pan with 5 layers of foil, overlapping the sheets to cover the entire outside of the pan. This keeps water from the water bath out of the pan.
4. Spray the inside of the pan with non-stick cooking spray.
5. For the crust: crush 3 cups of graham crackers into crumbs. Mix the graham cracker crumbs, 3 tablespoons brown sugar, and ½ teaspoon cinnamon together.
6. Add ½ cup melted butter and combine until it looks like wet sand.
7. Press the mixture into the bottom and sides of the foil-lined pan. Bake at 350°F for 10 minutes.
8. For the cinnamon apples: peel, core, and thinly slice 2 large apples. Add the apples, 2 tablespoons brown sugar, and 1 ½ teaspoons cinnamon to a mixing bowl. Toss until the apples are well coated. Set aside.
9. For the crisp: combine ½ cup flour, ½ cup brown sugar, and 1 teaspoon cinnamon. Mix well until no clumps remain.
10. Add in ½ cup quick oats and mix well.
11. Add in ¼ cup melted butter and mix until it is the consistency of wet sand. Set aside.
12. For the cheesecake batter: add the 3 boxes of cream cheese, ¾ cup granulated sugar, and ¼ cup brown sugar to a mixing bowl. Cream together on medium-high speed until smooth.
13. Add in 2 tablespoons flour and ½ teaspoon cinnamon. Mix on low, scraping down sides as needed, until well combined.
14. Add in ½ cup sour cream and 1 teaspoon vanilla extract. Mix on medium-low until well combined.
15. Add in 3 eggs and mix on medium speed until well combined.
16. Pour the cheesecake batter into the pan with the baked crust.
17. Line the top of the cheesecake with a single layer of cinnamon apples.
18. Sprinkle an even layer of the crisp topping on top of the apples.
19. Place the cheesecake in a large roasting pan. Add hot water to the roasting pan so it comes halfway up the side of the springform pan.
20. Place the roasting pan with the water bath into the oven and bake at 350°F for 60 minutes.
21. Remove from oven and allow the water bath to cool to room temperature.
22. Remove the springform pan from the water bath. Cover the cheesecake with foil and chill for at least 8 hours before serving.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
- The cheesecake is done when the top is firm and the center only has a slight jiggle when shaken lightly.
- When laying the apples on the cheesecake batter, do it in a single layer, lifting them out of the bowl one at a time rather than pouring them on top, to avoid excess liquid.
- Line the springform pan carefully with foil on the outside, overlapping each layer — otherwise water can seep into the cheesecake during the water bath.
- Use a fork to press out any clumps that form when mixing the flour, brown sugar, and cinnamon for the crisp.
- Soften the cream cheese at room temperature for about an hour before mixing, to avoid over-mixing the batter.
- Storage: keep in the fridge for 3 days in an airtight container, or freeze for up to 3 months.
- Substitutions/variations: use a store-bought graham cracker crust to save time; substitute Fuji apples for Granny Smith; chop the apples instead of slicing for a more mixed texture; drizzle with salted caramel sauce, whipped cream, or vanilla ice cream when serving.
