# Buttery Bread Pudding With Bourbon Butter Sauce

## Description
A buttery bread pudding with a decadent bourbon butter sauce — the ultimate weekend (or weekday) baking therapy, and a scrumptious breakfast, brunch or dessert. With just 15 minutes of simple prep, it's easy to make; the hardest part is waiting for it to bake.

## Ingredients
_(for 12 servings)_

**Bread Pudding**
- 1 loaf French bread, cut into 1-inch cubes
- 4 cups milk
- 3 eggs
- 2 cups granulated sugar
- 3 tablespoons butter
- 2 1/2 tablespoons vanilla extract

**Bourbon Sauce**
- 8 tablespoons (1 stick) butter, softened
- 1 cup granulated sugar
- 1 egg
- 2 tablespoons bourbon
- 1/2 teaspoon vanilla extract

## Instructions
1. Put the bread cubes into a large mixing bowl. Pour the milk over the bread and allow it to soak about 10 minutes. (To make this recipe gluten-free, use gluten-free bread.)
2. Add the remaining bread pudding ingredients to the bowl. Mix well.
3. Pour the mixture into a lightly greased 3-quart casserole dish or 13x9-inch baking pan. Bake in a preheated 325-degree F oven for about 1 hour. (Baking time will vary depending on the type of pan you choose.)
4. To make the bourbon sauce, melt the butter and sugar together in a saucepot. Add the egg and whisk to combine. Cook for 1 minute. Remove from the heat and stir in the bourbon and vanilla.
5. To serve, spoon the bread pudding into serving bowls and top with the bourbon sauce. Store leftovers in an airtight container in the refrigerator.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
Use gluten-free bread to make this recipe gluten-free. One reader (John Jones, in the comments) reported that pouring the milk over the bread pieces doesn't saturate them evenly — he recommends stirring repeatedly to fully saturate the bread — and that the sugar quantities in both the pudding and the bourbon sauce are excessive as written (a stick of butter reportedly won't fully dissolve into 2 tablespoons of bourbon, leaving undissolved sugar); he suggests cutting the sugar in both components by half or more. 30Seconds said they would re-test the recipe in response. This cookbook has not independently verified either version.
