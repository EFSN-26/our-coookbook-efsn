# Baked Bananas in Brown Sugar

## Description
A truly satisfying two-ingredient treat made with just bananas and brown sugar. Bananas caramelize as the heat develops their sweetness, and the brown sugar imbues the fruit with a deep, toffee-like taste. (Note: the source article does not state ingredient quantities.)

## Ingredients

- bananas, just on the precipice of ripeness (not green, with no soft or mushy spots)
- brown sugar
- melted butter (optional, to help the sugar stick)

## Instructions
1. Preheat the oven to 350 degrees Fahrenheit.
2. Optionally coat the bananas in melted butter to help the sugar stick better.
3. Roll the bananas around in the brown sugar.
4. Bake for around 15 minutes, until thoroughly caramelized with a nice, evenly browned crust.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
- Use bananas that are just on the precipice of ripeness so they hold up in the oven; they don't need to be green, but shouldn't have any soft, mushy spots.
- You can flavor the bananas with spices such as cinnamon, nutmeg, and cloves, or powdered ginger for a fiery side. Mix the spices with the brown sugar or add them to the melted butter before brushing it onto the bananas.
- For toppings, try candied ginger, toasted chopped nuts (almonds, walnuts, or pecans), a dash of caramel or melted milk and dark chocolate with flaky sea salt, or boozy vanilla/white chocolate whipped cream with cinnamon chips.
