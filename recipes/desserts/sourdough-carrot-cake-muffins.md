# Sourdough Carrot Cake Muffins

## Description
Easy-to-make sourdough discard muffins that deliver all the classic flavors of carrot cake in handy muffin form, with a slightly lower sugar ratio than typical carrot cake so the warm spices and carrot flavor really shine through.

## Ingredients
_(quantities as given by the source, which used grams for dry/wet ingredients — see §8 for scaling; servings/muffin count not stated by the source)_

- 80 grams all-purpose flour
- ½ teaspoon salt
- ½ teaspoon baking soda
- 2.6 grams ground cinnamon
- 2.6 grams mixed spice*
- 0.55 grams ground nutmeg
- 0.55 grams ground ginger
- 130 grams granulated sugar
- 2 eggs
- 120 grams sourdough discard
- 50 grams coconut oil, melted and cooled
- 200 grams carrots, finely grated

*Mixed spice is a classic blend of warm spices typically including cinnamon, coriander seed, caraway, nutmeg, ginger, cloves, allspice, and mace.

## Instructions
1. Preheat oven to 190°C (374°F) and line a muffin pan with paper liners.
2. Sift the flour, baking soda, salt, cinnamon, mixed spice, nutmeg, and ginger into a large mixing bowl.
3. Mix in the sugar, ensuring it's thoroughly incorporated with the sifted dry ingredients.
4. In another bowl, whisk together the eggs, sourdough discard, and melted coconut oil until smooth.
5. Mix the grated carrots into the wet ingredients, stirring until evenly distributed into the batter.
6. Gently fold the wet ingredients into the dry, stirring until just combined and avoiding overmixing.
7. Spoon batter into lined muffin cups, filling each to about ¾ full.
8. Bake for 15 minutes, or until the tops are golden and a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
- Using only 130 grams of sugar (less than the usual recipe) slightly lowers the sweetness, allowing the natural flavors and spices to shine through more while keeping the muffins delightfully flavorful.
- Serve warm, ideally with a dollop of cream cheese frosting.
- Flour: all-purpose flour works best, but you could try a 50/50 mix of whole wheat and all-purpose flour.
- Sugar: regular granulated sugar works well, or use brown sugar for more depth.
- Coconut oil: use a neutral-flavored or subtly coconut-flavored oil, or substitute a mild olive, avocado, or vegetable oil.
- For mini muffins, bake at 350°F (177°C) for 13–14 minutes or until slightly golden and passing the toothpick test.
- Don't overmix the batter — mix just until you no longer see dry ingredients, or the muffins can turn dense, tough, and chewy.
- **Long-fermented (overnight) version:** mix the batter through step 6, cover tightly, and refrigerate overnight (or up to 3 days) using a more active sourdough starter. The next day, resume with step 7 (fill and bake).
- Store baked, cooled muffins in a covered container at room temperature for several days, or in the refrigerator for up to 1 week. Muffins freeze well for up to 3 months; thaw at room temperature or warm in the oven/microwave before eating.
