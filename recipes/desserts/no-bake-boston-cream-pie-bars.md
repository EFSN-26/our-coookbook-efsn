# No-Bake Boston Cream Pie Bars

## Description
A layered no-bake dessert inspired by Boston cream pie: a buttery vanilla wafer crust, a thick vanilla pudding filling lightened with whipped cream, and a glossy chocolate ganache topping. Written up by a professional baker as her new favorite summer no-bake treat. [Source](https://www.aol.com/m-professional-baker-no-bake-190000422.html)

## Ingredients
_(quantities not specified by source — this article describes the process rather than providing a full recipe card)_

- Vanilla wafer cookies
- Granulated sugar
- Unsalted butter, melted
- Vanilla instant pudding mix
- Cold heavy cream (for the pudding layer)
- Semi-sweet chocolate, chopped
- Heavy cream (for the ganache, brought to a simmer)

## Instructions
1. **Make the crust:** Process vanilla wafer cookies and granulated sugar in a food processor. Drizzle in melted unsalted butter and pulse until evenly moistened. Transfer to a baking pan, press into an even layer, and freeze to set.
2. **Make the pudding layer:** Whisk together vanilla instant pudding mix and cold heavy cream in a large bowl until smooth (the mixture will be very thick). Spread evenly over the crust, then refrigerate while making the ganache.
3. **Make the chocolate ganache:** Place chopped semi-sweet chocolate in a medium bowl. Bring heavy cream to a simmer in a saucepan over medium heat. Pour the hot cream over the chocolate and let it sit, then whisk until the chocolate is melted and the ganache is smooth.
4. **Finish, chill, and serve:** Pour the ganache over the pudding layer in an even layer. Refrigerate until set, then slice and serve.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
- Try dark chocolate for the ganache — it complements the sweetness of the pudding layer, making the bars feel even more decadent.
- Make the crust ahead of time: wrap in plastic wrap and refrigerate, or freeze, a day or two before you're ready to use it.
- For perfectly clean slices, cut with a hot knife — run the knife under hot water and wipe it dry before each cut.
