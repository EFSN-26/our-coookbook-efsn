# Julie Andrews' Carrot Cake

## Description
Julie Andrews' own spin on carrot cake, originally printed in the USO of Pioneer Valley Celebrity Cookbook and tested here by Allrecipes. It's egg- and dairy-free, naturally sweetened with honey, loaded with carrots and raisins, and uses surprising ingredients like tofu (in the frosting) and cornmeal.

## Ingredients
_(for 8 servings — see §8 for scaling)_

Cake:
- 1 3/4 cups pastry flour or all-purpose flour
- 2 1/4 ounces cornmeal (scant 1/2 cup)
- 1 1/2 teaspoons baking powder
- 1 1/2 teaspoons baking soda
- 1 teaspoon allspice
- 1 teaspoon cinnamon
- 1/2 teaspoon cloves
- 1/2 teaspoon nutmeg
- 1/4 teaspoon salt
- 1 1/4 cups water
- 1/2 cup honey
- 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
- 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
- 2 cups shredded carrots
- 2 cups golden raisins

Frosting:
- 1 pound medium-firm tofu, drained
- 2 tablespoons, plus 1 teaspoon honey
- 1 teaspoon orange zest
- 1/4 teaspoon orange extract
- 1/4 cup shredded coconut, toasted (plus extra for topping cake)

## Instructions
1. Preheat the oven to 350 degrees F with a rack set in the middle position. Grease two 9-inch round pans and line bottoms with parchment paper.
2. In a large bowl, whisk together flour, cornmeal, baking powder, baking soda, spices, and salt.
3. In a liquid measuring cup, whisk together water, honey, oil, and vanilla; pour into flour mixture and stir until nearly combined.
4. Add carrots and raisins and mix to combine.
5. Divide batter evenly between prepared cake pans. Bake until a toothpick inserted in the center comes out clean, about 25 minutes.
6. While the cakes cool, make the frosting: combine tofu, honey, orange zest, orange extract, and toasted coconut in a food processor, stand mixer, or large bowl with hand mixer, and mix until smooth and creamy.
7. Transfer one cake to a serving platter and spread half of the frosting over top. Place the second cake on top and frost with the remaining frosting. Sprinkle remaining toasted coconut on top.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
From Allrecipes' review/testing of this recipe (by Lauren Grant):
- The original recipe suggested silken tofu for the frosting, but it wasn't sturdy enough in testing — medium-firm tofu is recommended instead. If the frosting is still too loose, whisk in a few spoonfuls of powdered sugar, or add some cream cheese.
- If you don't have pastry flour, all-purpose flour works fine — the dense mix-ins (cornmeal, carrots, raisins) make the difference negligible.
- The reviewer found the allspice and cloves overpowering and would use less next time.
- To toast shredded coconut efficiently, spread it in an even layer and toast it in a hot oven right after the cakes come out — watch closely and stir, as it can go from toasted to burnt quickly.
