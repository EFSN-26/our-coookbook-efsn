# Italian Lemon Cream Cake

## Description
A buttermilk-based layer cake bright with lemon, filled and frosted with a whipped mascarpone cream, and topped with a buttery vanilla crumble — inspired by the lemons of Italy's Amalfi Coast and Sorrento Peninsula. [Source](https://www.tastingtable.com/1953962/italian-lemon-cream-cake-recipe/)

## Ingredients
_(for 8 servings/slices)_

#### For the cake:
- 2 1/4 cups cake flour
- 1 tablespoon baking powder
- 1/2 teaspoon salt
- 4 egg whites
- 1 1/4 cups buttermilk
- 1 1/2 cups granulated sugar
- Zest from 2 lemons
- 1/2 cup butter, softened
- 1 teaspoon vanilla

#### For the mascarpone cream:
- 1 cup heavy whipping cream
- 8 ounces mascarpone cheese, softened at room temperature
- 2 cups powdered sugar
- 2 tablespoons lemon juice
- Zest from 1 lemon

#### For the cake crumble:
- 3/4 cup cake flour (pre-cooked or baked — see notes)
- 3/4 cup powdered sugar, plus more for topping
- 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
- Zest from 1 lemon
- 6 tablespoons cold butter, cut into cubes

## Instructions
1. Preheat the oven to 350°F.
2. Grease two 9-inch round cake pans, then line with parchment and grease again.
3. Sift together the flour, baking powder, and salt for the cake. Set aside.
4. In a separate bowl, whisk the egg whites and buttermilk until combined.
5. In a large bowl, combine the sugar and lemon zest, massaging with your hands to incorporate the lemon oils into the sugar.
6. Add the butter to the sugar mixture and beat until light and fluffy, about 2 to 3 minutes.
7. Add the vanilla and 1/2 cup of the flour mixture to the butter/sugar mixture and beat to combine.
8. Alternate adding the eggs mixture and the remaining flour, incorporating each slowly. Beat until completely combined.
9. Transfer the batter to the cake pans and bake for 30 to 35 minutes, or until a toothpick comes out clean.
10. Let the cakes cool slightly, then transfer to a wire rack to cool completely.
11. Whip the heavy cream to soft peaks using an electric whisk or stand mixer.
12. In a separate bowl, beat the mascarpone, powdered sugar, lemon juice, and zest until smooth.
13. Fold the whipped cream into the mascarpone mixture. Set aside.
14. For the crumble, add the (pre-baked) flour, powdered sugar, vanilla extract, and lemon zest to a food processor and pulse to combine.
15. Add the butter and pulse until the mixture looks crumbled.
16. To build the cake, trim the cakes flat if needed. Spread half of the mascarpone cream onto one cake, then top with the other cake.
17. Spread the remaining mascarpone cream across the top and sides of the cake until thinly frosted.
18. Sprinkle the crumbles on top of the cake.
19. Sift powdered sugar across the top of the cake. Slice and serve.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
- **Cooking the crumble flour:** The crumble is meant to be a textural topping, like a streusel, and raw flour should be heat-treated first. Either (a) spread the flour alone on a baking sheet and bake in a preheated oven for 5 minutes before combining with the other crumble ingredients, or (b) combine all crumble ingredients first, then spread on a baking sheet and bake until browned for a crunchier, more caramelized topping.
- **By hand:** Whipped cream can be made by hand with a cold bowl and whisk (up to 10 minutes of whisking); beat the mascarpone and sugar separately with a spatula, then fold the two together. For the crumble by hand, soften the butter slightly, then combine all ingredients with gloved hands until shaggy, and shake the bowl to round the pieces into crumbles — don't overwork the butter into a paste.
