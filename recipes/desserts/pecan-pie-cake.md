# Amazing Pecan Pie Cake

## Description
A pecan pie cake baked in two stages: first a butter pecan cake mix crust, then an ooey, gooey pecan pie topping. Not the most attractive dessert, but a favorite that rivals grandma's pecan pie — perfect for Thanksgiving.

## Ingredients
_(for 12 servings)_

- 16 ounces (1 box) butter pecan cake mix
- 1 stick (1/2 cup) butter, softened
- 1 cup light brown sugar, packed
- 2 eggs
- 2 tablespoons water
- 2 cups chopped pecans
- 1 stick (1/2 cup) butter, softened
- 2 eggs
- 1 cup granulated sugar
- 1 cup light corn syrup
- 1 cup milk

## Instructions
1. Measure out 2/3 cup of the cake mix and set aside. Put the remaining cake mix into a bowl. Add 1 stick of butter, the light brown sugar, 2 eggs and water. Mix well with an electric mixer. Fold in the pecans. Pour the batter into a 13x9-inch baking pan (or disposable aluminum pan if traveling) that's been sprayed with nonstick cooking spray. Bake in a preheated 325-degree F oven for 25 minutes. (If you can't find a butter pecan cake mix, substitute a vanilla cake mix.)
2. About 5 minutes before the crust is done baking, combine the other stick of butter, 2 eggs, white sugar, corn syrup and milk, along with the 2/3 cup of cake mix that was set aside. Remove the crust from the oven when baking time is complete and pour the second batter over the top. Top with a few whole pecans, if desired.
3. Return the pan to the oven and bake another 30 to 35 minutes. The cake will be jiggly in the center.
4. Serve warm with vanilla ice cream or whipped cream. Store the cake covered on the counter (not refrigerated). Reheat leftovers in the microwave.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
- It's not the most attractive dessert, but you won't care after one bite.
- Serve warm with vanilla ice cream — it's a favorite way to enjoy it.
- Don't cut this cake into neat slices; scoop it out into a bowl and top with ice cream, since it's a looser, gooey cake.
- Store covered on the counter rather than in the fridge.
