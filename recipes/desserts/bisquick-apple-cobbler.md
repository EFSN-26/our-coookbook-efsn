# Bisquick Apple Cobbler

## Description
A quick, old-fashioned apple cobbler made easy with Bisquick mix — warm, cinnamon-spiced Honeycrisp apples baked over a buttery batter, best served hot with vanilla ice cream. [Source](https://xoxobella.com/bisquick-apple-cobbler/)

## Ingredients
_(for 6 servings — see §8 for scaling)_

- 1 stick (1/2 cup) salted butter, melted
- 3 cups apples, diced (the source used Honeycrisp apples)
- 1 cup light brown sugar, packed
- 1 1/2 cups Bisquick mix
- 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
- 1/2 teaspoon ground nutmeg
- 1 1/2 cups milk
- 1 teaspoon vanilla extract

## Instructions
1. Preheat the oven to 375°F.
2. Pour the melted butter into a 9-inch square baking pan. Set aside.
3. Peel, core and dice the apples.
4. In a large mixing bowl, mix the apples with 2 tablespoons of the light brown sugar. Set aside.
5. Whisk the Bisquick mix, remaining brown sugar, cinnamon and nutmeg in a large bowl.
6. Add the milk and vanilla extract. Mix until all ingredients are combined into a batter.
7. Pour the batter into the baking pan on top of the butter — don't mix the batter with the butter.
8. Evenly add the apples on top of the batter — again, don't mix.
9. Bake for 40 to 45 minutes, or until the top is golden and the apples are softened to your liking. Cover with aluminum foil if the top browns too quickly.
10. Allow the cobbler to cool for 5 minutes, then serve hot with ice cream.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
- Choose firm apples like Honeycrisp or Granny Smith for a balanced texture and flavor; Gala or Fuji apples also work.
- Try cardamom or cloves in place of nutmeg for a different spice profile.
- For a gluten-free version, use a gluten-free baking mix in place of Bisquick.
- For dairy-free, use almond or coconut milk and a dairy-free margarine instead of butter.
- Sprinkle chopped pecans or walnuts on top before baking for added crunch.
- Refrigerate leftovers for 2-3 days once completely cooled, or freeze tightly wrapped for up to 3 months; thaw in the refrigerator overnight. Reheat individual portions in an air fryer at 330°F for 3-4 minutes.
