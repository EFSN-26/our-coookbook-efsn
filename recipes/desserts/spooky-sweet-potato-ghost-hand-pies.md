# Spooky Sweet Potato Ghost Hand Pies

## Description
Ghost-shaped hand pies made with a flaky all-butter pie crust and a spiced sweet potato filling, finished with either cinnamon sugar or a light glaze and candy eyes — a fun, not-overly-sweet Halloween treat for kids and adults alike.

## Ingredients
_(for 16 hand pies)_

### For the pie dough
- 2 1/2 cups all-purpose flour, plus additional, for rolling
- 2 teaspoons granulated sugar
- 1/2 teaspoon kosher salt
- 1 cup cold unsalted butter, cubed
- 1/4 cup cold water

### For the filling
- 1 (15-ounce) can sweet potato puree
- 1/4 cup dark brown sugar, packed
- 1/2 teaspoon kosher salt
- 1/4 teaspoon ground cinnamon
- 1/4 teaspoon ground ginger
- 1/8 teaspoon ground nutmeg
- 1 tablespoon bourbon (or 2 teaspoons vanilla extract)
- 2 tablespoons unsalted butter, melted
- 1 large egg (for egg wash)

### Optional
- 1/4 cup chopped pecans, toasted
- Cinnamon sugar, for sprinkling
- 1 cup confectioners' sugar
- 1 tablespoon whole milk or water
- 1 pinch kosher salt
- Candy eyes, for decorating

## Instructions
1. Prepare the pie dough: in the bowl of a stand mixer fitted with a paddle attachment, mix to combine the flour, sugar, and salt.
2. Add the butter and mix until only small pieces remain visible.
3. Add 1/4 cup cold water and mix lightly just until the dough becomes shaggy.
4. Transfer the dough to a clean surface and form it into a flat disk.
5. Wrap with plastic wrap and refrigerate for 30 minutes.
6. In the meantime, prepare the filling: in a medium bowl, stir to combine the sweet potato puree, brown sugar, salt, cinnamon, ginger, nutmeg, and bourbon (or vanilla).
7. Add the pecans, if using, and melted butter; stir to combine. Set aside until ready to use.
8. Preheat the oven to 375°F.
9. Line two baking sheets with parchment paper.
10. Lightly dust a clean surface with flour.
11. Roll the dough to 1/8-inch thickness.
12. Cut out ghost shapes using a ghost cookie cutter.
13. Transfer to the prepared baking sheets. Reroll the scraps and repeat. You should have about 32 ghosts.
14. Stir to combine the egg with a splash of water for the egg wash.
15. Brush the edges of 16 ghosts with the egg wash.
16. Dollop about 1 tablespoon of filling into the center of each egg-washed ghost.
17. Top with the remaining ghosts and press the edges lightly around the filling to seal.
18. Use a small fork to crimp the edges.
19. Brush the egg wash over the sealed ghosts to coat.
20. If desired, top some or all of the ghosts with cinnamon sugar.
21. Bake for about 25 minutes, or until golden brown. Set aside to cool slightly.
22. If desired, prepare the glaze: stir to combine 1 cup confectioners' sugar, 1 tablespoon milk (or water), and 1 pinch kosher salt until smooth.
23. Brush the glaze over the ghosts. Top the glazed ghosts with candy eyes before the glaze sets.
24. Serve warm or at room temperature.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
- Swap the sweet potato puree for pumpkin puree and use pumpkin-shaped cutters, then tint the glaze with orange food coloring and decorate with festive sprinkles, for a variation that still keeps the Halloween theme. For Thanksgiving, switch to a turkey-shaped cutter, or use an apple-shaped cutter with apple pie filling for everyday fall gatherings.
- Try replacing the cinnamon sugar topping with cardamom or pumpkin pie spice sugar, or swap the glaze for a drizzle of melted white chocolate. Add festive Halloween sprinkles, or simply dust cooled hand pies with confectioners' sugar.
- Make-ahead: prepare and fill the hand pies as directed, crimp them with a fork, then wrap the baking sheets tightly with plastic wrap and refrigerate overnight, or flash freeze the hand pies until firm and transfer to freezer-safe zip-top bags for up to two months. Thaw overnight in the refrigerator if frozen, then top with egg wash and bake as directed.
- The pie dough alone can be made in advance: wrap the disk tightly and refrigerate for up to two days or freeze for up to two months. Thaw overnight in the refrigerator and let stand at room temperature about 10 minutes before rolling. The sweet potato filling can also be made a day ahead and refrigerated in an airtight container.
