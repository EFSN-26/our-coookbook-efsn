# Tiramisu

## Description
Traditional tiramisu, from the Veneto region of Italy, is a no-bake dessert made with layers of espresso-dipped ladyfingers, mascarpone cream, egg yolks and a dusting of cocoa powder — no heavy cream shortcuts, no strawberries or chocolate chips, just a balance of bittersweet coffee and velvety mascarpone. [Source](https://www.gamintraveler.com/2026/02/15/how-to-make-tiramisu/)

## Ingredients
_(for servings not specified by source)_

- 6 large egg yolks
- 3/4 cup granulated sugar
- 1 cup mascarpone cheese
- 1 1/2 cups heavy cream
- 2 cups strong espresso or strong coffee, cooled
- 1/2 cup coffee liqueur (optional, like Kahlua)
- 1 package ladyfingers (about 24 pieces)
- unsweetened cocoa powder, for dusting
- dark chocolate, for shaving (optional)

## Instructions
1. In a shallow dish, combine the espresso with the coffee liqueur (if using). Set aside to cool if it's still warm from brewing.
2. In a large mixing bowl, whisk the egg yolks and sugar until thick and pale, about 5 minutes (an electric mixer on medium-high speed works well). Add the mascarpone cheese and beat until smooth.
3. In another bowl, whip the heavy cream until it forms stiff peaks. Gently fold the whipped cream into the mascarpone and egg mixture, keeping the mixture light and airy.
4. Quickly dip each ladyfinger into the coffee mixture, ensuring they are moist but not soggy. Arrange the soaked ladyfingers in a single layer in the bottom of your dish.
5. Spread half of the mascarpone mixture over the ladyfingers. Add another layer of soaked ladyfingers and top with the remaining mascarpone mixture. Use a spatula to smooth the top layer.
6. Cover the tiramisu with plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 4 hours, preferably overnight.
7. Just before serving, dust the top generously with unsweetened cocoa powder and, if desired, dark chocolate shavings. Use a sieve for even, light coverage of the cocoa powder.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
If concerned about raw eggs, use pasteurized eggs or cook the egg yolk mixture over a double boiler until it reaches 160°F (71°C) to make it food-safe. Dip the ladyfingers quickly rather than soaking them, to avoid them becoming too mushy. The source notes the initial preparation (mixing, brewing coffee, layering) takes about 30 minutes, but the dessert needs at least 4 hours refrigeration afterward — overnight is recommended for the creamiest result. Serve with espresso, cappuccino, or a dessert wine like Vin Santo or Marsala; also pairs well with fresh berries, dark chocolate shavings, or amaretti cookies.
