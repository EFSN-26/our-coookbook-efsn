# Luscious Lemon Ricotta Cake

## Description
A light, airy lemon cake made with whole-milk ricotta, baked in a springform pan and finished with powdered sugar or whipped cream — easy enough for a beginner but tastes like it came from a seasoned baker. [Source](https://30seconds.com/food/tip/74161/Luscious-Lemon-Ricotta-Cake-Recipe-May-Give-You-Baker-Status)

## Ingredients
_(for 10 servings — see §8 for scaling)_

- 1/2 teaspoon baking soda
- 1/2 teaspoon salt
- 1 1/2 cups all-purpose flour (or gluten-free 1:1 flour)
- 3/4 cup unsalted butter, softened
- 1 1/2 cups granulated sugar
- 15 ounces ricotta cheese (whole milk), at room temperature
- 3 eggs, at room temperature
- 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
- juice from 1 large lemon
- zest from 1 large lemon

**Optional garnishes**
- powdered sugar
- whipped cream

## Instructions
1. Grease a 9-inch springform pan with butter and preheat the oven to 350°F.
2. Sift the flour, salt and baking soda into a bowl.
3. In a large mixing bowl, cream the butter and sugar on medium until completely combined.
4. Add the ricotta and mix on medium until the mixture is light and fluffy. Add the eggs one at a time, blending well between each addition.
5. Add the lemon juice, zest and vanilla and blend.
6. Pour the flour mixture into the wet ingredients and use a rubber spatula to mix until just blended — do not over-mix.
7. Scrape the batter into the prepared pan.
8. Bake for 45 to 50 minutes. About halfway through, place a piece of aluminum foil over the top of the cake to keep it from browning too much. Remove from the oven when a toothpick inserted into the middle comes out clean.
9. Cool for 15 minutes before releasing the springform, then cool for another 30 minutes or more. Transfer to a platter and remove the bottom. Dust with powdered sugar and serve as-is or with whipped cream.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
- Room-temperature butter, eggs and ricotta blend more smoothly into the batter.
- If you forget to bring ingredients to room temperature: warm eggs in a bowl of warm (not hot) water for 10 minutes; soften butter briefly in the microwave in small increments; set the ricotta container in a bowl of warm (not hot) water.
- This cake pairs well with a lemon whipped cream, per the source.
