# Limoncello Bundt Cake

## Description
A light, fluffy cake bursting with bright lemon flavor from limoncello liqueur, topped with a sweet limoncello glaze. A gourmet, citrusy centerpiece for Easter, brunch, or any celebration.

## Ingredients
_(for the servings this recipe yields — servings count not stated by source, see Missing Information)_

For the Cake:
- 1 Cup granulated sugar
- 1/3 Cup vegetable oil
- 1 Cup sour cream
- 2 Eggs
- 3 Tablespoons limoncello
- lemon zest, from 1 lemon
- 1 Teaspoon vanilla extract
- 2 or 3 Drops food coloring, yellow, optional
- 2 Cups all-purpose flour
- 1 Teaspoon baking powder
- 1/2 Teaspoon baking soda
- 1/4 Teaspoon salt

For the Glaze:
- 2 Cups confectioner's sugar
- 4 Tablespoons limoncello
- lemon zest, from 1 lemon, for garnish

## Instructions
1. Heat the oven to 350 degrees F and spray a bundt pan well with cooking spray. Dust with flour, making sure all the crevices are coated.
2. Beat the sugar and oil together, then add the eggs, sour cream, limoncello, lemon zest, vanilla and food coloring and combine.
3. In a separate bowl, combine the flour, baking powder, baking soda and salt, then stir into the cake batter.
4. Pour the cake batter into the prepared pan and bake for 50 minutes, or until a toothpick comes out clean.
5. Remove and cool for 15 minutes. Gently loosen the sides by sliding a knife down the edges. Place a plate over the cake and flip the cake over onto the plate.
6. Beat the confectioner's sugar and limoncello together and drizzle over the cooled cake.
7. Sprinkle the top of the cake with the lemon zest and serve.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
- Cool in the pan until you can touch the sides without burning your fingers before flipping it out; let it cool completely on a plate before glazing.
- Don't overmix the batter, or the cake will come out chewy.
- Let ingredients come to room temperature before mixing for a smoother batter.
- Optional: once cool, poke holes in the top of the cake with a skewer before glazing so some of the glaze seeps in.
- Alcohol-free option: substitute the limoncello with a mixture of water, lemon extract, and sugar in the same total liquid amount (or half lemon juice / half water with a pinch of sugar).
- Store in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 5 days; freeze for up to 3 months; thaw overnight in the fridge or a few hours at room temperature.
