# Marv 'n' Joe Tomato Sandwich

## Description
An open-faced sandwich created by two engineering professors, Marv and Joe, who bonded over their love of garlic tomato sandwiches. Toasted bread, juicy heirloom tomato, a splash of vinegar and oil, and melted provolone and Parmesan come together for a simple, satisfying bite. [Source](https://www.simplyrecipes.com/marv-n-joe-tomato-sandwich-recipe-11777919)

## Ingredients
_(for 2 servings)_

- 1 large slice sourdough bread, cut in half, or 2 slices of smaller bread
- 2 tablespoons garlic butter, divided (see Cook's Notes for homemade version)
- 2 large slices heirloom tomato
- 1 teaspoon olive oil, divided
- 1 teaspoon balsamic vinegar, divided
- 2 slices provolone cheese
- Shaved Parmesan cheese, to taste

## Instructions
1. Preheat the oven on broil, or the toaster oven on the toast setting.
2. Place the bread on a baking sheet. Spread the garlic butter on each half or slice. (If you don't have garlic butter, spread with butter and sprinkle with garlic salt.)
3. Add a slice of tomato to each piece, then drizzle each tomato with the vinegar and olive oil (drizzle onto the tomato, not the bread, so the tomato doesn't slide off).
4. Place the provolone on top of each tomato slice, then sprinkle Parmesan over top.
5. Broil until the cheese is melted and bubbly (exact time not specified by source).

## Cook's Notes & Tips
- Homemade garlic butter: mix 1/2 cup softened butter with 1 teaspoon garlic salt and 1 tablespoon chopped parsley; keep a batch in the fridge.
- The order of assembly matters: drizzle oil and vinegar onto the tomato (not the bread) before adding cheese, so the tomato stays put and the cheese "glues" it to the sandwich.
- Bread swaps: split bagel, Texas toast, ciabatta, naan.
- Cheese swaps: mozzarella, Gruyere, Swiss, or feta for extra saltiness.
- Add fresh basil or oregano after toasting for extra flavor.
- Add a couple of slices of deli turkey before the tomato for extra protein.
