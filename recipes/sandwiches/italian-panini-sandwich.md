# Italian Panini Sandwich

## Description
A chef's next-level prosciutto panini: focaccia bread layered with a homemade kalamata olive tapenade, mozzarella, prosciutto, tomatoes and basil, then pressed until golden and crisp. This article is a narrative walkthrough of a chef's technique rather than a standard recipe card, so exact ingredient quantities aren't given by the source. ## Ingredients
_(for 2 sandwiches — quantities not specified by source, see Missing Information)_

- fresh basil leaves, bright green, a generous amount
- prosciutto di Parma, a package
- buffalo bocconcini (small fresh mozzarella balls), a few, thinly sliced
- 1 loaf focaccia, freshly baked, halved and then halved horizontally
- mayonnaise, a bit
- olive oil spray
- 2 vine-ripened tomatoes, thinly sliced
- capers, chopped, for the tapenade
- kalamata olives, chopped, for the tapenade
- shallots, chopped, for the tapenade
- red pepper flakes, a pinch
- sea salt
- black pepper
- olive oil, for the tapenade

## Instructions
1. Make the kalamata olive tapenade: combine chopped shallots, chopped capers and chopped kalamata olives in a bowl, then give everything a hefty douse of olive oil.
2. Set a cast iron grill pan over medium-high heat.
3. Cut a loaf of focaccia in half, then halve the two pieces horizontally, to create the bread for two sandwiches.
4. Spread the kalamata olive tapenade on one side of the bread, followed by neat rows of thinly sliced mozzarella (bocconcini).
5. Slather a bit of mayo on the other slice of bread ("almost everything is better with mayo") and arrange the prosciutto on top.
6. Add thinly sliced tomatoes on top of the mozzarella and season with sea salt, black pepper, and a pinch of red pepper flakes.
7. Pile whole basil leaves on top of the prosciutto — don't bother tearing them; more is more, and the leaves shrivel a little and the stems soften once pressed.
8. Close the sandwiches. Spray the hot griddle with olive oil spray, then add the sandwiches to the griddle and put the panini press lid on top.
9. Let the press do its work for 2-3 minutes without touching it, until the sandwich is hot and grill marks form on the focaccia.
10. Remove from the press and rotate the panini a quarter turn with a spatula to create hash marks, then put the lid back on for another 1-2 minutes.
11. Flip the panini and repeat the same pressing steps on the other side.
12. Move the panini off the heat onto a cutting board and slice.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
- Focaccia is considered the best bread for paninis: its flavor, texture, and ability to flatten to half its original size once pressed make it well-suited to the job.
- A quality panini press helps — cast iron grill pans with a press lid conduct heat better than electric presses, according to the chef. A DIY press can be made with a skillet, a plate, and a heavy object (or a few large cans) on top.
- Homemade spreads amp up the umami factor; a quick pesto or store-bought spread can substitute for the tapenade if needed, or simply drizzle olive oil on the bread.
- Don't skimp on the basil — use full leaves along one whole side of the panini rather than a light chiffonade.
- More Italian meats can be layered in alongside the prosciutto, such as salami, pepperoni, capicola, or mortadella.
