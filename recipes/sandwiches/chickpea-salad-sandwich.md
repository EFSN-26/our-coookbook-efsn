# Chickpea Salad Sandwich

## Description
A vegetarian take on the classic tuna sandwich — mashed chickpeas skip the mayo entirely, tossed in a bright yogurt-tahini dressing with bell peppers, parsley and oregano, and layered with lettuce, red onion and cherry tomatoes. [Source](https://lentillovingfamily.com/chickpea-salad-sandwich/)

## Ingredients
_(for 3 to 4 sandwiches — see §8 for scaling)_

For the chickpea salad:
- 1½ cups cooked chickpeas (use canned or cook dried chickpeas at home)
- ¼ cup plain yogurt (sub with Greek yogurt for a creamier texture)
- 1 tablespoon tahini
- 2 tablespoons lemon juice
- 1 tablespoon yellow mustard
- ½ cup bell peppers, finely chopped
- ¼ cup parsley, finely chopped
- 1 teaspoon dried oregano
- Salt and pepper to taste

For the sandwich:
- 6-8 slices bread
- 6-8 lettuce leaves
- 1 red onion, thinly sliced
- A handful cherry tomatoes, cut in half

## Instructions
1. In a mixing bowl, mash the chickpeas with a fork or potato masher. Keep it slightly chunky for added texture.
2. In another small bowl, combine together yogurt, tahini, lemon juice, yellow mustard, salt, pepper, and dried oregano. Mix well to make the dressing.
3. Combine the mashed chickpeas with bell peppers, parsley, and the dressing. Mix well.
4. Take two bread slices and place lettuce leaves on them. Spread the chickpea salad mixture onto one slice. Arrange sliced red onions and cherry tomatoes over the lettuce. Top with the other slice of bread to form sandwiches.
5. Serve immediately, or wrap sandwiches in foil or plastic wrap and refrigerate until ready to eat.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
- For a spicier kick, add a pinch of red pepper flakes to the dressing or diced jalapeno to the chickpea salad.
- Customize the vegetables based on preference — diced cucumber, celery, or carrots are great options.
- Adding lettuce leaves at the bottom of the filling creates a barrier between the bread and the filling, helping prevent it from becoming too soggy.
- Breadless option: skip the bread and use lettuce wraps or bell pepper boats for a low-carb or gluten-free alternative.
- Storage: store the chickpea salad and bread separately; the salad keeps in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 5 days. Freezing is not recommended — the chickpeas and vegetables can become mushy.
