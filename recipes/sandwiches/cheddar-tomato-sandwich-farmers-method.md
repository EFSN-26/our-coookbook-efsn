# Cheddar Tomato Sandwich, a Tomato Farmer's Method

## Description
North Carolina tomato farmer, author, and educator Craig LeHoullier's approach to the perfect tomato sandwich: thick slices of heirloom tomato and sharp cheddar between buttered sourdough, seared in a skillet until crispy — no mayonnaise involved. He calls it "summer heaven." [Source](https://www.simplyrecipes.com/best-tomato-sandwich-recipe-12006084)

## Ingredients
_(for 1 serving; exact quantities not specified by source)_

- Heirloom tomato (Cherokee Purple preferred, or yellow heirloom / beefsteak as alternatives), cut into thick slices
- 2 slices crusty sourdough bread (artisanal or self-baked, ideally with a whole wheat blend)
- Sharp cheddar cheese (e.g., Shelburne aged cheddar or Trader Joe's Unexpected Cheddar), thinly sliced
- Salted butter, for spreading
- Optional: bacon, fresh basil leaves

## Instructions
1. Cut thick slices of juicy heirloom tomato.
2. Cut two slices of bread from a crusty sourdough loaf.
3. Add a sharp cheddar cheese, cut into thin slices.
4. Spread butter evenly on the outside of both slices of bread — an even layer helps the bread crisp evenly.
5. Add half of the cheese slices to the inside of one piece of bread (this helps secure the tomato and keep it from slipping out). Add the tomato, then top with the remaining sliced cheese and close the sandwich.
6. Cook the sandwich in a hot skillet until the bread is crispy, the cheese melts, and the tomato warms through.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
- Craig LeHoullier is staunchly anti-mayonnaise for this sandwich.
- Avoid tomatoes with cracks and never refrigerate them — refrigeration diminishes flavor and texture.
- If growing your own tomatoes, pick them when half-ripened rather than letting them fully ripen on the vine (fully vine-ripened tomatoes are more prone to cracking and pests); finish ripening on the counter, not a sunny windowsill, and never in the fridge.
- Easy upgrades: add bacon (won't overshadow the other flavors) or fresh basil leaves.
