# Bacalhau ao forno com batatas e azeitonas pretas
(Baked Salt Cod with Potatoes and Black Olives)

## Description
This baked salt cod with potatoes and black olives, taught by Rodrigo Hilbert on the program "Tempero de Família," is ideal for serving at year-end holiday celebrations and Easter. The recipe uses few ingredients and comes together quickly. Translated from the original Portuguese source. Note: the source uses "bacalhau" (salt cod) interchangeably with "abrótea" (a related whitefish) fillets.

## Ingredients
_(for 6 servings)_

- 4 fillets of abrótea (cusk-eel) or salt cod
- 3 potatoes
- 2 red onions
- Black olives, to taste
- 500 milliliters olive oil

## Instructions
1. Desalt the fish by changing the water four or five times over the course of a day (or buy pre-desalted salt cod).
2. In a baking dish, place a layer of peeled, sliced potatoes, then a layer of the cod fillets, and on top pour a generous amount of olive oil and add the black olives.
3. Bake until the potatoes are cooked and tender.
4. Peel the red onion, slice it, and sauté it in olive oil.
5. Remove the cod when it is golden and top with the sautéed onion.
6. Serve right away.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
The cook desalts the fish himself in the recipe, but notes you can buy the salt cod already desalted to simplify preparation. A suggested side is plain day-to-day rice, which the source calls a delicious pairing with the baked cod, potatoes, and olives.
