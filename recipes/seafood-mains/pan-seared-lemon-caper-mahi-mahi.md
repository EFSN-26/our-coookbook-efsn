# Pan-Seared Lemon Caper Mahi Mahi

## Description
Pan-seared mahi mahi fillets topped with a bright, buttery, tangy lemon-caper sauce. A combination of tangy lemon, salty capers, and plenty of garlic makes both a marinade and a pan sauce that highlights the mild, flaky fish.

## Ingredients
_(for 4 servings)_

- 3 cloves garlic, finely chopped
- 2 tablespoon capers, finely chopped
- ¼ cup + 2 tablespoons olive oil, divided
- 3 lemons, divided
- 4 (4-ounce) mahi mahi fillets
- kosher salt, to taste
- 4 tablespoons cold butter
- 1 tablespoon chopped parsley

## Instructions
1. In a small bowl, combine the garlic, capers, and ¼ cup olive oil.
2. Zest and juice 2 of the lemons and whisk into the caper mixture. Slice the remaining lemon and set aside.
3. Place the mahi mahi fillets in a bowl and coat with half of the marinade, refrigerating for 1 hour. Set aside the remaining marinade.
4. Heat a skillet with the remaining 2 tablespoons of oil over medium-high heat.
5. Season the fillets with salt and cook on one side for 5 minutes before flipping and cooking on the remaining side for 5 more minutes, or until cooked through.
6. Remove from the pan and set aside.
7. Lower the heat, add the reserved half of the marinade, the lemon slices, and the cold butter.
8. Swirl until a thick sauce forms.
9. Spoon the sauce over the fish and serve with sliced lemons and parsley.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
- Start with great quality raw fish — the flesh should be pink to light beige and smell fresh like the ocean, not overly fishy. If frozen, thaw in the refrigerator overnight.
- Pat the fish completely dry before cooking to prevent a caramelization barrier.
- Decide skin-on or skin-off before cooking; if skin-on, start cooking skin side down.
- Heat a stainless steel pan over medium-high heat and ensure the oil is lightly smoking before adding the fish. Sear without disturbing for at least 3-4 minutes; if the fish sticks when nudged with tongs, it hasn't cooked long enough yet.
- The fish is done when the flesh turns opaque and noticeably firmer; use a thermometer to check for about 125°F if unsure.
- Serving suggestions: coconut rice, Hawaiian macaroni salad, blanched asparagus, roasted potatoes, a tangy vinaigrette over mixed greens, Parmesan risotto, fondant potatoes, or simple steamed broccoli. Pairs well with a chilled Sauvignon Blanc or Pinot Grigio.
