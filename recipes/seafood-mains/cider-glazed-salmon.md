# Cider-Glazed Salmon

## Description
A less-cliched St. Patrick's Day dish: wild salmon fillets basted in a tangy apple cider glaze with whole-grain mustard, fresh herbs and leeks. The cider reduces into a naturally sweetened, tart sauce that's also wonderful spooned over mashed potatoes or rice.

## Ingredients
_(for 2 servings)_

- 1 cup apple cider
- 1 tablespoon whole-grain mustard
- 1/2 tablespoon fresh rosemary, finely chopped
- 1/2 tablespoon fresh thyme, finely chopped
- 1/2 tablespoon fresh sage, finely chopped
- 1/2 teaspoon ground pepper, plus more to taste
- 1/4 teaspoon salt, divided
- 1 pinch nutmeg
- 2 tablespoons unsalted butter, divided
- 2 (4-ounce) wild salmon fillets, skin on
- 2 tablespoons leeks, finely diced

## Instructions
1. Whisk 1 cup cider and 1 tablespoon mustard together in a small saucepan. Stir in the rosemary, thyme, sage, pepper, 1/8 teaspoon of the salt and the nutmeg. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat to maintain a low simmer. Simmer, stirring often, until the cider has reduced and thickened, about 30 minutes. Remove from heat and stir in 1 tablespoon butter until melted.
2. Season the salmon fillets with the remaining 1/4 teaspoon salt and pepper to taste. Melt the remaining 1 tablespoon butter in a medium nonstick pan over medium heat. Add the salmon, skin-side up, and cook until the fish flakes apart easily with a fork, 4 to 6 minutes per side. During the last 2 minutes of cook time, add the leeks. Baste the salmon with the sauce. Serve the salmon with the sauce drizzled over it.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
Use wild salmon for a richer flavor, but note it's leaner than farmed salmon, so avoid overcooking it and be generous with the butter. Leftover sauce is great over mashed potatoes. One reviewer doubled the recipe and substituted 1 teaspoon each of dried thyme and sage for the fresh tablespoon amounts with good results.
