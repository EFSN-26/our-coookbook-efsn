# Baked Halibut

## Description
Halibut fillets topped with a garlic lemon butter and baked to perfection in just 10-15 minutes — a restaurant-quality dish that comes together easily at home.

## Ingredients
_(for 4 servings)_

- 1 pound halibut fillets (4 fillets, skin on is okay)
- ¼ teaspoon coarse salt
- 2 tablespoons butter (salted, slightly melted)
- lemon zest from ½ of a lemon
- 1 tablespoon lemon juice
- 1 teaspoon garlic, finely minced
- 1 teaspoon fresh parsley, chopped
- ⅛ teaspoon pepper
- ⅛ teaspoon paprika

## Instructions
1. Preheat oven to 415°F.
2. Place fillets, skin side down, on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper.
3. Pat the fillets with a paper towel to remove any excess moisture.
4. Sprinkle fillets with salt.
5. Make the butter mixture: combine the partially melted butter with garlic, lemon zest, lemon juice, and freshly chopped parsley.
6. Spread the butter mixture over the top of each fillet.
7. Sprinkle with paprika and pepper.
8. Bake in the preheated 415°F oven for 10-15 minutes until the fish is cooked through. The fish will be opaque and flakey.
9. Remove from oven and allow fish to rest for 2-3 minutes before removing from baking sheet.
10. To serve, slide a metal spatula between the fish and the skin — the fish will easily come away from the skin. Hold the fish gently in the center while you slide the fillet off the skin, if needed.
11. Serve with lemon wedges.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
- Get the freshest fillets available — they should be glossy and white without dried-out or yellow edges, and smell subtle, not fishy. Frozen fillets can be used; thaw fully before using.
- Preheat the oven fully before baking to ensure even cooking.
- Bake uncovered to allow heat to circulate.
- Cooking time varies with fillet thickness — watch closely, as halibut can dry out quickly if overcooked.
- The fish is done when it's opaque throughout (not translucent) and flakes easily with a fork; internal temperature should reach 145°F (63°C).
- Let the baked halibut rest for 2-3 minutes before serving to redistribute the juices.
- Recipe variations: swap in fresh dill, minced rosemary, or fresh thyme in the butter mixture; add a pinch of chili or chipotle powder for spice; or use lime juice and zest in place of lemon.
- If halibut isn't available, this recipe also works well with grouper, sea bass, tilapia, or haddock.
- Plan on 4-5 ounces of halibut per person for a typical fillet, or 2 fillets per person for bigger appetites.
