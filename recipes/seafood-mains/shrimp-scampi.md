# Shrimp Scampi

## Description
A classic Italian-American dish of tender shrimp in a rich garlic butter sauce brightened with white wine and lemon, ready in well under 30 minutes. [Source](https://www.gamintraveler.com/2025/08/16/how-to-make-shrimp-scampi/)

## Ingredients
_(for servings not specified by source)_

- 1 lb (450 g) large shrimp, peeled and deveined
- 4 tablespoons (1/2 stick) unsalted butter
- 2 tablespoons olive oil
- 4 cloves garlic, minced
- 1/4 teaspoon red pepper flakes, optional, for a bit of heat
- 1/2 cup dry white wine (or chicken broth for a non-alcoholic option)
- 1 lemon, juiced and zested
- 1/4 cup fresh parsley, chopped
- Salt and pepper, to taste
- Optional, for serving: 8 oz linguine or spaghetti, cooked al dente; crusty bread, for dipping

## Instructions
1. **Prepare the shrimp:** Pat the shrimp dry with paper towels and season with salt and pepper. Heat 2 tablespoons of butter and 2 tablespoons of olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat.
2. **Cook the shrimp:** Add the shrimp to the skillet in a single layer. Cook for 1-2 minutes per side until pink and opaque. Remove the shrimp and set aside.
3. Add the minced garlic and red pepper flakes (if using) to the skillet. Cook for about 1 minute until fragrant, being careful not to burn the garlic.
4. **Deglaze with white wine:** Pour in the wine and bring to a simmer. Let it reduce by half, about 2-3 minutes.
5. Stir in the lemon juice, lemon zest, and the remaining 2 tablespoons of butter. Whisk until the butter melts and the sauce is smooth.
6. Add the shrimp back to the skillet. Toss to coat in the sauce and heat through for 1-2 minutes.
7. **Garnish and serve:** Remove from heat, sprinkle with fresh parsley, and adjust seasoning with salt and pepper. Serve immediately over pasta, with crusty bread, or on its own.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
- Use large shrimp for the best texture; frozen shrimp are fine if thawed beforehand.
- Avoid overcrowding the skillet when cooking shrimp to ensure even cooking.
- A dry white wine like Sauvignon Blanc or Pinot Grigio adds depth; if substituting broth, adjust seasoning to balance flavor.
- Shrimp cook quickly and become rubbery if overcooked — remove them as soon as they turn pink.
- Approximate calories per the source: 450-500 with pasta (linguine), 300-350 without pasta.
- Serving variation noted in a near-identical republish of this recipe: pair with zucchini noodles or fresh greens instead of pasta for a lower-carb option.
