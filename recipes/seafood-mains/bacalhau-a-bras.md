# Bacalhau à Brás

## Description
One of the most iconic Portuguese dishes, bacalhau à brás is a simple sauté of salt cod, matchstick potatoes and soft custard-like eggs — Portugal's answer to Italy's carbonara, where eggs bind together a group of independent ingredients.

## Ingredients
_(for 4 servings as an appetizer, 2 as a main course)_

- 1/2 pound salt cod loin, presoaked for 36-48 hours (water changed every eight hours), bones and skin removed after soaking, chopped fine
- 1 pound potatoes (approximately 2 medium to large white or yukon gold), cut into matchstick strips
- 3 eggs, whole large, beaten
- 1 egg yolk, beaten with the whole eggs
- 1 onion, medium, cut into 1/4 inch slices
- 2 cloves garlic, minced
- 2 tablespoons olive oil (for the air fryer method)
- 3 tablespoons olive oil (for the sauté)
- vegetable oil, for deep frying the potatoes (about 1 1/2 cups)
- 2 bay leaves
- 1 tablespoon cilantro, chopped fine
- salt, to taste
- pepper, to taste
- 4 olives, black or green

## Instructions

### Frying the potatoes — Option 1: Air frying
1. Mix the potato matchsticks with 2 tablespoons olive oil in a metal bowl.
2. Place the mixture in the air fryer and cook at 400°F / 200°C for 15 to 20 minutes, or until just browned on the edges. Stir once after 7 minutes.
3. Once cooked, keep the air-fried matchstick potatoes in the air fryer tray or dry them on a paper towel-lined plate until ready to use.

### Frying the potatoes — Option 2: Deep frying
1. Heat about 1 1/2 cups of vegetable oil in a 10-inch cast iron pan and heat to 375°F / 190°C.
2. Once the oil is heated, gently drop in the matchstick potatoes. Fry for about 5 to 10 minutes in 2 to 3 batches until just browned on the edges. Drain on a paper towel-lined plate or a cooling rack until ready to use.

### Cooking the bacalhau à brás (both options)
1. Heat 3 tablespoons olive oil in a 12-inch fry pan over medium heat.
2. Cook the onions until softened and translucent. Add the garlic and sauté for one minute until fragrant. Add a small amount of salt to the onion/garlic sauté along with the bay leaves and stir.
3. Add the salt cod to the fry pan and sauté for about 5 minutes until the mixture is incorporated. Add the cilantro and stir.
4. Add the fried potato matchsticks and cook until they're soft.
5. Turn the heat to low and pour in the egg mixture. Stir constantly until the eggs are incorporated. Remove from the heat and stir until it's slightly thick and custard-like.
6. Serve in an oval baking dish or mold individual servings with a ring mold. Garnish with the olives.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
Start soaking the salt cod at least 36 hours in advance, changing the water every 8 hours; soaking longer than 36 hours (up to 50 hours max) risks losing the fish's briny flavor, so be judicious with added salt if you soak on the shorter end. Use cod loin (lombo) for the best flake and texture, or pre-shredded "desfiado" salt cod for less prep work. Slice potatoes into matchsticks with a sharp chef's knife rather than a mandoline for a rustic, uneven cut and to avoid injury; optionally soak the matchsticks first to remove excess starch (dry thoroughly before frying if you do). Chop the salt cod fine while the potatoes fry. When adding the beaten eggs, keep the heat low and keep stirring — there's a risk they'll scramble, but continuous stirring for about a minute prevents this. Kalamata olives are the author's preference, though many Portuguese people traditionally prefer canned black olives. Double the portion size to serve it as a main course rather than an appetizer.
