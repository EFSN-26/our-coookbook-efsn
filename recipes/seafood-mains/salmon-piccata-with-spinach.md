# Salmon Piccata with Spinach

## Description
Perfectly pan-seared salmon and tender spinach in a lemon butter caper sauce, all made in one skillet — a quick and easy dish bursting with light, bright flavors.

## Ingredients
_(for 6 servings)_

- 6 pieces fresh salmon, about 2" wide/4 ounces each
- 12 ounces baby spinach
- juice and zest of 1 lemon
- 1/4 cup white wine (Pinot Grigio)
- 1/4 cup butter
- 3 cloves garlic, minced
- 2 tablespoons capers
- 2 tablespoons olive oil
- 1 tablespoon fresh parsley, chopped
- 1 teaspoon salt
- 1/2 teaspoon ground black pepper

## Instructions
1. Season the salmon with half the salt and pepper. Heat a large skillet to medium-high heat and add in the olive oil.
2. Sear the salmon for a few minutes, skin side up. Flip the salmon over and cook until it's cooked through. Then remove and set aside.
3. Add in the garlic and sauté for about 20 seconds, then add in the wine. Add the butter, lemon juice, zest and capers.
4. Spoon out and reserve some of the sauce to use later. Add in the spinach and the remaining salt and pepper. Toss in the sauce, then cover and steam the spinach for 2 minutes or until tender.
5. Add the salmon back in and spoon the reserved sauce over the tops. Garnish with the parsley and serve.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
Wild-caught salmon is preferred over farm-raised for richer flavor, though farm-raised is safe to eat; sockeye contains about 25% more omega-3 than pink salmon. Fresh salmon should be moist with edges that aren't dried or curled, and is best cooked the same day it's purchased. Leave the skin on unless stated otherwise — it helps keep the fish moist during quick cooking. Store leftovers in an airtight container in the fridge up to two days; reheat covered with foil in a 350°F oven until warmed through. Freezing is not recommended. Variations: swap in dill, thyme or tarragon for the herbs; add cherry tomatoes, bell peppers or asparagus; stir in a splash of heavy cream or coconut milk for a creamier sauce; use lime or orange instead of lemon; top with toasted pine nuts, almonds or walnuts; add red pepper flakes or hot sauce for heat; or stir in whole grain mustard for tang.
