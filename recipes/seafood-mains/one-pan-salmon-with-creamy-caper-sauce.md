# One-Pan Salmon with Creamy Caper Sauce

## Description
A Mediterranean Diet-inspired skillet salmon in a creamy lemon-caper pan sauce, ready in 15 minutes with 10 ingredients. Suggested pairing: a favorite vegetable and a crisp salad. [Source](https://30seconds.com/food/tip/20154/Spectacular-One-Pan-Salmon-Recipe-With-Creamy-Caper-Sauce-15-Minutes-10-Ingredients)

## Ingredients
_(for 4 servings)_

- 4 skinless salmon fillets
- 1 tablespoon olive oil
- 4 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 1/4 cups chicken broth
- 2 teaspoons cornstarch
- 1/3 cup heavy cream
- 1 lemon, juiced and zested
- 1 tablespoon chopped fresh dill
- 1 tablespoon butter
- 2 tablespoons capers
- salt and pepper (for seasoning the salmon; quantity not specified by source)

## Instructions
1. Season the salmon fillets with salt and pepper. Heat the olive oil in a skillet and cook the salmon about 3–4 minutes on the first side, then flip and continue cooking until browned and cooked through, about 3 more minutes. Transfer to a plate.
2. Add the garlic to the pan and cook about 30 seconds.
3. Mix a couple tablespoons of the chicken broth with the cornstarch to make a slurry. Add the remaining chicken broth to the pan and reduce by half. Add the slurry and cook until thick, about 1 minute.
4. Add the cream, butter, dill, lemon juice, 1 teaspoon of the zest, and capers. Heat through. Return the salmon to the pan. Serve.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
- Capers are the unopened flower bud of *Capparis spinosa*, native to the Mediterranean; most sold in the U.S. are brined. Salted capers are fresher, firmer, and more flavorful — rinse before use if using the salted variety (per source).
