# Bacalao a la Vizcaína

## Description
A traditional Basque dish of desalted salt cod simmered in a rich roasted red pepper and tomato sauce — one of the most iconic dishes of Northern Spain, historically served for holidays and Sunday gatherings. [Source](https://www.gamintraveler.com/2025/08/06/how-to-make-bacalao-a-la-vizcaina/)

## Ingredients
_(for servings not specified by source)_

- 4 pieces salt cod fillets, about 1 lb total (desalted)
- 4 large red bell peppers
- 2 large onions, finely chopped
- 3 cloves garlic, minced
- 4 ripe tomatoes, chopped (or 1 can diced tomatoes)
- 1 cup fish or vegetable broth
- 1/4 cup extra virgin olive oil
- 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
- Salt and pepper, to taste
- Fresh parsley, chopped, for garnish, optional

## Instructions
1. **Desalt the cod:** Soak the salt cod fillets in cold water for 24-48 hours, changing the water every 6-8 hours to remove excess salt. Pat the fillets dry with paper towels afterward.
2. **Roast the red peppers:** Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C). Place the red bell peppers on a baking sheet and roast for about 20-25 minutes, turning occasionally, until the skins are charred and blistered. Remove from the oven and place in a bowl covered with plastic wrap; let steam for about 10 minutes. Peel off the skins, remove the seeds, and chop the peppers into small pieces.
3. **Prepare the sauce:** In a large skillet, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onions and garlic, and sauté until the onions are soft and translucent.
4. Add the chopped roasted peppers, tomatoes, and smoked paprika to the skillet. Stir well to combine. Pour in the fish or vegetable broth and bring to a simmer. Cook for about 20-30 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the sauce thickens and the flavors meld.
5. **Blend the sauce:** Use an immersion blender, or transfer to a blender, and blend until smooth. Return the sauce to the skillet and season with salt and pepper to taste.
6. **Cook the cod:** Place the desalted cod fillets in the sauce, making sure they're submerged. Cover the skillet and simmer on low heat for about 10-15 minutes, or until the cod is cooked through and flakes easily with a fork.
7. **Serve:** Carefully transfer the cod fillets to serving plates and spoon the sauce over the top. Garnish with chopped fresh parsley, if desired.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
- Properly desalting the cod is crucial to avoid overly salty fish — be patient and change the soaking water regularly.
- Roasting the peppers adds smoky depth; jarred roasted red peppers can be used for convenience.
- Blending the sauce ensures a smooth, velvety texture; adjust consistency by adding more broth if needed.
- Avoid overcooking the cod so it stays tender and flaky.
- Traditional Basque versions rely on rehydrated chorizo (choricero) peppers rather than tomatoes for the sauce's color and flavor; this version uses tomatoes for convenience.
- Approximate calories per the source: about 320 per serving (total batch approximately 1,280 calories).
- Serving suggestions from the source: boiled or roasted potatoes, crusty bread, sautéed greens or roasted vegetables; pairs with Basque Txakoli or a young Tempranillo.
