# Gambas al Ajillo (Spanish Garlic Shrimp)

## Description
One of the most beloved tapas in Spanish cuisine: shrimp sizzled in olive oil with garlic and chili, served piping hot, often in the same dish it was cooked in — perfect for soaking up the garlicky oil with crusty bread. [Source](https://www.gamintraveler.com/2025/07/26/how-to-make-gambas-al-ajillo/)

## Ingredients
_(servings not specified by source)_

- 1 pound (450g) raw shrimp, peeled and deveined (tails left on for presentation, if desired)
- 4-5 cloves garlic, thinly sliced or minced
- 1/2 cup olive oil
- 1-2 small dried chilies, crushed (or 1/2 teaspoon red pepper flakes for heat)
- 1/4 cup dry sherry or white wine (optional)
- Salt, to taste
- Fresh parsley, finely chopped, for garnish
- 1 lemon, cut into wedges, for serving

## Instructions
1. Ensure the shrimp are thoroughly cleaned, peeled, and deveined. Pat dry with paper towels to remove excess moisture — crucial for a good sear.
2. In a large skillet, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the garlic and crushed dried chilies (or red pepper flakes). Sauté until the garlic is just golden — be careful not to let it burn.
3. Increase the heat to medium-high and add the shrimp in a single layer. Sprinkle with salt. Cook about 1-2 minutes on each side, or until the shrimp turn pink and are just cooked through. Avoid overcooking.
4. Pour in the dry sherry or white wine, if using, and let it simmer for a minute to let the alcohol evaporate; this also lifts the browned bits from the bottom of the pan.
5. Immediately transfer the shrimp and garlic oil to a warm plate, or serve right in the skillet. Garnish with chopped parsley and serve with lemon wedges.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
- Use fresh shrimp if possible; frozen shrimp works too, just make sure it's fully thawed and patted dry.
- Adjust garlic and chili quantities to taste — more garlic delivers the traditional stronger flavor.
- Serve with crusty bread to soak up the garlic-infused oil; the dish is meant to be eaten the moment it arrives, as shrimp continue cooking in the hot oil.
