# Lemon-Garlic Salmon & Broccoli Bowls

## Description
These salmon and broccoli rice bowls are an easy sheet-pan dinner paired with brown rice and a creamy lemon-dill sauce. Roasting the broccoli and salmon together keeps cleanup simple, while the bright, tangy sauce ties everything together for a balanced meal.

## Ingredients
_(for 4 servings)_

- 4 cups broccoli florets (1-inch)
- 2 tablespoons extra-virgin olive oil, divided
- 1 teaspoon salt, divided
- 1/2 teaspoon ground pepper, divided
- 1/4 cup whole-milk plain strained yogurt (Greek-style)
- 2 tablespoons reduced-fat sour cream
- 4 teaspoons lemon juice
- 2 teaspoons Dijon mustard
- 1 teaspoon finely chopped fresh dill, plus more for garnish
- 1/4 teaspoon garlic powder
- 4 (5-ounce) skinless salmon fillets, cut into 1½-inch chunks
- 3 cups hot cooked brown rice

## Instructions
1. Preheat oven to 400°F. Line a large rimmed baking sheet with foil. Toss 4 cups broccoli with 1 tablespoon oil, 1/4 teaspoon salt and 1/4 teaspoon pepper on the prepared baking sheet until evenly coated; spread into an even layer. Roast for 8 minutes.
2. Meanwhile, combine 1/4 cup yogurt, 2 tablespoons sour cream, 4 teaspoons lemon juice, 2 teaspoons mustard, 1 teaspoon dill, 1/4 teaspoon garlic powder and 1/4 teaspoon salt in a small bowl; stir until smooth.
3. Place salmon chunks in a medium bowl. Add the remaining 1 tablespoon oil, 1/2 teaspoon salt and 1/4 teaspoon pepper; toss to coat. Remove the baking sheet from the oven; push the broccoli to one side and arrange the salmon in a single layer on the other side.
4. Roast until the salmon flakes easily with a fork, 6 to 8 minutes.
5. Divide 3 cups rice among 4 shallow bowls. Top with the salmon and broccoli. Spoon the sauce over the top. Garnish with additional dill, if desired.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
Cut broccoli into evenly sized florets so it roasts uniformly. Precooked or microwavable brown rice can save time. Quinoa, white rice, or farro can be swapped in for the brown rice. A squeeze of fresh lemon juice or a pinch of red pepper flakes just before serving enhances brightness/heat. Frozen broccoli is not recommended (won't get the same fresh, crisp roast). Store leftovers in an airtight container in the fridge up to 2 days, ideally keeping the sauce in a separate container; sauce can be made up to 2 days ahead. Reheat best in an air fryer, or a 275°F oven for 10-15 minutes, or microwave in 30-second increments on medium. Recipe developed by Jasmine Smith. Nutrition per serving (per source, serving size about 3½ oz salmon, 3/4 cup broccoli, 3/4 cup rice & 1½ tbsp sauce): 500 calories, 18g fat, 4g saturated fat, 81mg cholesterol, 44g carbs, 3g total sugars, 0g added sugars, 38g protein, 4g fiber, 744mg sodium, 898mg potassium.
