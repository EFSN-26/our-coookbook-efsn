# Blackened Halibut With Mango-Avocado Relish

## Description
A dish that will transport you to the fabled tables of New Orleans: halibut coated in a blackening spice mix and seared in butter, topped with a cool, creamy mango-avocado relish. A celebration of contrasts between the fiery crust and the cool salsa. [Source](https://www.tastingtable.com/1528312/blackened-halibut-with-mango-avocado-relish-recipe/)

## Ingredients
_(for 2 servings)_

For the blackening mix:
- ½ teaspoon dried oregano
- ½ teaspoon dried thyme
- ½ teaspoon dried onion flakes
- 1 teaspoon granulated garlic
- ½ teaspoon mustard powder
- ½ teaspoon cayenne pepper
- 1 teaspoon sweet paprika
- 2 teaspoons smoked paprika
- ½ teaspoon freshly ground black pepper
- ¼ teaspoon salt

For the fish:
- 2 (roughly 8-ounce) halibut filets
- 3 tablespoons unsalted butter
- 1 tablespoon olive oil

For the salsa:
- 1 large mango, peeled, pitted, and diced
- 1 small avocado, peeled, pitted, and diced
- ½ medium jalapeño, seeded and finely chopped
- ¼ cup finely chopped red bell pepper
- ¼ finely chopped red onion (quantity as printed by source)
- 3 tablespoons chopped cilantro, plus more for serving
- juice of 1 large lime
- 1 tablespoon honey
- salt, to taste

For serving:
- lime wedges

## Instructions
1. Make the blackening mix by combining the ingredients in a small bowl. Set aside.
2. Pat the halibut filets dry and season liberally with the prepared blackening mix.
3. Make the salsa by combining all the ingredients in a bowl and seasoning with a pinch of salt. Set aside.
4. In a cast-iron skillet over medium-high heat, melt the butter with the olive oil until very hot.
5. Add the fish filets and cook for 4 minutes per side, until the fish turns opaque inside and the internal temperature reaches 145°F.
6. Transfer the cooked halibut to a plate.
7. Top the blackened halibut filets with the prepared salsa.
8. Serve immediately with more lime and cilantro.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
- Other firm white fish work well in place of halibut, such as cod, mahi-mahi, or sea bass; adjust cook time for filet thickness. Ensure internal temperature reaches the FDA-recommended 145°F.
- Reduce the cayenne in the blackening mix for less heat, or increase it (or use ground Thai chili peppers) for more. Adjust the amount of jalapeño, or remove the seeds, to control the salsa's heat.
- Serve with dirty rice or quinoa, roasted vegetables (bell peppers, zucchini, sweet potatoes), a bed of mixed bitter greens dressed with vinaigrette, or grilled asparagus.
- Note: the source's ingredient list prints the red onion quantity as "¼ finely chopped red onion" without stating a unit (likely a typo for ¼ cup, matching the red bell pepper measurement above it) — recorded verbatim above rather than guessed.
