# Cedar Plank Salmon

## Description
Salmon grilled on a soaked cedar plank, marinated in a mustard-soy glaze inspired by chef Billy Grant of Bricco restaurant in West Hartford, CT, and adapted by the author's chef-husband. The plank gives the fish a smoky flavor and keeps it from sticking to the grill.

## Ingredients
_(for salmon fillets sized to your cedar plank — see Missing Information; exact quantities were not given by the source)_

- Cedar grilling planks (soaked in water for about 1 hour before use)
- Salmon fillets (the source used Ora king salmon; 3 small fillets fit on one plank)
- Dijon mustard, equal parts with the whole grain mustard below
- Whole grain mustard, equal parts with the Dijon mustard above
- Soy sauce, roughly equal parts with the mustards
- Garlic, minced (about 2 cloves per salmon fillet, or more to taste)
- Lemon, thinly sliced (about 3 slices per fillet)

## Instructions
1. **Soak the planks.** Place the cedar planks in a shallow bowl filled with water, weighted down with something heavy to keep them submerged, and let them soak for about 1 hour. (Dry planks over an open flame are a fire hazard, and soaking also helps keep the fish moist.)
2. **Make the marinade.** Whisk together the Dijon mustard, whole grain mustard, soy sauce, and minced garlic in a bowl (roughly equal parts mustard and soy sauce).
3. **Marinate the fish.** Coat the salmon in the marinade and refrigerate for 4–6 hours.
4. **Plank it up.** Place the salmon fillets on a soaked plank (the source used 3 small fillets per plank), brush on more marinade, and arrange thin lemon slices on top of each fillet.
5. **Preheat the grill.** Always preheat the grill — cooking on a cold grill can cause sticking, and food cooks better on a hot grill.
6. **Grill it up.** Preheat the grill over high heat until it reaches 450°F, then add the planks and close the lid. Check every 5 minutes or so, until the salmon is cooked through, about 15–18 minutes total. The plank edges will be charred when the fish is ready.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
- Use a restaurant-grade soy sauce for the most umami flavor (the source cites Haku Mizunara Whisky Barrel Aged Shoyu and Momofuku's Soy Sauce by chef David Chang); swap in gluten-free tamari or coconut aminos for a gluten-free version.
- Don't season the fish with additional salt and pepper — the marinade's salty-sweet nature is enough, and extra seasoning risks making it too salty.
- Don't touch or flip the salmon once it's on the planks — you can move the planks around the grill to avoid flare-ups, but the fish roasts in place in its marinade juices.
- Portion the salmon to the plank size: the source used 1 plank for 3 small salmon fillets, but a larger plank can hold a whole side of salmon.
- Serve with a side of jasmine rice, grilled asparagus, and morel mushrooms skewered and roasted in garlicky parsley butter.
