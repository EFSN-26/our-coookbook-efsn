# Sheet Pan Mediterranean Garlic Butter Baked Salmon

## Description
A high-protein, low-carb sheet-pan salmon made by slathering fillets with a quick compound butter of parsley, orange juice, garlic and whole peppercorns, then baking until the butter melts into the fish for a moist, succulent result.

## Ingredients
_(for 6 servings)_

- 2 pounds salmon fillets
- 4 tablespoons butter, softened
- 1/4 cup fresh chopped parsley (or whatever fresh herbs you have)
- 1/4 cup orange juice
- 4 cloves garlic, minced
- 1/2 tablespoon whole peppercorns
- 1/4 cup grated Parmesan cheese, optional
- Salt and pepper, to taste

## Instructions
1. In a bowl, stir together the butter, parsley, peppercorns, orange juice, garlic and Parmesan, if using. (Omit the peppercorns if you don't want spice in the salmon.)
2. Season the salmon fillets with salt and pepper and put them onto a parchment paper-covered baking sheet. Spread the butter mixture evenly over all the fillets. (Parchment paper keeps the salmon from sticking and helps with cleanup.)
3. Bake in a preheated 400-degree F oven for about 15 minutes or until the fish flakes easily. Serve. Store the salmon in an airtight container in the fridge.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
- The orange juice in the butter adds a touch of citrus, while the whole peppercorns add flecks of spiciness — omit the peppercorns for a milder dish.
- Serve with broccolini, asparagus, brussels sprouts, carrots, green peas, roasted potatoes, or quinoa.
- This sheet-pan recipe can easily be cut in half for dinner for two.
