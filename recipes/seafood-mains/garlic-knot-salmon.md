# Garlic Knot Salmon

## Description
Roasted salmon topped with buttery, garlicky panko breadcrumbs and parmesan — inspired by the flavors of garlic knots and ready in about 25 minutes, making it an easy weeknight main.

## Ingredients
_(for 4 servings — see §8 for scaling)_

- 4 tablespoons butter, melted
- 3 cloves garlic, grated or minced
- 1/4 cup panko breadcrumbs
- 1/4 cup finely grated parmesan
- 4 (6-ounce) salmon fillets, thawed if frozen
- 1/2 teaspoon kosher salt, or to taste
- 2 tablespoons minced fresh parsley
- 4 lemon wedges, for serving

## Instructions
1. Preheat the oven to 425°F.
2. Combine the melted butter and garlic in a small bowl. Add half of the mixture to a baking dish large enough to hold all 4 salmon fillets without touching.
3. In the small bowl with the remaining garlic butter, add the panko breadcrumbs and parmesan. Stir to combine.
4. Pat the salmon fillets dry and season with salt. Place them in the buttery baking dish (skin-side down, if using skin-on fillets). Heap the breadcrumb mixture on top of the fillets, scooping up and repiling any that falls off. Press lightly to help the breadcrumb mixture adhere.
5. Bake until the salmon is done to your liking and the breadcrumbs are crisp — about 135°F in the center for medium, which takes 12 to 16 minutes depending on fillet thickness. Transfer the fillets to plates, drizzle with any remaining garlic butter, and top with parsley. Serve with lemon wedges.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
- If using frozen salmon fillets, thaw them completely, preferably overnight in the fridge; drain off any liquid and pat dry before seasoning and baking.
- Look for equally-sized fillets so they cook evenly.
- Pack the breadcrumbs on with your hand to help them adhere and catch any clumps that fall off.
- To remove the skin before serving, slide a thin metal spatula between the skin and the fillet, leaving the skin behind.
