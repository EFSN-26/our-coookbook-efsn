# Easy 1-Pan Garlic Shrimp and Ramen Noodles

## Description
A satisfying weeknight dinner that comes together in a single skillet, so there are fewer dishes to wash but still a deep, complex flavor. In 30 minutes, plump shrimp, perfectly cooked noodles, and tender broccoli come together, all coated in a sweet, salty, spicy, and tangy sauce.

## Ingredients
_(for 4 servings)_

- 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
- 2 tablespoons minced garlic
- 1 tablespoon minced ginger
- 1/2 cup thinly sliced scallions
- 1 pound large shrimp, peeled and deveined
- 2 (3-ounce) packets instant ramen noodles
- 4 cups vegetable broth
- 2 cups small broccoli florets
- 1 tablespoon soy sauce
- 1 tablespoon rice vinegar
- 1 tablespoon honey
- 1/2 teaspoon sesame oil
- 1/2 teaspoon crushed red pepper
- 1 tablespoon cornstarch

Optional, for garnish:
- Additional thinly sliced scallions
- Additional crushed red pepper
- Toasted sesame seeds

## Instructions
1. Start a large skillet over high heat and add the vegetable oil.
2. Add the garlic, ginger, and scallions, and cook for 2 minutes, until fragrant.
3. Add the shrimp and cook for around 3 minutes, or until just cooked.
4. Remove the shrimp from the skillet.
5. Add the ramen noodles and broth to the pan.
6. Once the broth is boiling, add the broccoli and cook the noodles and broccoli for around 6 minutes, or until the noodles are done.
7. Stir in the soy sauce, rice vinegar, honey, sesame oil, and crushed red pepper.
8. In a small bowl, make a slurry with the cornstarch and a tablespoon or two of water.
9. Add the cornstarch slurry to the pan and mix well. Cook until the sauce thickens.
10. Return the shrimp to the pan and toss to coat with sauce. Remove from the heat.
11. Serve immediately, garnished with thinly sliced scallions, crushed red pepper, and toasted sesame seeds, if desired.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
- Fresh ramen noodles, soba, or udon can be substituted for instant ramen — cooking times and broth amounts will differ, so check package instructions and adjust.
- Vegetable broth was chosen to keep the dish pescatarian-friendly and because it's widely available, but any light broth (chicken, shrimp, etc.) works.
- For more shrimp flavor, simmer the shrimp shells in the vegetable broth for 5–10 minutes before starting, or make a homemade shrimp stock.
