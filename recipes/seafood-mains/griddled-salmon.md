# Back-to-Basics: How to Make Salmon on the Griddle

## Description
A technique-focused recipe for cooking skin-on salmon on a flat-top griddle rather than open grill grates, using Steven Raichlen's Mediterranean rub and a lemon brown sugar barbecue sauce for a crispy-skinned, evenly-seared fillet with no risk of the fish falling through grates.

## Ingredients
_(servings not specified by source)_

- Salmon fillet(s), skin on
- Olive oil, for the skin and for oiling the griddle
- Kosher salt
- Black pepper, freshly cracked
- Steven Raichlen's Mediterranean rub, applied liberally
- Steven Raichlen's lemon brown sugar barbecue sauce, for glazing

## Instructions
1. Clean the salmon skin with a paper towel and check the fillet for any small bones.
2. Apply olive oil to the salmon skin and season it with kosher salt and freshly cracked black pepper.
3. Season the salmon liberally with Steven Raichlen's Mediterranean rub.
4. Preheat the griddle to medium-to-medium-high heat. It is ready when a drop of water placed on the surface evaporates immediately.
5. Lightly oil the griddle so the salmon won't stick.
6. Place the salmon on the griddle skin side down. Cook for 4-5 minutes, based on the thickness and size of the salmon, until it releases naturally from the griddle.
7. Flip the salmon and sear the top for about 1 minute.
8. Flip it back over and paint the top of the salmon with the lemon brown sugar barbecue sauce.
9. Flip once more and sear the sauce into the top for about 30 seconds.
10. Total cooking time is about 7 minutes; times will vary based on the size and thickness of the salmon.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
- Leaving the skin on helps hold the salmon together while cooking and minimizes sticking; the skin also crisps up nicely if seasoned and cooked skin-side down.
- A griddle offers a large, solid, evenly-heated surface with no hot spots and no grates for the fish to fall through, unlike a traditional grill.
- To test griddle readiness, place a drop of water on the surface — if it evaporates immediately, the griddle is hot enough.
- If the griddle is too cool, the salmon won't sear properly; if too hot, it will burn before cooking through evenly.
- The source served the salmon with griddled asparagus, rice pilaf, and a griddled cut lemon to squeeze over the fish and asparagus.
