# Lebanese Salad Dressing

## Description
It's simple and refreshingly sour, ready in minutes. Made with fresh lemon juice, extra virgin olive oil and seasoning, this is the go-to Lebanese salad dressing everyone in Lebanon loves. [Source](https://plantbasedfolk.com/lebanese-salad-dressing/)

## Ingredients
_(for 1 cup — see §8 for scaling)_

- ½ cup lemon juice, freshly squeezed
- ½ cup olive oil, extra virgin
- 1 teaspoon salt, or to taste
- ½ teaspoon black pepper, or to taste

Optional — add one or a combination of these:
- 1-3 cloves garlic, crushed, to taste
- 1 teaspoon ground sumac, to taste
- 1 teaspoon ground mint, to taste
- 2 teaspoon pomegranate molasses, to taste

## Instructions
1. In a mason jar or bowl, thoroughly combine all the ingredients by whisking or shaking until fully incorporated.
2. Adjust the ingredients to taste, and drizzle over your favorite salad ingredients. Store leftovers in the fridge.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
- At the basic level, equal parts fresh lemon juice and the best extra virgin olive oil are used with salt and pepper; adjust the amount of oil to taste (the author likes to reduce it slightly).
- Garlic, ground sumac, ground mint, or pomegranate molasses can be added to taste — the author's photos show a version using ground mint and crushed garlic.
- Keeps well in the fridge in an airtight container or jar for up to one week.
- If the oil solidifies in the fridge, let it sit at room temperature until liquid again. Give the jar a good shake before using, as the oil and lemon will separate.
- The author uses this dressing on her Arabic salad and on a Lebanese potato salad recipe.
