# Avocado Cilantro Dressing

## Description
This easy homemade Avocado Cilantro Dressing is fresh, delicious, and super creamy. This silky sauce has a vibrant green color and rich texture. Simple to make and ready in under 5 minutes!

## Ingredients
_(for 4 servings)_

- ½ avocado
- 1 tbsp lime juice
- ½ cup cilantro leaves
- ½ tbsp olive oil
- 1 clove garlic, minced
- ½ cup water
- ¼ tsp fine sea salt
- ¼ tsp freshly ground pepper

## Instructions
1. Scoop the flesh from the avocado and cut it into large chunks.
2. Place all ingredients into a large jar (or blender/food processor).
3. Blend, using an immersion blender or regular blender, and completely mix everything together until smooth and pourable.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
- Lemon juice can be used in place of lime juice; substitute fresh parsley if you aren't a fan of cilantro. Any neutral oil works in place of olive oil (MCT oil is also good for a mild flavor).
- Expert tip for picking a ripe avocado: remove the small stem or cap at the top; if it comes off easily and you see green underneath, it's likely ripe. Brown discoloration or a hard-to-remove stem means it may be overripe.
- Variations: make it extra creamy by adding a few dollops of Greek yogurt or sour cream before blending; add diced jalapeños or serrano peppers for heat; add chipotle peppers in adobo sauce for a smoky, slightly spicy flavor.
- Storage: Store any leftovers in the refrigerator in an airtight container for 3 to 4 days. Freezing is not recommended — the texture changes and it is not as flavorful after thawing.
