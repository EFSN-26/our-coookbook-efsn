# 5-Minute Homemade French Salad Dressing

## Description
An everyday vinaigrette that's a foundation of the author's cooking repertoire — a simple, riffable French-style dressing that goes with everything, made with garlic, olive oil, lemon juice and oregano. [Source](https://www.thekitchn.com/everyday-salad-dressing-23725171)

## Ingredients
_(quantities as given by the source; no serving count was stated)_

- 1 clove garlic, minced
- 1 big pinch kosher salt
- 3 tablespoons olive oil
- 1 tablespoon lemon juice
- 1 teaspoon oregano, dried or fresh

## Instructions
1. Make garlic paste: mince one clove of garlic. Sprinkle a big pinch of kosher salt on top and use the flat of the knife blade to crush the garlic and salt together until they form a paste. Alternatively, finely grate the garlic on a rasp grater and add the salt directly to a bowl or small jar.
2. Add oil and lemon juice: the magic ratio of oil to acid for salad dressing is 3-to-1. Add 3 tablespoons of olive oil and 1 tablespoon of lemon juice.
3. Add oregano: use dried or fresh; both are great. Use about 1 teaspoon of oregano for this volume of dressing. If unsure about flavor, start with less and taste before adding more.
4. Shake or whisk: whisk the dressing together, or if using a jar, put the lid on and shake until well-mixed. Taste and add additional salt if desired.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
- Scales up well — the dressing keeps in the fridge, so a double or triple batch can be made ahead. The oil will solidify in the fridge; let the dressing come to room temperature before tossing with salad.
- Very riffable: for more umami, add a minced anchovy. For a little sweetness, try a teaspoon or two of honey. For extra flavor, add a bit of Dijon mustard or a few chopped capers.
