# Sugar Free Honey Lime Dressing

## Description
A sweet and tangy dressing to add a little zing to your next salad — made without any refined sugar using sugar substitutes, so it tastes like the classic honey lime dressing but is blood-sugar friendly. [Source](https://thatsugarfreelife.com/sugar-free-honey-lime-dressing/)

## Ingredients
_(for 8 servings)_

- ¼ cup Dijon mustard
- ¼ cup allulose honey
- 2 tablespoons sugar substitute erythritol (Swerve)
- 2 tablespoons grapeseed oil or sesame oil
- 2 tablespoons apple cider vinegar
- 2 tablespoons lime juice, fresh squeezed

## Instructions
1. Add all ingredients to a small blender.
2. Blend on high speed 30 to 60 seconds, until uniformly smooth.
3. Place in a jar with lid and refrigerate until ready to use.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
- Both granulated and confectioners' (powdered) Swerve work well; Monkfruit sweetener would be another good choice.
- The honey substitute used was Allulose honey, which has a thinner texture but tastes remarkably like honey.
- Great on a simple green salad; also delicious drizzled on fruit, such as pineapple or watermelon.
- This is the author's sugar-free adaptation of a honey lime dressing that also appears (made with regular honey and granulated sugar) in this cookbook's Watermelon Feta Salad with Honey Lime Dressing recipe, from the same author's other site, Sula and Spice.
