# Lemon-Dill Vinaigrette

## Description
This quick lemon-dill vinaigrette balances bright lemon juice with sweet honey, fresh dill and a touch of Dijon. It's perfect drizzled over greens, roasted vegetables or grilled fish. [Source](https://www.eatingwell.com/lemon-dill-vinaigrette-11915285)

## Ingredients
_(for 6 servings)_

- 1 teaspoon grated lemon zest
- 3 tablespoons lemon juice
- 1 tablespoon honey
- 1 teaspoon Dijon mustard
- ¼ teaspoon salt
- ¼ teaspoon ground pepper
- ¼ cup extra-virgin olive oil
- 2 tablespoons chopped fresh dill

## Instructions
1. Whisk the lemon zest, lemon juice, honey, mustard, salt and pepper together in a medium bowl until combined.
2. Gradually whisk in the olive oil until emulsified. Stir in the chopped dill.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
Using Dijon mustard and honey helps emulsify the dressing and keep the ingredients from separating; whisk in the oil slowly. Tarragon can be substituted for the dill, and maple syrup can replace the honey for a deeper sweetness. If using dried dill instead of fresh, use only 2 teaspoons dried in place of the 2 tablespoons fresh (dried herbs are more concentrated). To store: refrigerate in an airtight container for up to 3 days; let it sit at room temperature for 5 minutes, then shake or whisk well before using, and add fresh dill just before serving for peak flavor. Great over green salads, cucumber salad, roasted salmon, cod, shrimp, tofu, couscous, green beans, new potatoes, carrots or asparagus. Recipe developed by Jasmine Smith.
