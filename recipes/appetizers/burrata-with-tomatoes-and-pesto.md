# Burrata with Tomatoes and Pesto

## Description
Creamy burrata cheese, juicy heirloom and cherry tomatoes, tangy olives, and a fresh homemade basil pesto come together on a platter for a rustic Italian appetizer that doubles as a light salad or side dish.

## Ingredients
_(for 4 servings — see §8 for scaling)_

For the Salad:
- 3 heirloom tomatoes, sliced
- 1 cup cherry tomatoes, or grape, halved
- ½ cup fresh basil
- ¼ cup olives, Kalamata or green
- 3 to 4 burrata cheese, large
- Salt and black pepper, to taste

For the Basil Pesto:
- 2 cups basil leaves
- ½ cup pine nuts
- 2 tablespoons lemon juice, fresh
- 3 cloves garlic, small
- ¼ teaspoon salt
- ¼ teaspoon black pepper
- ⅓ cup extra virgin olive oil, more if needed
- ⅓ cup parmesan cheese, freshly grated

## Instructions
1. Layer the tomato slices, basil, olives, and burrata cheese on a large platter.
2. Add the basil, pine nuts, lemon juice, garlic, salt, and pepper to a food processor.
3. Pulse a few times until everything is very small.
4. Turn on the food processor and slowly add the olive oil, then process until smooth.
5. Add the parmesan and pulse a couple more times.
6. Drizzle the salad with the pesto and add salt and pepper as needed.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
- Adjust the seasonings: try truffle salt or flaky sea salt instead of regular salt for extra umami flavor.
- Use the best tomatoes you can find — a mix of heirloom colors and cherry/grape tomatoes looks great on the platter.
- Try adding thinly sliced fresh figs or wafer-thin ham slices for contrast; substitute large capers or caperberries for the olives, or try roasted tomatoes instead of raw ones.
- No burrata? Substitute fresh mozzarella (buffalo mozzarella is best).
- Add a protein like chicken, shrimp, or sliced steak to turn this into a main course.
- Store leftovers in an airtight container in the refrigerator for 2–3 days. Does not freeze well.
