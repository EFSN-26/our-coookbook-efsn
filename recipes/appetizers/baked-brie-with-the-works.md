# Baked Brie with Honey, Walnuts, Figs, and Rosemary

## Description
While visiting a friend in Paris years ago, Forough Vakili, founder of Atlanta's Le Bon Nosh, had her first taste of baked brie and it's been a staple on her hosting menu ever since. This version stars honey, walnuts, dried figs, and rosemary, warmed just until soft and fragrant. Her biggest tip: choose a good-quality cheese from a cheese shop or counter, since "it makes all the difference."

## Ingredients
_(for 4 servings)_

- 1 (8 oz.) wheel brie cheese (Brie de Meaux or Camembert both work well)
- 2 tablespoons honey
- 1/4 cup chopped walnuts
- 3–4 dried figs, chopped
- 1–2 sprigs fresh rosemary, chopped
- Freshly ground black pepper, to taste
- Crackers or toasted fresh baguette slices, for serving

## Instructions
1. Preheat your oven to 350°F.
2. Unwrap the brie and place it in a small, oven-safe dish or on a lined baking sheet. Score the top of the brie with a sharp knife in a crisscross pattern to allow the toppings to seep in.
3. Drizzle 2 tablespoons of honey over the brie. Sprinkle the chopped walnuts, figs, and rosemary evenly across the top. Add a dash of freshly ground black pepper.
4. Place the brie in the oven and bake for 10–15 minutes, until the cheese is warm and soft but not completely melted. The toppings should be golden and fragrant.
5. Remove from the oven and serve immediately with crackers or slices of toasted fresh baguette.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
- Instead of walnuts, try pecans or almonds; instead of figs, apricots and cranberries also work well.
- For an indulgent treat, top the dish with shaved fresh black truffles.
- Choose a good-quality cheese from a cheese shop or counter — "it makes all the difference."
