# Patatas Bravas

## Description
One of Spain's most iconic tapas: crispy fried potato cubes topped with a spicy tomato-based bravas sauce and, optionally, a drizzle of creamy garlic aioli. Originally from Madrid, the dish has spread across Spanish tapas bars, with regions adding their own twist to the sauce.

## Ingredients
_(for the recipe as written — servings not specified by source)_

- 3-4 large potatoes (about 1.5 pounds), peeled and cut into rough 1-inch cubes
- salt, to taste
- vegetable oil, for frying

### For the Brava Sauce
- 1 tablespoon olive oil
- 1 small onion, finely chopped
- 3 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
- 1/2 teaspoon cayenne pepper, adjust based on spice preference
- 1 can (14 ounces) crushed tomatoes
- 1 tablespoon red wine vinegar
- salt, to taste

### For the Aioli (Garlic Mayo, optional)
- 1/2 cup mayonnaise
- 1 clove garlic, minced
- 1 tablespoon lemon juice
- salt, to taste

## Instructions
1. **Prepare the potatoes:** Boil the potato cubes in salted water for about 5-7 minutes until slightly tender but not fully cooked. Drain and let dry. Heat vegetable oil in a deep fryer or large frying pan to 375°F (190°C). Fry the potatoes in batches until golden and crispy. Remove with a slotted spoon and drain on paper towels. Sprinkle with salt while still hot.
2. **Make the brava sauce:** Heat the olive oil in a saucepan over medium heat. Add the onion and garlic and sauté until the onion is soft and translucent. Stir in the smoked paprika and cayenne pepper, cooking for a minute until fragrant. Add the crushed tomatoes and red wine vinegar and bring to a simmer. Let simmer for 10-15 minutes to blend the flavors. Season with salt to taste. For a smoother sauce, blend with a blender or food processor.
3. **Prepare the aioli (if using):** Combine the mayonnaise, minced garlic, lemon juice and salt in a small bowl. Mix well until smooth.
4. **Assemble and serve:** Place the crispy potatoes on a serving plate. Spoon the warm brava sauce over the potatoes. Drizzle aioli over the top or serve on the side for dipping. Optionally garnish with chopped parsley.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
- Boiling the potatoes before frying gives them a softer interior and a crispier exterior after frying.
- Adjust the thickness of the sauce with a little water if it becomes too thick.
- Best served hot and fresh, and commonly enjoyed as part of a larger tapas spread.
- Traditionalists double-fry the potatoes (a lower-temperature fry to soften, then a hotter fry to crisp); this recipe uses a single fry.
- Estimated calories: 400-600 per serving (potato ~150 calories before frying, brava sauce ~50-70 calories, aioli ~90-100 calories), per the source.
- Pairs well with Spanish chorizo, olives, gambas al ajillo, or manchego cheese, and a glass of sangria, albariño, or a cold beer.
