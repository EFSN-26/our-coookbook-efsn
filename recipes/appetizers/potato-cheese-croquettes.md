# Crispy Potato Cheese Croquettes

## Description
A timeless appetizer with a crisp, golden exterior giving way to a soft, cheesy center. Made from classic pantry staples — mashed potato, a cheese cube center, and a breadcrumb coating — these are easy to prep ahead and great for party platters. [Source](https://lentillovingfamily.com/potato-cheese-croquettes/)

## Ingredients
_(yields 25 to 30 croquettes — exact number of servings not specified by source)_

For the Croquettes:
- 6 medium potatoes (about 2 pounds), peeled
- Salt, to taste
- 1 cup all-purpose flour
- 10 ounces mozzarella cheese, cut into small cubes
- Oil, for deep frying

For the Coating:
- 2 large eggs
- 3 tablespoons milk
- 1/2 cup all-purpose flour
- 1/2 teaspoon salt
- 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
- 1/2 teaspoon paprika
- 1 to 1 1/2 cups bread crumbs, panko or regular

## Instructions
1. Place the peeled potatoes in a large pot of salted water. Bring to a boil and cook until fork-tender. Drain well. While still warm, mash thoroughly until smooth with no lumps.
2. Add salt and flour to the mashed potatoes. Mix well until a smooth, cohesive dough forms. Lightly flour your hands to prevent sticking.
3. Take a small portion of the potato dough and flatten it in your palm. Place a cheese cube in the center. Enclose the cheese completely and roll into a smooth ball. Repeat with the remaining dough and cheese.
4. In a large shallow bowl, whisk together the eggs, milk, flour, salt, black pepper, and paprika. Place bread crumbs in a separate shallow dish.
5. Dip each potato ball into the egg mixture, ensuring it is evenly coated. Roll it in the bread crumbs until fully covered. Press lightly to help the crumbs adhere.
6. Heat oil in a deep fryer or heavy-bottomed pot to 350°F. Fry croquettes in small batches for 2-3 minutes each, or until golden brown and crispy. Do not overcrowd the pot.
7. Remove with a slotted spoon and drain on a paper towel-lined plate. Let cool slightly before serving with your favorite dipping sauce.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
- Use starchy potatoes like Russet or Yukon Gold for a smooth mash.
- Seal the potato dough well around the cheese to prevent leaking during frying; chilling the shaped croquettes before frying helps them hold their shape.
- Panko breadcrumbs give an extra-crispy finish, but regular breadcrumbs work fine.
- Baking alternative: bake at 400°F until golden brown. Air fryer alternative: air fry at 375°F for 8-10 minutes after brushing with oil.
- To freeze: shape and bread the croquettes, then freeze in a single layer on a baking sheet before transferring to an airtight container or freezer bag. Fry directly from frozen, adding 1-2 extra minutes.
