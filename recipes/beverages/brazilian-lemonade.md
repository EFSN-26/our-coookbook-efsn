# Brazilian Lemonade

## Description
Brazilian lemonade isn't exactly a lemonade, but a limeade. The drink consists of whole limes blended together with sugar, ice, and sweetened condensed milk, then strained, creating a smooth, creamy, and extra cool lime flavored treat. (Note: the source article does not state ingredient quantities.)

## Ingredients

- limes, cut into halves or quarters
- sugar
- sweetened condensed milk
- ice
- rum or vodka (optional, to add an extra kick)

## Instructions
1. Cut your limes into halves or quarters.
2. Place in a blender with sugar, sweetened condensed milk, and ice.
3. Pulse the ingredients in a few short intervals rather than fully blending (over-blending releases more bitter oils and pith and will make the drink bitter).
4. Strain and serve fresh.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
- Avoid fully blending the mixture; simply pulse the ingredients in short intervals to avoid a bitter taste.
- The drink is best served fresh to prevent separation, and should not sit out too long (on a counter or in the fridge) before serving, as it will become bitter over time.
- To extend the drink's shelf life, you can remove the peels before blending, though this may remove some of the floral, aromatic notes signature to the drink.
- You can add an alcohol such as rum or vodka to give the drink an extra kick, though it's perfectly delicious on its own.
