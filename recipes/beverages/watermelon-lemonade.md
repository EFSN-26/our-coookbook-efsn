# Easy Homemade Watermelon Lemonade

## Description
A refreshing summer drink blending fresh watermelon juice with tart lemonade — sweet, hydrating, and easy to customize with herbs, other citrus, or a splash of liquor. [Source](https://www.kimschob.com/recipes2-watermelon-lemonade/)

## Ingredients
_(servings not specified by source; recipe yields roughly one pitcher)_

- 3 1/2 cups water
- 1/2 cup white sugar
- 7 cups cubed watermelon (approximately 1 seedless watermelon, or 1/2 a normal watermelon), seeds removed
- 1/2 cup lemon juice

## Instructions
1. Bring the sugar and water to a simmer in a saucepan over medium-low heat, then reduce by half.
2. Remove the sugar water from the heat and cool.
3. Puree the seedless watermelon chunks in a blender until smooth.
4. Strain the watermelon juice through a fine mesh strainer, discarding seeds and pulp.
5. Add 3 cups of the watermelon puree to a pitcher.
6. Mix in the sugar water with a large spoon, then add the lemon juice.
7. Taste and adjust if necessary, then serve with ice and a watermelon wedge garnish.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
- Variations: add fresh herbs like mint, basil, or rosemary; swap sweeteners (honey, agave nectar, maple syrup); add a splash of vodka or rum for a cocktail version; add juice from other fruits (orange, grapefruit, pineapple); freeze into ice cubes and blend for a slushie; add sparkling water/soda for a fizzy version; swap lemon for lime or grapefruit juice.
- For best flavor, chill the watermelon and lemon juice before mixing.
- Storage: refrigerate leftovers in an airtight container for up to 3 days. Can be frozen in ice cube trays or freezer-safe containers, though texture may change slightly after thawing.
- Nutrition per serving (per source): 73 kcal, 19g carbohydrates, 1g protein, 0.2g fat, 17g sugar.
