# Creamy Spinach and White Bean Soup

## Description
A riff on Marcella Hazan's classic three-ingredient white bean soup, built up with baby spinach for color and a boost of vegetables. Thick, rich, garlicky, and ready in 20 minutes with zero chopping required. [Source](https://www.simplyrecipes.com/creamy-spinach-and-white-bean-soup-recipe-11850199)

## Ingredients
_(for 4 servings)_

- 1/4 cup extra virgin olive oil
- 4 cloves garlic, grated or crushed
- 3 (15-ounce) cans cannellini or great Northern beans, drained and rinsed
- 1/4 teaspoon freshly ground black pepper
- 2 cups vegetable broth
- 1 (5-ounce) package baby spinach
- 2 tablespoons lemon juice, optional
- Kosher salt, to taste
- Crusty bread, for serving, optional

## Instructions
1. **Simmer the beans:** Add the oil and garlic to a medium pot over medium-low heat. Once the garlic starts to sizzle, cook for 30 seconds; do not let it burn. Add the beans and pepper to the pot and stir to coat.
2. Cover the pot with a lid and cook for 5 minutes. Add the broth, bring it to a simmer, and cook for 5 minutes more.
3. **Add the spinach:** Add the spinach to the pot one handful at a time, stirring and allowing each addition to wilt before adding more. Once all the spinach is wilted, cook the soup for 1 minute more.
4. **Blend and serve:** Remove the pot from the heat. Scoop out about half of the soup and set aside. Using an immersion blender, purée the soup still in the pot until the spinach is broken down and the mixture is creamy and speckled with green. Return the reserved soup to the pot, add the lemon juice (if using), and season with salt and pepper to taste. Serve with bread, if desired.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
- If you don't own an immersion blender, you can purée the soup in a food processor or upright blender. Be careful when blending hot liquids — remove the blender lid's removable center and loosely drape a kitchen towel over the opening so steam can escape.
- Salt is added at the very end since canned beans and store-bought broth can already be quite salty (about 1/4 teaspoon fine sea salt is typical).
