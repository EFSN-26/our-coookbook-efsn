# Spinach Lentil Soup

## Description
A hearty, high-fiber and high-protein spinach lentil soup from the cookbook The Fiber Effect, packed with the health benefits of lentils, spinach, celery and carrots. [Source](https://30seconds.com/food/tip/20970/This-Spinach-Lentil-Soup-Recipe-Has-24-Grams-of-Fiber-20-Grams-of-Protein-Per-Serving)

## Ingredients
_(for 6 servings)_

- 2 cups dry green lentils or brown lentils, rinsed
- 1/4 cup olive oil or 1/4 cup vegetable broth for oil-free
- 1 medium yellow onion, chopped
- 3 medium carrots, chopped
- 2 celery stalks, chopped
- 3 garlic cloves, minced
- 1 teaspoon dried oregano
- 1 teaspoon mustard seed
- 1 teaspoon cumin seed
- 1 teaspoon fennel
- 1/4 teaspoon cayenne or 1/2 teaspoon crushed red pepper flakes (optional)
- 28 ounces (1 can) crushed tomatoes
- 4 cups vegetable broth
- 4 cups water
- 2 cups chopped spinach

## Instructions
1. Soak lentils in warm water for 1 hour. Drain and set aside.
2. In a large soup pot, heat oil or vegetable broth over medium heat.
3. Add mustard, cumin and fennel seeds for 1 minute.
4. Add carrots, celery, onion and cook until onion is soft and tender, about 5 minutes.
5. Add garlic, oregano and, if you want the soup to be spicy, add cayenne or red pepper.
6. Stir in lentils, veggie broth, water and crushed tomatoes.
7. Bring to a boil then reduce heat and simmer for 1 hour. Just before serving stir in spinach and add salt and pepper to taste. (Try this recipe with kale instead of spinach.)
8. Garnish with cilantro, basil, nutritional yeast and cashew cheese. Store the soup in an airtight container in the fridge and reheat in the microwave or in a saucepot on the stovetop over low heat.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
- Try this recipe with kale instead of spinach.
- Garnish with cilantro, basil, nutritional yeast and cashew cheese.
- Store in an airtight container in the fridge; reheat in the microwave or on the stovetop over low heat.
