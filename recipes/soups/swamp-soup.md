# The Best Swamp Soup

## Description
A hearty Southern soup built on smoked sausage, beans, turnip greens, and tomatoes with green chiles — delicious with a side of cornbread. [Source](https://www.allrecipes.com/the-best-swamp-soup-recipe-11796972)

## Ingredients
_(for 8 servings)_

- 1 pound smoked sausage, such as Conecuh, cut into 1-inch pieces
- oil, for browning the sausage (amount not specified by source)
- 1 cup finely chopped onion
- 2 jalapenos, seeded and chopped
- 4 medium gold potatoes, diced
- 1 (28-ounce) can seasoned turnip greens
- 1 (14-ounce) can Hoppin' John or seasoned black-eyed peas
- 1 (10-ounce) can diced tomatoes and green chiles
- 1 can navy beans, drained
- 2 cups water
- 2 teaspoons chicken bouillon paste, such as Better Than Bouillon, or 2 chicken bouillon cubes

## Instructions
1. Heat oil in a large pot over medium-high heat and add the sausage. Cook, stirring often, until the sausage is browned and the fat has rendered out, about 5 minutes.
2. Add the onion and peppers and cook, stirring constantly, until the onion softens, about 4 minutes. Add the potatoes and cook, stirring often, 2 minutes. Add the turnip greens, Hoppin' John, diced tomatoes and green chiles, navy beans, water, and bouillon paste; stir to combine.
3. Bring the mixture to a boil, cover, reduce heat, and simmer until the potatoes are tender, 25 to 30 minutes.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
- This could be cooked in a crockpot: brown the sausage, then add the remaining ingredients to a slow cooker and cook on Low for 4 to 6 hours.
