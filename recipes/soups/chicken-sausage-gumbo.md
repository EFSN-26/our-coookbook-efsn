# Easy Chicken & Sausage Gumbo

## Description
Gumbo is a rich stew-like dish that originated in Louisiana in the 1800s, always starting with a roux, the "holy trinity" (celery, bell peppers and onions), stock, and proteins. While some gumbos simmer all day, this version is ready in about 30 minutes, perfect for busy weeknights.

## Ingredients
_(for 6 servings)_

- 5 tablespoons butter
- 4 tablespoons flour (all-purpose or gluten-free flour)
- 1 onion, chopped
- 1 red bell pepper, chopped
- 1 green bell pepper, chopped
- 2 stalks celery, chopped
- 8 ounces andouille or smoked sausage, sliced
- 4 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 teaspoon cayenne pepper (or to taste)
- 2 teaspoons paprika
- 1 bay leaf
- 1 teaspoon garlic powder
- 1 teaspoon Cajun seasoning (or to taste)
- 4 cups chicken broth
- 1 cup sliced fresh or frozen okra
- 2 cups shredded cooked chicken
- rice, for serving
- chopped green onions, for garnish (optional)

## Instructions
1. Heat a large soup pot over medium-high heat. Add the butter and melt. Whisk in the flour. Constantly whisk until the flour starts to turn golden brown, about 4 to 5 minutes. (For a gluten-free gumbo, use gluten-free flour.)
2. Add the onion, bell peppers and celery. Cook, stirring, about 2 minutes.
3. Add the sausage, garlic, cayenne, paprika, bay leaf, garlic powder and Cajun/creole seasoning. Stir and cook about 2 minutes.
4. Pour in the chicken broth. Stir well and bring to a boil. Reduce heat to low. Add the okra and gently simmer about 10 minutes.
5. Stir in the cooked chicken. Season with salt and pepper. Cook a few minutes more and then ladle into serving bowls. Serve with rice and garnish with chopped green onions, if desired.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
You could throw some cleaned shrimp into the pot about 5 minutes before it's finished cooking. Store any leftover gumbo in an airtight container in the refrigerator and reheat in the microwave.
