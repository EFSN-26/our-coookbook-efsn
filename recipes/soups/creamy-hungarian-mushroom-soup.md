# Creamy Hungarian Mushroom Soup

## Description
Hungarian mushroom soup has a creamy texture from sour cream, with a deeper dimension of taste from paprika, dill and a surprising bit of lemon juice. [Source](https://30seconds.com/food/tip/96977/Creamy-Hungarian-Mushroom-Soup-Recipe-Is-Incredibly-Delicious-Nutritious)

## Ingredients
_(for 6 servings)_

- 4 tablespoons unsalted butter
- 2 cups onions, chopped
- 1 pound fresh mushrooms, sliced
- 2 cups chicken broth or vegetable broth
- 1 tablespoon soy sauce (or low-sodium soy sauce or gluten-free tamari)
- 1 tablespoon paprika
- 2 teaspoons dried dill
- 1 cup milk
- 3 tablespoons all-purpose flour or gluten-free flour
- 1/2 cup sour cream
- 1/4 cup fresh parsley, chopped
- 2 teaspoons lemon juice
- 1 teaspoon salt (or to taste)
- ground black pepper, to taste

## Instructions
1. Place a large stock pot or dutch oven on the stovetop on medium heat. Add the butter and let it melt.
2. Add the onions and cook until they are soft and translucent, about 5 minutes.
3. Add the sliced mushrooms and cook for another 5 minutes.
4. Pour in the broth, soy sauce, paprika and dill. Cover and simmer on low for 15 minutes. (For a gluten-free soup, use tamari instead of soy sauce.)
5. In a small bowl, add flour and milk and whisk together. (For a gluten-free soup, use gluten-free flour.)
6. Stir the milk-flour mixture into the soup, cover and simmer for another 15 minutes. Stir occasionally.
7. Remove lid and stir in sour cream, parsley, lemon juice and salt and pepper to taste.
8. Heat and stir for 2 to 3 minutes.
9. Serve. Store any leftover soup in an airtight container in the fridge and reheat in the microwave or in a saucepot on the stove.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
For a gluten-free version, use tamari instead of soy sauce and gluten-free flour instead of all-purpose flour.
