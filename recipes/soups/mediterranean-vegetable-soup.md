# Mediterranean Vegetable Soup

## Description
A nourishing, flavor-packed vegetable soup built on a rich tomato broth with fennel, zucchini, sun-dried tomatoes, and kale — simple enough for a weeknight but comforting enough for a slow family dinner.

## Ingredients
_(for 6 servings)_

- 3 tablespoons olive oil
- 1 small onion, finely chopped (70g / 2.5oz)
- 2 ribs celery, diced (80g / 2.8oz)
- 2 medium carrots, diced (110g / 3.9oz)
- 1 fennel bulb, finely sliced (200g / 7.1oz)
- 1 red pepper, diced (130g / 4.6oz)
- 3 cloves garlic, minced
- 3 zucchini (courgettes), diced (250g / 8.8oz)
- 3/4 teaspoon flaky salt
- 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
- 2 bay leaves
- 1 teaspoon dried thyme
- 1 (14 oz) can cherry tomatoes, chopped, or fire-roasted diced tomatoes
- 2 tablespoons tomato puree
- 4 sun-dried tomatoes
- 4 cups vegetable or chicken stock
- 2 tablespoons chopped basil
- 2 tablespoons chopped parsley
- 2 leaves kale, stemmed and chopped
- 1/2 - 1 teaspoon lemon juice, to taste

_To serve:_
- 1/4 cup Parmesan (30g / 1.1oz)
- 1 tablespoon olive oil

## Instructions
1. Heat 3 tablespoons olive oil in a Dutch oven or large soup pot over medium heat. Add the onion, celery, carrots, and fennel; sauté for 3-4 minutes until softened. Add the garlic and cook for another minute until fragrant.
2. Add the red pepper, zucchini, salt, black pepper, bay leaves, and dried thyme. Cook for a further 2 minutes, stirring occasionally.
3. Stir in the chopped tomatoes, tomato puree, sun-dried tomatoes, and stock. Bring the soup to a boil, then reduce the heat to medium-low and simmer for 15-20 minutes so the flavors can develop.
4. Add the kale and cook for about 1 minute until just wilted. Stir in the lemon juice and adjust seasoning to taste. Finish by stirring in the chopped basil and parsley.
5. Serve warm, topped with grated Parmesan and a drizzle of olive oil.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
- No fennel: substitute extra celery or leek.
- No kale: spinach or Swiss chard work well.
- For a vegetarian version, use vegetable stock instead of chicken stock.
- Dairy-free: skip the Parmesan or replace it with nutritional yeast.
- Add protein by stirring in white beans, chickpeas, or shredded chicken.
- Add a handful of small pasta (like ditalini) to make the soup more filling, or stir in cooked quinoa or brown rice for a heartier meal.
- Make it spicy with chili flakes or a spoonful of harissa.
- Chop vegetables into similar sizes so they cook evenly.
- The flavor improves after a day in the fridge.
- Storage: keep in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 3 days, or freeze for up to 2 months; thaw overnight in the fridge before reheating gently on the stovetop or in the microwave.
- Approximate nutrition per the source (per serving): 217 kcal, 19g carbohydrates, 8g protein, 13g fat, 4g fiber, 10g sugar.
