# Smoked Salmon and Leek Soup

## Description
This unique, smoky soup is almost impossibly simple to make, combining sauteed leek and potato in chicken broth with cream, smoked salmon, and fresh herbs — bright, herbaceous flavors find the perfect foil in umami smoked salmon.

## Ingredients
_(for 4 servings — see §8 for scaling)_

- 1 tablespoon unsalted butter
- 1 large leek, sliced
- 1 pound potatoes, peeled and diced
- ½ teaspoon Dijon mustard
- 4 cups chicken stock
- ½ cup heavy cream
- 1 teaspoon chopped fresh dill
- 1 teaspoon chopped fresh chives
- 4 ounces smoked salmon, sliced
- Salt and pepper, to taste
- 1 tablespoon watercress, to serve

## Instructions
1. Heat the butter in a large, deep pan to a medium temperature.
2. Add the sliced leek and saute for 6–8 minutes, until the leek is soft.
3. Add the potatoes, Dijon mustard, and chicken stock to the pan.
4. Let the broth simmer for 15 minutes, until the potatoes are soft.
5. Add the cream to the pan and bring the liquid to a simmer.
6. Mix in the dill, chives, and smoked salmon. Season with salt and pepper, to taste.
7. Dish up the soup, and garnish each bowl with a few watercress leaves before serving.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
- To make dairy-free, swap the butter and cream for dairy-free varieties; to make gluten-free, use a gluten-free stock.
- Regular (non-smoked) salmon can be used to cut cost; add a splash of liquid smoke to keep the smoky flavor. Trout or sea bass work well in place of salmon; shrimp or other shellfish can also be added.
- Sweet potatoes can replace regular potatoes, or swap in rice or noodles (adjust cooking time accordingly). Dill and chives can be swapped for parsley, thyme, or basil.
- Serve with crusty bread or toast, homemade croutons, or in a hollowed-out bread bowl; a simple side salad also pairs well. As a starter, follow with creamy pasta, risotto, roast chicken, grilled fish, other seafood mains, or roasted vegetables. Pairs well with a chilled, light, citrusy white wine.
