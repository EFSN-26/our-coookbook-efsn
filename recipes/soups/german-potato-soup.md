# Traditional German Potato Soup

## Description
A comforting, filling one-pot soup from Germany and Austria, built on potatoes, soup vegetables (carrots, leek, celery root and parsley root), bacon and parboiled sausage. It involves some chopping, but comes together in about 30 minutes of cooking.

## Ingredients
_(for 4 servings)_

- 1 kg potatoes, peeled and cut into 2x2cm cubes
- 500 g soup vegetables (3-4 carrots, 1/4 celery root, parsley root), peeled and cut into 2x2cm cubes
- 1 leek, cut into thin slices
- 1 onion, peeled and chopped
- 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
- 200 g bacon, cubed
- 1.5 liters water or vegetable broth
- 100 ml cream
- salt, to taste
- nutmeg, to taste

### For Serving
- 4 parboiled sausages
- 2 tablespoons parsley or chives, chopped

## Instructions
1. Wash and peel all vegetables apart from the leek.
2. Cut the vegetables into 2x2cm cubes (smaller than an inch).
3. Cut the leek into thin slices.
4. Preheat the oil in a large pot. Fry the onion with the bacon on medium heat until the onion turns translucent. Add the rest of the vegetables and turn the heat to medium-high.
5. Fry the vegetables until they develop roasting aromas (this is when they start to brown).
6. Add the water or vegetable broth to the pot, cover with a lid, and bring to a boil. Turn the heat to medium-low and let the soup simmer for 10 minutes.
7. After 10 minutes, add the sausages and cook them in the soup for another 10 minutes. Meanwhile, wash and chop the herbs.
8. Take out the sausages and cut them into slices.
9. Season the soup with salt and nutmeg.
10. Pour the soup into bowls and decorate with a few sausage slices and fresh herbs.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
- For a creamier soup, use starchy potatoes, which release starch and fall apart during cooking. For potato cubes that hold their shape, use waxy potatoes.
- To thicken the soup, mash it a few times with a potato masher, or dissolve a tablespoon of starch (the source uses Mondamin, a German cornstarch-based thickener) in cold water and stir it in to avoid lumps.
- Leave out the meat for a vegetarian variation, and the cream for a vegan version.
- Regional variations include creamy potato soup (mashed potatoes), a version with pear slices (Northern German style), a Bremen version with bacon and brown shrimp, a Saxon version with grilled sausage, versions from Baden/Pfalz/Swabia served with steamed dumplings or plum/apple cake, and a very simple "sour broth" of onions, potatoes, vinegar, water and spices.
- Storage: keeps in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3 days, or in the freezer for up to 3 months.
