# Cajun Potato Soup

## Description
Smoky andouille sausage, tender Yukon Gold potatoes, and colorful bell peppers come together in a rich, creamy Cajun-spiced soup — hearty enough to scoop into a bread bowl, but comforting enough for any night. [Source](https://www.thekitchn.com/cajun-potato-soup-recipe-23751621)

## Ingredients
_(for 4-5 servings, makes about 8 1/2 cups)_

- 2 tablespoons olive oil
- 1 medium yellow onion, small diced (about 1 1/2 cups)
- 1 medium green bell pepper, small diced (about 1 cup)
- 1 medium red bell pepper, small diced (about 1 1/4 cups)
- 1 medium jalapeño pepper, small diced (remove seeds before dicing for less heat, about 1/4 cup)
- 3 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 1/4 teaspoons Old Bay or Cajun seasoning with salt
- 1 teaspoon smoked paprika
- 1/4 teaspoon freshly ground black pepper, plus more as needed
- 12 ounces andouille sausage, halved lengthwise, then thinly sliced crosswise
- 1 pound Yukon gold potatoes (3 medium), small dice (about 2 3/4 cups)
- 1 (32-ounce) carton low-sodium chicken broth (4 cups)
- 3/4 cup heavy cream
- Kosher salt, to taste
- Finely chopped fresh chives, for garnish (optional)

## Instructions
1. Heat 2 tablespoons olive oil in a Dutch oven or medium pot over medium heat until shimmering. Add the diced onion, green bell pepper, red bell pepper, and jalapeño. Cook until softened, 4 to 5 minutes. Stir in the minced garlic and cook until fragrant, about 30 seconds.
2. Stir in the Old Bay seasoning, smoked paprika, and black pepper. Stir in the sliced andouille sausage and diced potatoes. Cook, stirring often, until the potatoes go from translucent to opaque, 2 to 3 minutes.
3. Stir in the chicken broth and bring to a simmer. Simmer, reducing the heat as needed, until the potatoes are tender, 15 to 17 minutes.
4. Turn off the heat. Stir in the heavy cream. Taste and season with kosher salt and black pepper as needed. Garnish with finely chopped fresh chives, if desired.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
- Cajun seasoning brands vary in flavor — the recipe developers recommend a bold option like Old Bay or Slap Ya Mama.
- Andouille sausage is called out as essential, since it infuses the potatoes and broth with deep, smoky flavor — other sausages are not recommended as a substitute.
- Substitution: yellow bell pepper can replace green bell pepper; the soup will be slightly sweeter.
- Storage: refrigerate leftovers in an airtight container for up to 3 days, or freeze for up to 2 months. Reheat in the microwave or over low heat.
