# This Simple Hungarian Goulash Recipe Is So Easy to Make (8 Ingredients)

## Description
A rich meat stew seasoned with paprika that originated in Hungary — simple to make and filling, with beef stew meat simmered slowly with onions, garlic and tomato paste. [Source](https://30seconds.com/food/tip/14991/This-Simple-Hungarian-Goulash-Recipe-Is-So-Easy-to-Make-8-Ingredients)

## Ingredients
_(for 8 servings)_

- 3 tablespoons vegetable oil, divided
- 3 pounds beef stew meat
- 2 large onions, sliced
- 2 tablespoons Hungarian paprika
- 2 teaspoons salt
- 1/2 teaspoon black pepper, or to taste
- 6 ounces tomato paste
- 2 cups water
- 2-3 cloves garlic, chopped

## Instructions
1. Combine the paprika, salt and pepper in a small bowl. Set aside.
2. Heat 1 tablespoon of the oil in a Dutch oven. Add the onions and cook until soft. Remove and set aside.
3. Combine the paprika mixture and beef; mix well. Add the remaining 2 tablespoons of oil to the same Dutch oven and cook the beef until browned on all sides.
4. Add the onions, tomato paste, water and garlic. Bring to a simmer, reduce heat and cook on low heat, covered, about 2 hours. Sprinkle with chopped parsley and serve over noodles, potatoes or rice.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
- For a richer goulash, use chicken or beef broth instead of water.
- Store in an airtight container in the fridge; reheat on the stove over low heat or in the microwave.
- Serve over rice, noodles or potatoes with crusty bread and a fresh salad.
- Some readers of the source added hot sauce or a little mustard for extra flavor.
- Several commenters on the source page (including self-identified Hungarian readers) note that this stew-style dish is closer to "pörkölt" and that traditional Hungarian "gulyás" is a soup, not a stew — included here for context, not as a correction to the recipe as published.
