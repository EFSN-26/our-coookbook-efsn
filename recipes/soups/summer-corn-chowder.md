# Summer Corn Chowder

## Description
This summer corn chowder is bursting with earthy, rich, creamy and spicy flavors, made deeper by a homemade corn stock built from the stripped cobs, leek tops, garlic and jalapeño. The kernels are roasted until caramelized before going into the finished chowder, so the corn flavor runs through every layer of the bowl rather than sitting on the surface.

## Ingredients
_(for 4 servings)_

### For the Corn Stock
- 5 ears fresh corn, kernels removed and reserved, cobs broken in half
- 2 leeks, dark green tops and outer leaves only
- 3 garlic cloves, smashed
- 1 jalapeño, sliced
- 1 teaspoon kosher salt
- 8 cups water

### For the Roasted Corn
- 2 tablespoons unsalted butter, melted
- 1 teaspoon flaky salt

### For the Chowder
- 2 tablespoons unsalted butter
- 2 leeks, white and light green parts only, halved lengthwise and sliced 1/2-inch thick
- 1 jalapeño, finely diced
- 4 garlic cloves, minced
- 1 cup heavy cream
- 1/4 cup fresh basil leaves, torn
- 1 teaspoon kosher salt
- 1/2 teaspoon white pepper

### For the Toppings
- 1/2 cup crème fraîche
- 1/3 cup pickled jalapeños, roughly chopped
- 2 tablespoons chili crisp
- Fresh basil leaves, for garnish

## Instructions
1. Preheat the oven to 425 F.
2. Line a large rimmed baking sheet with parchment paper.
3. Cut kernels off all ears of corn and set aside. Break the corn cobs in half.
4. To make the corn stock, add the cobs, leek tops, smashed garlic, jalapeño, and salt to a large pot. Cover with 8 cups of water.
5. Bring to a boil, then reduce to a steady simmer. Cook, uncovered, for 45 minutes.
6. Strain the stock through a fine mesh strainer, pressing firmly on the solids. You should have about 6 cups of stock. Keep the stock warm and discard the solids.
7. To make the roasted corn, spread the corn kernels in a single even layer on the prepared baking sheet. Drizzle with melted butter and sprinkle with flaky salt.
8. Roast for 20–25 minutes, stirring once halfway, until kernels are golden and caramelized in spots. Remove from oven and set aside.
9. Begin the chowder: melt butter in a large heavy-bottomed pot over medium heat.
10. Add the leeks and jalapeño. Cook, stirring occasionally, until the leeks are completely soft and just starting to turn golden, about 10 minutes.
11. Add minced garlic and cook 1 minute more, stirring constantly.
12. Stir in the 6 cups of corn stock and heavy cream.
13. Add the roasted corn kernels. Bring to a simmer and cook for 10 minutes.
14. Stir in torn basil leaves. Season with salt and white pepper.
15. Blend the soup using an immersion blender or standing blender until the texture is creamy but not thin. Taste and adjust seasonings as desired.
16. Ladle the chowder into bowls and top with crème fraîche, pickled jalapeños, chili crisp, and fresh basil. Serve right away.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
- **To make it less spicy:** swap the jalapeño in the chowder for a poblano, and skip the pickled jalapeño and chili crisp toppings. The author doesn't recommend omitting the peppers in the stock/chowder entirely, since they add depth even in small amounts.
- **To make it spicier:** keep the seeds, membrane and ribs in the jalapeño, add a second jalapeño, or stir in smoky chipotle peppers/adobo sauce.
- **Make-ahead:** the corn stock can be made ahead, cooled, and frozen (flat bag or portioned cubes) for up to 3 months, or refrigerated for 3 days. Roasted corn can be prepped a few days ahead and kept covered in the fridge (kernels will dry out if exposed to air). The finished chowder keeps well for up to 5 days; reheat with a couple tablespoons of water or stock since it thickens as it cools.
- Don't refrigerate the chowder with the toppings already added — they can make it acidic/bitter and cause it to spoil faster.
- The chowder cannot be frozen once assembled, because cream-based soups separate when frozen and thawed.
