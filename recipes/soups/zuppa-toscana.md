# Zuppa Toscana (Olive Garden Copycat)

## Description
This homemade version of Zuppa Toscana (which translates to Tuscan soup) is jam-packed with flavor and hearty ingredients like sausage, kale, white beans, and potatoes. While some versions are cream-laden, this recipe adds a little flour to help thicken the soup, then adds some half-and-half at the end (cream can be used for more richness).

## Ingredients
_(for 6 to 8 servings, about 4 quarts)_

- 1 tablespoon olive oil
- 1 pound raw spicy Italian sausage, casings removed if using links
- 1 medium white onion, chopped
- 2 stalks celery, chopped
- 3 cloves garlic, minced
- 3 tablespoons all-purpose flour
- 8 cups (2 quarts) chicken broth
- 1 pound new potatoes, diced
- 1 (15-ounce) can cannellini or other white beans, rinsed and drained
- 1 cup heavy cream or half-and-half
- 5 cups stemmed and chopped kale (about 5 ounces)
- Salt and freshly ground black pepper, to taste

## Instructions
1. **Brown the sausage:** In a large pot over medium heat, add the olive oil and sausage. As the sausage browns, use a sturdy spatula to break it up into bite-sized pieces. Continue until cooked through, about 6 minutes. Remove the sausage with a slotted spoon to a bowl or plate and set aside, leaving the oil in the pot.
2. **Cook the aromatics and add the broth:** To the pot, add the onion, celery, and garlic and cook until the onion is mostly translucent, about 4 minutes. Add the flour and cook, stirring constantly, until it thoroughly coats the vegetables, 1 minute. Slowly stir in the chicken stock and bring to a simmer, scraping the bottom of the pot.
3. **Add the remaining ingredients:** Add the potatoes and white beans. Turn the heat down to low, cover, and simmer until the potatoes are tender, about 15 minutes. Add the kale and simmer just until tender, 4 to 5 minutes.
4. Remove from the heat and wait until the soup stops simmering. Stir in the half-and-half or cream. Add the sausage back to the soup and taste, seasoning with salt and pepper as needed.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
- This is a flexible soup — substitute as needed: try sweet sausage or turkey sausage instead of spicy Italian sausage; add other vegetables like carrots, red peppers, or Swiss chard; make it vegetarian by using mushrooms instead of sausage and vegetable broth; leave out the half-and-half for a brothy, non-creamy soup.
- Especially delicious with crusty bread — toasted and olive-oil drizzled for dunking, or as homemade croutons (combine 3 cups cubed bread, 2 tablespoons melted butter, and 1 tablespoon everything bagel seasoning on a baking sheet, toss, and bake at 400°F for 10-15 minutes, stirring halfway).
- Keeps well for 5 days in the fridge. Reheat in the microwave, covered, or on the stovetop on low heat until steaming hot.
- Nutrition per serving (source-stated): 400 calories, 25g fat, 29g carbs, 16g protein.
