# Chicken and Sausage Gumbo (Classic Dark Roux)

## Description
A classic, slow-simmered Louisiana-style gumbo built on a rich, dark roux with the "holy trinity" of onion, celery and bell pepper, Andouille sausage, shredded rotisserie chicken and okra. [Source](https://xoxobella.com/chicken-and-sausage-gumbo/)

## Ingredients
_(for 6 servings)_

- 1 rotisserie chicken, large, skin removed, deboned and shredded
- 19.5 ounces Andouille sausage, Cajun-style, sliced 1/4-inch thick
- 6 cups chicken broth, more as needed
- 14.5 ounces diced tomatoes, petite style, canned, drained
- 2 teaspoons Cajun seasoning (or Creole seasoning)
- 1 bay leaf, dried
- 1 cup vegetable oil
- 2 green bell peppers, chopped
- 1 onion, chopped
- 3 celery stalks, chopped
- 1/2 teaspoon cayenne pepper
- 1/2 teaspoon dried thyme
- 12 ounces okra, frozen, cut
- 5 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 cup all-purpose flour
- salt and black pepper, to taste
- parsley, chopped fresh, for optional garnish

## Instructions
1. Heat a large pot over medium-high heat and brown the sausage on both sides. Remove it from the pot.
2. Turn the heat down and add the oil and flour. Cook for about 35 minutes, stirring often, to make a brown roux.
3. Add the bell peppers, celery, garlic, and onion, and cook for 5 to 8 minutes, or until the onions begin to turn translucent. The roux will keep darkening and thickening.
4. Return the sausage to the pot and add the chicken.
5. Add the chicken broth, diced tomatoes, cayenne pepper, Cajun/Creole seasoning, thyme, bay leaf, salt and pepper. Stir well.
6. Bring the mixture to a boil, then turn down the heat and gently simmer, uncovered, for 1 to 2 hours, stirring occasionally.
7. Add the okra during the last 30 minutes and keep cooking, stirring occasionally, until the gumbo reaches the right consistency.
8. Adjust the seasonings if needed and serve hot, garnished with fresh parsley if liked.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
- Make the roux slowly: don't rush it. Keep the heat low to medium and stir often so the flour doesn't scorch or stick to the pan; don't step away from the pot.
- Roux color: the darker the roux, the tastier the gumbo — aim for a milk-chocolate or medium-brown shade. If short on time, a light brown/tan roux still tastes good.
- Let the gumbo cool completely before covering and refrigerating, otherwise condensation will drip back in and water it down.
- Store leftovers in an airtight container in the fridge for 3-4 days, or freeze up to 3 months (freezing in individual portions with a bit of rice works well). Thaw overnight in the fridge and reheat on the stove, adding more broth if it's too thick.
- Substitutions: use chicken breasts/thighs (cooked and shredded) instead of rotisserie chicken, or add seafood for a seafood gumbo. Swap Andouille for smoked beef sausage, hot links, or kielbasa. For gluten-free, use gluten-free flour, broth and sausage.
- Per the source, one commenter (self-identified as Louisiana-raised) noted this leans more Creole than Cajun because of the tomatoes, thyme and bay leaf — the author agreed the version is more Creole-leaning.
- Nutrition per the source (per serving, of 6): approx. 626 kcal, 30g carbohydrates, 51g protein, 34g fat.
