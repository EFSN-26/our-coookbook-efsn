# Kristen Bell's Pickle Soup

## Description
Actress Kristen Bell's family holiday tradition: a creamy, tangy dill pickle soup — built on a base of sautéed onion, celery, and carrots simmered with broth, dill pickles and pickle juice, frozen potatoes, and mixed vegetables, finished with a milk-sour cream-flour slurry. Bell says the recipe (believed to be Polish) came from her grandmother, by way of her mother. [Source](https://www.goodmorningamerica.com/food/story/kristen-bells-pickle-soup-recipe-ready-holidays-ease-81465901)

## Ingredients
_(quantities as stated by the source; some ingredients are listed without an amount — see Missing Information)_

- 8 cups chicken or vegetable broth
- Chopped onion
- 1 tablespoon butter or oil
- Shredded or chopped carrots, coarsely grated
- Cubed frozen potatoes ("I like the square hash browns or Southern style," per Kristen Bell)
- 1 cup celery, thinly sliced
- 5 Polish dill pickles, chopped fine
- 1/3-1/2 cup dill pickle juice (depending on taste)
- 1/2 cup milk
- 2 tablespoons flour
- 5 tablespoons sour cream
- Salt and pepper, to taste
- 1 tablespoon sugar
- Mixed vegetables: corn, peas, carrots (avoid green beans)

_Garnish (optional):_
- 1/2 teaspoon fresh parsley, finely chopped
- 1/2 teaspoon fresh dill, finely chopped

## Instructions
1. In a pan, sauté the onion, celery, and carrots.
2. In a Crock-Pot or slow cooker, add the chicken or vegetable broth, dill pickle juice, chopped dill pickles, cubed potatoes, and the sautéed onion, celery, and carrots. Add salt, pepper, and sugar to taste.
3. Cook in the Crock-Pot for approximately 4 hours, until all the vegetables are soft.
4. In a small bowl, beat the milk, sour cream, and flour until smooth. Stir in a small amount of the hot soup to temper it, then add the tempered mixture back into the soup slowly, stirring until smooth.
5. Keep the soup warm but do not boil it — it will curdle.
6. Garnish with parsley and dill, if desired.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
- Kristen Bell recommends "Southern-style" diced frozen potatoes, but shredded hash browns also work well as a substitute (per The Kitchn's review of this recipe).
- Don't turn the heat up once the milk/sour cream mixture is stirred in, or it may curdle.
- If simmering on the stovetop instead of a slow cooker, keep it at a low simmer for a couple of hours, stirring occasionally, until the vegetables are cooked through.
- Swapping in vegetable broth makes this a vegetarian dish.
- Also reviewed and cooked by The Kitchn: [The Easy Kristen Bell Soup Recipe I'm Making to Stay Cozy Right Now](https://www.thekitchn.com/kristen-bell-pickle-soup-23769663) — that review notes the pickle flavor comes through as a soft, underlying note rather than dominant, and suggests adding extra chopped pickles and juice if you want a stronger pickle taste.
