# Luxurious Lobster Bisque

## Description
This rich, silky, deeply flavorful lobster bisque features a scratch-made stock and is the most perfectly elegant starter for a special occasion meal. As developer Patterson Watkins puts it, the bisque base is silky with a balance between cream, sherry and homemade lobster stock, with rustic vegetal notes from the mirepoix and buttery richness from the lobster and cream. [Source](https://www.chowhound.com/2094162/luxurious-lobster-bisque-recipe/)

## Ingredients
_(for 6 servings)_

For the stock
- 4 cooked lobster tails
- 2 celery stalks, quartered
- 1 carrot, peeled and quartered
- 1 shallot, peeled and quartered
- 4 garlic cloves, peeled
- 2 bay leaves
- 1 sprig fresh thyme
- 1 sprig fresh tarragon
- ½ teaspoon black peppercorns
- 1 teaspoon salt
- ¼ teaspoon white pepper
- 6 cups water

For the bisque
- 6 tablespoons salted butter
- ½ cup diced shallot
- ½ cup diced celery
- ½ cup diced carrots
- 2 garlic cloves, peeled and minced
- 1 cup sherry wine
- ¼ cup all-purpose flour
- 1 cup heavy cream
- 4 tablespoons tomato paste
- 1 tablespoon chopped fresh chives
- 1 tablespoon chopped fresh tarragon

## Instructions
1. To make the stock, separate the shells from the lobster meat.
2. Place the shells in a large pot.
3. Cut the lobster meat into bite-sized pieces and refrigerate until ready to use.
4. Place the celery, carrot, shallot, garlic, bay leaves, thyme, tarragon, peppercorns, salt and white pepper in the pot with the lobster shells.
5. Cover the shells and veggies with water and bring to a boil over high heat.
6. Cover the pot with a lid, reduce the heat to low, and cook for 30 minutes, stirring occasionally.
7. Drain the stock through a fine mesh strainer, discard the shells and dregs, and set the stock aside.
8. To make the bisque, melt butter in a large pot over medium heat.
9. Once melted, add the shallot, celery, carrots and garlic, and saute for 3 minutes.
10. Deglaze with sherry and bring to a simmer.
11. Cook, stirring occasionally, until the sherry has almost completely evaporated, about 5 minutes.
12. Add the flour, stir to combine with the veggies, and cook for 1 minute more.
13. Add the stock (about 6 cups), cream and tomato paste to the pot and whisk to combine.
14. Bring to a low simmer, whisking frequently, and cook for 20 minutes or until the soup has thickened and the veggies are very tender.
15. Transfer the soup to a blender (working in batches, if necessary) and blend until smooth.
16. Return the blended soup to the pot and heat over medium-low until reheated.
17. To serve, divide the lobster meat between bowls.
18. Ladle the soup over the lobster meat and garnish with chopped chives and tarragon.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
This bisque is made with precooked lobster, so that step is already done for you, but you can cook your own and refrigerate it for up to 3 days. If using whole lobsters instead of tails, rinse them to remove any lingering tomalley, boil the shells briefly, then cover and simmer; skim off any foam. The stock can be made ahead and refrigerated up to 5 days, or frozen for months. For serving, the recipe developer suggests pairing with blueberry muffins (a Maine tradition alongside seafood soups), a well-prepared steak for a surf-and-turf starter, or a green salad and crusty French or Italian bread for a lighter meal.
