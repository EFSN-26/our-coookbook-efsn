# Ukrainian Mushroom Soup

## Description
Unlike Hungarian mushroom soup's whole mushrooms in a creamy broth, this Ukrainian version is blended into a silky, pureed mushroom soup, with reserved mushrooms scattered on top for texture. [Source](https://30seconds.com/food/tip/40332/Ukrainian-Mushroom-Soup-Recipe-Is-Silky-Smooth-Delicious-9-Ingredients)

## Ingredients
_(for 4 servings)_

- 2 tablespoons olive oil (divided)
- 1 onion, chopped fine
- 1 carrot, chopped
- 8 ounces baby bella, cremini, shiitake or your favorite mushrooms, sliced
- 4 cloves garlic, pressed
- 4 cups chicken broth or vegetable broth
- 2 russet potatoes, diced
- 1 teaspoon dried thyme
- 1 cup half and half or whole milk
- chopped parsley, for garnish (optional)

## Instructions
1. Heat 1 tablespoon of the olive oil in a soup pot. Add the onion and carrot and cook until softened, about 5 minutes. Remove to a plate.
2. Add the remaining olive oil and the mushrooms to the same pot. Cook until they soften and begin to brown, about 6 minutes. Remove to another plate.
3. Add the garlic to the pot and cook 30 seconds. Pour in the broth and bring to a boil. Add potatoes, reduce heat and cook about 10 minutes.
4. Add the carrots, onion and 3/4 of the mushrooms back to the pot along with the thyme. Season with salt and pepper. Cook about 5 minutes.
5. Add the half and half and bring to just a simmer. Remove from the heat.
6. Puree the soup with an immersion blender (or in batches in a regular blender, carefully, as hot liquid can splash).
7. Ladle the soup into bowls and top with the remaining mushrooms.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
Try a combination of wild mushrooms for extra flavor. If you don't have an immersion blender, puree the soup in batches in a regular blender. Some reviewers used cashew cream and vegetable broth to make it vegan, and a dollop of sour cream is a traditional Ukrainian topping. Store in an airtight container in the fridge (per reader comments from the recipe author, no longer than about 4 days) and reheat in the microwave.
