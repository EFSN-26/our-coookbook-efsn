# Italian Zuppa di Farro

## Description
A rustic Tuscan farro and cannellini bean soup dating back to ancient Rome, simmered with carrots, celery, onion, garlic, tomatoes, rosemary and bay leaf. Humble, slow-built, and traditionally kept simple with minimal garnish. [Source](https://www.gamintraveler.com/2025/08/31/how-to-make-italian-zuppa-di-farro/)

## Ingredients
_(for servings not specified by source)_

- 1 cup (200 g) farro (spelt), rinsed
- 1 cup (200 g) dried cannellini beans (or 2 cups canned, rinsed and drained)
- 6 cups (1.5 liters) vegetable broth
- 2 carrots, diced
- 2 celery stalks, diced
- 1 medium onion, finely chopped
- 2 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 cup (240 ml) canned diced tomatoes
- 3 tablespoons olive oil
- 1 sprig fresh rosemary
- 1 bay leaf
- Salt and pepper, to taste
- Optional garnish: chopped fresh parsley, grated Parmesan cheese, extra virgin olive oil (a drizzle per bowl)

## Instructions
1. **Prepare the beans:** Soak dried beans overnight in cold water. Drain, rinse, and boil in fresh water until tender (about 1-1.5 hours). Drain and set aside. (Skip this step if using canned beans.)
2. **Sauté the vegetables:** Heat the olive oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add the onion, garlic, carrots, and celery. Sauté until softened and fragrant, about 5-7 minutes.
3. Stir in the diced tomatoes, rosemary, and bay leaf. Cook for 2-3 minutes to meld the flavors.
4. **Cook the soup:** Add the rinsed farro and the cooked or canned beans to the pot. Pour in the vegetable broth and bring to a boil.
5. Reduce the heat to low, cover, and simmer for 30-40 minutes, or until the farro is tender but still chewy, stirring occasionally to prevent sticking.
6. **Season and finish:** Remove the rosemary sprig and bay leaf. Season with salt and pepper to taste. Adjust consistency with more broth or water if needed.
7. **Serve:** Ladle into bowls and garnish with fresh parsley, grated Parmesan, and a drizzle of extra virgin olive oil, if desired.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
- Pearled farro cooks faster (about 20-25 minutes) and doesn't need soaking; semi-pearled or whole farro may need soaking and longer cooking.
- Blend a portion of the soup with an immersion blender and stir it back in for a creamier texture.
- Sauté a small amount of pancetta or bacon with the vegetables for added flavor (traditionalists keep the soup vegetarian; rural households often add pancetta or pork broth).
- Add other vegetables like zucchini, kale, or spinach for variety.
- Stores well in the refrigerator for up to 3 days; reheat with a splash of broth to loosen it up.
- Approximate nutrition per the source: ~250-300 kcal, ~8-10 g protein, ~40-45 g carbohydrates, ~8-10 g fat, ~6-8 g fiber per serving.
