# Colombian Chicken Sancocho

## Description
A traditional Colombian one-pot chicken stew combining chicken, yucca, plantains, corn and potatoes for a cozy, complete meal — taught to the author by her Colombian mother-in-law.

## Ingredients
_(for 6 servings)_

- 2 chicken breasts
- 2 parsley sprigs
- 2 cilantro sprigs
- 1 green onion, plus more for garnish
- 3 corn cobs
- 500 g frozen yucca, heart removed
- 5 small red potatoes
- 1/2 onion
- 3 small tomatoes, diced
- 1 garlic clove, minced
- 1 teaspoon achiote powder, optional
- black pepper, to taste
- salt, to taste
- oil, for frying
- 1 cup chopped plantains

## Instructions
1. To a large pot, add 2 liters of water, chicken breasts, parsley, cilantro, and whole green onion with roots trimmed slightly.
2. Grate 1/4 of the yucca. Cut corn into quarters, yucca into 2cm pieces, and peel potatoes.
3. Add the yucca pieces, corn, potatoes, and plantains to the pot.
4. Bring to a boil, cover, and cook for 30 minutes. After 15 minutes, check the chicken and remove if fully cooked. Continue cooking the soup until the vegetables are tender. Remove the cilantro, parsley and whole green onion stems, and discard.
5. Make the guiso: in a pan, heat a touch of oil over medium heat. Add diced onion and cook for 2 minutes.
6. Add diced tomatoes, garlic, achiote powder (if using), and black pepper. Cook for 5 minutes until the tomatoes break down slightly.
7. Once the soup is done, shred the cooked chicken and add it back to the pot along with the guiso mixture.
8. Season to taste with salt and pepper.
9. Serve hot, garnished with chopped cilantro or green onions if desired.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
Sancocho comes from the Spanish word sancochar, meaning "to parboil." Achiote (annatto) powder is used for color and depth in the guiso; it's optional. Vegan option: follow the steps starting at the guiso stage without using any chicken, and serve with avocado and white rice (dried porcini mushrooms are a flavorful, if less authentic, addition). For extra starch, other cooks add taro (yautía), yams (ñame), or kabocha squash (auyama) to the pot. Serve with tostones and pico de gallo as a starter, plus white rice, avocado and Colombian aji for a full Colombian meal. Keeps in the fridge for a few days and can be frozen for up to a couple of months; reheat on the stove or in the microwave.
