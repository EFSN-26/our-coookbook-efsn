# Traditional Colombian Sancocho

## Description
A hearty, slow-simmered Colombian soup of beef shank or pork ribs, chicken, plantain, corn, potatoes, yuca and carrot in a well-seasoned broth — a dish traditionally cooked for Sunday family gatherings. [Source](https://www.gamintraveler.com/2026/05/29/how-to-make-sancocho-colombian-soup/)

## Ingredients
_(servings not stated by source — see §8 for scaling)_

- 1 pound beef shank (bone-in) or pork ribs
- 1 pound chicken (legs or thighs)
- 1 large plantain, peeled and cut into chunks
- 2 ears of corn, cut into pieces
- 2 medium potatoes, peeled and cut into chunks
- 2 medium yuca (cassava) roots, peeled and cut into chunks
- 1 large carrot, peeled and cut into chunks
- 1 medium onion, finely chopped
- 4 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 bell pepper, chopped
- 2 tomatoes, chopped
- 1 bunch cilantro, chopped (reserve some for garnish)
- 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
- 8 cups water or beef/chicken broth
- 2 teaspoons ground cumin
- 1 teaspoon oregano
- salt and pepper, to taste

**For serving**
- lime wedges
- avocado slices
- white rice (optional)

## Instructions
1. In a large pot or Dutch oven, heat the vegetable oil over medium-high heat. Add the beef shank or pork ribs and the chicken pieces. Brown on all sides, about 5 to 7 minutes. Remove the meat from the pot and set aside.
2. In the same pot, add the chopped onion, garlic, bell pepper and tomatoes. Sauté until soft and fragrant, about 5 minutes.
3. Return the browned meat to the pot. Add the water or broth, ground cumin, oregano, salt and pepper. Bring to a boil, then reduce the heat and simmer for about 30 minutes.
4. Add the plantain, corn, potatoes, yuca and carrot to the pot. Continue to simmer for another 30 to 40 minutes, or until the vegetables and meat are tender.
5. Taste and adjust the seasoning with more salt and pepper if needed. Stir in the chopped cilantro (reserving some for garnish).
6. Ladle the sancocho into bowls, making sure each serving gets a mix of meat, vegetables and broth. Garnish with additional cilantro. Serve with lime wedges, avocado slices, and white rice if desired.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
- A combination of beef, pork and chicken can be used for more depth of flavor; some variations also include sausage.
- Other root vegetables like taro, sweet potatoes or squash can be substituted or added.
- Simmer the soup slowly to ensure the meat becomes tender and the flavors develop fully.
- Sancocho varies significantly by Colombian region — some versions use only chicken, others three meats; some favor a lighter broth, others a thicker, stew-like consistency.
- Traditionally served with white rice, avocado slices and lime wedges (rice can be added directly to the soup or eaten on the side).
