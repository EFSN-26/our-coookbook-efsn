# Tuscan White Bean Soup (Minestra di Fagioli)

## Description
A hearty, creamy Tuscan bean soup with peasant origins, built from soaked cannellini beans, aromatic vegetables, rosemary, and parsley, with half the beans pureed back into the pot for a rich, velvety texture.

## Ingredients
_(for 6 servings)_

- 1 pound cannellini beans, soaked overnight in the refrigerator
- 1 yellow onion, diced
- 3 celery stalks, diced
- 3 garlic cloves
- 1 leek, thinly sliced and chopped
- 1 carrot, diced
- 2 rosemary sprigs, needles separated from the stem and finely chopped
- 3 parsley sprigs, finely chopped
- 1 tablespoon tomato paste
- 6 cups chicken stock, low sodium
- Salt and pepper, to taste
- 3 tablespoons olive oil
- Parmesan cheese, freshly grated, for serving

## Instructions
1. Place the dry cannellini beans in a large bowl and cover with water by at least 2 inches. Refrigerate overnight. Stir and remove any loose skins that come to the surface, then drain and rinse the beans under cold water, repeating a couple of times.
2. In a large pot, place the rinsed beans and cover with water. Bring to a boil, lower the heat to medium, and cook until almost tender, about 1 hour. Remove from the heat.
3. While the beans are cooking, chop the onion, celery, garlic, leek, carrot, rosemary, and parsley.
4. In another large Dutch oven pot, heat the olive oil. Add all the chopped vegetables, rosemary, garlic, and parsley. Cook for about 10 minutes until tender.
5. In a blender, add half of the partially cooked cannellini beans along with some chicken stock, and puree.
6. Add the pureed beans and the whole beans to the vegetables. Dilute the tomato paste with a couple of tablespoons of the stock and add it to the beans.
7. Add the remaining chicken stock, salt, and pepper. Cook over medium heat for about 45 minutes to 1 hour, or until the beans are fully cooked and tender, partially covered with a lid, stirring occasionally and scraping the bottom of the pot to prevent burning.
8. Serve hot with grated Parmesan cheese over it.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
- Cannellini beans can be substituted with navy beans or Great Northern beans.
- For more flavor and a richer soup, add some pancetta or ham.
- Pasta can be served with this soup — either cook it separately in boiling water and add it to the bowl, or cook it directly in the bean soup for a more starchy, creamy result.
- For a vegetarian version, use vegetable stock instead of chicken stock, or roast your favorite vegetables and boil them in a stockpot for 30 to 45 minutes to make a rich, savory vegetable stock.
- Storage: the soup will thicken as it stands. Reheat gently over medium-low heat and add chicken stock or water to reach the desired consistency. Keeps up to 3 days in the refrigerator.
- Freezing: freezes well for up to 3 months. Leave room in the container, as the soup will expand; separate into smaller portions for individual servings.
