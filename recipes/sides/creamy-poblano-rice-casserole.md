# Creamy Poblano Rice Casserole

## Description
A creamy, cheesy Mexican rice side dish built on charred corn, roasted poblano peppers, sour cream, white cheddar, and heavy whipping cream, baked until hot and bubbly. The author notes it won 3rd place in the Katy Rice Festival recipe contest. [Source](https://30seconds.com/food/tip/15598/This-Creamy-Poblano-Rice-Casserole-Recipe-Is-Award-Winning-9-Ingredients)

## Ingredients
_(for 12 servings)_

- 1 cup converted rice
- 3 ears corn on the cob
- 1 large poblano pepper
- 1 tablespoon olive oil
- 1 onion, chopped
- 2 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 small bunch cilantro, chopped (optional)
- 1 cup sour cream
- 8 ounces white cheddar cheese, grated
- 1/4 cup heavy whipping cream

## Instructions
1. Cook the rice according to package directions. Set aside. Char the corn on a grill or in a cast iron skillet. Slice off the kernels. Set aside. (Frozen corn can be used if you're in a rush.)
2. Roast the poblanos, then peel, seed, and chop.
3. Heat the olive oil in a skillet. Add half the onion, chopped poblano, and corn. Cook about 5 minutes. Add the garlic and cook about 2 minutes more. (For heat, stir in some chopped fresh jalapeño when cooking the onions.)
4. Combine the cooked vegetables, sour cream, cilantro, remaining onion, rice, cheese, and whipping cream. Season with black pepper, to taste. Pour into a 3-quart baking dish. Bake at 350°F for about 30 minutes.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
- The author won 3rd place in the Katy Rice Festival recipe contest with this casserole.
- Substitutions readers/the author have tried: smoked Gouda in place of white cheddar; lime zest and juice plus a little cumin in place of cilantro; frozen steamed corn charred alone in a hot pan if fresh corn isn't available.
- Serve as a side dish with chicken, pork, beef, seafood, or whatever protein you like for dinner.
