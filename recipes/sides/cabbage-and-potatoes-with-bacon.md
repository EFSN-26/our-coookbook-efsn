# Cabbage & Potatoes with Bacon

## Description
A hearty, budget-friendly comfort dish with origins in European peasant cooking, popularized in the U.S. in the 1920s-1950s. Crispy bacon, tender cabbage, and buttery potatoes simmer together in chicken broth until the liquid cooks down into a flavorful, condensed sauce.

## Ingredients
_(for 6 servings)_

- 4 slices bacon
- 1 cup onion, diced
- 3 cloves garlic, minced
- 4 cups green cabbage, chopped
- 1 pound yellow potatoes, diced
- 1 1/2 cups chicken broth, low or no sodium-added
- 1/2 teaspoon salt, to taste
- 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
- 1/2 teaspoon smoked paprika
- 1 tablespoon apple cider vinegar
- parsley, chopped, optional for garnish

## Instructions
1. Cook the bacon in a large skillet or Dutch oven over medium heat until crispy. Remove with a slotted spoon and set aside, leaving the bacon fat in the pan.
2. Sauté the onion in the bacon fat until softened, about 6 minutes.
3. Add the garlic and cook for another 30 seconds until fragrant.
4. Stir in the chopped cabbage and cook for about 5 minutes, stirring occasionally, until it starts to soften.
5. Add the diced potatoes, chicken broth, salt, black pepper, and smoked paprika (if using).
6. Partially cover and let simmer over medium-low heat for 15-20 minutes, or until the potatoes are fork-tender, stirring occasionally to prevent sticking. Most of the liquid should evaporate, leaving behind a condensed sauce.
7. Stir in the apple cider vinegar if using, and adjust seasoning as needed.
8. Top with the crispy bacon and garnish with chopped parsley before serving.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
- Cut the potatoes into even-sized pieces so they cook at the same rate.
- Drain off excess bacon fat if there's too much, leaving just enough to coat the vegetables.
- Use a waxy potato like Yukon Gold for a creamier texture, or Russets for a fluffier bite.
- The apple cider vinegar balances the richness of the bacon and broth; substitute a squeeze of lemon if needed.
- For a spicier kick, add a pinch of red pepper flakes while sautéing the onions.
- Storage: keeps in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3 days. Reheat gently on the stovetop with a splash of chicken broth. Freezing is not recommended, as the potatoes can turn grainy.
