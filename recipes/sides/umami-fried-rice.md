# Umami Fried Rice

## Description
A flavorful, family-pleasing fried rice that's fast, cheap and easy for busy weeknights — made with day-old rice, the secret to good fried rice texture. [Source](https://30seconds.com/food/tip/15509/Umami-Fried-Rice-Recipe-Is-a-Tasty-High-Fiber-High-Protein-Side-Dish-20-Minutes)

## Ingredients
_(for 8 servings)_

- 6 tablespoons butter
- 1 large yellow onion, very finely diced
- 2 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 large carrot, peeled and diced very small
- 6 cups day-old short-grain Japanese rice, room temperature
- 2 eggs, beaten
- 1/2 teaspoon garlic salt
- 1/8 - 1/4 cup soy sauce
- 1 cup frozen green peas, cooked (optional)
- 3 - 4 green onions (scallions), sliced in 1/4-inch diagonal slices

## Instructions
1. In a large wok or skillet, heat the butter. Add the onions and carrots. Cook until the carrots and onions begin to brown and the natural sugars start to caramelize, about 4 to 5 minutes. Add the garlic and cook an additional 1 minute.
2. Add the rice and stir until the carrot, onion and garlic are incorporated well. (Be sure to use day-old rice for this fried rice — make it the night before and pop it into the refrigerator.)
3. Add the beaten eggs and toss the rice quickly to cook through. Stir well. After everything is incorporated, turn up the heat to high and allow the rice to begin browning, stirring constantly, about an additional 3 to 4 minutes.
4. Season to taste with the soy sauce and add fresh cracked black pepper, if desired. (To reduce the sodium in the recipe, use low-sodium soy sauce.) Remove from the heat, add the cooked peas and scallions. Toss to fluff the rice up. Store any leftover rice in an airtight container in the fridge for up to five days.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
- Add sautéed shrimp or grilled chicken for a complete dinner.
- To enhance the umami flavors, use dashi broth instead of water when making the rice.
- Cauliflower rice can be substituted for a vegan-friendly version, per the author's suggestion.
