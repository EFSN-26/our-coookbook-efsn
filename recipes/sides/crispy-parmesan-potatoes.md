# Crispy Parmesan Potatoes

## Description
Boiled baby potatoes, smashed flat and roasted with olive oil, parmesan and garlic powder until crunchy on the outside and creamy in the middle. [Source](https://www.vanillabeancuisine.com/crispy-parmesan-potatoes/)

## Ingredients
_(for 4 servings — see §8 for scaling)_

- 1 1/2 pounds small yellow potatoes
- 3 tablespoons olive oil
- 2 1/2 tablespoons parmesan cheese
- 3/4 teaspoon garlic powder
- 1/2 teaspoon kosher salt
- 1/4 teaspoon freshly ground black pepper
- lemon wedges, optional, to serve

## Instructions
1. Preheat oven to 450°F.
2. Wash the potatoes, and cut out any discolored spots or eyes.
3. Fill a large saucepan with salted water, and bring to a boil over high heat.
4. Add the potatoes, and boil until just tender, about 20 minutes — this will take less time with very small potatoes. (Turn down the heat a little if the water is boiling too aggressively.) Drain well, then put back into the pot.
5. Toss gently with 2 tablespoons of the olive oil, 1 tablespoon of the parmesan cheese, the garlic powder, salt and pepper.
6. Spread in a single layer on a non-stick baking sheet.
7. Smash by pressing directly over the top of each potato with a sturdy jar or glass.
8. Bake for 10 minutes, then check that the bottoms of the potatoes are browned and will release from the pan.
9. Drizzle with the remaining tablespoon of oil, then flip the potatoes over and sprinkle with the rest of the cheese.
10. Bake for another 8 minutes, until crispy and browned on both sides.
11. Serve with lemon wedges, ketchup, or aioli.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
- Use small potatoes of similar size — yellow, fingerling, or baby red potatoes all work; russet (baking) potatoes are not recommended since they're too big and starchy.
- If cutting any potatoes in half, make sure the skin is on the bottom when smashing so the insides don't squeeze out.
- If your baking sheet isn't very non-stick, use parchment paper on top of it, though it may not brown the potatoes quite as well.
- Use a sturdy plastic (not metal) spatula to flip the potatoes without damaging the pan.
- If the potatoes don't release from the pan after 10 minutes, let them bake a bit longer until they brown enough to flip easily.
- For more flavor, try adding red pepper flakes or Italian herb seasoning to the parmesan mixture.
- Leftovers keep in an airtight container in the refrigerator for 3-4 days; reheat in the oven or an air fryer to re-crisp (they will soften in the microwave).
- Not recommended to make fully in advance — best eaten right out of the oven at peak crispiness.
