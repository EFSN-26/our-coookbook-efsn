# Shiitake and Broccoli in Soy Garlic Sauce

## Description
Parboiled shiitake and broccoli florets tossed in a soy garlic sauce thickened with potato starch, with mirin lending a subtle sweetness. It isn't a stir-fry — the process is boiling, sauteeing and braising — and the whole meatless side dish comes together quickly.

## Ingredients
_(for 3 servings)_

- 6 to 8 shiitake caps, sliced (caps only)
- 1/2 head broccoli, cut into small florets
- 8 cloves garlic, peeled and pounded
- 1 onion, peeled and thinly sliced
- salt, to taste
- 1 teaspoon sesame seed oil, divided
- 1 tablespoon cooking oil
- 2 tablespoons light soy sauce
- 2 tablespoons mirin
- 1/4 teaspoon ground black pepper
- 1/4 cup vegetable broth (the water used to cook the broccoli and shiitake)
- 1 heaping teaspoon potato starch or cornstarch
- chili flakes, optional

## Instructions
1. Slice the shiitake caps.
2. Cut the broccoli into small florets.
3. Peel and pound the garlic cloves.
4. Peel and thinly slice the onion.
5. Boil four cups of water in a wok with two pinches of salt and half a teaspoon of the sesame seed oil.
6. Drop in the broccoli florets, stir and boil for a minute. Scoop out and dump into a bowl of icy water.
7. In the remaining water, boil the shiitake slices for a minute. Scoop out and add to the broccoli in the icy water.
8. Drain the broccoli and shiitake and spread on a rack or a kitchen towel.
9. Rinse and wipe the wok, pour in the cooking oil and heat.
10. Sauté the garlic and onion just until softened.
11. Mix together the soy sauce, mirin, black pepper, vegetable broth, remaining sesame seed oil and starch. Pour the mixed sauce over the garlic and onion, stir and leave to boil.
12. Lower the heat, stir once more, and cook for a minute.
13. Add the shiitake and broccoli. Toss until heated through.
14. Taste, adjust the seasonings if needed.
15. Optionally, sprinkle with chili flakes before serving.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
The cooking itself takes about five minutes (two minutes boiling, two minutes sauteeing/making the sauce, one minute tossing everything together), but prep takes about twice as long unless you can get pre-cut broccoli florets and pre-sliced shiitake — for a total of about 15 minutes.
