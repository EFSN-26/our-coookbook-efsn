# Easy Caribbean Rice and Beans

## Description
A vegan Caribbean-style rice and beans with the kick of Scotch bonnet peppers and jerk seasoning, simmered together with red beans, coconut milk and long-grain rice for a hearty, comforting one-pot meal. [Source](https://twocityvegans.com/caribbean-red-beans-and-rice/)

## Ingredients
_(for 8 servings — see §8 for scaling)_

- 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
- 2 garlic cloves, minced
- 1 onion, chopped
- 2 scallions, sliced
- 1-2 Scotch bonnets, chopped
- 2 teaspoons Jerk seasoning
- 2 cups long grain rice
- 1 can red beans, drained
- 1 can coconut milk
- 2 cups vegetable broth
- salt and pepper
- cilantro, sliced

## Instructions
1. Heat a heavy bottom pot to medium-high and add the oil. Add the garlic and onion and cook for 5-7 minutes.
2. Add the scallions and Scotch bonnets and cook for a couple more minutes.
3. Add the Jerk seasoning and mix until fully combined.
4. Add the red beans, rice, coconut milk, vegetable broth, salt and pepper. Mix well.
5. Bring to a boil and once it breaks boiling reduce the temperature and simmer for 12-15 minutes, or until the rice is tender and the liquid has evaporated.
6. Serve the rice with fresh cilantro.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
- Choosing the right rice: use long grain rice like Basmati or Jasmine — these hold up well during cooking and won't turn mushy.
- Rinse the rice before cooking to remove excess starch, which helps prevent clumping and ensures fluffy rice.
- Managing the heat: Scotch bonnets are very spicy — start with a small amount and adjust as needed; remove the seeds to reduce spice.
- Sautéing secrets: take time sautéing the onions and garlic until golden for a deeper, sweeter flavor base.
- Use full-fat coconut milk for the creamiest texture.
- Bean varieties: feel free to experiment with black beans or chickpeas instead of red beans.
- Fresh beans can be used instead of canned — soak overnight and cook until tender first.
- If not a fan of cilantro, try parsley or sliced green onions instead.
- Storage: cool to room temperature, then store in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 5 days, or freeze for up to 3 months. Reheat with a splash of water or broth to keep the rice moist.
