# High-Protein Crustless Spinach & Feta Casserole

## Description
A crustless, gluten-free-friendly spanakopita-like casserole made for family and gluten-free friends alike. The beauty of this high-protein casserole is that you can use any flour you like, including all-purpose flour. [Source](https://30seconds.com/food/tip/69033/High-Protein-Crustless-Spinach-Feta-Casserole-Recipe-Is-Greek-Greatness)

## Ingredients
_(for 6 servings)_

- 2 teaspoons olive oil
- 4 green onions or 2 shallots, chopped (or a combination)
- 12 ounces baby spinach, fresh or frozen, roughly chopped (if frozen, defrost first)
- 1 cup all-purpose flour, oat flour, almond flour or a gluten-free flour blend (the author used Bob's Red Mill gluten-free flour blend)
- 1 teaspoon baking powder
- 1 teaspoon sea salt
- 1 cup milk (almond, coconut, soy or regular)
- 2 large eggs, lightly beaten
- 2 tablespoons unsalted butter, melted
- 2 tablespoons fresh dill, chopped
- 4 ounces shredded mozzarella
- 4 ounces feta cheese
- 2 tablespoons grated Parmesan cheese

## Instructions
1. If using fresh spinach, place a frying pan on the stove on medium heat with a few tablespoons of water. Add the spinach and wilt it down, stirring with a spatula every now and then.
2. Place the fresh cooked or defrosted frozen spinach in a colander and squeeze out excess moisture. Be careful with fresh spinach as it will be hot. Use a clean towel or paper towel to press out as much moisture as possible. Set aside.
3. Using the same frying pan, add green onions or shallots and olive oil. Cook on medium heat for 2 minutes.
4. While the green onions cook, add the flour, salt and baking powder to a mixing bowl. Use a fork to gently blend.
5. Add the milk, eggs and melted butter. Blend. (If using a plant-based milk, make sure it is unsweetened.)
6. Add the green onions, dill, feta, mozzarella and spinach. Blend well.
7. Put the batter into a parchment paper-lined 8x8-inch baking pan and press it into the corners. Smooth the top with a spatula. Sprinkle the top with Parmesan cheese.
8. Bake in a preheated 375-degree F oven for 40 to 45 minutes. Remove when you press gently on the top and it springs back. Remove and cool for 10 to 15 minutes.
9. Cut, serve and enjoy! Store any leftovers in an airtight container in the fridge and reheat in the microwave.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
- You can prepare the recipe the day before and refrigerate it, covered, then bake the following day.
- This casserole also bakes very nicely as muffins, but decrease the cooking time by half and check for doneness.
- The author's photos show a doubled recipe baked in a 13x9-inch baking pan.
- Serve for breakfast, brunch, lunch or a vegetarian dinner with your favorite side dishes, or as a side dish alongside your favorite protein.
- Use a gluten-free flour blend (such as Bob's Red Mill) if a gluten-free result is needed; regular all-purpose flour also works.
