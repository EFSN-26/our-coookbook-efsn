# Mediterranean Roasted Greek Potatoes

## Description
A five-ingredient Mediterranean side dish full of Greek herbs and olive oil. Boiling the potatoes before roasting speeds up cooking and results in potatoes that are creamy on the inside, crispy on the outside. [Source](https://30seconds.com/food/tip/42724/Mediterranean-Diet-Roasted-Greek-Potatoes-Recipe-Is-Crispy-Creamy-5-Ingredients)

## Ingredients
_(for 10 servings)_

- 3 pounds (approximately) red potatoes, whole or sliced in half, depending on size and preference
- 1/4 - 1/3 cup extra virgin olive oil (depending on how many potatoes you use)
- 2 teaspoons dried oregano (or to taste)
- 5 cloves garlic, minced (or to taste)
- 1/4 cup fresh lemon juice
- salt and pepper, to taste

## Instructions
1. In a Dutch oven, boil potatoes until they're tender but not too soft, about 10 to 15 minutes. Drain potatoes. (Fingerling or Yukon gold potatoes could be used instead of red potatoes.)
2. In a large bowl, combine olive oil, lemon juice, oregano and garlic. Stir to combine. Add potatoes and coat evenly in the mixture. Put coated potatoes in two 13x9-inch baking pans or casserole dishes. Season with salt and pepper, to taste.
3. Bake in a preheated 375-degree F oven for about 20 to 25 minutes, or until golden brown. Store any leftovers in an airtight container in the fridge and reheat in the microwave.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
- Boiling the potatoes first speeds up the roasting process and yields potatoes that are creamy inside and crispy outside.
