# Greek Roasted Potatoes Recipe With Lemon & Feta

## Description
Crispy Mediterranean-inspired roasted fingerling potatoes made with nine ingredients including garlic, oregano and fresh rosemary, tossed in a lemon-Dijon-honey marinade and finished with crumbled feta cheese.

## Ingredients
_(for 14 servings)_

- 3 pounds fingerling potatoes, sliced in half, soaked in water then dried
- 1/4 cup extra virgin olive oil
- 1/4 cup fresh lemon juice
- 4 teaspoons minced garlic
- 2 teaspoons Dijon mustard
- 1 tablespoon honey
- 2 teaspoons dried oregano
- 3 tablespoons fresh rosemary
- 4 ounces feta cheese, crumbled
- lemon slices for serving (optional)

## Instructions
1. Slice the potatoes in half then soak them in a large bowl of cold water (while preparing the marinade) to remove some of the starch. Dry them before using. (Small Yukon gold, marble or red potatoes can be used instead of fingerling potatoes.)
2. In a small mixing bowl or large measuring cup, add the olive oil, lemon juice, garlic, mustard, honey, oregano and rosemary. Season with sea salt and black pepper, to taste, if desired. Whisk to blend.
3. Evenly spread the potatoes into a 13x9-inch baking pan or casserole dish. Pour the lemon mixture over the potatoes and toss until the potatoes are coated.
4. Bake in a preheated 400-degree F oven for about 60 minutes (ovens vary), stirring twice during baking to ensure even roasting, until tender, crispy and golden.
5. Remove from oven. Sprinkle the feta cheese evenly over the top of the potatoes. Serve with optional lemon slices, if desired. Store leftovers in a container in the refrigerator and reheat in the oven for maximum crispiness.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
- Soaking the halved potatoes in cold water before roasting helps remove starch for a crispier result.
- Stirring the potatoes twice during baking ensures even roasting and browning.
- Reheat leftovers in the oven rather than the microwave to restore crispiness.
