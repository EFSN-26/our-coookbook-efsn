# Buttery Garlicky Cannellini Beans With Feta and Sumac

## Description
Creamy, garlicky cannellini beans finished with cold butter, topped with slabs of feta and a generous shower of sumac. A quick, humble-ingredient dish the author associates with easy summer dinners, best served with grilled or toasted bread for scooping up the sauce. [Source](https://www.seriouseats.com/garlicky-cannelini-beans-recipe-12006461)

## Ingredients
_(for 4 servings)_

- 2 tablespoons (30 ml) extra-virgin olive oil, plus more for serving
- 12 cloves garlic, thinly sliced
- 2 (15-ounce; 425 g) cans cannellini beans, drained and rinsed
- 5 sprigs thyme
- 1/3 cup (80 ml) low-sodium chicken stock (store-bought or homemade), plus more for finishing
- 2 tablespoons cold unsalted butter (1 ounce; 28 g), cut into small pieces
- 1/2 teaspoon Diamond Crystal kosher salt, plus more to taste (for table salt, use half as much by volume)
- 4 teaspoons (20 ml) fresh lemon juice
- 1 teaspoon grated lemon zest
- 4 ounces (115 g) feta cheese, preferably cut into slabs
- Freshly ground black pepper, to taste
- Sumac, for serving (optional)
- Grilled, toasted, or seared bread, for serving

## Instructions
1. In a large skillet, combine the olive oil and garlic. Cook over medium heat, starting from cold, until the garlic is very lightly golden, about 5 minutes.
2. Add the thyme and cook until fragrant and sizzling, about 30 seconds.
3. Add the beans and salt, tossing to coat evenly. Add the stock and bring to a simmer. Cook until the flavors meld and the beans are creamy, about 7 minutes, lowering the heat as needed to maintain a gentle simmer.
4. Crush about 1/4 of the beans with the back of a wooden spoon to release their starch. Add more stock, 2 tablespoons at a time, to reach a thick, saucy consistency. Remove the thyme sprigs.
5. Off heat, stir in the butter a couple of pieces at a time until the sauce is glossy and emulsified. Stir in the lemon juice and zest. Season with additional salt to taste.
6. Transfer the beans to a serving dish and top with feta, a drizzle of olive oil, black pepper, and sumac (if using). Serve with bread on the side.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
- Starting the garlic in a cold skillet with the olive oil and bringing both up to temperature together coaxes sweetness and aroma from the garlic without burning it.
- Crushing a portion of the beans releases their starch, naturally thickening the cooking liquid into a creamy sauce.
- Cold butter stirred in off the heat emulsifies with the cooking liquid, producing a smooth, cohesive sauce.
- Special equipment: large skillet.
- Make-ahead/storage: leftovers can be refrigerated in an airtight container for up to 4 days.
- The author (from Jordan) notes sumac is a staple ingredient there, prized for its tart, citrusy flavor, and pairs the beans with grilled sourdough for dragging through the garlicky broth.
