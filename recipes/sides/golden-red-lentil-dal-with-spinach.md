# Golden Red Lentil Dal with Spinach

## Description
A vibrant, vegan one-pot dal made with golden red lentils (masoor dal) and fresh spinach, simmered in warm spices — red lentils cook in about 15 minutes, breaking down slightly for a naturally creamy texture. [Source](https://lentillovingfamily.com/red-lentil-dal/)

## Ingredients
_(for 4 servings — see §8 for scaling)_

- 1 cup red lentils, rinsed well
- 2 tablespoons oil
- 1 small onion, chopped
- 2 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 15 oz can diced tomatoes
- 1 teaspoon ground cumin
- 2 teaspoons ground coriander
- ½ teaspoon turmeric
- ½ teaspoon chili powder, adjust to your spice preference
- 1 teaspoon salt, adjust as per taste
- 4 cups water, add more if needed
- ½ teaspoon garam masala
- 1 cup spinach, chopped
- 2 tablespoons lemon juice

## Instructions
1. Heat oil in a pot over medium heat. Add onion and garlic. Saute for 4-5 minutes until the onions soften.
2. Add tomatoes, salt, and powdered spices (cumin, coriander, turmeric, and chili powder). Mix well and cook for 4-5 minutes or until the tomatoes begin to soften.
3. Add the rinsed red lentils and water. Mix well and bring it to a boil. Reduce heat, cover, and simmer for 25-30 minutes or until the lentils are tender.
4. Stir in spinach and garam masala. Cook for 1-2 minutes or until the spinach is wilted. Turn off the heat and stir in lemon juice.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
- Don't overcook the lentils — keep an eye on them so they don't turn mushy; cook just until tender but still holding their shape.
- Add the spinach towards the end of cooking to preserve its vibrant color and freshness; it only needs a couple of minutes to wilt into the dal.
- For a creamier dal, mash some of the cooked lentils against the side of the pot with a spoon, or add a splash of coconut milk or cashew cream for a richer texture.
- Slow cooker option: make the onion-tomato base in a frying pan, then transfer it to the slow cooker with the red lentils and water (same quantities). Cook on high for 3.5-4 hours.
- Storage: keeps in the refrigerator for up to 4-5 days, or freeze for up to 3 months; thaw overnight and reheat gently on the stove with a splash of water to restore the creamy consistency.
- Serve with steamed rice or naan bread.
