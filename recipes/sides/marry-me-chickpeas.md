# Marry Me Chickpeas

## Description
A vegetarian take on the viral "Marry Me Chicken," this dish features tender chickpeas bathed in a rich, creamy tomato sauce bursting with sun-dried tomatoes, garlic, and warm spices — ready in 20 minutes and just as saucy and satisfying as the original.

## Ingredients
_(for 6 servings — see §8 for scaling)_

- 2 15 oz cans chickpeas
- 2 tablespoons oil from sun-dried tomato jar
- 2 shallots, finely chopped
- 2 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 cup sun-dried tomatoes in oil, cut into thin strips
- 1½ cups vegetable broth
- 1 teaspoon Italian seasoning
- Salt and pepper, to taste
- 1 teaspoon paprika
- ½ cup heavy cream
- ½ cup parmesan cheese
- 2 tablespoons parsley

## Instructions
1. Place a frying pan or Dutch oven on medium heat and add oil from the sun-dried tomato jar. Add shallots and garlic. Sauté for 3 minutes or until the onion begins to soften.
2. Add the sun-dried tomatoes and cook until they begin to soften, about 2 minutes.
3. Stir in chickpeas, vegetable broth, Italian seasoning, paprika, salt, and pepper. Bring to a boil and cook for 6–8 minutes or until the mixture begins to reduce slightly.
4. Slowly add the cream and mix gently. Simmer for one minute, then turn off the heat.
5. Stir in the parmesan cheese and mix gently. Top with parsley and serve.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
- Use the oil from the jar of sun-dried tomatoes to sauté the shallots and garlic — it adds an extra layer of flavor and richness.
- Adjust the amount of heavy cream to taste for more or less creaminess.
- Substitute other beans like cannellini, kidney, or white beans for a different flavor profile.
- For a dairy-free version, substitute coconut cream for the heavy cream and omit the parmesan or use a dairy-free alternative.
- Leftovers keep in an airtight container in the refrigerator for 3–4 days or in the freezer for up to 1 month.
- Serve over pasta, rice, or with crusty bread to mop up the sauce.
