# Smashed Potatoes

## Description
Boiled petite gold potatoes, smashed flat and roasted with olive oil, flaky sea salt and fresh rosemary until crispy outside and fluffy inside — a simple four-ingredient side dish or appetizer. [Source](https://www.giangiskitchen.com/smashed-potatoes/)

## Ingredients
_(for 4 servings — see §8 for scaling)_

- 2 pounds Gold petite potatoes
- 3 tablespoons olive oil
- sea salt, as you wish
- fresh rosemary, as you wish

## Instructions
1. Preheat the oven to 375°F and place parchment paper on a baking sheet.
2. Place the potatoes in a pot filled with enough water to cover them by 1 inch and bring to a boil. Cook until the potatoes are soft and a knife inserts with no resistance, 15 to 20 minutes.
3. Drain the potatoes and let them cool off slightly. Place them over the parchment paper and, using the back of a measuring cup, flatten them out.
4. Drizzle the potatoes with olive oil, and sprinkle a generous amount of salt and fresh rosemary over each potato.
5. Roast for 25 to 35 minutes, or until golden brown and crisp around the edges.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
- Use small yellow golden potatoes of similar size for even cooking; fingerling or small red potatoes also work.
- Let the potatoes dry a bit before smashing for the best crispiness.
- Have the oven nice and hot and the rack positioned in the middle before baking.
- Use parchment paper on the baking sheet to prevent sticking and breaking.
- Do not use a glass to smash the potatoes, as it can break; use metal or plastic instead.
- Variations: add garlic powder or bacon bits; swap in other fresh herbs like chives or thyme; finish with chopped parsley, or sprinkle with parmesan cheese and a pat of butter, served with a dollop of sour cream.
- Best served immediately for maximum crispiness. Leftovers keep in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3 days; reheat in the oven with a splash of olive oil.
