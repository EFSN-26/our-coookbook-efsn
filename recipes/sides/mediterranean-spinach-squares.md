# High-Protein Mediterranean Spinach Squares

## Description
A savory, cheesy spinach bake — flavored with onion, garlic and fresh dill — cut into snack-cake-style squares (the source calls them "spinach brownies," though they are savory, not sweet). Packed with greens and protein. [Source](https://30seconds.com/food/tip/73438/Mediterranean-Arugula-Spinach-Squares-Recipe-Is-Nutritious-Delicious)

## Ingredients
_(for 9 servings — see §8 for scaling)_

- 2 tablespoons extra virgin olive oil
- 1 cup onion, diced
- 12 ounces fresh spinach
- 1 1/4 cups flour
- 1 teaspoon baking powder
- 2 eggs
- 1 cup milk (of choice)
- 1/2 cup (1 stick) butter, melted
- 2 tablespoons fresh dill
- 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder
- 1 1/4 cups mozzarella cheese, shredded (or parmesan or cheddar cheese)

## Instructions
1. In a large skillet or sauté pan over medium-high heat, add the olive oil and diced onions. Sauté until tender, about 3 minutes.
2. Add the spinach and sauté with the onions until wilted. Remove from heat. (Frozen spinach can be used instead of fresh — be sure to wring out excess moisture. Other greens like kale or arugula also work.)
3. In a medium mixing bowl, add the flour and baking powder and stir to combine. (Use gluten-free flour to make these gluten-free.)
4. Add the eggs, milk, butter, dill, garlic powder and cheese. Season with salt and pepper to taste, if desired. Stir to combine.
5. Using a slotted spoon to avoid adding excess moisture, stir the vegetable mixture into the batter. (If the vegetable mixture still has a lot of moisture, blot it on paper towels first.)
6. Pour the mixture into a baking pan or casserole dish sprayed with cooking spray or lined with parchment paper (the source used a 10x8-inch dish).
7. Bake in a preheated 375°F oven until golden brown, about 30 minutes, or until it passes the toothpick test.
8. Allow to cool before cutting into squares and serving. Garnish with fresh dill, if desired. Store leftovers in an airtight container in the refrigerator and reheat in the microwave or oven.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
- Despite the source's "spinach brownies" nickname, this is a savory dish, not a sweet dessert.
- Can be served as a vegetarian meal for breakfast, a light lunch, or a vegetarian side dish with dinner.
- You can substitute parmesan or cheddar (or a combination) for the mozzarella, and any milk of choice.
