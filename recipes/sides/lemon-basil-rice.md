# Lemon Basil Rice

## Description
A zesty side dish made with jasmine rice infused with sautéed shallot and garlic, then finished with fresh basil, lemon juice and lemon zest — ready in under 25 minutes. [Source](https://www.peelwithzeal.com/lemon-basil-rice/)

## Ingredients
_(for 4 servings — see §8 for scaling)_

- 2 Tablespoons unsalted butter
- 1 shallot, finely diced
- 2 garlic cloves, minced
- 2 cups chicken stock (or vegetable broth)
- 1 cup jasmine rice (or other long grain white rice)
- 2 lemons, zest and juice
- ¼ cup minced fresh basil, plus more for garnish

## Instructions
1. In a medium saucepan, heat the butter over medium heat. Add the shallot and garlic. Saute for 2 to 3 minutes, stirring frequently.
2. Add the broth and bring to a boil. Add the rice and salt (see note below). Cover and reduce heat to a simmer. Cook for 20 minutes or according to package directions.
3. Remove the rice from the heat. Allow to sit covered for 5 more minutes. Transfer rice to a bowl. Fluff with a fork. Stir in the lemon juice and zest, and basil. Salt and pepper to taste.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
- If you use low sodium stock or water you will need to add between ¼ and ½ teaspoon of kosher salt.
- Rinse rice before cooking until the water runs clear.
- Storing leftovers: Transfer to an airtight container and refrigerate for 3-4 days. Reheat in a microwave or on the stovetop over medium heat until warmed through, adding a splash of water or broth if needed.
- Check the rice package instructions to make sure the broth-to-rice ratio is correct — too much broth makes it soggy, not enough results in a dry texture.
