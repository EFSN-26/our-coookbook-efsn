# Mushroom Risotto

## Description
Classic Italian mushroom risotto combining creamy Arborio rice with tender, garlicky, earthy mushrooms. A cozy, comforting dish that's perfect for a weeknight dinner yet impressive enough for special occasions.

## Ingredients
_(for 6 servings)_

- 1 ½ cups arborio rice
- 12 ounces baby bella mushrooms, divided
- 4 ounces shiitake mushrooms, sliced
- 6 cups vegetable broth
- 1 cup white wine
- ⅓ cup parmesan cheese, grated
- 4 cloves garlic
- ½ cup shallot
- ¼ cup butter, divided
- 2 tablespoons olive oil
- 2 teaspoons lemon thyme (or regular thyme)
- ½ teaspoon salt
- ¼ teaspoon ground black pepper

## Instructions
1. Chop 8 ounces of the baby bella mushrooms and set them aside.
2. Slice the remaining 4 ounces of baby bella mushrooms and add them along with the shiitake mushrooms into a 10-inch skillet or braiser with the olive oil.
3. Sauté over medium heat until they are tender and starting to caramelize. Remove them and reserve for the risotto topping.
4. Add the chopped baby bella mushrooms into the same skillet and repeat the sauté process.
5. While the mushrooms are sautéing, heat a saucepan with the vegetable broth and keep it on low heat, as you will need to keep it hot as you gradually add it to the rice.
6. Remove the mushrooms and set them aside in a separate bowl from the shiitake mushroom mix. These will later be mixed into the risotto.
7. Wipe out the pan, then add in half the butter, shallots, salt, and pepper.
8. Sauté until soft, then add the minced garlic and sauté for another 15 seconds.
9. Pour in the rice and stir, allowing the grain to toast in the skillet for about 1 minute.
10. Pour in the white wine and stir. Allow it to simmer on low until the rice has absorbed the wine.
11. Over low heat, add in a ladle full of the hot vegetable broth and simmer on low, stirring often. Once the rice has almost absorbed the broth, add in another ladle full and continue the process until the rice is cooked through and al dente, about 25 minutes.
12. Add in the remaining butter, fresh thyme, chopped mushrooms, and grated Parmesan cheese. Mix until combined and serve immediately with the reserved slices of baby bella and shiitake mushrooms over the top.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
- Risotto is best served immediately — the longer it sits, the more it thickens and loses its creamy texture.
- Stir often as the rice cooks; this encourages the release of starch for a creamier texture and prevents sticking.
- Don't add cold broth to the rice — keep the broth hot as you add it.
- Taste before serving and adjust seasoning; the Parmesan adds saltiness, so you may need less salt than expected.
- Store leftovers in an airtight container; reheat gently on the stovetop or microwave with a splash of broth or water to restore creaminess.
- Substitutions: use chicken or beef broth instead of vegetable broth; Pecorino Romano instead of Parmesan; rosemary, sage, or basil instead of thyme; or mix in porcini, oyster, or chanterelle mushrooms.
- Additions: a dollop of mascarpone or splash of heavy cream for extra creaminess, toasted pine nuts or hazelnuts for crunch, thawed frozen peas, or a drizzle of truffle oil before serving.
- It's not necessary to rinse Arborio rice before cooking — the starchiness helps create the signature creamy texture.
- Leftover risotto can be rolled into balls, coated in breadcrumbs, and fried to make Arancini (Italian rice balls).
