# Farofa de Natal
(Brazilian Christmas Farofa)

## Description
Farofa de Natal is a tradition on the Brazilian Christmas Eve dinner menu. It can take on a special taste with sausage, bacon, eggs, and olives. Simple and easy, the recipe is made with cassava flour (farinha de mandioca) and is a traditional accompaniment to baked salt cod dishes and Christmas ham. Translated from the original Portuguese source.

## Ingredients
_(for 10 servings)_

- 3 tablespoons butter
- 100 grams bacon, chopped
- 100 grams calabresa (Brazilian smoked pork) sausage, chopped
- 1 onion, chopped
- 2 cloves garlic, chopped
- 1 green bell pepper, chopped
- 500 grams cassava flour (farinha de mandioca)
- 1/2 cup green olives, chopped
- 1 tablespoon dedo-de-moça chile pepper, seeded and chopped
- Chopped fresh herbs (parsley/chives), to taste
- 2 eggs, hard-boiled and chopped
- Salt, to taste

## Instructions
1. In a pan, melt 3 tablespoons of butter. Add the chopped bacon, chopped calabresa sausage, and chopped onion, and mix.
2. Add the chopped garlic and chopped green bell pepper, and mix.
3. Pour in the cassava flour and mix continuously until all the ingredients are fully incorporated.
4. Add the chopped green olives, the chopped dedo-de-moça pepper, and the chopped fresh herbs to taste, and mix.
5. Add the chopped hard-boiled eggs and salt to taste, and mix again. Serve right away.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
The source describes this as an indispensable dish for Brazilian Christmas Eve and New Year's Eve dinners, and notes it pairs well as an accompaniment to baked salt cod (bacalhoada) and Christmas ham (tender de Natal).
