# Sauteed Spinach & Kale

## Description
A quick, healthy side dish that combines fresh kale and spinach sauteed with onion, garlic, olive oil and a squeeze of lemon. It cooks up in about 10 minutes and is high in fiber and magnesium.

## Ingredients
_(for 6 servings)_

- 1 bunch kale, tough stems removed (about 1 pound)
- 1 small onion, chopped
- 1/4 cup olive oil
- 5 ounces fresh spinach
- 4 cloves garlic, minced
- juice from 1/2 a lemon (or to taste)
- salt, to taste
- black pepper, to taste

## Instructions
1. Heat the olive oil in a large skillet or dutch oven. Add the kale and onion. Cook until the kale wilts, about 5 to 7 minutes. Season with salt and black pepper.
2. Add the spinach and garlic. Cook until spinach wilts. (Don't have fresh garlic? Use garlic powder instead. Add some crushed red pepper flakes for a little spice.)
3. Pour in the lemon juice. Taste and add more salt and black pepper, if needed. (If you want more moisture in your dish, add a little water when cooking — not too much.) Serve. Store any leftovers in an airtight container in the fridge and reheat in the microwave.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
No fresh garlic? Substitute garlic powder. Add crushed red pepper flakes for a little heat. For more moisture, add a splash of water while cooking.
