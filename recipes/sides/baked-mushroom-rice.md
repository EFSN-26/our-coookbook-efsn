# Baked Mushroom Rice

## Description
An earthy, comforting one-pan side dish of Jasmine rice baked with mushrooms, garlic, onion, herbs and crushed tomatoes — simple enough for a weeknight and impressive enough for a potluck.

## Ingredients
_(for 4 servings)_

- 8 ounces cremini mushrooms or white button mushrooms, quartered
- 3 tablespoons olive oil, divided
- 1/2 large red onion, diced
- 3 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 1/2 cups Jasmine rice (or other medium-grain white rice)
- 1 1/4 cups crushed tomatoes, canned
- 1 cup vegetable broth
- 1 teaspoon dried oregano
- 1/2 teaspoon dried parsley
- 1/2 teaspoon dried rosemary
- 1/2 teaspoon sea salt

## Instructions
1. Preheat oven to 400°F/200°C.
2. In a mesh strainer, rinse the rice until the water runs clear (see note). Spread it out on a clean tea towel or a couple of layers of paper towel to remove most of the moisture.
3. Wash and dry (or wipe clean) the mushrooms. Trim the stems and slice into quarters. Mince the garlic and dice the onion.
4. In an all-metal cast iron or other oven-safe pan, heat 2 tablespoons of the olive oil on medium-high.
5. Add the mushrooms and cook for 2 minutes, stirring regularly, until starting to brown.
6. Reduce the heat to medium and add the remaining 1 tablespoon olive oil.
7. Add the minced onion, garlic, and rice. Cook for 2-3 minutes, stirring consistently, until the rice is slightly translucent.
8. Remove from heat and add in the oregano, parsley, rosemary, sea salt, broth, and tomatoes. Stir to evenly distribute everything.
9. Cover the pan completely with foil or a fitted, oven-safe lid.
10. Bake for 30 minutes or until all the liquid is absorbed. Remove from the oven and allow to sit for 5 minutes before fluffing with a fork and serving.
11. Garnish with fresh or dried parsley, chives, or nutritional yeast (or Parmesan for non-vegans).

## Cook's Notes & Tips
Rinsing the rice removes excess starch that would otherwise make it gluey; if you skip this step, add an additional 1/4 cup (60 ml) water or broth when adding the tomatoes. Jasmine rice is fragrant and slightly sticky; brown rice can be substituted for a nuttier flavor and more texture, but will need a longer cooking time. Cremini or white button mushrooms are suggested, but portobello or shiitake add unique flavors. Once the liquid and dish are in the oven, resist the urge to peek and stir — trust the process for the right texture. Letting the rice rest 5 minutes after baking is crucial for the best fluffiness and flavor. This recipe generously serves 4 as a side dish and is easily doubled for larger gatherings or meal prep. For more protein, add chickpeas or lentils before baking, or serve with grilled tofu or tempeh. For more vegetables, stir in baby spinach leaves or diced zucchini with the broth and tomatoes. To store: cool to room temperature, transfer to an airtight container, refrigerate within two hours of cooking, and eat within 5 days. Reheat in the microwave with a splash of water or broth to fluff it back up.
