# Apricot-Pistachio Overnight Oats

## Description
These creamy overnight oats are packed with nutrient-rich ingredients like whole-grain oats, dried apricots and nuts. A quick apricot puree adds natural sweetness and helps create a silky texture, making this a make-ahead-friendly breakfast. [Source](https://www.eatingwell.com/apricot-pistachio-overnight-oats-11967100)

## Ingredients
_(for 4 servings)_

- 1/2 cup boiling water
- 1/2 cup chopped dried apricots, plus 1 tablespoon, divided
- 2 cups old-fashioned rolled oats
- 2 cups unsweetened plain oat milk
- 1/2 cup nonfat plain strained yogurt, such as Greek-style
- 1/2 cup chopped unsalted roasted pistachios, plus 1 tablespoon, divided
- 1 1/2 tablespoons honey
- 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
- 1 1/2 tablespoons toasted unsweetened shredded coconut

## Instructions
1. Combine 1/2 cup boiling water and 1/2 cup chopped apricots in a food processor. Let stand until the apricots are softened and plumped, about 15 minutes. Process until mostly smooth, about 1 minute, stopping to scrape down the sides as needed. Transfer to a large bowl.
2. Add 2 cups oats, 2 cups oat milk, 1/2 cup yogurt, 1/2 cup pistachios, 1 1/2 tablespoons honey and 1 teaspoon vanilla to the apricot mixture; stir until combined. Cover and refrigerate until thickened, at least 8 hours or up to 4 days.
3. To serve, divide the oats evenly among 4 (8-ounce) jars. Top with 1 1/2 tablespoons coconut and the remaining 1 tablespoon each apricots and pistachios.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
- Be sure to fully soften the apricots in boiling water before blending, to ensure a smoother puree with no chewy bits.
- For thicker oats, use slightly less oat milk; for a looser, spoonable texture, stir in an extra splash before serving.
- To keep the pistachios crunchy and the coconut from softening in the fridge, layer on the toppings just before serving.
- Make ahead: refrigerate in an airtight container for up to 4 days.
- Substitutions: almond, dairy or soy milk can replace the oat milk; chopped almonds, walnuts, pecans, pumpkin seeds or sunflower seeds can replace pistachios.
