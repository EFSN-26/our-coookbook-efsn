# Cheesy Baked Polenta and Egg with Bacon

## Description
Creamy polenta meets gooey cheese, crispy bacon, and a perfectly baked egg in this Romanian-inspired dish, discovered by the author at a restaurant in Sinaia, Romania. A simple, comforting, and indulgent meal.

## Ingredients
_(for 2 servings)_

To prepare the polenta:
- 1 cup polenta, dry, uncooked
- 4 cups water
- 1/4 teaspoon salt

To assemble:
- 1/2 cup cooked bacon bits (cook raw with some oil, or purchase cooked)
- 1/2 cup shredded cheese
- 2 eggs
- 1 pinch salt
- 1 pinch black pepper, ground
- 2 tablespoons green chives or green onion stalks, chopped, to garnish

## Instructions
1. Preheat oven to 400°F (204°C).
2. Cook the polenta first: heat a pan with the polenta and gradually pour in the water. Season with salt and stir continuously until the polenta thickens and bubbles up.
3. Divide the cooked bacon pieces and shredded cheese between the baking dishes.
4. Split the polenta between the two baking dishes.
5. Make a slight well into the polenta and break an egg into each baking dish.
6. Bake the polenta and egg for about 20 minutes at 400°F (204°C), or until the egg is cooked through, uncovered.
7. When baked, let cool slightly and top with chopped fresh chives or green onions. Enjoy hot.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
Serve with dark brown or wholegrain bread slices, and a small side salad (shredded carrot salad, green lettuce salad, or turnip salad) for a complete meal. Can be prepared in advance by baking as directed with the egg, then cooled, covered with foil, and refrigerated for up to 2-3 days; reheat gradually in the oven. Freezing is not recommended because of the egg. The author notes that a version without the bacon was a flop — the bacon buried under the hot polenta with the cheese is what makes the dish special.
