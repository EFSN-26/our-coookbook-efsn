# Zucchini and Eggs Skillet Recipe

## Description
A quick skillet meal with sliced zucchini and scrambled eggs, cooked together in one pan — meatless, vegetarian and accidentally gluten-free. A fast last-minute dinner (or any-meal) option for when zucchini is in season. [Source](https://www.masalaherb.com/zucchini-and-eggs-skillet/)

## Ingredients
_(for 2 servings)_

- 1 large onion (purple or red preferred for color contrast), sliced
- 1 medium zucchini, sliced
- 1 tablespoon oil (extra virgin olive oil or sunflower oil)
- 1 teaspoon oregano, dried (or marjoram, or fresh herbs)
- ½ teaspoon salt
- ⅛ teaspoon black pepper, ground
- 2 eggs
- 1 tablespoon parsley, fresh, chopped

## Instructions
1. Peel the onion, cut off the ends, halve and slice. Set aside.
2. Rinse the zucchini well, cut off ends, halve and slice into pieces. Set aside.
3. Heat the oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat and sauté the onion slices until they appear a bit soft.
4. Stir in the zucchini slices and sauté for a few minutes, keeping the heat at medium-high.
5. Season with the oregano (or marjoram), ground black pepper and salt; mix in.
6. Keep sautéing the zucchini and onion until the texture looks almost cooked through.
7. Reduce the heat, push the vegetables to the side of the pan to make space, and break in the eggs.
8. Increase the heat again to medium and let the egg whites turn white; start to scramble and mix the eggs into the zucchini-onion mixture at the same time.
9. Once everything looks cooked, turn off the heat and sprinkle with the freshly chopped parsley.
10. Serve hot.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
- The stated serving size of 2 is a smaller portion, intended to be served alongside a side salad and bread. For larger appetites or without sides, the source notes the base recipe is closer to a single serving.
- Yellow zucchini squash was used for color in the source's photos, but regular green zucchini works the same — only the color differs.
- Optional additions the source suggests: bacon, ham, or sliced sausage (meats); shredded cheddar/mozzarella, feta, goat cheese or cottage cheese (dairy); milk whisked into the eggs before cooking; quail eggs in place of chicken eggs; or vegetables like tomatoes, spinach, mushroom, asparagus, peppers, zucchini flowers, or tofu.
- To peel or not to peel: the source doesn't peel small, young, well-rinsed zucchini, but recommends peeling and removing the fibrous core of large zucchini. Similarly, seeds can be left in young/small zucchini but should be removed from older, larger ones.
- Storage: keep leftovers in an airtight (ideally glass) container in the fridge; can also be frozen in batches for a few months.
