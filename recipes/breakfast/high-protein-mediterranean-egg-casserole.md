# High-Protein Mediterranean Egg Casserole

## Description
A light, fluffy, almost custard-like vegetarian egg casserole built on a Mediterranean-Diet-friendly base of sautéed peppers, onions and spinach, with cottage cheese blended in for extra protein alongside parmesan and mozzarella.

## Ingredients
_(for 8 servings — see §8 for scaling)_

- 1 1/2 tablespoons extra virgin olive oil
- 3/4 cup red bell pepper, diced
- 1/2 cup yellow onion, diced
- 5 ounces fresh spinach, roughly chopped
- 12 medium eggs
- 2 cups cottage cheese
- 1 cup shredded Parmesan cheese (divided)
- 1 cup shredded mozzarella cheese
- 4 scallions (green onions), diced (optional)

## Instructions
1. To a large skillet or sauté pan with sides over medium heat, add the olive oil, onions, peppers and scallions (if using). Sauté until crisp-tender, about 3 minutes. Add the spinach and sauté until wilted, about 1 minute. Remove from heat.
2. Place the eggs and cottage cheese in a blender and whip until smooth, about 30 seconds.
3. Pour the cottage cheese mixture into a medium mixing bowl. Add the mozzarella cheese and half of the Parmesan cheese. Season with salt and pepper, to taste, if desired. Stir to combine.
4. Add the vegetable mixture to the cheese mixture and stir to combine.
5. Pour the mixture into a baking pan or casserole dish that's been sprayed with cooking spray. Top with the remaining shredded Parmesan cheese, and season with additional salt and pepper, to taste, if desired.
6. Bake in a preheated 350-degree F oven for 25 to 35 minutes, or until the casserole is cooked through and golden. Allow to cool for a few minutes before slicing and serving.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
- This recipe is flexible on vegetables — swap in broccoli, mushrooms, etc. Meats such as bacon or ham can also be added, but pre-cook vegetables and meats first.
- Store leftovers in an airtight container in the refrigerator or freezer — a great meal-planning recipe.
- The source author used a medium-sized Le Creuset casserole dish measuring 10x8 inches.
