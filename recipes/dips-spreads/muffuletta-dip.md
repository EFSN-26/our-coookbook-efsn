# Muffuletta Dip

## Description
A scoopable version of the classic New Orleans muffuletta sandwich — three cured meats, a plethora of olives, roasted red peppers, and other brined ingredients, baked warm inside a hollowed-out bread bowl. Perfect for a Mardi Gras celebration or any gathering.

## Ingredients
_(for 10-12 servings — see §8 for scaling)_

- 4 garlic cloves, finely chopped
- 1 (8-oz.) package cream cheese, softened
- 1 cup chopped pitted mixed olives
- 1 cup shredded provolone
- 1/2 cup chopped roasted red peppers (store-bought or homemade)
- 1/2 cup chopped giardiniera (store-bought or homemade)
- 1/4 cup shredded Parmesan
- 3 oz mortadella, chopped
- 2 oz coppa, chopped
- 2 oz sweet soppressata, chopped
- 2 Tbsp drained capers
- 1 (1-lb.) sesame or plain round boule
- Crackers, for serving

## Instructions
1. Arrange a rack in the center of the oven; preheat to 350°F. In a large bowl, combine garlic, cream cheese, olives, provolone, red peppers, giardiniera, Parmesan, mortadella, coppa, soppressata, and capers.
2. Leaving a 1" border, cut out the center of the bread boule and scoop out the center to make a bread bowl. Tear or cut the scooped-out bread into 1" pieces. Place bread bowl and bread pieces on a baking sheet. Fill bread bowl with dip.
3. Bake dip, removing bread pieces if they start to get too dark, until warmed through, 30 to 35 minutes.
4. Serve dip warm with toasted bread and crackers alongside.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
If you can find a true muffuletta loaf (what the sandwich is named after), use that; otherwise a sesame or other seed-crusted sourdough boule works well — even a plain loaf works in a pinch (add sesame seeds to the dip if you like). Use the bread pieces carved from the boule as dippers, and once the dip runs low, slice more bread from the bowl itself.
