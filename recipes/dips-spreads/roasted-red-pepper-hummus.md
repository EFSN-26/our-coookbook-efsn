# Roasted Red Pepper Hummus

## Description
Chickpeas blended with fire-roasted red bell peppers, garlic, tahini, and smoked paprika for a smoky-sweet, silky hummus - a favorite for mezze platters and BBQ sides.

## Ingredients
_(for 6 servings — see §8 for scaling)_

- 1 can (15 oz) chickpeas, drained and rinsed
- 3 roasted red bell peppers, peeled and seeded
- 2 garlic cloves, minced
- 1/4 cup tahini
- 3 tablespoons extra virgin olive oil, plus more for drizzling
- 2 tablespoons lemon juice
- 1 teaspoon ground cumin
- 1/2 teaspoon smoked paprika, plus more for garnish
- 1/4 teaspoon cayenne pepper, optional, for heat
- salt and freshly ground black pepper, to taste

## Instructions
1. If using fresh peppers: place directly over an open flame on a gas stove or under a broiler, turning occasionally until the skins are blackened and blistered (about 10-15 minutes). Transfer to a bowl, cover, and let steam for 5 minutes. Once cool enough to handle, peel off the charred skins, remove the seeds, and roughly chop.
2. In a food processor or blender, combine chickpeas, roasted red peppers, garlic, tahini, olive oil, lemon juice, cumin, smoked paprika, and cayenne pepper. Blend until smooth, scraping down the sides as needed.
3. If the hummus is too thick, add 1-2 tablespoons of cold water or more olive oil to reach the desired consistency.
4. Add salt and black pepper, then blend again. Taste and adjust seasoning - more lemon juice for brightness, more smoked paprika for depth, or an extra drizzle of olive oil for richness.
5. Transfer to a serving bowl, smoothing the top. Drizzle with olive oil, sprinkle with smoked paprika, and add optional garnishes like fresh parsley or toasted pine nuts.
6. Serve with warm pita, fresh vegetables, or crunchy crackers.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
- Let the peppers blister and blacken fully before peeling - this is where the smoky flavor develops. High-quality jarred roasted peppers can be substituted if short on time.
- Blend longer than you think for the smoothest texture.
- A splash of ice-cold water helps create a fluffier consistency without adding extra oil.
- Keeps in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to five days; flavor improves as it sits.
