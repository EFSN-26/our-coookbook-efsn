# Sweet Potato Hummus

## Description
Traditional chickpea hummus with roasted or boiled sweet potato blended in for natural sweetness, paired with garlic, tahini, and paprika. The source describes it as gluten-free and vegan.

## Ingredients
_(for 4 servings — see §8 for scaling)_

- 1 small sweet potato
- 1 can chickpeas
- 2 garlic cloves
- 1 teaspoon paprika (or smoked paprika, for a smoky flavor)
- 1/2 lemon
- 1/4 cup tahini
- 2 tablespoons olive oil
- salt and pepper

## Instructions
1. Cook the sweet potato: to boil, cut into cubes, place in a saucepan, cover with water, and boil about 20 minutes until tender.
2. Roasting option: poke the sweet potato with a fork, wrap in foil, and bake in the oven until fork-tender. Let cool.
3. Combine tahini, cooked chickpeas (reserving some for garnish), sweet potato chunks, and garlic in a food processor. Add paprika. Blend until smooth and creamy, scraping the sides as needed.
4. Taste and adjust the flavors - add more garlic, tahini, or salt if desired.
5. Transfer to a bowl or plate. Garnish with reserved chickpeas, a sprinkle of paprika, and cilantro.
6. Serve with warm pita bread, vegetables, or crackers.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
- For the smoothest hummus, boil chickpeas with a pinch of baking soda to help tenderize them (if not using canned).
- A high-powered blender or food processor helps achieve the creamiest texture.
- Adding a little water while blending makes the hummus airier and more spreadable.
- Keeps in an airtight container in the refrigerator for 4-5 days, or in the freezer for up to four months (thaw overnight in the fridge).
