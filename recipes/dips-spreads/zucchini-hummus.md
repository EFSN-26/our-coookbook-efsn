# Zucchini Hummus

## Description
A raw, no-cook dip that uses fresh zucchini instead of chickpeas, blended with tahini, garlic, olive oil, and lemon juice - a light, cool alternative to traditional hummus and a good way to use up extra zucchini. Makes about 2 cups.

## Ingredients
_(for 8 servings — see §8 for scaling)_

- 2 medium zucchini, cut into chunks
- 2 cloves garlic
- 3 tablespoons tahini
- 1-2 tablespoons olive oil
- 1 tablespoon lemon juice
- 1 teaspoon fine sea salt

## Instructions
1. In a food processor or blender, add the zucchini and pulse to chop into small pieces.
2. Optional: scoop the zucchini into a mesh colander and press with the back of a spoon to remove excess water, then return to the processor.
3. Add the garlic, tahini, 1 tablespoon olive oil, lemon juice, and salt, and blend everything together until smooth. Add the second tablespoon of olive oil if desired.
4. Store in the refrigerator until ready to serve.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
- Peeling the zucchini is unnecessary - the skin adds nutrients and color.
- If using larger squash, discard the seeded center and use only the outer part to prevent excess water.
- The dip may release liquid as it rests; stir it back in or drain before serving.
- Leftovers keep in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to a week; freezing is possible but reduces creaminess.
