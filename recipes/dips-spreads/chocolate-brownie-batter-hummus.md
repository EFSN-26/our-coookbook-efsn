# Chocolate Brownie Batter Hummus

## Description
A black bean hummus that tastes like fudgy brownie batter - a simple dessert dip that satisfies a sweet tooth, served with fruit, pretzels, or graham crackers. Recipe yields about 2 cups; total time includes a 1-hour chill.

## Ingredients
_(for 12 servings — see §8 for scaling)_

- 1 can (15 oz / 425 g) black beans, drained
- 1/4 cup (60 g) tahini
- 4 tablespoons unsweetened cocoa powder
- 1/3 cup (113 g) maple syrup
- 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
- 1/2 cup (80 g) mini chocolate chips, optional
- a pinch of sea salt, optional topping

## Instructions
1. Add the drained black beans, tahini, cocoa powder, maple syrup, and vanilla extract to the bowl of a food processor or high-speed blender.
2. Blend or pulse until the ingredients are well-combined and the mixture is smooth.
3. Stir in the mini chocolate chips and top with sea salt, if using.
4. Allow to chill for 1 hour.
5. Serve with graham crackers, animal crackers, cookies, or fresh fruit.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
- Black beans are used for their mild flavor; chickpeas can be substituted for a more traditional hummus texture, though the result will be thicker.
- Tahini can be swapped for another nut butter (e.g. almond butter) if unavailable, which will change the flavor.
- Keeps in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 6 days, or in the freezer for up to 2 months.
