# Spicy Harissa Hummus

## Description
Classic chickpea hummus kicked up with harissa paste - a Tunisian chile paste blended with garlic, caraway, coriander, and olive oil - for a spicy, creamy dip.

## Ingredients
_(for 6 servings — see §8 for scaling)_

- 1 can low-sodium chickpeas, drained and rinsed
- 1 garlic clove, finely chopped
- 1/4 cup tahini
- 3 tablespoons lemon juice, plus more to taste
- 1/4 cup harissa paste, plus more to garnish
- 1 teaspoon tomato paste
- 3 tablespoons olive oil
- 1-2 tablespoons ice water
- 1 1/2 teaspoons kosher salt, plus more to taste
- freshly ground black pepper, to taste

## Instructions
1. Process chickpeas, garlic, tahini, lemon juice, harissa, tomato paste, and olive oil until smooth.
2. Add ice water 1 tablespoon at a time until you reach the consistency you want.
3. Blend in the salt.
4. Taste, and add additional salt and/or lemon juice as desired.
5. Garnish with a spoonful of harissa on top.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
- Harissa paste and tahini vary in runniness by brand - you may need more or less water depending on your ingredients; if both are runny, consider reducing the lemon juice slightly.
- Stir tahini well before using, since it tends to separate.
- For a super-smooth hummus, peel the chickpeas before processing.
- Keeps in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to a week; can be frozen for up to two months (texture may soften slightly - re-blend with a splash of water after thawing).
