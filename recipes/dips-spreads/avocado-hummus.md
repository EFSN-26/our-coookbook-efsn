# Avocado Hummus

## Description
This ultra-creamy avocado hummus blends chickpeas with ripe avocado, tahini, garlic, and lemon for a fresh, modern twist on a classic dip - smooth, rich, and good with pita or vegetables.

## Ingredients
_(for 6 servings — see §8 for scaling)_

- 1 can (15 oz) chickpeas, drained and rinsed
- 2 ripe avocados, pitted and peeled
- 2 garlic cloves, minced
- 1/4 cup tahini
- 3 tablespoon fresh lemon juice
- 2 tablespoon extra virgin olive oil, plus more for drizzling
- 1 teaspoon ground cumin
- salt and freshly ground black pepper, to taste
- black and white sesame seeds, optional, for topping

## Instructions
1. Drain and rinse chickpeas, pit and peel avocados, and gather all ingredients.
2. In a food processor, combine chickpeas, avocado, garlic, tahini, lemon juice, 2 tablespoons olive oil, and cumin. Process until smooth and creamy.
3. Taste and adjust with salt and black pepper as needed. Blend again to incorporate.
4. Transfer to a serving bowl, smoothing the top for a polished presentation.
5. Drizzle with olive oil and sprinkle with sesame seeds for added texture.
6. Serve immediately with pita, fresh vegetables, or crackers.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
- For the smoothest, silkiest hummus, peel the chickpeas before blending, or blend an extra minute or two for a creamier finish.
- Use avocados that are ripe (soft but not mushy) - underripe avocados won't blend smoothly, and overripe ones can taste bitter.
- To store, press plastic wrap directly onto the surface of the hummus before sealing in an airtight container; it keeps in the fridge for up to a day this way. A thin layer of olive oil on top also helps prevent oxidation for longer storage.
