# Tzatziki

## Description
A refreshing Greek yogurt-based dip made with grated cucumber, garlic, olive oil and dill — a cooling staple of Mediterranean cuisine that pairs with grilled meats, pita or roasted vegetables. [Source](https://www.gamintraveler.com/2026/06/06/how-to-make-homemade-tzatziki/)

## Ingredients
_(servings/yield not specified by source)_

- 2 cups (500 grams) Greek yogurt (full-fat for creaminess, but low-fat or non-fat can be used)
- 1 large cucumber (preferably seedless, such as English cucumber)
- 3 to 4 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 tablespoon fresh dill, chopped (mint, or a combination of dill and mint, can also be used)
- 1 tablespoon fresh lemon juice (about half a lemon)
- 1 tablespoon extra virgin olive oil, plus more for drizzling before serving
- salt, to taste
- a dash of white vinegar, for extra tang (optional)

## Instructions
1. Peel the cucumber (optional, for a smoother texture) and grate it using the large holes of a box grater. Place the grated cucumber in a clean kitchen towel or cheesecloth, gather the ends, and squeeze out as much water as possible — this step is crucial to prevent watery tzatziki.
2. In a medium bowl, combine the yogurt with the grated, drained cucumber. Stir in the minced garlic and chopped dill (or mint). Start with a little garlic and add more to taste.
3. Add the lemon juice, olive oil and a pinch of salt. Mix well. Taste and adjust the seasoning with more salt, lemon juice, or a dash of vinegar if needed.
4. Cover the bowl with plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes to allow the flavors to meld — tzatziki tastes best served chilled.
5. Before serving, drizzle a little olive oil on top and garnish with a sprig of dill or a few slices of cucumber. Serve with warm pita bread, as a side for grilled meats like souvlaki or gyros, or as a dip for vegetables.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
- For a thicker tzatziki, use strained Greek yogurt; if using regular yogurt, strain it through cheesecloth for a few hours first.
- Garlic flavor intensifies as it sits — use slightly less if making ahead of time and adjust later.
- Mint, or a combination of dill and mint, works well as an alternative to dill alone.
- Tzatziki can be made a day ahead; the flavors blend even more beautifully overnight.
- Draining the cucumber is the most time-consuming step (20 to 30 minutes) but ensures a thick, creamy result; once that's done, mixing everything together takes no more than 10 minutes. The source estimates the whole process, including chilling, takes under 45 minutes from start to finish.
- A typical 2-tablespoon serving contains approximately 30-50 calories, depending on the type of yogurt and amount of olive oil used.
- Also works as a sandwich, wrap or burger spread in place of mayonnaise.
