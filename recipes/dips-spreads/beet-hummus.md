# Beet Hummus

## Description
Roasted or boiled beets blend with chickpeas, tahini, garlic, and a touch of sesame oil for a vibrant, earthy-sweet hummus with a striking pink color. The source notes this vegan hummus is also rich in fiber, vitamin C, folate, and potassium from the beets.

## Ingredients
_(for 6 servings — see §8 for scaling)_

- 2 small beets (or 1 medium beet), cooked until tender
- 1 can (15 oz) chickpeas, drained and rinsed
- 3 tablespoons tahini
- juice of 1 lemon
- 1-2 garlic cloves, minced
- 1-2 teaspoons sesame oil
- 1/2 teaspoon salt
- sesame seeds, optional
- flaky sea salt, optional
- black pepper, optional
- parsley, optional

## Instructions
1. Cook the beets by boiling or roasting until tender. To boil: cut into quarters, cover with water in a saucepan, bring to a boil, then simmer 30-45 minutes until fork-tender. To roast: wrap each beet in foil with a drizzle of olive oil and roast at 204°C (400°F) for 45-60 minutes until fork-tender.
2. In a food processor, blend the cooked beets, chickpeas, tahini, lemon juice, garlic, sesame oil, and salt until smooth. Add 1-2 tablespoons of water or olive oil if too thick.
3. Taste and adjust seasoning if necessary.
4. Serve on a plate topped with chickpeas, sesame seeds, olive oil, flaky salt, black pepper, and parsley.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
- Sesame oil is the source's stated "secret ingredient" - it enhances the nutty undertones of the tahini.
- Store leftovers in an airtight container in the refrigerator for 3-4 days; the hummus thickens over time, so stir in a little water or olive oil to loosen before serving.
