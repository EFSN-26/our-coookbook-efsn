# Blue Cheese Dressing

## Description
A rich and creamy homemade blue cheese dressing made from pantry staples — tangy, chunky, and ready in minutes. Perfect for drizzling over wedge salads or as a dip for veggies and wings.

## Ingredients
_(for 12 servings — see §8 for scaling)_

- ½ cup mayonnaise
- ½ cup sour cream
- 3 tablespoons milk
- 1 tablespoon lemon juice, or white wine vinegar
- ½ cup blue cheese, crumbled
- ½ teaspoon sea salt
- ⅛ teaspoon black pepper
- ⅛ teaspoon garlic powder

## Instructions
1. Add all ingredients to a small mixing bowl.
2. Mix well until fully combined.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
- Choose a high-quality, strong-tasting blue cheese — the quality makes a big difference to the end result.
- Decide whether you prefer the dressing chunky with visible crumbles or smoother and creamier, and adjust the amount of mashing or blending accordingly.
- Balance the flavors to taste: add more lemon juice (or white vinegar) for acidity, more mayonnaise or sour cream for creaminess, or more blue cheese for a stronger flavor.
- If the dressing is too thick, thin it out with a little more milk.
- Chill in the refrigerator for at least an hour before serving for the best flavor.
- Variations: swap the mayonnaise for Greek yogurt for a lighter version; add fresh herbs (parsley, chives, or dill), a touch of honey or maple syrup, hot sauce or a pinch of cayenne, crumbled cooked bacon, or chipotle peppers in adobo sauce.
- Store in an airtight container or jar for up to 7 days. Does not freeze well.
