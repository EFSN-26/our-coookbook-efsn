# Tzatziki Recipe

## Description
Enjoy a timeless Greek favorite with this refreshing and creamy tzatziki recipe. The combination of creamy yogurt, crisp cucumbers, garlic, and fresh dill makes a classic Greek sauce that is a perfect companion to grilled meats, pita bread, or fresh vegetables.

## Ingredients
_(for 4 servings)_

- 1 cup Greek yogurt, plain
- 5 ounces cucumber, grated and excess liquid squeezed out
- 2 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 tablespoon fresh dill, chopped
- 1 tablespoon lemon juice, freshly squeezed
- salt and black pepper, to taste
- 1 tablespoon Greek extra virgin olive oil

## Instructions
1. Grate the cucumber using a box grater or food processor.
2. Squeeze out excess liquid from the grated cucumber using a clean kitchen towel or paper towels.
3. In a mixing bowl, combine the Greek yogurt, grated cucumber, minced garlic, fresh dill, and lemon juice.
4. Mix well until all ingredients are evenly incorporated.
5. Season the tzatziki with salt and pepper to taste. Adjust seasoning as needed.
6. Cover the bowl and refrigerate the tzatziki for at least 1 hour to allow the flavors to meld together. Before serving, drizzle with the olive oil.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
- Drain the cucumber well — squeezing firmly in paper towels prevents a watery tzatziki sauce.
- Feel free to add parsley, mint, or a little ground cumin to make the recipe your own.
- Chilling the sauce for an hour before serving lets the flavors meld together.
- If the sauce separates, just give it a stir before serving.
- Substitutions: swap dill for fresh mint; swap some Greek yogurt for sour cream or goat yogurt; strain the yogurt through a sieve first for a thicker result; add more garlic for a stronger flavor; swap Greek yogurt for dairy-free yogurt for a vegan version.
- Storage: keep leftovers in an airtight container in the refrigerator and eat within 2-3 days. Do not freeze — it will separate and get watery.
- Serving ideas: as a condiment with baked salmon, Italian steak, or Mediterranean chicken orzo bowls; in chicken gyros; as a dip with chips, crackers, or pretzels; or as a sandwich spread in place of mayonnaise.
