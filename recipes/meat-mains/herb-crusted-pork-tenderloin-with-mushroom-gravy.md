# Herb Crusted Pork Tenderloin With Mushroom Gravy

## Description
Juicy, tender pork tenderloin crusted with a savory blend of herbs, seared to perfection and topped with a rich, creamy mushroom gravy. The gravy is thickened with heavy cream instead of flour, keeping it keto and low-carb while staying velvety and flavorful.

## Ingredients
_(for 6 servings)_

- 2 pounds pork tenderloin
- 1 pound baby bella mushrooms, sliced
- 1 cup chicken stock
- 1/2 cup heavy cream
- 1/2 cup white wine (Pinot Grigio recommended)
- 3 cloves garlic, minced
- 3 tablespoons olive oil
- 2 tablespoons butter
- 2 tablespoons Herbs de Provence

## Instructions
1. Preheat oven to 400°F.
2. Heat an oven-proof pan or skillet to medium-high heat. Drizzle olive oil over the pork and rub the Herbs de Provence all over the tenderloin. Once the pan is hot, sear all sides, then put in the oven to finish cooking for about 20 minutes or until it reaches an internal temperature of 145°F when checked with a meat thermometer.
3. Remove from the pot and tent with foil. Allow to rest for 10 minutes while you make the mushroom gravy.
4. In the same pot, heat to medium on the stovetop and add the butter, olive oil and mushrooms. Sauté until they have some good color, about 5 minutes.
5. Push the mushrooms to one side of the pan and add the garlic. Sauté for about 20 seconds, careful not to burn. Deglaze the pan with white wine, scraping up the brown bits.
6. Add the chicken stock and cook for another 5 minutes or until the liquid has reduced. Stir in the heavy cream and continue cooking while you slice the pork into medallions.
7. Serve the pork with the mushroom gravy over the top and enjoy.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
- Use a meat thermometer to make sure the internal temperature hits 145°F.
- Don't skip the sear — it helps lock in the juices.
- Let the pork rest under foil for at least 10 minutes so the juices stay in the meat, not on the cutting board.
- Stir the heavy cream in last so it doesn't curdle from high heat.
- Slice the pork into medallions just before serving so it stays warm and juicy.
- Substitutions: swap baby bellas for cremini or shiitake mushrooms for an earthier flavor; add fresh thyme or rosemary to the gravy; stir in a spoonful of Dijon mustard for tang; add finely chopped shallots with the garlic; add crushed red pepper flakes to the herb mix for heat.
- One reader reported success substituting red wine for white wine and cream cheese for heavy cream.
- Storage: refrigerate leftover pork and gravy in an airtight container for up to 3 days. Freeze sliced pork and cooled gravy separately for up to 2 months; thaw in the fridge overnight before reheating.
- Serving suggestions: cheesy mashed cauliflower, shredded Brussels sprouts, creamed spinach, grilled broccolini, or green beans almondine.
