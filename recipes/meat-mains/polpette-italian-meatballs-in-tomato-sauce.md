# The Best Meatballs Ever! (Italian Polpette Recipe)

## Description
Elena Davis's nonna Laura's family recipe for tender, melt-in-your-mouth Italian meatballs (polpette), made with milk-soaked bread for softness and simmered raw directly in a simple onion-and-tomato sugo rather than being fried or baked first.

## Ingredients
_(for 4-6 servings)_

#### Polpette (meatballs)
- 1 pound 80-85% lean ground beef
- 2 slices stale bread
- 1/2 cup milk
- 1/2 cup Parmigiano Reggiano, finely grated
- 1 egg
- 3 tablespoons fresh minced parsley
- 1 tablespoon fresh minced garlic, optional
- Salt and pepper, to taste
- Breadcrumbs, for rolling the meatballs by hand
- Extra Parmigiano, for serving

#### Sugo (sauce)
- 1 large can (28 oz) good-quality crushed tomatoes
- 1/2 onion, diced
- 2 tablespoons extra virgin olive oil
- 5 fresh basil leaves, whole

## Instructions

### Make the sugo
1. Pour the olive oil into a large frying pan and brown the onion over medium heat, about 7 minutes. Add the crushed tomatoes and simmer for 10-15 minutes. Turn off the heat and cover with a lid.

### Make the polpette
2. Place the bread and milk in a large mixing bowl and let sit for 2 minutes, until soft.
3. Add the egg to the bread and milk mixture and mix to combine.
4. Add the ground meat, parsley, Parmigiano, salt, and pepper (and garlic, if using). Mix well with your hands.
5. Take a spoonful of the meatball mixture and gently roll it between your hands to form a 1 1/2-inch meatball, using breadcrumbs to prevent sticking. Set the prepared meatballs on a cookie sheet and cover with plastic wrap.
6. Plunge the meatballs into the pot with the tomato sauce. Turn the heat to medium-low. Cook for 20 minutes, or until the meatballs are fully cooked in the center. Once finished cooking, stir in the basil, cover with a lid, and turn off the heat.
7. Cook your favorite pasta or gnocchi, as directed by the package, to serve with the meatballs, or serve them on their own with crusty bread. Generously top with Parmigiano and serve hot.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
- Soaking the bread in milk is the key to soft, tender meatballs — it puffs up when cooked, creating little air pockets that soften the mixture.
- The meatballs are cooked raw directly in the tomato sauce rather than fried or baked first — this saves a pan, and the juices flavor the sauce while the sauce flavors the meatballs, which is the traditional way it's done in Italy, per the source.
- Meat options: the source uses 80-85% lean ground beef, but half beef/half ground pork, or Italian sausage with the casing removed, can also be used.
- Milk can be omitted if needed — use water or broth as a substitute liquid for binding.
- Gluten-free adaptation: use a slice of gluten-free bread and a gluten-free breadcrumb variety for rolling.
- Storage: refrigerate in an airtight container for up to 3-4 days.
- Freezing: cool completely, portion as desired, use freezer-safe containers or bags removing excess air, label with the date, and freeze for up to 3 months. Thaw in the fridge overnight and reheat gently on the stove. Meatballs can also be frozen raw (uncooked) and added directly to sauce or broth when ready to use.
- Approximate nutrition per the source (per serving): 541 kcal, 25g carbohydrates, 31g protein, 36g fat.
