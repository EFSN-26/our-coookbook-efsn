# Abbacchio al Forno (Italian Roast Lamb)

## Description
A traditional Roman dish deeply rooted in Italian culinary history, especially enjoyed during Easter and festive family gatherings. Young lamb is seasoned simply with garlic, rosemary, sage, and lemon zest, then roasted until golden and aromatic.

## Ingredients
_(for UNVERIFIED servings — see §8 for scaling)_

- 2-3 lbs lamb (leg or shoulder, cut into pieces)
- 4 cloves garlic, minced
- 2 tablespoons fresh rosemary, chopped
- 2 tablespoons fresh sage, chopped
- Zest of 1 lemon
- 1/4 cup olive oil
- 1/2 cup white wine
- Salt and black pepper, to taste
- Optional: 2 anchovy fillets, mashed (for added depth of flavor)

## Instructions
1. **Marinate the lamb:** In a small bowl, combine the garlic, rosemary, sage, lemon zest, olive oil, and mashed anchovy fillets (if using). Rub the mixture over the lamb pieces, coating well, and season generously with salt and black pepper. Cover and marinate for at least 1-2 hours, or preferably overnight in the refrigerator.
2. **Roast the lamb:** Preheat the oven to 400°F (200°C). Place the marinated lamb in a roasting pan and pour the white wine around the meat. Roast for 20 minutes, then reduce the temperature to 350°F (175°C) and continue roasting for an additional 40-50 minutes, depending on the thickness of the lamb. Baste occasionally with the pan juices.
3. **Check for doneness:** The lamb is done when its internal temperature reaches about 145°F (63°C) for medium-rare or 160°F (71°C) for medium. Remove from the oven and let rest for 10-15 minutes before serving.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
Lamb shoulder or leg both become tender and flavorful when roasted. Marinating overnight gives the deepest flavor. A bit of mashed anchovy in the marinade adds umami without tasting fishy. Baste with pan juices while roasting to keep the meat moist — add a splash of water or broth to the pan if it starts to dry out. Traditionally served with potatoes roasted alongside the lamb.
