# Costolette di Maiale ai Funghi (Pork Chops with Mushroom Cream Sauce)

## Description
A classic Emilia-Romagna dish of thin-cut pork chops browned and then simmered briefly in a rich mushroom cream sauce — a pork adaptation of an Italian veal chop classic, built around the author's favorite mushroom cream sauce.

## Ingredients
_(for 4 servings)_

- 4 thin-cut rib pork chops, bone in
- 250 g (1/2 lb) fresh mushrooms (oyster mushrooms are particularly nice), roughly chopped
- 1-2 shallots, finely chopped
- 250 ml (1 cup) cream, or as much as you need to make ample sauce
- Olive oil
- Butter
- Flour
- A handful of fresh parsley, finely chopped
- Salt and pepper

## Instructions
1. Dry the chops well with paper towels, dust them with flour, and brown them in olive oil over a moderate flame until nice and golden brown on both sides. Remove them from the skillet and set aside.
2. Add the mushrooms to the skillet and sauté them in the remaining oil. Season the mushrooms with a bit of salt and pepper as soon as you've added them to encourage them to exude their liquid. Let all of that liquid evaporate, then continue sautéing until the mushrooms start to brown nicely.
3. Add a dab of butter and the chopped shallot to the mushrooms. Allow the shallot to sauté lightly, literally for just 30 seconds or so.
4. Add a ladleful of homemade broth to the mushrooms, and let that evaporate completely as well — this gives an extra layer of savory flavor, especially nice if using mild cultivated mushrooms. If using wild chanterelles or cèpes (porcini), this step can be skipped to maintain the pure mushroom flavor. Now add the cream and let it reduce down over fairly high heat until the fat has separated out.
5. At this point, the dish can be set aside until ready to serve. A few minutes before serving, turn the heat back on to a moderate flame and add the chops back into the skillet on top of the cream sauce, along with any accumulated juices. Loosen the sauce with a bit of milk or water, then turn the chops in the sauce until well coated.
6. Continue cooking for a few minutes, just long enough to warm the chops through and thicken the sauce again to a consistency just thick enough to coat the back of a spoon, adding a bit of finely chopped parsley as they cook.
7. Serve the pork chops with mushrooms immediately, topped with some more finely chopped parsley.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
- This recipe is a modified version of the traditional one, which calls for braising the chops in the sauce. Most modern pork is raised lean, and rib/loin chops are too lean for braising, so browning them and returning them to the pan just long enough to warm through keeps the pork flavorful without drying out.
- Use thin-cut chops so they cook in the short time called for; thin-cut chops are also more typically Italian, allowing a nice balance of flavors between the meat and its condiment.
- Just about any mushroom variety works, even plain cultivated button mushrooms, though wild mushrooms give the dish more character. The author used a mix of oyster mushrooms and chiodini/pioppini mushrooms. Dried mushrooms can also be used for a more intense mushroom flavor, or mix some dried porcini and their soaking liquid with cultivated mushrooms to mimic the flavor of fresh porcini.
- The recipe was inspired by a veal chop recipe from Giuliano Bugialli's "Foods of Italy," which called for browning the veal chops in butter rather than oil and using fresh porcini mushrooms; the dish comes from Emilia-Romagna.
- Reader tip: soaking an ounce of dried porcini mushrooms in a cup of water, then chopping the soaked mushrooms and adding them and the soaking liquid in place of the stock, works very well.
