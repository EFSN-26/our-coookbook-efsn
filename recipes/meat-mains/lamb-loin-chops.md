# Lamb Loin Chops

## Description
Lamb loin chops are easy to make and a less expensive cut with the same great flavor as traditional lamb chops — a mini porterhouse-like steak that's simple to pan-sear at home.

## Ingredients
_(for 3 servings)_

### Garlic oil
- 3 cloves garlic, minced
- 1/4 cup avocado or olive oil
- Kosher salt & black pepper
- red pepper flakes, optional

### Chops
- 7-8 lamb loin chops
- Kosher salt & black pepper
- fresh rosemary or thyme
- 1 TBS butter

## Instructions
1. Combine all ingredients for the garlic oil in a small bowl or ramekin and let it stand for 20-30 minutes for the flavors to mingle. If in a rush, use the back of a spoon to "mash" the garlic into the oil a bit.
2. Pull the chops from the fridge, place in a shallow dish or plate, pat dry with paper towels and coat with the garlic oil. Let sit for 30 minutes at room temp.
3. When ready to cook, heat a heavy-bottomed pan or cast iron pan. Once searing hot, add in a bit of avocado oil/olive oil or some of the garlic oil you made (without the garlic pieces, or they will burn).
4. Place chops in the pan one by one and cook until seared on one side, flip and cook to your desired temperature (see notes).
5. A couple minutes after flipping the chops, add the butter and fresh herbs (if using) and baste the tops using a spoon. Add more butter if necessary.
6. Allow the chops to rest for 5-8 minutes before enjoying.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
7-8 lamb loin chops may serve 2 or 3 depending on their size and the person — to be safe, assume 3-4 chops per person. For fresh herbs, feel free to add whole sprigs; you can also chop them and use as garnish. Always let the meat rest at least 5-8 minutes — the temperature will continue to rise. For medium-rare, look for 135°F internal; for medium, look for 140°F internal. When using a digital thermometer, be careful not to hit the bone, or you'll get a false (higher) temperature read. Don't cook chops straight from the fridge — let them come up to room temperature for about 20 minutes. Preheat the pan well so the oil is shimmering before searing. For the grill: preheat to about 375°F and grill 3-4 minutes per side, or until 130-135°F internally for medium-rare; rest 5 minutes before serving. Store cooked lamb chops in the fridge up to 4 days; enjoy cold or reheat in the microwave or on the stovetop.
