# Batata Rosti com Carne-Seca

## Description
A crisp, golden potato rosti filled with shredded Brazilian dried beef (carne-seca). The potato forms a crunchy crust thanks to butter, while the savory filling is made by sautéing onion and folding in the shredded beef. Served as a main course for lunch or dinner, or as an afternoon snack.

## Ingredients
_(for 4 servings)_

**Carne-seca filling**
- 1/2 onion, chopped
- 2 tablespoons olive oil
- salt, to taste
- 350 grams carne-seca (dried/salted beef)
- parsley, to taste
- black pepper, to taste

**Potato rosti**
- 1 kilogram Asterix or white potatoes
- 1 pinch salt
- 2 tablespoons olive oil
- 2 tablespoons butter
- extra butter, for releasing the rosti from the pan

## Instructions
1. Filling: Desalt the carne-seca overnight, changing the water once or twice. Pressure-cook until very tender and easy to shred; shred it, removing excess fat if desired. (To skip this step, buy carne-seca that's already desalted, cooked and shredded.)
2. In a skillet, heat 2 tablespoons olive oil with the chopped onion and a pinch of salt and pepper. Sauté the onion, add the beef and cook 2 to 3 minutes. Turn off the heat and stir in parsley or another fresh herb. Set aside.
3. Rosti: Peel and grate the potatoes. Place the grated potato in a bowl of water to keep it from oxidizing.
4. In a medium skillet, add 1 tablespoon olive oil and 1 tablespoon butter and melt over low heat.
5. Drain the potatoes, place them in the center of a clean cloth, gather into a bundle and squeeze out all the water.
6. Once the butter has melted, add half the potatoes to the skillet. Spread the filling over them, then top with a second layer of potatoes, using a wooden spoon to tidy the edges.
7. Cover and cook over low heat about 10 minutes. Uncover, raise the heat and cook — watching closely so it doesn't burn — to form a crust.
8. After about 5 minutes it should be ready to flip. Add a little butter around the edges to help release it. Using a plate larger than the skillet and a dish towel to protect your hands, flip the rosti.
9. Add another tablespoon each of olive oil and butter to the skillet, let melt, and return the rosti to brown the other side. Cook about 5 minutes over medium-high heat for a golden crust, adding a little butter at the edges again to help release it.
10. Turn the rosti out onto a plate and garnish with chives, sprouts or fresh herbs. Serve with an arugula salad dressed with olive oil, salt, pepper and a touch of lemon or vinegar.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
Keep grated potatoes in water so they don't discolor, and squeeze them well right before using. Keep the filling away from the edges so the rosti seals when folded. Butter is your best ally for flipping and unmolding — add a little around the pan edges before each stage. Other fillings that work well: chicken with requeijão, or cheese. The episode pairs the rosti with a pink lemonade made from lime juice blended with red berries (strawberries and blueberries).
