# Sausage Stuffed Butternut Squash

## Description
A comforting, hearty roasted butternut squash stuffed with a savory-sweet filling of sausage, ground pork, spinach, apple, almonds, chestnuts and dried cranberries, finished with a splash of brandy. [Source](https://www.giangiskitchen.com/sausage-stuffed-butternut-squash/)

## Ingredients
_(for 2 servings — see §8 for scaling)_

- 1 butternut squash, medium to large
- 1 tablespoon olive oil
- 2 pork sausage with hatch green chiles, or Italian spicy sausage
- 1/2 pound ground pork
- 1/2 yellow onion, diced
- 2 garlic cloves, minced
- 3 cups baby spinach
- 1 apple, peeled, cored and chopped
- 1/2 teaspoon fresh rosemary, finely chopped
- 2 tablespoons Brandy
- 1/4 cup almond, chopped
- 1/2 cup steamed chestnuts, chopped
- 1/3 cup dried cranberries
- salt and pepper, to taste

## Instructions
1. Preheat the oven to 400°F.
2. With a very sharp knife, slice the butternut squash in half lengthwise. Scoop the seeds out with the help of a spoon. Lightly oil the butternut squash and season generously with salt and pepper.
3. Lay the butternut squash cut side down on a baking sheet, and bake for 40 to 55 minutes.
4. In a skillet, add the sausage and the ground pork over medium heat. Use a spatula to break up the sausage and cook until slightly browned.
5. Add the onion, garlic and spinach and stir for 3 to 4 minutes until the spinach is wilted.
6. Add the apples, rosemary, almond, cranberries and chestnut and cook until the apples have softened. Add the brandy and cook over medium heat until all the liquid has evaporated. Set the stuffing aside.
7. When the butternut squash halves are cooked, remove them from the oven, flip them over and remove some of the flesh to make room for the filling. Save the removed flesh to make mashed squash later.
8. Turn on the broiler. Fill the butternut squash with the apple sausage filling and place it under the broiler, about 6 inches away from the heat source, and broil for up to 5 minutes or until golden on top.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
- Use parchment paper or aluminum foil on the baking sheet to prevent the squash from sticking while it roasts.
- If the butternut squash is too hard to cut, boil it in hot water for a couple of minutes first to soften the skin.
- Save the scooped-out flesh to make butternut squash soup or add it to mashed potatoes.
- Baking time depends on your oven and altitude — adjust as needed.
- Reheat leftovers in a preheated oven at 350°F for about 30 minutes, or until heated through. Refrigerate leftovers in an airtight container for up to 3 days.
- Variations suggested by the source: kale instead of spinach; raisins or dried cherries instead of cranberries; shallots instead of yellow onion; ground turkey or ground beef instead of the pork; fresh sage instead of rosemary; a sprinkle of nutmeg/cinnamon, parmesan or Gruyère, or sautéed mushrooms.
