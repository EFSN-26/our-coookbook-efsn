# Slow Cooker Lamb Shanks With Red Wine Gravy

## Description
Fall-apart tender lamb shanks braised low and slow in a rich red wine gravy, with just minutes of hands-on prep. The slow cooker transforms a tough cut of meat into a luxurious, melt-in-the-mouth dish worthy of a special occasion.

## Ingredients
_(for 2 generous portions)_

- 2 lamb shanks, approx. 400g each
- 1 tablespoon neutral oil
- 1 small onion, roughly chopped
- 2 garlic cloves, roughly chopped
- 1 carrot, peeled and roughly chopped
- 1 tablespoon flour
- 300 ml (1⅓ cups) full-bodied red wine
- 400 ml (1⅔ cups) chicken, beef, or lamb stock
- 2 tablespoons tomato paste
- 2 sprigs rosemary
- 1 bay leaf
- salt and black pepper, to taste

## Instructions
1. Heat oil in a large frying pan. Season lamb shanks with salt and pepper.
2. Brown the lamb shanks on all sides in a hot pan, then transfer them into a slow cooker bowl.
3. To the same pan, add the onions, carrot, and garlic and sauté over medium heat for 6-8 minutes, until starting to brown and soften.
4. Add the flour to the vegetables and stir to coat for 1 minute.
5. Add the tomato paste, pour in the red wine, and stir, scraping the bottom of the pan to deglaze. Add the bay leaf and rosemary sprigs. Stir for a minute, until just starting to thicken.
6. Pour the wine mixture over the lamb shanks. Add the stock — the shanks should be just about submerged in liquid.
7. Cover the slow cooker with a lid. Set it on low for 8 hours.
8. Once cooked, carefully take the lamb shanks out of the slow cooker into a deep dish and cover with foil to keep warm.
9. Spoon off any fat that settles on top of the gravy in the slow cooker and discard it. Strain the red wine gravy into a medium pan, discarding the herbs and vegetable solids. Reduce the sauce to the desired consistency and serve drizzled over the lamb shanks.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
Fresh rosemary may be replaced with a few sprigs of fresh thyme, or 1 teaspoon of dried herbs if no fresh herbs are available. To thicken the sauce quickly without over-reducing it, make a slurry of 1 teaspoon cornstarch and 2 tablespoons water, then stir it into the gravy over low-medium heat. Serve with mashed potatoes, smashed garlic potatoes, creamy polenta, cauliflower mash, or celeriac purée, plus a green side like garlic tenderstem broccoli, minted peas, or garlic roasted green beans. Store leftovers in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 3 days, or freeze with the braising liquid for up to 3 months; reheat gently on the stovetop or in the oven with a splash of stock or water.
