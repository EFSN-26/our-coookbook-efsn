# Porchetta

## Description
A home-friendly take on the classic Italian herb-stuffed pork roast: pork belly rolled around pork loin with garlic, rosemary, thyme, fennel seeds and lemon zest, refrigerated uncovered to dry the skin, then slow-roasted and finished at high heat for crackling-crisp skin. A showstopping centerpiece for holiday dinners or Sunday feasts.

## Ingredients
_(servings not specified by source — see Missing Information)_

- 1 boneless pork belly (about 5-6 pounds), skin on
- 2-3 pounds pork loin
- 2 tablespoons olive oil
- 6 cloves garlic, minced
- 2 tablespoons fresh rosemary, chopped
- 2 tablespoons fresh thyme, chopped
- 1 tablespoon fennel seeds
- zest of 1 lemon
- salt and pepper, to taste
- 1 tablespoon crushed red pepper flakes, optional

## Instructions
1. Lay the pork belly skin-side down on a cutting board. Score the meat side of the belly in a crisscross pattern, being careful not to cut through the skin.
2. In a small bowl, mix the minced garlic, rosemary, thyme, fennel seeds, lemon zest, salt and pepper. Add red pepper flakes if using. Rub the seasoning mixture evenly over the scored meat side of the pork belly. Place the pork loin in the center of the pork belly.
3. Roll the pork belly around the pork loin to form a tight cylinder. Use kitchen twine to tie the roast at 1-inch intervals to secure it.
4. Refrigerate the porchetta uncovered for at least 24 hours to allow the flavors to meld and the skin to dry out for better crackling.
5. Preheat the oven to 325°F (165°C). Place the porchetta on a rack in a roasting pan. Rub the skin with olive oil and sprinkle with additional salt. Roast for about 3 hours, or until the internal temperature reaches 160°F (71°C).
6. Increase the oven temperature to 450°F (230°C) and roast for an additional 30-40 minutes, or until the skin is crisp and golden.
7. Let the porchetta rest for at least 15 minutes before slicing.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
- Leaving the porchetta uncovered in the refrigerator helps dry out the skin, which is crucial for achieving crispy crackling.
- Scoring the meat allows the seasoning to penetrate deeper, enhancing the flavor. Slow roasting at a lower temperature ensures the meat is tender, while the high-temperature finish crisps up the skin.
- Letting the porchetta rest before slicing ensures the juices redistribute, keeping the meat moist.
- Serve with roasted potatoes, grilled seasonal vegetables, sautéed green beans, garlic butter mushrooms, or a fennel and orange salad, or in sandwiches on crusty ciabatta or focaccia.
