# Shortcut Budget Beef Tips With Savory Brown Gravy

## Description
A rich, hearty, savory comfort food dinner: budget-friendly beef chuck roast simmered into tender bite-sized tips in a savory brown gravy, ready with just 7 ingredients. [Source](https://30seconds.com/food/tip/80540/Shortcut-Budget-Beef-Tips-Recipe-With-Savory-Brown-Gravy-7-Ingredients)

## Ingredients
_(for 8 servings)_

- 2 tablespoons olive oil
- 1 onion, chopped
- 1 beef chuck roast (about 2 pounds), cut into small bite-sized pieces
- 10.5 ounces (1 can) low-sodium beef broth
- 10.5 ounces (1 can) French onion soup (condensed)
- 1 tablespoon Worcestershire sauce
- 3 tablespoons cornstarch
- 1/3 cup water
- Salt and black pepper, to taste

## Instructions
1. Season the beef pieces with salt and black pepper, to taste. Heat the olive oil in a large soup pot or dutch oven over medium-high heat. Add the beef in batches (don't crowd the pan) and brown on all sides. Remove to a plate.
2. Add the onion and cook until soft, about 5 minutes.
3. Add the beef back to the pot along with the broth, soup and Worcestershire sauce. Bring to a boil, reduce heat, cover and gently simmer for 1 1/2 to 2 hours, or until the beef is tender.
4. In a small bowl, whisk together the cornstarch and water. Whisk into the beef and cook a minute more, or until it thickens the gravy. Taste and add salt and black pepper, to taste.
5. Serve over mashed potatoes, egg noodles, rice or your favorite grain. Store any leftovers in an airtight container in the fridge. Reheat on low in a saucepot on the stove or in the microwave.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
- For even more flavor, add minced fresh garlic or a bay leaf to the pot while it simmers.
- You could skip the "budget" cut and use ribeye, sirloin or your favorite cut of beef instead of chuck roast.
- Serve with a vegetable or salad for a complete dinner.
