# Homemade Salisbury Steak

## Description
Seasoned ground beef patties, seared and then simmered in a rich mushroom gravy built right in the same pan — a simple trick that captures all the flavor left behind from searing for an unforgettable, comforting dinner.

## Ingredients
_(for 4 servings — see §8 for scaling)_

- 1 lb ground beef
- 1 egg
- 1/2 cup breadcrumbs, plain or seasoned
- 1 packet onion mushroom soup
- 8 ounces mushrooms, sliced
- 2 tablespoons olive oil
- 1/4 cup flour, for thickening the gravy
- 2 cans mushroom gravy
- 1 tablespoon Worcestershire sauce
- Water, follow the instructions on the mushroom gravy cans, adjusting for desired consistency
- salt and pepper to taste

## Instructions
1. In a large bowl, combine the ground beef, egg, onion-mushroom soup mix, Worcestershire sauce, and breadcrumbs until the mixture reaches a firm consistency and holds together.
2. Shape the ground meat mixture into round or oval patties, ensuring they are the same size for consistent cooking.
3. Heat 2 tablespoons of olive oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Sear the patties for approximately 4-5 minutes on each side until they develop a golden brown crust. Set the patties aside.
4. In the same skillet over medium heat, add a tablespoon of oil as well as the sliced mushrooms. Cook them for about 8 minutes or until they soften.
5. Add the mushroom gravy, adjusting the water quantity for your preferred gravy consistency.
6. Season the gravy with salt and pepper, then return the browned patties to the skillet, ensuring they are in a single layer and well-coated with the rich gravy.
7. Reduce the heat to low, cover the skillet, and let it simmer for about 15-20 minutes. This allows the flavors to meld and the patties to absorb the rich, thickened mushroom gravy.
8. Ensure the salisbury steaks are thoroughly cooked by cutting into one to confirm there is no pink in the center. The internal temperature should be at least 160°F (71°C).
9. Carefully transfer the salisbury steaks onto a serving platter.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
- A good sear on the patties adds flavor and locks in juices; keep patties the same thickness for even cooking.
- Cooking the mushrooms in the same skillet used to sear the patties deglazes the pan, incorporating those flavorful browned bits into the gravy.
- Use fresh mushrooms for the best texture and flavor.
- Salisbury steak is essentially seasoned ground beef patties, seared and then smothered in gravy (often with mushrooms) — named after Dr. James Salisbury, dating back to the Civil War era.
- Storage: keep in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3 days.
- Serving suggestions: creamy mashed potatoes, buttered egg noodles, or roasted green beans.
- Note: the source's ingredient list includes 1/4 cup flour "for thickening the gravy," but the printed step-by-step instructions build the gravy from canned mushroom gravy without separately calling for the flour to be used — included here exactly as the source listed it.
