# Maminha com Manteiga Temperada

## Description
A Brazilian TV recipe ("É de Casa") from presenter and cook Talitha Morete: seared maminha steaks (a Brazilian beef cut, roughly equivalent to tri-tip or rump cap) topped with a caramelized onion, bacon, and garlic compound butter, served with vegetables grilled in the same butter. Marked "fácil" (easy) by the source and intended as an impressive weekend lunch dish. This entry is translated from the original Portuguese-language recipe; ingredient names and instructions are translated as faithfully as possible. [Source](https://receitas.globo.com/receitas-da-tv/e-de-casa/maminha-com-manteiga-de-cebola-caramelizada.ghtml)

## Ingredients
_(for 5 servings)_

- 1 piece maminha (Brazilian beef cut, similar to tri-tip/rump cap), cut into thick steaks
- Salt, to taste
- Black pepper, to taste
- 200 g unsalted butter, at room temperature
- 100 g bacon
- 1/2 red onion
- 1 clove garlic
- Fresh parsley, to taste
- 100 g carrot
- 1/2 head broccoli
- 100 g cherry tomatoes
- 150 g potatoes (source lists this ingredient as "batata calabresa" — see note below)

## Instructions
1. Chop the onion and caramelize it. Chop the bacon, fry it, and reserve the rendered fat. Chop the garlic and parsley. Mix the softened butter with the caramelized onion, fried bacon, garlic, and parsley. Roll the mixture into a log using plastic wrap and refrigerate.
2. Season the beef steaks with salt and black pepper, and sear them in a skillet.
3. Cut discs from the chilled seasoned butter, place them on top of the steaks, and brown with a kitchen torch. If you don't have a torch, let the butter come to room temperature and place it on the steaks instead.
4. Grill the carrot, broccoli, cherry tomatoes, and potatoes in the seasoned butter (the rendered bacon fat/butter mixture from the pan). Serve immediately.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
- Talitha Morete is a presenter and cook on the Brazilian television show "É de Casa."
- Maminha is a Brazilian beef cut commonly translated as tri-tip or rump cap/cover, well suited to grilling or searing.
- The source describes this as an easy dish suited for an impressive weekend lunch, best served with simple vegetable accompaniments.
