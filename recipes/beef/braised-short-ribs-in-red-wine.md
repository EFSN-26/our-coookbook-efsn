# Fall-Off-the-Bone Braised Short Ribs

## Description
Short ribs braised in a red wine sauce are fall-off-the-bone deliciousness. The short ribs are browned on the stovetop, then cooked with carrots, onion and garlic in wine and beef stock, and baked low and slow until they almost fall off the bone.

## Ingredients
_(for 6 servings)_

- 3 pounds short ribs
- 2 tablespoons olive oil
- 4 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 onion, chopped
- 1/2 cup chopped carrots
- 2 tablespoons tomato paste
- 750 ml (1 bottle) red wine (cabernet or merlot work well)
- 1 cup beef stock
- 2 bay leaves
- 1 teaspoon dried thyme
- salt and pepper, to taste

## Instructions
1. Season the ribs with salt and pepper. Heat the olive oil in a Dutch oven. Brown the ribs on all sides, working in batches. Remove to a plate.
2. Add the carrots, onion and garlic. Cook about 5 minutes. Add the tomato paste and cook about 30 seconds more.
3. Pour in the wine and beef stock. Add the bay leaves and thyme. Season with salt and pepper, to taste. (You don't have to use an expensive wine for this recipe — just choose one that you would drink.)
4. Place the ribs back into the wine liquid, making sure they are completely covered. Bake in a preheated 325-degree F oven for about 3 hours. When ribs are tender, remove and place on a serving platter.
5. Strain the liquid to remove any solids into a saucepot. Bring to a boil and reduce by half. Season with salt and pepper, to taste. Pour over the ribs and serve.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
Serve with mashed potatoes or roasted carrots. Shred any leftovers for short rib sandwiches. One reader noted the ribs should be kept submerged/covered while cooking and that a bit more wine and beef stock may be needed to keep them fully covered, depending on pot size; the reduced sauce can be thickened further with a little cornstarch if desired.
