# Rib Roast With Red Wine Jus

## Description
A bone-in ribeye roast cooked over a bed of onions, carrots and celery with red wine and beef stock, based on a recipe shared by Jeff Hemmig, former head chef at 85 Main in Putnam, Connecticut — festive enough for a holiday table but simple enough for a special weeknight dinner. [Source](https://30seconds.com/food/tip/13361/This-Perfect-Rib-Roast-Recipe-From-a-Head-Chef-Is-Top-Secret)

## Ingredients
_(for 12 servings)_

- 1 ribeye roast, on the bone (weight not specified by source)
- 1 onion, chopped
- 5 carrots, chopped
- 2 ribs celery, chopped
- 1 cup red wine
- 2 cups beef stock
- 3 cloves garlic, chopped
- 2 sprigs fresh rosemary, chopped
- olive oil, for drizzling (amount not specified)
- 5 pinches kosher salt
- 3 pinches black pepper

## Instructions
1. Put the vegetables on the bottom of a roasting pan. Add the red wine and beef stock.
2. Smear garlic and drizzle olive oil over the roast. Sprinkle with rosemary, kosher salt and black pepper. Put the meat on top of the vegetables, rib side down.
3. Cook in a preheated 450°F oven for 15 minutes. Reduce heat to 350°F. For rare, cook 18 minutes per pound; for medium-rare, 20 minutes per pound; for medium, 22 minutes per pound. Use a thermometer and pull the meat out at 125°F for rare or 135°F for medium — it will continue to rise in temperature after coming out of the oven.
4. Strain the jus and skim the fat off. Let the meat rest for 15 minutes before slicing so the juices stay in the meat.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
- A rib roast is also called prime rib; per the source (citing MasterClass), a rib roast is generally boneless while prime rib is the same primal cut left bone-in and undivided into individual ribeye steaks — this recipe uses a bone-in roast.
- Resting the meat for 15 minutes before slicing is important so the juices don't run out.
- Suggested side dishes from the source: potatoes Romanoff, steakhouse creamed spinach, steakhouse mashed potatoes, garlic-thyme mushrooms.
