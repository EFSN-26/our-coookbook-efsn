# Ground Beef Casserole

## Description
A delicious family dinner favorite. Browned beef is combined in a rich tomato sauce, layered with hearty, comforting potatoes, and topped with cheese.

## Ingredients
_(for 6 servings)_

- 1/2 tablespoon olive oil
- 1/2 tablespoon butter
- 1/2 cup chopped onions
- 4 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 pound lean ground beef
- 1/2 teaspoon fine sea salt
- 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
- 8 ounces (1 can) plain tomato sauce
- 14 ounces (1 can) fire-roasted tomatoes
- 2 large russet potatoes
- 3/4 cup shredded cheese (marble/mozzarella-cheddar blend)
- 1 tablespoon freshly chopped basil

## Instructions
1. Set the oven to 375°F. Grease an 8x11 casserole dish and set it aside.
2. Add the butter and oil to a wide-bottomed saucepan or large skillet over medium heat. Once hot, add the chopped onions. Stir and cook for 3-5 minutes until softened. Add the minced garlic and stir for one more minute.
3. Place the beef in the pan with the onions and break it up with a wooden spoon. Season with salt and pepper. Cook until the beef is browned and cooked through with no visible pink, about 5-7 minutes.
4. Pour in the tomato sauce and crushed fire-roasted tomatoes. Stir well and bring to a quick boil. Lower the heat to medium-low and simmer, stirring occasionally, for 15 minutes.
5. Meanwhile, peel the russet potatoes and slice them into 1/4-inch thick rounds.
6. Layer half of the potato slices in the bottom of the greased baking dish, followed by a layer of the sauce, then a layer of the rest of the potatoes and sauce. Finish by topping the sauce with the shredded cheese.
7. Cover the dish with foil and bake in the preheated oven for 40 minutes. Remove the foil and bake for 10-15 more minutes.
8. Let the casserole rest for 5 minutes. Sprinkle with fresh chopped basil and serve warm.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
- Let the dish rest for 5 minutes after baking so the juices and sauce can redistribute from the edges back into the casserole — this also makes it easier to cut into clean portions.
- Substitutions: use leaner protein such as ground turkey or ground chicken; use sweet potatoes or fingerlings instead of russets; use garlic powder and onion powder in a pinch instead of fresh; substitute freshly chopped oregano for basil for a more woodsy flavor.
- Recipe variations: give it a southwestern vibe with black beans, corn, and taco seasoning; top with homemade tater tots; stir a couple of spoonfuls of cream cheese or sour cream into the sauce after it simmers for a creamier sauce.
- Can be assembled ahead of time, covered, and stored in the refrigerator for up to 24 hours; let it come to room temperature for 10-15 minutes before baking.
- Storage: fridge leftovers keep in an airtight container up to 3 days, reheat in the microwave or a 350°F oven for 10-15 minutes. Freezer: up to 3 months; thaw overnight in the fridge before reheating.
- Serving suggestions: a simple lettuce salad or arugula salad on the side; egg noodles, whole grain or gluten-free pasta, or brown rice cooked in broth for a heartier meal.
