# Garlic Butter Bacon Cheeseburger

## Description
A decadent "butter burger" — beef patties stuffed with a core of roasted garlic compound butter, grilled, then loaded with melty cheddar, caramelized onions, crispy bacon and roasted garlic aioli. [Source](https://girlcarnivore.com/garlic-butter-bacon-cheeseburger/)

## Ingredients
_(for 4 servings — see §8 for scaling)_

- 1.5 lbs ground beef
- 1 tsp salt
- 4 tbsp roasted garlic compound butter
- 8 strips bacon, cooked
- 1 onion, diced
- 1 tbsp butter
- 4 slices cheddar cheese (aged sharp cheddar recommended)
- buns
- 4 tbsp roasted garlic aioli
- Arugula

## Instructions
Prep the Burgers:
1. Divide the pound and a half of beef into 8 equal portions. Gently press into 8 thin patties, about ¼" thick each.
2. Sprinkle each burger patty with a little salt.
3. Gently dimple the center of each thin patty with your thumb.
4. Add a tablespoon of garlic butter to the center of four of the patties.
5. Top the buttered patties with the remaining four patties and gently press the edges to seal.
6. Place the garlic butter burgers back in the fridge for 2 to 4 hours.

Prep the Grill:
7. Prep your grill for direct heat grilling. Clean and oil the grates. If adding woodchips, add them now. Nest the grill grates in place.
8. Close the grill and adjust the air vents to allow the grill to preheat to 400-500 degrees F.

Grill the Onion and Burgers:
9. Add a small cast iron skillet or griddle to half of the grill to allow it to preheat until just smoking.
10. Add the onions to the dry griddle and allow them to start to caramelize for 2 to 3 minutes.
11. Add the butter on top and fold it into the onions as they cook, keeping a good eye on them to not burn them over the heat. When the onions are soft, golden, and translucent (6-9 minutes), transfer them and cover to keep warm.
12. Meanwhile, sprinkle the outside of the burger patties with salt and place them on the griddle. Cook for 2 to 4 minutes, flip, and cook 2 to 4 minutes longer to your desired internal temperature.
13. Because they have butter inside, it will melt and can cause flare-ups. Do not leave these unattended, and be prepared with a long spatula to flip and maneuver the burgers for even cooking.
14. Add the cheese during the last minute of cooking to melt.
15. Carefully transfer the cooked burgers to a platter to rest while you build the burgers.

Assemble:
16. Toast the buns, if desired.
17. Arrange the arugula on the bottom of the buns, then place a garlic butter burger on top.
18. Add the bacon and grilled onions.
19. Slather the top half of the bun with the roasted garlic aioli and place over top of the burger.
20. Serve immediately.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
- Recommended wood chips: Hickory, when grilling beef burgers.
- For a charcoal grill: prep for direct heat, light charcoal in a chimney, and when 70% ashed over, add it to the grill center; add grates, cover, and preheat to 450-500°F.
- For a gas grill: preheat all burners, clean and oil the grates, close the lid, and preheat about 10 minutes to 450-500°F.
- Pre-ground beef works fine, but for the juiciest results consider grinding your own — control the fat content (around 80/20) and freshness. Ground chuck or ground sirloin work well.
- Use a meat thermometer to check the internal temperature.
- For an umami boost, toss in a few sprinkles of Worcestershire sauce.
- Leftover burgers keep in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 4 days. Reheat on the stovetop with a pat of butter, or wrap loosely in foil and warm in a 300°F (150°C) oven for 10-15 minutes.
