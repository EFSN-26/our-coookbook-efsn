# Succulent Italian Pot Roast (Stracotto)

## Description
Americans have Sunday pot roast, and Italians have stracotto, which means "overcooked" and is a slow-cooked Italian beef roast. This succulent, juicy, and tender pot roast overcooks, if you will, to dinner perfection — the meat is fall-apart tender and melts in your mouth, floating in a deeply flavored, enriched tomato sauce that's heavenly over mashed potatoes, pasta, or polenta.

## Ingredients
_(for 8 servings)_

- 2 tablespoons olive oil
- 1 beef chuck roast (about 3 pounds)
- salt and black pepper, to taste
- 1 onion, chopped
- 1 cup diced carrot
- 1 cup sliced celery
- 1 tablespoon minced garlic
- 2 heaping tablespoons tomato paste
- 1/2 teaspoon crushed red pepper flakes, or to taste (optional)
- 2 cups beef broth (or 1 cup beef broth and 1 cup red wine)
- 14.5 ounces (1 can) crushed tomatoes
- 1 sprig fresh thyme or 1/2 teaspoon dried thyme
- 1 sprig fresh rosemary or 1/2 teaspoon dried rosemary
- 1 teaspoon Italian seasoning
- 2 bay leaves

## Instructions
1. Heat the olive oil in an oven-proof Dutch oven until hot. Season the beef with salt and black pepper on both sides and put into the hot oil. Cook until well seared and browned on the bottom.
2. Flip the meat over and continue to cook until well seared and browned on the other side. Remove to a plate.
3. Add the onion, carrot, and celery. Season with salt and black pepper. Cook until soft, about 5 minutes.
4. Add the garlic, tomato paste, and crushed red pepper flakes, if using. Cook, stirring, about 1 minute.
5. Add the beef broth (and wine, if using), crushed tomatoes, thyme, rosemary, Italian seasoning, and bay leaves.
6. Put the roast back into the pot, submerging it in the liquid.
7. Cover and put into a preheated 275°F oven.
8. Bake the roast for about 2 to 4 hours, or until the meat is fall-apart tender.
9. Taste the gravy and add more salt and black pepper, if needed. Tear or cut the meat into smaller pieces. Either serve from the Dutch oven or place the meat on a serving platter and spoon the gravy over.
10. Serve with mashed potatoes, polenta, pasta, or whatever you like. Store any leftover beef and gravy in an airtight container in the fridge. Reheat in the microwave.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
- Some cooks add bacon to their stracotto: fry bacon in the Dutch oven until crisp, remove it, and sear the roast in the bacon grease instead of olive oil; crumble the bacon and add it back to the pot before baking.
- To further enhance the tomato gravy, you could add balsamic vinegar (about 1 tablespoon), Worcestershire sauce (about 1 tablespoon), or grated Parmesan cheese.
- Stovetop method: prepare through step 6, then bring to a boil, reduce heat, and gently simmer covered about 2 to 4 hours, or until the beef is fall-apart tender.
- Slow cooker method: prepare through step 6, then transfer everything to a slow cooker, cover, and cook on low for 8 to 10 hours or high for 4 to 6 hours.
