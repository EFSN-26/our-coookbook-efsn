# One-Pan Ground Beef and Vegetables

## Description
A one-pan, effortless ground beef skillet or wok dish with whatever vegetables are sitting in your refrigerator, waiting to be used. The seasoning adds tasty depth to this quick, flexible weeknight dinner.

## Ingredients
_(for 3 servings)_

- 2 tablespoons oil
- 1 onion, sliced
- 2 cloves garlic, chopped
- 3½ cups mixed vegetables, diced or chopped (cabbage, zucchini, bell pepper, carrots, peas, baby corn, corn, green beans, other squash, or potatoes — use what you have; frozen vegetables work too)
- salt, to taste
- ¼ teaspoon black pepper
- ½ teaspoon ground cumin seeds
- ½ teaspoon ground cayenne pepper, optional
- ½ teaspoon ground coriander seeds
- 1 teaspoon oregano
- ½ teaspoon paprika (sweet, spicy or smoked)
- 1 pound ground beef
- ½ cup water
- cilantro or parsley, fresh, to top (optional)

## Instructions
1. Slice the onion, chop the garlic and cut the vegetables small.
2. Heat a large deep skillet or a wok with the oil over medium heat. Sauté the onion slices until soft, then add the chopped garlic and stir, cooking briefly.
3. Stir in the cut mixed vegetables over medium heat.
4. Season with salt, black pepper, ground cumin seeds, ground coriander seeds, oregano, paprika and cayenne pepper. Mix well and cook for 2 to 3 minutes.
5. Add the ground beef. Use two spatulas to break it up and mix it into the vegetables. Regulate the heat to medium-high.
6. Pour in the water and combine. Keep on medium-low heat and cook down the meal, stirring occasionally so nothing sticks to the bottom.
7. Remove from heat once all the liquids have dissolved. Garnish with freshly chopped cilantro or parsley.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
- This is a "clean out the fridge" zero-waste meal — use up small vegetable leftovers or fresh vegetables before they turn bad. Frozen vegetables also work well and reduce prep time.
- You can skip the listed seasoning blend and use a store-bought seasoning mix instead — the source suggests Cajun seasoning, Creole seasoning, or an Italian seasoning blend for a Mediterranean twist.
- Serve with a side such as polenta, grits, steamed rice, couscous, spätzle, or buttered pasta, or simply with fresh bread (the source recommends a hot baguette).
- The source occasionally uses a 50/50 mix of ground beef and pork instead of all beef.
