# Fogo de Chao Picanha (Copycat)

## Description
A copycat version of the Brazilian steakhouse favorite from Fogo de Chao — picanha steak seasoned with nothing but kosher salt, skewered in a C-shape with the fat cap outside, and grilled over two-zone heat for a tender, beefy result.

## Ingredients
_(for 8 servings)_

- 1 picanha (3 to 4 pounds)
- Salt

### Equipment
- Skewers (large, heavy, and flat, to prevent the meat from uncurling)

## Instructions
1. Trim any silverskin from the picanha.
2. Cut the meat against the grain into strips, about 2 to 2 1/2 inches wide. You may want to trim the fat cap on the meat to 1/4 to 3/8 of an inch (optional; the fat cap will vary on each roast).
3. Salt the meat on all sides.
4. Curl the meat into a C shape with the fat on the outside and place it on the skewer, as close to the center as possible. Each skewer should accommodate 2 or 3 pieces of meat. (If you do not have large flat skewers, use 2 bamboo skewers pushed through the meat about 1 inch apart to prevent the meat from uncurling.)
5. Preheat one side of the grill to between 475°F and 500°F. Heat the other side of the grill to about 350°F.
6. Place the skewers on the hot side of the grill and cook for about 5 minutes. Flip them over and cook for another 5 minutes. Be sure to avoid the flames to prevent the fat cap from catching fire; monitor the meat while cooking.
7. After the crust has developed, move the meat to the cool side of the grill and cook until you reach your desired level of doneness. Remove the meat from the grill and let it rest for about 5 minutes before serving.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
- Alternate stovetop method: slice the picanha about 1 1/2 inches thick and trim the fat cap to about 1/4 inch. Let the meat come to room temperature, pat dry, and season well with kosher salt on both sides. Heat a cast iron pan over medium-high heat and sear the meat on one side for about 4 minutes. Flip and cook 3 to 4 minutes until you reach your desired temperature, then hold the meat with tongs to sear the fat cap for about a minute. Rest a few minutes before serving.
- Serve with cilantro lime rice, creamed spinach, or corn salsa.
- Reheating: warm on a baking tray in a 250°F oven for about 20 minutes, then sear in a hot pan for about a minute per side to avoid drying out the steak.
- Storage: the steak will last about 3 days in an airtight container in the fridge.
- Picanha can be hard to find at a regular supermarket; ask a specialty butcher or look for it online if needed.
