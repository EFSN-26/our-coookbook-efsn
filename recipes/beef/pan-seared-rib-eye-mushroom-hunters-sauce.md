# Pan-Seared Rib-Eye With Mushroom-Hunter's Sauce

## Description
A thick boneless rib-eye seared in cast iron and basted with garlic-thyme butter, finished in the oven, then topped with a rich mushroom sauce chasseur built on shiitakes, white wine and mushroom stock. Look for a two-inch-thick steak for best results.

## Ingredients
_(for 2 servings)_

**Pan-Seared Rib-Eye**
- 2 tablespoons neutral oil (such as canola or vegetable)
- 1 (1 1/2-pound) boneless rib-eye steak (2 inches thick)
- 2 teaspoons kosher salt
- 1 teaspoon black pepper
- 4 tablespoons unsalted butter
- 2 garlic cloves, crushed
- 5 (4-inch) thyme sprigs, plus leaves for garnish

**Mushroom-Hunter's Sauce**
- 1/2 cup unsalted butter, cut into 1/2-inch pieces, divided
- 2 cups thinly sliced fresh, stemmed shiitake mushrooms (from 2 [3.5-ounce] packages)
- 1 small shallot, finely chopped (about 2 tablespoons)
- 1/2 cup dry white wine
- 1/4 cup tomato puree
- 1 cup mushroom stock
- 2 teaspoons chopped fresh thyme
- 1/2 teaspoon kosher salt
- 1/4 teaspoon black pepper

## Instructions
1. Preheat the oven to 400°F. Heat the oil in a large cast-iron skillet over medium-high until shimmering and just beginning to smoke, 2 to 3 minutes. Pat the steak dry and season with the salt and pepper.
2. Add the steak to the skillet and cook, undisturbed, until evenly browned, 4 to 5 minutes. Reduce heat to medium and flip. Add the butter, garlic and thyme; cook, basting the steak constantly with the butter mixture, until the garlic caramelizes and the sides are no longer pink, 4 to 5 minutes.
3. Transfer the skillet to the oven and cook until a thermometer in the thickest part reads 130°F, 6 to 9 minutes. Move the steak to a cutting board and rest, uncovered, 5 minutes.
4. For the sauce: Melt 2 tablespoons of the butter in a large skillet over medium. Add the mushrooms and cook, stirring occasionally, until lightly browned, 5 to 6 minutes. Add the shallot and cook, stirring, until tender, about 1 minute. Add the wine and cook undisturbed until almost dry, about 2 minutes. Add the tomato puree and mushroom stock; cook, stirring occasionally, until bubbling and thickened, 8 to 10 minutes.
5. Remove from heat. Whisk in the remaining 6 tablespoons butter one tablespoon at a time until glossy and well combined. Stir in the thyme, salt and pepper.
6. Slice the steak into 1/2-inch-thick slices. Spoon the sauce over the sliced steak and garnish with thyme leaves. Serve immediately.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
The key to success is the thickness of the meat — look for a two-inch-thick steak and ask your butcher to cut one if needed. The sauce calls for a homemade mushroom stock. Served as is, this steak-and-mushroom dish may not need a side at all.
