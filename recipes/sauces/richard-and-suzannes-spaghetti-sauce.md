# Richard and Suzanne's Famous Spaghetti Sauce

## Description
A lighter, non-thick tomato sauce created by a couple after Suzanne found classic thick spaghetti sauce not to her taste. Kept a family secret for over 20 years before being shared on Allrecipes, it's praised for its fresh flavor and versatility with spaghetti, pizza, lasagna or as a dipping sauce. [Source](https://www.allrecipes.com/richard-and-suzannes-spaghetti-sauce-12013816)

## Ingredients
_(for 6 servings)_

- 2 tablespoons olive oil
- 4 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 large onion, diced
- 1 large green bell pepper, chopped
- 6 fresh basil leaves, minced
- 1 bunch green onions, diced
- 1 bay leaf
- 1 teaspoon salt
- 1 teaspoon ground black pepper
- 2 (10 ounce) cans tomato sauce
- 1 (15 ounce) can stewed tomatoes
- 1 (10 ounce) can diced tomatoes with green chilies, undrained
- 2 1/2 cups water
- 2 teaspoons white sugar

## Instructions
1. Pour the olive oil into a skillet and place over medium heat. Cook the garlic in the hot oil 1 to 2 minutes.
2. Add the onion, bell pepper, basil, green onion, and bay leaf and cook until the bell pepper softens; season with salt and pepper.
3. Stir in the tomato sauce, stewed tomatoes, diced tomatoes with chiles, water, and sugar; continue to cook 20 to 30 minutes, stirring every 5 to 10 minutes.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
For a thicker sauce, omit the water or simmer longer — several reviewers note the sauce is thin as written. For a heartier, meat-based version, stir in cooked ground Italian sausage, or add a few anchovies to the sautéed vegetables for extra umami. Reviewers have also added extra garlic, zucchini, fresh garden tomatoes, or swapped the onion for fresh jalapeños.
