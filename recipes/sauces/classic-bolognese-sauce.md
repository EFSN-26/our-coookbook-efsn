# Classic Bolognese Sauce

## Description
A hearty, slow-simmered Bolognese sauce built on sautéed onion, carrot, and celery, browned lean ground beef, whole milk and dry white wine reduced in stages, and whole peeled tomatoes simmered with chicken broth until deeply flavorful. Makes about 5 cups, enough to sauce a batch of pappardelle or your favorite pasta shape. [Source](https://www.thekitchn.com/bolognese-recipe-23710291)

## Ingredients
_(for 4 servings, makes about 5 cups)_

- 2 tablespoons olive oil
- 1 small yellow onion, finely diced (about 1 cup)
- 1 small carrot, peeled and finely diced (about 1/3 cup)
- 1 small stalk celery, finely diced (about 1/4 cup)
- 3 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 pound lean ground beef (85% lean recommended)
- 1 1/2 teaspoons kosher salt, plus more as needed
- 1/4 teaspoon freshly ground black pepper
- 1/8 teaspoon ground or freshly grated nutmeg
- 1 cup whole milk
- 1 cup dry white wine
- 1 (28-ounce) can whole peeled tomatoes
- 1 cup low-sodium chicken broth

## Instructions
1. Heat 2 tablespoons olive oil in a Dutch oven or large pot over medium heat until shimmering. Stir in the finely diced onion, carrot, celery, and minced garlic. Cook, stirring occasionally, until the vegetables are softened, 8 to 10 minutes.
2. Add the ground beef, kosher salt, black pepper, and nutmeg. Cook, breaking up the beef into smaller pieces, until cooked through, 5 to 7 minutes.
3. Stir in the whole milk and cook, stirring occasionally, until evaporated, 10 to 12 minutes. Stir in the dry white wine and cook, stirring occasionally, until evaporated, 10 to 12 minutes.
4. Stir in the whole peeled tomatoes and their juices, breaking up the tomatoes with your hands into bite-size pieces as you add them to the pot, along with the chicken broth. Bring to a simmer. Reduce the heat to low to maintain a very gentle simmer. Simmer, stirring occasionally and breaking up any large pieces of tomato with a wooden spoon, until the sauce is thickened and the flavors meld, about 1 1/2 hours.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
- Use lean ground beef (ideally 85% lean) so the sauce isn't greasy.
- Whole milk is preferred for richer flavor and to tenderize the meat and balance the acidity of the tomatoes and wine; 2% milk works in a pinch, but 1% or skim are not recommended.
- Crushed or diced tomatoes (with their juices) can substitute for whole peeled tomatoes — if using diced, consider puréeing them first.
- Low-sodium vegetable or beef broth can substitute for chicken broth.
- While not traditional, other ground meats (or a combination) can be used in place of beef.
- Storage: refrigerate in an airtight container for up to 4 days, or freeze for up to 3 months.
- Don't skimp on the simmer time — the long, slow cook is what builds the full-flavored sauce.
