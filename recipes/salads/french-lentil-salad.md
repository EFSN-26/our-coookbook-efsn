# French Lentil Salad

## Description
This hearty French lentil salad is packed with fresh vegetables and tossed in a bright lemon-Dijon dressing. French green lentils hold their shape well, making them ideal for salads, while herbs add freshness and depth of flavor. It's a satisfying make-ahead option for lunches or light dinners.

## Ingredients
_(for 6 servings)_

- 1½ cups French green lentils, rinsed and picked over
- 1 medium shallot, thinly sliced
- 2 cloves garlic, minced
- 2 teaspoons grated lemon zest
- 1/4 cup lemon juice
- 6 tablespoons extra-virgin olive oil
- 2 tablespoons Dijon mustard
- 1 teaspoon salt
- 1 teaspoon ground pepper
- 1 teaspoon honey
- 2 cups cherry tomatoes, coarsely chopped
- 2 cups chopped English cucumber
- 1 cup thinly sliced carrots
- 1 cup thinly sliced celery
- 1/2 cup chopped fresh herbs, such as parsley, tarragon and chives

## Instructions
1. Place lentils in a medium saucepan and add enough water to cover by about 2 inches. Bring to a simmer over medium heat. Reduce heat to maintain a gentle simmer; cover and cook until tender, 20 to 25 minutes. Drain and rinse with cold water until cool.
2. Meanwhile, combine sliced shallot, minced garlic, 2 teaspoons lemon zest and 1/4 cup lemon juice in a large bowl; let stand for 15 minutes, stirring occasionally. Whisk in 6 tablespoons oil, 2 tablespoons mustard, 1 teaspoon salt, 1 teaspoon pepper and 1 teaspoon honey until combined.
3. Add 2 cups tomatoes, 2 cups cucumber, 1 cup carrots, 1 cup celery, the lentils and 1/2 cup herbs; toss to combine.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
Cook lentils at a gentle simmer, not a brisk boil, so they retain their shape; season with salt only after cooking (early salting can toughen skins and slow cooking). A mix of parsley, tarragon and chives gives classic French flavor, but dill or basil work too. French green (Puy) lentils are recommended for holding their shape; brown lentils are an acceptable substitute (softer); avoid red or yellow lentils, which will fall apart. About 3 cups of precooked/packaged lentils can substitute for the dried lentils. Bell peppers, radishes, green beans or snap peas can be used alongside or in place of the vegetables. For a more substantial dish, top with crumbled feta, a jammy egg, or flaked salmon. Make ahead: refrigerate in an airtight container up to 3 days; stir before serving. Nutrition per serving (per source): 337 calories, 15g fat, 41g carbs, 14g protein.
