# Roasted Eggplant and Chickpea Buddha Bowl

## Description
Air-fried eggplant and chickpeas over rice with cucumber, bell pepper, and mint, finished with a garlicky yogurt sauce. A satisfying bowl that works for a weeknight dinner and doubles well for meal prep.

## Ingredients
_(for 2 servings)_

- 1 large eggplant, cut into 1-inch cubes
- 1 tablespoon olive oil
- 1 can chickpeas, drained and rinsed
- ½ small red onion, thinly sliced
- 1 small cucumber, diced
- ½ bell pepper, any color, diced
- 2 tablespoon mint leaves, chopped
- 1 cup cooked red rice
- salt and pepper, to taste

### For the yogurt sauce
- 1 cup plain yogurt
- 1 tablespoon olive oil
- 1 clove garlic, minced
- ½ teaspoon dried oregano
- ¼ teaspoon paprika
- salt and pepper, to taste

## Instructions
1. Cut the eggplant into 1-inch cubes. Toss with olive oil, salt, and pepper. Place in a single layer in the air fryer basket. Air fry at 400°F (200°C) for 8-10 minutes, or until tender and slightly golden.
2. While the eggplant is roasting, prepare the yogurt sauce: whisk together the yogurt, olive oil, garlic, dried oregano, paprika, salt, and pepper until smooth and creamy.
3. Divide the cooked rice between two bowls. Add the roasted eggplant, chickpeas, cucumber, bell peppers, and mint leaves. Season with salt and pepper.
4. Drizzle generously with the yogurt sauce, or place the sauce in a small bowl in the center. Serve immediately.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
- Plan the cooking order to manage timing: start with roasting the eggplant, then make the yogurt sauce and prep the veggies while it air fries.
- Opt for firm, medium-sized eggplants with smooth skin; avoid wrinkled or overly soft ones.
- Roast the eggplant until golden brown and tender but not overly mushy, for the best texture balance.
- For meal prep, store each component (cooked grains, roasted eggplant, sauce) separately in airtight containers for 3-4 days; reheat the eggplant and grains and assemble just before serving.
- Quinoa, brown rice, farro, or buckwheat are all great substitutes for the red rice.
