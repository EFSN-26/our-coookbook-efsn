# Goat Cheese Salad With Green Beans

## Description
A fresh, fancy-feeling salad that pairs crisp green beans with creamy, tangy goat cheese, crunchy almonds and walnuts, all tossed in a light homemade vinaigrette. [Source](https://cookingblast.com/salad-with-goat-cheese/)

## Ingredients
_(for 4 servings)_

- 1/2 head romaine lettuce, finely sliced
- 1 pound green beans, trimmed
- 1/4 cup red onion, thinly sliced
- 1/4 cup fresh basil
- 2 tablespoons sliced almonds
- 2 tablespoons walnuts, chopped
- 2 ounces goat cheese, crumbled
- salt and pepper, to taste

**Dressing:**
- 3 tablespoons olive oil
- 1 tablespoon apple cider vinegar
- 1 teaspoon Dijon mustard
- 1 teaspoon honey
- 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder
- salt and pepper, to taste

## Instructions
1. Bring a pot of salted water to a gentle boil and cook the green beans for 2 minutes until crisp-tender. Immediately transfer them to an ice water bath to keep their color and crunch, then pat dry.
2. In a small bowl, whisk the olive oil, Dijon mustard, garlic powder, apple cider vinegar, honey, salt and pepper until smooth and slightly thickened.
3. Chop the romaine into bite-sized ribbons, trim the green beans, slice the red onion thinly, and tear the basil leaves.
4. Place the romaine, green beans, basil and red onion in a large salad bowl. Drizzle with half of the vinaigrette and toss gently to coat. Sprinkle on the goat cheese, almonds and walnuts, then finish with the remaining dressing and a pinch of salt and pepper.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
- Mix in arugula or spinach with the romaine to balance a peppery flavor; add cranberries for sweetness and toasted pecans for crunch.
- Feta can be substituted for goat cheese for a similar creamy, tangy texture.
- Toast the nuts lightly in a skillet before adding for a richer, nuttier flavor.
- A vinaigrette made with red wine vinegar and a touch of balsamic is a nice variation.
- Store salad greens separately from toppings (nuts, cheese, dressing) to prevent sogginess; keep everything in airtight containers in the fridge.
- Dressing can be frozen in a freezer-safe container or ice cube tray; thaw in the fridge and whisk before using.
- Pairs well with shredded slow-cooker chicken, BBQ chicken, or boneless pork chops for a complete meal.
