# Niçoise Salad

## Description
A classic Mediterranean dish from Nice, on the French Riviera, made with tender flaky tuna and a colorful medley of potatoes, green beans, tomatoes, eggs, artichoke hearts, olives and capers, all topped with a zesty citrus vinaigrette. [Source](https://www.nutritiousdeliciousness.com/nicoise-salad/)

## Ingredients
_(for 8 servings — see §8 for scaling)_

- ½ pound baby Yukon gold potatoes
- 12 ounces tuna in olive oil, drained
- 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
- ⅔ cup artichoke hearts, halved
- ⅔ cup green and black olives
- 5 ounces green beans
- 4 whole eggs
- ⅓ cup red onion, sliced thin
- 1 batch Citrus Vinaigrette
- 2 tablespoons capers, drained
- Salt and pepper to taste

## Instructions
1. Heat a saucepan of water to boil and boil your eggs. Slightly under hard-boiled is recommended, but boil to your liking. Once they're ready, peel and slice them in half.
2. While the eggs are boiling, prepare the Citrus Vinaigrette. Place it in the fridge to chill while you prepare the salad.
3. Using the same boiling water from the eggs, boil the potatoes until they are fork tender — about 10 minutes, varying with potato size.
4. Once the potatoes are ready, drain and set them aside to cool. Use the same boiling water to boil the green beans for 6-8 minutes, depending on how firm or soft you like them.
5. Plunge the cooked green beans into an iced water bath to stop the cooking. This also sets the beautiful green color.
6. Prepare the remaining salad ingredients, placing the green beans, potatoes and halved tomatoes in groups on your platter.
7. Continue arranging the remaining ingredients on the platter, also in similar groups. Alternatively, arrange the ingredients in strips or simply mix them all together.
8. Once the salad is assembled, sprinkle salt and pepper over the tops.
9. When you are ready to serve, drizzle the dressing over the salad.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
- Use tuna packed in olive oil for more flavor — you can even add some of the oil to the dressing for a more intense tuna flavor. Light tuna packed in water gives a milder flavor.
- After boiling the eggs, place them in an ice bath immediately — this makes them easier to peel and helps achieve a slightly under-hard-boiled texture.
- After boiling the green beans, ensure they are completely dry before adding them to the salad so the vinaigrette coats them better and they stay crisp.
- If red onion is too strong, soak it in cold water for 10 minutes before adding it to the salad to reduce its sharpness.
- Make sure the olives are pitted.
- Storage: stored in an airtight container, the ingredients (including tuna, boiled eggs, vegetables and dressing) hold up well for a couple of days without becoming soggy — give the salad a gentle toss before serving.
