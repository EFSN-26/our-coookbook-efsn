# Asparagus and Pea Salad

## Description
Crisp asparagus and sweet peas are among the first arrivals of spring, and they pair beautifully here in a refreshing, bright salad, lightened with a lemony vinaigrette.

## Ingredients
_(for 4 servings — see §8 for scaling)_

- 1 pound asparagus
- 2 cups fresh peas or frozen and thawed
- 4 cups lettuce
- 1/4 cup fresh mint
- 4 oz goat cheese, crumbled
- 1/4 cup sunflower seeds

For the vinaigrette:
- 2 tbsp fresh lemon juice
- 1 tbsp Dijon mustard
- 1 tsp fresh thyme
- 1/4 tsp kosher salt
- 1/4 cup olive oil

## Instructions
1. Add the lemon juice, dijon mustard, fresh thyme, salt and olive oil to a mason jar and shake well to combine. Set the dressing aside while you prepare the rest of the salad. (Alternatively, add the lemon juice, mustard, thyme, and salt to a small bowl and whisk in the olive oil until well combined.)
2. Trim the ends of the asparagus and slice into 2-3" long pieces.
3. Add the asparagus and peas to a pot of boiling water and cook for 1 minute. Transfer the vegetables to a bowl filled with ice water to blanch them and stop the cooking.
4. Transfer the asparagus and peas to a bowl and add in the vinaigrette, toss to combine.
5. Lay down a bed of lettuce on the serving platter, or divide the lettuce among 4 plates. Place the dressed asparagus and peas over the lettuce and top with fresh mint, crumbled goat cheese and a sprinkle of sunflower seeds. Serve as desired.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
This salad is perfect on its own as a light lunch or dinner, or make it more filling with the addition of chicken or shrimp. Feta or shaved parmesan can be used in place of goat cheese.
