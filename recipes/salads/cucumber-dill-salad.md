# Cucumber Dill Salad

## Description
A simple 10-minute side dish made with thin cucumbers, red onion, and fresh dill tossed in a sharp white vinegar dressing. There's no cooking involved — just slicing, whisking, and a short rest before serving.

## Ingredients
_(for the quantities below — see Missing Information)_

- 2 cucumbers, large, thinly sliced
- 1/4 red onion, small, thinly sliced
- 2 tablespoons fresh dill, chopped
- 1 tablespoon granulated sugar
- 2 tablespoons extra-virgin olive oil
- 1/2 teaspoon salt
- 1/4 teaspoon black pepper
- 1/3 cup white vinegar

## Instructions
1. In a large bowl, combine the sliced cucumbers with the dill and red onion.
2. Toss gently to distribute the ingredients evenly.
3. In a small bowl, whisk together the olive oil, vinegar, white sugar, salt and black pepper. Alternatively, shake the dressing ingredients in a small jar. Keep whisking or shaking until the sugar is dissolved.
4. Pour the dressing over the cucumber mixture and toss to coat.
5. Let the salad sit for at least 10 minutes before serving, so the flavors can blend.
6. Serve at room temperature or chilled, as you prefer.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
- **Slice evenly:** cucumber slices all the same size and thickness pick up the dressing more evenly and look better too.
- **Keep the onion thin:** thick raw onion can dominate the dill and cucumber; thin slices give the right amount of bite without taking over.
- **Dissolve the sugar:** whisk the dressing until the sugar and salt fully dissolve, or it will have a gritty texture.
- **Storage:** keeps in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 2 days; the cucumbers will soften during storage. Stir before serving, since the dressing collects at the bottom. Do not freeze — cucumbers lose their crisp texture after thawing.
- **Substitutions:** English cucumber for thinner skin/fewer seeds; apple cider vinegar for a fruitier tang or rice vinegar for a milder dressing; less sugar for a sharper salad; more red onion for a stronger bite; a spoonful of sour cream or plain Greek yogurt stirred in for a creamy version; 2 teaspoons dried dill in place of the fresh dill (stir into the dressing a few minutes ahead so it softens), though fresh dill is noted as tastier.
- Peel the cucumbers if the skin is thick or waxy; leave the skin on if it's thin and mild.
- Best the day it's made — cucumbers release water as the salad sits, which dilutes the dressing over time.
