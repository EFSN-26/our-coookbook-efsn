# Greek Salmon Salad

## Description
This vibrantly flavored Greek Salmon Salad is loaded with tender, flaky baked salmon, tangy feta, and a colorful mix of veggies, tossed in a creamy lemony dressing — the perfect healthy lunch or dinner.

## Ingredients
_(for 6 servings — see §8 for scaling)_

For the Salmon:
- 1 1/2 pounds raw salmon
- 2 teaspoons olive oil
- 1/2 teaspoon dried oregano
- 1/2 teaspoon salt
- 1/4 teaspoon ground black pepper

For the Salad:
- 1/3 cup Persian or English cucumber, diced
- 1/3 cup mayonnaise
- 1/3 cup red bell pepper, diced
- 1/4 cup red onion, diced
- 1/4 cup Kalamata olives, quartered
- 1/4 cup feta cheese, crumbled
- Zest and juice from 1/2 lemon
- 1 clove garlic, grated
- 1 tablespoon fresh parsley, chopped
- 1 tablespoon fresh chives, chopped
- 1/4 teaspoon salt
- 1/4 teaspoon ground black pepper

## Instructions
1. Preheat oven to 425°F.
2. Add the salmon to a baking sheet lined with aluminum foil that's been sprayed with olive oil.
3. Brush the olive oil over the salmon, then sprinkle the oregano, salt and pepper over the top.
4. Bake for 15-18 minutes or until cooked through.
5. Allow the salmon to cool for a few minutes while you prepare the other ingredients.
6. Add the mayonnaise, grated garlic, lemon juice, lemon zest, salt and pepper to a small mixing bowl.
7. Whisk until combined, then set aside while you prepare the salad.
8. Once the salmon has cooled to room temperature, take a fork and flake off bite-sized pieces. If some are too big, don't worry — they will break up more when you mix up the salad.
9. Add the flaked salmon to a mixing bowl, along with the diced red bell pepper, red onion, Kalamata olives, feta cheese, parsley and chives.
10. Pour the reserved dressing over the top, then gently toss to combine and serve at room temperature.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
- Pat the salmon dry before baking so the oil and seasoning adhere better and it cooks more evenly.
- Watch the salmon closely so it doesn't overcook and become rubbery — the USDA recommends an internal temperature of at least 145°F.
- Chop vegetables to a similar, fairly small size so ingredients distribute evenly in every bite.
- Let the salmon cool before adding it to the salad so the vegetables stay cool and crisp.
- Use Persian or English cucumbers — their thin, tender, edible skin works better here than regular waxy-skinned slicing cucumbers.
- Best served fresh; leftovers keep in the fridge for a couple of days.
- Substitutions: other fish like trout or cod, or canned salmon (drained and flaked) in a pinch; Greek yogurt can replace the mayonnaise in the dressing (thin with a little heavy cream if needed).
