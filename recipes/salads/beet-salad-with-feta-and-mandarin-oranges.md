# Beet Salad with Feta

## Description
An easy, colorful salad of beets, butter lettuce, mandarin oranges, feta, and toasted pecans, tossed with a homemade balsamic-orange vinaigrette. Works well as a light dinner or a hearty side.

## Ingredients
_(for 4 servings)_

### Salad
- 3 cups beets, prepared/store-bought, not pickled
- 3 mandarin oranges, peeled and in sections
- 4 cups butter lettuce salad mix
- 1 cup feta cheese, crumbled
- ½ cup pecans, whole, toasted
- kosher salt and black pepper, to taste

### Dressing
- ¼ cup balsamic vinegar
- ½ cup extra virgin olive oil
- 2 tablespoons fresh orange juice
- 2 teaspoons Dijon mustard
- 1 tablespoon garlic, finely minced
- 2 tablespoons fresh orange zest

## Instructions
1. Preheat your oven to 350°F.
2. While the oven is heating, combine the balsamic vinegar, olive oil, orange juice and zest, mustard, and garlic in a glass bowl and whisk together until well incorporated. Set aside.
3. Toast the pecans in the preheated oven for 7-10 minutes.
4. Cut the beets into cubes.
5. Place the greens in the bottom of a large bowl.
6. Top with the cubed beets, orange sections, and feta.
7. Drizzle with the vinaigrette to taste and mix until the salad is lightly covered with the dressing.
8. Top with the toasted pecans.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
- The source uses pre-cooked, prepared beets from the produce section (Love Beets brand) to save time; to roast your own, place beets on a rimmed baking sheet and bake in a preheated oven for about an hour until tender, then cool, peel, and cube.
- This salad is also great with goat cheese in place of feta; walnuts, pumpkin seeds, or pine nuts can replace the pecans.
- Any salad green, such as romaine or a spinach blend, works in place of butter lettuce. Red onion adds a nice bite but shouldn't be overdone. Fresh herbs like basil, parsley, or dill add extra flavor.
- Most salads wilt if stored overnight; keep dressing separate if you have leftovers you won't finish the same day, and store leftovers in the fridge in an airtight container.
- The balsamic dressing keeps up to a week and is also good drizzled over green beans or asparagus.
- Serve with a warm crusty baguette, or pair with a bowl of soup for a heartier meal.
- A good protein pairing: blackened, grilled, or baked salmon; rare steak; or grilled/rotisserie chicken.
