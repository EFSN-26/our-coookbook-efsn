# Mediterranean Potato Salad With Smashed Olives and Mint

## Description
A no-mayo, vinaigrette-based potato salad bursting with bright flavors — smashed Castelvetrano olives and shallots marinate in a red wine vinegar and Dijon dressing, finished with fresh mint and parsley. [Source](https://30seconds.com/food/tip/101958/Mediterranean-Potato-Salad-Recipe-With-Smashed-Olives-Shallots-Mint-8-Ingredients-30-Minutes)

## Ingredients
_(for 10 servings, plus cooling time)_

- 3 pounds small potatoes of about equal size (such as Yukon gold or small red potatoes)
- 1/4 cup red wine vinegar
- 1 tablespoon Dijon mustard
- 2/3 cup extra virgin olive oil
- 1 1/2 cups (about 6 ounces) Castelvetrano olives (or other green olives), pitted and smashed
- 1 large shallot (about 1/2 cup), diced
- 1/2 cup fresh mint, chopped
- 1/3 cup fresh parsley, chopped
- 1/4 cup capers, smashed (optional)
- salt and black pepper, to taste (the source used about 3/4 teaspoon of each)

## Instructions
1. In a large pot over medium-high heat, add the potatoes and enough water to cover them. Add a couple of teaspoons of salt, if desired. Bring to a boil and cook until just tender, about 15 to 20 minutes. Do not overcook. Drain and set aside.
2. While the potatoes cook, make the dressing: in a large mixing bowl, whisk together the red wine vinegar and Dijon mustard, along with salt and pepper if using.
3. Slowly add the olive oil, whisking to combine.
4. Add the smashed olives, shallots and capers (if using). Stir to combine, then set aside to marinate for 10 to 20 minutes.
5. Once the potatoes have cooled a bit, slice them in half or quarters, depending on size.
6. Add the potatoes to the mixing bowl and gently stir to combine with the dressing. Set aside to allow the potatoes to absorb the dressing, tossing occasionally as they continue to cool.
7. Add the chopped mint and parsley. Season with additional salt and pepper to taste, if desired. Stir to combine.
8. Serve at room temperature. Garnish with additional fresh herbs, if desired. Store leftovers in an airtight container in the refrigerator.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
- Because it has no mayo, this salad is great for summer picnics and potlucks and holds up well outdoors.
- Any green olives can be used in place of Castelvetrano; kalamata olives also work.
- Olives can be smashed with a spatula, the bottom of a jar, a wooden spoon or other flat utensil.
- Serve for lunch or as a side dish with just about any dinner.
