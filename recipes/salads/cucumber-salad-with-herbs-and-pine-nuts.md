# Cucumber Salad with Herbs and Pine Nuts

## Description
A crisp, refreshing cucumber salad loaded with fresh herbs, toasted pine nuts and red onion, tossed in a tangy homemade apple cider vinaigrette. It's vegan-friendly (with agave in place of honey) and low carb.

## Ingredients
_(for 6 servings)_

### For the Salad
- 2 English cucumbers, sliced thin
- 1/3 cup red onion, thinly sliced
- 1/4 cup pine nuts (pignoli), toasted
- 1 tablespoon fresh dill
- 1 tablespoon fresh chives, chopped
- 1 tablespoon fresh mint, chopped

### For the Dressing
- 1/4 cup apple cider vinegar
- 2 tablespoons olive oil
- 1 tablespoon sugar-free honey (or agave if vegan, or honey if not low carb)
- 1/4 teaspoon salt
- 1/4 teaspoon ground black pepper

## Instructions
1. **To make the dressing:** Add the dressing ingredients to a salad dressing shaker cup. Shake until well combined and emulsified, then set aside.
2. **To make the salad:** Wash, dry and slice the cucumbers thinly. Add the cucumbers to a large mixing bowl.
3. Cut the red onion into thin slices.
4. Add the red onion, toasted pine nuts, chopped dill, chives and mint to the bowl with the cucumbers.
5. Pour the dressing over the top. Toss to combine, making sure everything is coated with the dressing.
6. Serve immediately, or chill in the fridge for at least 1 hour to allow the flavors to meld before serving.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
- Use English cucumbers — they're less watery and have thinner skins, so peeling isn't necessary.
- A mandoline slicer helps create thin, even slices quickly.
- Toasting the pine nuts brings out their nutty flavor; don't skip this step.
- If cucumbers release too much water, slice them, sprinkle with a little salt, and let them sit in a colander for 30 minutes to drain before assembling.
- Substitutions: rice vinegar or white wine vinegar for the apple cider vinegar; add feta, avocado, basil or parsley, sesame or sunflower seeds, or cherry tomatoes.
- Storage: keeps in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 2 days. Not recommended for freezing, as the cucumbers and herbs lose texture.
