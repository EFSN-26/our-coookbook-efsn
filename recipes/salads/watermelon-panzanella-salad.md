# Watermelon Panzanella Salad

## Description
Juicy watermelon, salted tomatoes, crisp cucumber, air-fryer-toasted ciabatta, and creamy feta tossed in a bright white balsamic vinaigrette made from the orange-tomato juice collected during a salting step. A hearty, sweet-and-savory summer panzanella built for cookouts and backyard dinners. [Source](https://confettiandcasseroles.com/watermelon-panzanella-salad/)

## Ingredients
_(for 4 servings)_

**For the salad:**
- ½ medium red onion, thinly sliced
- ¼ cup orange juice
- 1 pound Campari tomatoes, quartered
- 2½ teaspoons sea salt, divided
- ½ teaspoon onion powder
- ½ teaspoon garlic powder
- ½ teaspoon Italian seasoning
- ¼ teaspoon black pepper
- 2 tablespoons extra virgin olive oil
- 8 ounces ciabatta bread (or other crusty Italian bread), sliced ¾-inch thick
- 4 cups seedless watermelon, balled or cubed
- ½ medium cucumber, seeds removed, halved lengthwise and sliced
- 3 tablespoons fresh basil, julienned
- 3 ounces feta cheese, crumbled and divided

**For the vinaigrette:**
- ¼ cup reserved orange-tomato juice
- 2 tablespoons white balsamic vinegar
- 3 tablespoons extra virgin olive oil
- 1 tablespoon honey
- ½ teaspoon reserved seasoning mixture (from the salad seasoning blend below)
- 1 teaspoon Dijon mustard

## Instructions
1. Add the sliced red onion to a large mixing bowl. Pour the orange juice over the onion and toss well so the onion is coated and mostly submerged.
2. Place a metal colander over the bowl. Add the quartered tomatoes to the colander and sprinkle with 2 teaspoons of the sea salt. Toss gently and let sit for 30 minutes so the tomato juices drain into the bowl below.
3. In a small bowl, combine the remaining ½ teaspoon sea salt, onion powder, garlic powder, Italian seasoning, and black pepper. Stir to combine and set aside.
4. Preheat the air fryer to 400°F and set the cook time to 4 minutes.
5. Brush both sides of the ciabatta slices with olive oil. Sprinkle generously with the seasoning mixture on both sides, reserving ½ teaspoon of the seasoning blend for the vinaigrette.
6. Place the bread slices in the air fryer basket. Cook for 2 minutes, flip, then cook for another 2 minutes until crisp and lightly golden.
7. Remove the colander from the bowl. Reserve ¼ cup of the collected orange-tomato juice for the dressing. Add the softened onions and salted tomatoes to a clean large bowl.
8. Prepare the vinaigrette by combining the reserved orange-tomato juice, white balsamic vinegar, olive oil, honey, reserved seasoning mixture, and Dijon mustard in a jar with a lid. Shake vigorously until combined.
9. Add the watermelon, cucumber, basil, and 2 ounces of the crumbled feta to the bowl with the tomatoes and onion. Toss gently to combine.
10. Tear the toasted ciabatta into bite-sized pieces and add to the salad just before serving. Drizzle with some of the vinaigrette and toss gently. Transfer to a serving platter or bowl, top with the remaining feta, and serve immediately with extra vinaigrette on the side.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
- Add the toasted bread just before serving so it stays crisp around the edges while soaking up some of the dressing.
- Cherry or heirloom tomatoes work well in place of Campari tomatoes.
- Cubed watermelon works just as well as melon balls and saves prep time.
- White balsamic vinegar keeps the dressing light and bright; regular balsamic can be used but may overpower the watermelon.
- Don't skip the salting step — it produces both the vinaigrette base and the mellowed red onion.
- If your air fryer runs hot, check the bread at the 2-minute flip so it stays golden and firm rather than dark or crumbly.
