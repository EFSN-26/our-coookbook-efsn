# Matthew McConaughey's Tuna Salad

## Description
A recreation of the "badass tuna fish salad" actor Matthew McConaughey described making weekly on the "2 Bears, 1 Cave" podcast — canned tuna tossed with apple, corn, peas, red onion, dill pickle gherkins and crushed jalapeño chips in a Kewpie mayo, wasabi, lemon and white wine vinegar dressing, for a crunchy, kitchen-sink take on tuna salad. Yield and timing as given by the source (2-4 servings; prep 15 min, cook 1 min, total 16 min).

## Ingredients
_(for 2-4 servings — see Missing Information)_

- 2 cans tuna packed in olive oil, drained
- 1/2 medium red onion, chopped into 6-mm cubes
- 2 celery stalks, chopped into 6-mm cubes
- 3 tablespoons dill pickle gherkins, chopped into 6-mm cubes
- 1/4 cup apple, peeled and chopped into 6-mm cubes
- 1/3 cup fresh or frozen corn kernels
- 1/3 cup frozen green peas or edamame
- 1/3 cup jalapeño chips, crushed
- 1/3 cup Japanese Kewpie mayonnaise, more if desired
- 2 teaspoons white wine vinegar
- 1 tablespoon fresh lemon juice
- 2 teaspoons wasabi paste
- 2 teaspoons Italian salad dressing
- 1 teaspoon extra virgin olive oil
- salt and pepper, to taste

Optional:
- 1 tablespoon capers
- 2 tablespoons fresh dill herbs, roughly chopped

## Instructions
1. Wash and pat dry the vegetables and apple with paper towels. Trim both ends and cut into approximately 6-mm cubes.
2. Dice the dill pickle gherkins into 6-mm cubes.
3. Give the fresh dill herbs a rough chop, if using. Reserve for later.
4. With a can opener, open the canned tuna and drain well — even if the tuna is packed in olive oil, drain that oil off and use fresh olive oil instead.
5. In a large mixing bowl, combine the drained tuna, chopped vegetables, red onion, dill gherkins, chopped apple, corn kernels, peas or edamame, and the salad dressing (olive oil, mayo, wasabi paste, salt and pepper to taste).
6. Add the optional capers if desired.
7. Fold in the crushed jalapeño chips and fresh dill herbs.
8. Serve and enjoy.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
- This salad tastes better after refrigeration because the flavors have time to mingle and develop — wrap the mixing bowl or store in an airtight container in the fridge.
- Meal-prepped, it keeps in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to three days.
- Bulk it up into a tuna pasta salad (e.g. with bow-tie pasta) or a chickpea tuna salad.
- Any type of apple works, though the source recommends a tangier, crispy variety like Ambrosia, Fuji, or Pink Lady.
- Dill pickles can substitute for gherkins; celery can be omitted if you don't care for the crunch (no direct substitute given).
- White wine vinegar can be substituted 1:1 with apple cider vinegar.
