# Cucumber Salad

## Description
A cool and creamy cucumber salad made with thin-sliced English cucumbers and red onion in a tangy sour cream, red wine vinegar, and fresh dill dressing. A light, refreshing side for backyard dinners, picnics, or a quick lunch.

## Ingredients
_(for 4 servings)_

- 2 large English cucumbers, sliced thin
- 1/4 red onion, sliced thin
- 1/2 cup sour cream
- 2 tablespoons red wine vinegar
- 1 tablespoon fresh dill, chopped
- 1 teaspoon sugar
- 1/4 teaspoon garlic powder
- salt and pepper, to taste

## Instructions
1. Rinse the cucumbers and red onion, then slice them thinly using a sharp knife or a mandoline. Set the veggies aside while you make the dressing.
2. In a mixing bowl, whisk together the sour cream, red wine vinegar, dill, sugar, and garlic powder.
3. Add salt and pepper to taste.
4. Add the sliced cucumber and onions, and toss to combine.
5. Chill for at least 1 hour. Before serving, taste and add salt as needed.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
Use English cucumbers — thinner skin, fewer seeds, and less water keeps the salad creamy instead of runny. Full-fat sour cream gives the best creamy texture. Variations: swap sour cream for Greek yogurt for a tangier, higher-protein option, or add crumbled feta for a Mediterranean twist. Store leftovers in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 3 days (stir before serving); do not freeze, as the cucumbers will turn mushy.
