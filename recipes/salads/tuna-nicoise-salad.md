# Tuna Nicoise Salad

## Description
A quintessential summer dish featuring vibrant seasonal ingredients like fresh asparagus, baby potatoes, and juicy tomatoes, tossed with canned tuna and a lemon vinaigrette. Simple to make and works well with a fresh baguette on the side. [Source](https://kellystilwell.com/tuna-nicoise-salad/)

## Ingredients
_(for 2 servings)_

- 1 can canned tuna
- 2 boiled eggs
- 1 tomato, chopped
- 10-12 asparagus (spears)
- 1/2 cup iceberg lettuce leaves
- 1 tbsp oil, for frying
- 1/2 cup new potatoes, boiled, cut into pieces

Lemon Vinaigrette Dressing:
- 1 tsp Dijon mustard
- 1 tbsp lemon juice
- 2 tbsp olive oil
- 1/3 tsp salt
- 1/3 tsp pepper

To serve:
- finely sliced shallot
- pitted Niçoise or Kalamata olives

## Instructions
1. Warm olive oil in a skillet. Once hot, fry the boiled potatoes over medium heat until golden brown, stirring occasionally, about 3-4 minutes.
2. Start by layering lettuce leaves in the bottom of a large bowl. Top the lettuce with chopped tomatoes, fried potatoes, and flaked tuna.
3. Bring a pot of salted water to boil. Break off the ends of the asparagus stalks and add them to the boiling water, cooking for three minutes. Immediately rinse the asparagus with ice water to keep it crisp.
4. Cut each boiled egg into four pieces. Add egg and asparagus to the bowl, arranging as in the photo.
5. To make the salad dressing, whisk olive oil, lemon juice, salt, pepper, and mustard in a bowl until well combined.
6. Just before serving, dress the salad by drizzling the dressing over everything, scattering the shallots and olives over the top.
7. You could also sprinkle the salad with pepper or herbs, like dill or parsley.
8. Serve the niçoise salad chilled.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
- Boil the new potatoes ahead of time and let cool in the fridge; peeling them is optional.
- For a healthier (and easier) cooking method, bake the potatoes and asparagus in the oven first: toss with oil, salt, and pepper, and bake for about 25 minutes.
- Replacing the asparagus with green beans is also delicious; the traditional version uses haricots verts (or regular green beans).
- Red onion and radishes are a nice optional addition, though the author sometimes finds them overpowering. Capers, artichoke hearts, or pepperoncini could substitute for or supplement the olives.
- Though typically made with canned tuna, it would also be delicious with an oil-packed tuna filet or seared tuna steak.
- Dress the salad just before serving; if making for one, split the salad and dressing and store half before dressing, to keep it fresh for later.
- Chill a large platter in the fridge beforehand if serving on a platter, or place a bag of ice on it about 15 minutes before serving.
