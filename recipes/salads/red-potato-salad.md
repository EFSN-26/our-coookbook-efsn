# Red Potato Salad

## Description
A creamy make-ahead potato salad built on tender red potatoes, with fresh dill and parsley, scallions, celery, and dill pickle relish keeping the mayonnaise-based dressing from tasting too rich. [Source](https://www.thekitchn.com/red-potato-salad-recipe-23782221)

## Ingredients
_(for 6 servings, makes about 6 cups)_

- 2 pounds red potatoes (5 large or 10 medium), cut into 3/4-inch chunks
- 2 tablespoons apple cider vinegar
- 1 tablespoon kosher salt (for cooking the potatoes)
- 2 medium stalks celery, halved lengthwise and thinly sliced crosswise (about 1 cup)
- 3 medium scallions, thinly sliced (about 1/3 cup)
- 3 tablespoons dill pickle relish, or finely chopped dill pickle spears (about 1 1/2 spears)
- 2 tablespoons fresh parsley leaves, finely chopped (about 10 sprigs)
- 2 tablespoons fresh dill fronds, finely chopped (about 1/2 small bunch)
- 1/2 cup mayonnaise
- 1 tablespoon Dijon mustard
- 1/2 teaspoon kosher salt (for the dressing), plus more as needed
- 1/4 teaspoon black pepper, freshly ground, plus more as needed

## Instructions
1. Place the potato chunks in a large saucepan and add enough cold water to cover by about 1 inch. Add the apple cider vinegar and 1 tablespoon kosher salt, and bring to a boil over high heat. Reduce the heat to maintain a gentle simmer and cook until the potatoes are fork-tender, about 10 minutes. Meanwhile, make the dressing.
2. Stir the celery, scallions, dill pickle relish, parsley, dill, mayonnaise, Dijon mustard, remaining 1/2 teaspoon kosher salt, and 1/4 teaspoon black pepper together in a large bowl. Refrigerate until ready to use.
3. Drain the potatoes well. Transfer to a rimmed baking sheet, spread into a single layer, and let cool to room temperature, about 15 minutes.
4. Add the potatoes to the bowl of dressing and gently fold together until combined. Cover and refrigerate for at least 1 hour, or up to 1 day, before serving. Stir again, then taste and season with more salt and pepper as needed.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
- Sweet relish can be used in place of dill pickle relish for a sweeter, less tart salad.
- Rice vinegar can be substituted for the apple cider vinegar.
- Whole-grain mustard can replace Dijon, but taste the finished salad and add more as needed since whole-grain doesn't pack as much punch.
- Make ahead: can be made up to 1 day ahead and refrigerated in an airtight container.
- Storage: leftovers keep in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 4 days; give it a good stir before serving.
- Red potatoes are preferred here for their firmer, waxy texture that holds together, and because the skins don't need peeling.
