# Beet and Feta Salad with Pistachios and Pears

## Description
A colorful, jewel-toned salad of vacuum-packed cooked beets, crisp pear, creamy feta, and toasted pistachios, tied together with a vibrant beet-juice vinaigrette. Ready in about 10 minutes since the beets come pre-cooked. [Source](https://immigrantstable.com/10-minute-beet-and-feta-salad/)

## Ingredients
_(for 2 servings)_

- 4 cups mixed greens
- 1 pack vacuum-sealed cooked beets, thinly sliced
- 1 pear, thinly sliced
- 1/2 cup feta cheese, crumbled
- 1/4 cup toasted pistachios or toasted walnuts
- 2 tablespoons pumpkin seeds
- Salt, to taste
- 3 tablespoons beet juice, reserved from the beet package
- 3 tablespoons olive oil
- 2 tablespoons apple cider vinegar
- 1 teaspoon Dijon mustard
- 1 teaspoon honey
- 1-2 teaspoons garlic, minced

## Instructions
1. In a small bowl, whisk together the beet juice, olive oil, apple cider vinegar, Dijon mustard, honey, and garlic until the dressing is smooth, glossy, and a vibrant ruby color. Season with salt to taste.
2. Spread the mixed greens across a large serving platter or shallow bowl, arranged loosely rather than piled into a mound.
3. Arrange the sliced beets and pears over the greens, alternating colors as you place them.
4. Scatter the feta, toasted nuts, and pumpkin seeds evenly across the salad.
5. Drizzle the beet vinaigrette over the salad immediately before serving, and serve right away while the greens are crisp.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
- Use the pear while it's still firm — Bosc or Anjou pears hold their shape well; an overripe pear softens into the dressing too quickly.
- Dress the salad right before serving; the beet vinaigrette wilts delicate greens fast.
- Save the beet juice from the vacuum-sealed package — it gives the vinaigrette its color and ties the dressing to the beets.
- If using freshly roasted beets instead of vacuum-sealed ones, you won't have reserved beet juice; substitute a splash of pomegranate juice or make the vinaigrette without it.
- Substitutions: goat cheese can replace feta for a creamier texture; walnuts can replace pistachios.
- Storage: best fresh. Leftovers keep in an airtight container in the fridge up to 1 day, though greens soften. For meal prep, store the dressing (up to 3 days), beets, pears, greens, and toppings separately and assemble just before serving. Freezing is not recommended — greens lose texture and pears become watery.
