# Italian Tuna Potato Salad

## Description
A chunky, Mediterranean-style potato salad (insalata di patate e tonno) made with potatoes, canned tuna, olives, capers and red onion, tossed in an extra virgin olive oil and lemon vinaigrette instead of mayonnaise. [Source](https://xoxobella.com/italian-tuna-potato-salad/)

## Ingredients
_(for 4 servings — see §8 for scaling)_

- 2 Pounds baby potatoes, yellow
- ¼ Cup olive oil, extra-virgin
- 1 lemon, zest and juice
- 1 Tablespoon apple cider vinegar
- 1½ teaspoons garlic powder
- ¼ Teaspoon salt, plus more to taste
- ½ Teaspoon black pepper, plus more to taste
- 5 Ounces canned tuna, in olive oil, drained
- ½ red onion, finely sliced
- 1 Cup Kalamata olives, pitted
- 1 Tablespoon capers, drained
- 1 Handful parsley, fresh, chopped, optional for garnish

## Instructions
1. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Add potatoes and boil for 8 to 10 minutes, or until a knife can be inserted into a potato with ease.
2. Add olive oil, lemon zest, lemon juice, 1 teaspoon of the garlic powder, salt, and pepper to a small mixing bowl. Whisk until combined.
3. Drain potatoes and let them cool slightly for 5 minutes before slicing them in half.
4. Transfer potatoes to a large serving bowl and season with remaining ½ teaspoon of garlic powder.
5. Add tuna, sliced onions, olives, capers, and parsley. Pour in the dressing and toss the salad to coat the potatoes.
6. Season with more salt and pepper to taste, if desired. Serve warm.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
- Boil the potatoes whole: If you cut them before cooking they will crumble more easily, so cook them first and let cool.
- Generously season: As you aren't using a mayonnaise-based dressing, ensure you add plenty of salt, pepper and garlic powder to add maximum flavor to the tangy dressing.
- Tone down the onion: If red onion is too strong for you, substitute a shallot instead.
- Storage: Keep leftovers in a covered container in the refrigerator for up to 3 days. Doesn't freeze well — the potatoes thaw mushy and the dressing separates.
