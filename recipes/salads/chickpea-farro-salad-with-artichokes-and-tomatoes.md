# High-Fiber Chickpea & Farro Salad with Artichokes & Tomatoes

## Description
A filling, vegan grain-and-vegetable salad with farro, sundried tomatoes, chickpeas, artichoke hearts, cherry tomatoes, and arugula, tossed in a simple vinegar dressing. Easy to put together while the farro cooks — great for a barbecue, lunch, or dinner.

## Ingredients
_(for 8 servings)_

- 1 cup farro
- 1/2 cup oil-packed sundried tomatoes, thinly sliced
- 1/4 cup sundried tomato oil (from the jar)
- 1/4 cup white wine vinegar, plus more as needed
- 1 large shallot, finely chopped
- 15 ounces chickpeas (1 can), drained and rinsed
- 14 ounces artichoke hearts (1 can), drained and sliced into halves or quarters
- 3 cups cherry or grape tomatoes (about 1 pound), cut in half
- 4 cups arugula leaves, packed (or fresh spinach)
- 1/4 cup fresh mint, thinly sliced (optional)

## Instructions
1. Cook the farro according to package directions. When cooked, drain and set aside. (The grain can be cooked ahead of time and added to the vegetables when ready to assemble.)
2. While the farro cooks, add the sundried tomatoes, sundried tomato oil, vinegar, shallot, chickpeas, artichoke hearts, tomatoes, and a good pinch of salt and freshly ground pepper into a large salad bowl. Toss well to combine. (If serving will be longer than two hours away, wait to add the greens and place the salad in the fridge until serving time.)
3. When ready to serve, add the arugula, a bit more salt and pepper, and the mint if desired. Toss and enjoy. Store any leftovers in an airtight container in the fridge.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
Cook time will depend on the type of farro used (10-minute farro, such as from Trader Joe's, was used in testing). Because farro is a type of wheat, it is not gluten-free; substitute quinoa for a gluten-free version. Feel free to substitute different beans or vegetables.
