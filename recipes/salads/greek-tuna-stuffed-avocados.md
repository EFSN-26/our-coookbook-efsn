# Greek Tuna Stuffed Avocados

## Description
Creamy avocados filled with tender, flaky tuna tossed in classic Mediterranean ingredients — briny Kalamata olives, feta, red bell pepper, and mint — finished with a vibrant lemony dressing. A quick, refreshing, pescatarian and low-carb lunch.

## Ingredients
_(for 2 servings — see §8 for scaling)_

- 2 Haas avocados
- 10 ounces Albacore tuna in oil, drained of excess oil
- ¼ cup mayonnaise
- 2 tablespoons red bell pepper, diced
- 2 tablespoons red onion, diced
- 2 tablespoons Kalamata olives, quartered
- 1 tablespoon feta cheese, crumbled
- 1 tablespoon fresh mint, chopped
- 1 tablespoon fresh lemon juice
- Zest of ½ lemon
- ½ teaspoon dried oregano
- ¼ teaspoon salt
- ¼ teaspoon ground black pepper

## Instructions
1. Add the mayonnaise, lemon juice, zest, oregano, salt, and ground black pepper to a small mixing bowl. Whisk until combined, then set aside.
2. Add the tuna to a mixing bowl and break up any larger pieces with a fork.
3. Add in the red onion, Kalamata olives, red bell pepper, and mint. Pour in the dressing. Toss to combine and set aside.
4. Slice each avocado in half lengthwise and fill with ¼ of the tuna mixture.
5. Top with the crumbled feta cheese and some more chopped mint, then serve.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
- Choose ripe but firm avocados — they should yield slightly to gentle pressure but not feel mushy, so they hold their shape when stuffed.
- If meal prepping or planning leftovers, generously rub the avocado flesh with fresh lemon juice before filling to help prevent browning in the fridge.
- Drain the tuna well to prevent the filling from becoming watery.
- Mix gently to avoid mashing the avocados or breaking up the tuna too much — you want a chunky texture.
- Chill in the refrigerator for at least 30 minutes before serving for the best flavor and texture.
- Substitutions: canned salmon instead of tuna; Greek yogurt or mashed avocado instead of mayonnaise; diced cucumber, celery, or cherry tomatoes instead of bell pepper; shallots or scallions instead of red onion; green olives or capers instead of Kalamata olives; diced jalapeños, red pepper flakes, or hot sauce for heat.
- The filling can be made ahead and stored in an airtight container in the refrigerator; fill the avocado halves just before serving.
- Store leftovers in an airtight container and eat within a day or two. Not recommended for freezing.
