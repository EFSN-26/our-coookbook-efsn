# Bavarian Potato Salad

## Description
A warm German potato salad from Bavaria, made with a vinegar and oil dressing and finished with hot broth, which gently seasons the potatoes without overpowering them. Known as Kartoffelsalat or Erdäpfelsalat in German, it's a staple side dish at Bavarian beer halls and family grill-outs, traditionally served warm rather than chilled.

## Ingredients
_(for 4 servings)_

- 3-4 large potatoes, mealy type (i.e. Russet)
- 1 small shallot (purple or red onion is ok too)
- 1/2 teaspoon salt
- 1/4 teaspoon ground black pepper
- 1/8 teaspoon grated nutmeg (do not skip)

### Dressing
- 1 tablespoon oil (sunflower or canola)
- 1 tablespoon vinegar (apple cider, white wine, or distilled white)
- 1/2 cup broth (vegetable or beef)

### Garnish
- fresh chives or parsley, chopped

## Instructions
1. Boil the potatoes until soft. Leave them to cool down and peel.
2. Cut the potatoes into thick slices and place them into a salad bowl.
3. Chop the shallot or onion small and add to the potatoes in the salad bowl.
4. Season with the salt, black pepper, and nutmeg.
5. Combine the vinegar and oil, and pour over the potato salad.
6. Heat up the broth, then pour the hot broth over the potato salad in the mixing bowl.
7. Mix up the potato salad.
8. Serve warm with freshly chopped chives or parsley as a garnish.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
This salad differs from a conventional German potato salad because hot vegetable or meat broth is poured over the cooked potatoes, which "seasons" them without being overpowering; it's served warm and never cooled. Use soft, mealy potatoes so they soften and take in the flavors. You can serve this potato salad cold too — this might be the safer choice for food safety in an arid, hot or tropical climate; simply follow the recipe and leave to cool in the fridge. Serve with sauerkraut, Semmelknödel and sausage, grilled pork sausages, roast pork, Leberkäse (a local pink meatloaf), pan-fried or grilled fish such as trout or carp, or roast chicken (Grill Hendl). Store leftovers in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 3 days, and bring to room temperature before serving for the best flavor. Freezing is not recommended — the salad turns watery when thawed.
