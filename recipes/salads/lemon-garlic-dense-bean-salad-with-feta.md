# Lemon-Garlic Dense Bean Salad with Feta

## Description
Lemon and red-wine vinegar brighten this hearty bean salad, while creamy feta and crunchy roasted almonds add richness and texture. Serve it on its own for a quick lunch or pair it with grilled chicken or fish for a simple dinner. [Source](https://www.eatingwell.com/lemon-garlic-dense-bean-salad-with-feta-11946417)

## Ingredients
_(for 4 servings)_

- 1/4 cup extra-virgin olive oil
- 1 teaspoon lemon zest
- 2 tablespoons lemon juice
- 1 tablespoon red-wine vinegar
- 2 teaspoons Dijon mustard
- 1/2 teaspoon ground pepper
- 1 small clove garlic, grated
- 1/4 teaspoon salt
- 2 (15-ounce) cans no-salt-added white beans, such as cannellini or great northern, rinsed
- 1 cup celery, diagonally sliced, plus celery leaves for garnish
- 1/2 cup crumbled feta cheese
- 1/2 cup unsalted roasted almonds, coarsely chopped
- 1/4 cup fresh basil, coarsely chopped, plus more for garnish

## Instructions
1. Whisk 1/4 cup oil, 1 teaspoon lemon zest, 2 tablespoons lemon juice, 1 tablespoon vinegar, 2 teaspoons mustard, 1/2 teaspoon pepper, the grated garlic and 1/4 teaspoon salt in a large bowl until combined.
2. Add rinsed beans, 1 cup celery, 1/2 cup feta, the chopped almonds and 1/4 cup basil; toss to coat. Let stand for 5 minutes, tossing occasionally, to allow the beans to absorb the dressing. Garnish with additional basil and/or celery leaves, if desired.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
- For the best flavor and creaminess, use block feta packed in brine instead of pre-crumbled feta.
- Fresh lemon is essential; both zest and juice add layered citrus flavor — bottled juice will not provide the same brightness.
- Use a microplane to grate the garlic for a smoother, more evenly distributed paste.
- Feta can be substituted with goat cheese for a milder finish; parsley, dill or mint can replace or complement the basil.
- Chickpeas can be used instead of cannellini/great northern beans for a firmer bite; butter beans also work.
- Make ahead: refrigerate in an airtight container for up to 4 days. Stir before serving.
