# Creamy Green Bean Potato Salad With Avocado Dressing

## Description
A wonderful new take on potato salad: roasted potatoes and green beans get a protein and fiber boost from white beans and are topped with a tasty, creamy avocado jalapeno dressing. [Source](https://30seconds.com/food/tip/71199/Creamy-Green-Bean-Potato-Salad-Recipe-With-Avocado-Dressing-High-Protein-High-Fiber)

## Ingredients
_(for 8 servings)_

Potato Salad
- 2 pounds small potatoes or Yukon gold potatoes, scrubbed and cut into 1- to 2-inch pieces
- extra-virgin olive oil, for drizzling
- 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder
- 1/2 teaspoon onion powder
- 1 pound (16 ounces) green beans, trimmed and cut into 2-inch pieces
- 15 ounces (1 can) cannellini beans or great northern beans, drained and rinsed
- 1 lime, halved
- fresh dill

Avocado Jalapeno Dressing
- 1 ripe avocado
- 1/2 cup vegan mayonnaise or regular mayonnaise
- 1/2 cup chopped dill
- 3 tablespoons extra virgin olive oil
- juice of 1 lime (2 to 3 tablespoons)
- 1 garlic clove, peeled and roughly chopped
- 1 jalapeno, seeded and roughly chopped
- 1/2 teaspoon kosher salt

## Instructions
1. Place the potatoes, a good drizzle of olive oil, garlic powder and onion powder on a parchment paper-lined baking sheet. Toss to combine. Put the pan into a preheated 425°F oven and cook the potatoes for 25 minutes.
2. While the potatoes cook, make the dressing by combining all the dressing ingredients in a food processor (preferred) or a blender. Blend until smooth. Taste and add a second jalapeno for more heat, if desired; blend again until smooth.
3. After 25 minutes, take the potatoes out of the oven and mix them well. Push the potatoes to one side of the pan and place the green beans on the open side. Drizzle the beans with a bit of olive oil, salt and black pepper. Toss gently.
4. Return the pan to the oven and cook for 10 to 15 more minutes, checking at 10 minutes so the beans don't overcook. The beans should not be soggy, and the potatoes should be brown outside and fork tender inside.
5. Place the beans and potatoes in a large salad bowl and allow them to cool for at least 5 minutes. Add the white beans and half the avocado dressing and toss well. Taste and add more salt and pepper if needed.
6. When ready to serve, squeeze the lime over the salad and top with some chopped dill. Serve with the extra salad dressing on the side.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
- The extra dressing can be stored in the fridge for up to four days; use it on sandwiches, salads or wraps.
- For even more spice in the dressing, add some crushed red pepper flakes.
- Use vegan mayonnaise for a vegan potato salad.
- Store the potato salad in an airtight container in the fridge.
