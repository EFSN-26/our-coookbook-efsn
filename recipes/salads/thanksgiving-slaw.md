# Thanksgiving Slaw

## Description
A crunchy, no-wilt slaw of shredded cabbage, toasty almonds, sweet cranberries, and parsley in a maple and apple cider vinegar dressing — built to sit out on a holiday buffet for hours without going soggy. [Source](https://www.thekitchn.com/recipe-thanksgiving-slaw-237475)

## Ingredients
_(for 8 servings)_

#### For the dressing:
- 1/2 medium red onion, finely chopped
- 1/3 cup vegetable or olive oil
- 1/4 cup apple cider vinegar
- 2 tablespoons maple syrup
- 4 teaspoons Dijon mustard
- 1/2 teaspoon kosher salt

#### For the salad:
- 1 small head green cabbage (about 2 1/2 pounds, or about 10 cups shredded cabbage or coleslaw mix)
- 1 bunch fresh parsley (about 2/3 cup chopped leaves)
- 3/4 cup toasted, sliced almonds
- 3/4 cup dried cranberries
- Kosher salt, to taste
- Freshly ground black pepper, to taste

## Instructions
1. **Make the dressing:** Finely chop the red onion and add it to a large bowl. Whisk in the vegetable oil, apple cider vinegar, maple syrup, Dijon mustard, and kosher salt. Let sit for at least 10 minutes for the flavors to meld. Meanwhile, prepare the cabbage.
2. **Make the salad:** Cut the green cabbage into 8 wedges through the core, then cut the core from each piece. Thinly slice the cabbage wedges crosswise to shred (about 10 cups). Pick the parsley leaves and coarsely chop until you have about 2/3 cup.
3. Add the shredded cabbage, chopped parsley, sliced almonds, and dried cranberries to the dressing. Toss to combine. Taste and season with kosher salt and black pepper as needed.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
- **Make ahead:** This slaw can sit out at room temperature for up to 2 hours without wilting, making it a great make-ahead or buffet dish.
- **Storage:** Refrigerate leftovers in an airtight container for up to 1 day.
- **Swaps:** Golden raisins can replace dried cranberries; slivered almonds (or another toasted, chopped nut) can replace sliced almonds; red cabbage can replace green cabbage for a more vibrant color.
- **Half recipe:** Use 3 tablespoons vegetable oil, 2 tablespoons apple cider vinegar, 1 tablespoon maple syrup, 2 teaspoons Dijon mustard, 1/4 teaspoon kosher salt, and 1/4 small red onion (finely chopped) for the dressing; 1 (about 14-ounce) bag shredded green cabbage or coleslaw mix and 1/3 cup each toasted sliced almonds, dried cranberries, and chopped fresh Italian parsley for the salad.
- Soak the chopped onion in the dressing for at least 10 minutes to tame its raw bite before adding the rest of the ingredients.
