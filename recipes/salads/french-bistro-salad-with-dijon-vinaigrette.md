# French Bistro Salad with Punchy Dijon Vinaigrette

## Description
An elegant yet hearty salad of red leaf lettuce and escarole with tarragon, parsley and chives, dressed in a punchy Dijon vinaigrette with minced shallots, and topped with chopped ham, hard-boiled eggs and crumbled chèvre. Works as a side dish or a full main course.

## Ingredients
_(for 4 servings)_

### For the Vinaigrette
- 2 tablespoons white wine vinegar
- 2 tablespoons shallot, minced
- 2 teaspoons Dijon mustard
- 1/4 teaspoon salt
- 1/4 teaspoon pepper
- 4 tablespoons olive oil

### For the Salad
- 2 cups escarole, roughly chopped
- 2 cups red leaf lettuce, roughly chopped
- 1/4 cup tarragon, roughly chopped
- 1/4 cup parsley, roughly chopped
- 1/4 cup chives, roughly chopped
- 2 hard boiled eggs, quartered
- 1/4 cup ham, diced
- 2 tablespoons chèvre, crumbled

## Instructions
1. Add the vinegar, shallot, mustard, salt and pepper to a small bowl and mix together.
2. Drizzle the olive oil into the bowl in a thin stream while whisking. Set aside.
3. In a large bowl, combine the lettuces and herbs, and toss to mix well.
4. Divide the greens between two plates for a dinner salad, or four plates for side salads. Top with eggs, ham and chèvre.
5. Drizzle the dressing over the salads and serve immediately.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
- If out of Dijon mustard, a smooth spicy brown or stone-ground mustard makes a solid substitute; even yellow, English, honey, or beer mustard will work, each adding its own character.
- The lettuce base can be swapped: try green leaf lettuce or romaine mixed with frisée or arugula instead of red leaf lettuce and escarole.
- Other delicate herbs — basil, cilantro, mint, thyme, oregano — can stand in for the tarragon, parsley and chives.
- Other dressings, cheeses and cured meats can be substituted for the ham, eggs and chèvre to change the character of the salad.
