# Crab Salad

## Description
This easy Crab Salad is full of fresh, crisp veggies and tender crab meat tossed in a creamy homemade dressing. Perfect for brunch, lunch, or an easy side, this delicious salad comes together in just minutes.

## Ingredients
_(for 6 servings)_

- 14 ounces flaked imitation crab meat
- 1/3 cup mayonnaise
- 1/3 cup celery, finely diced
- 1/4 cup sour cream
- 3 tablespoons red onion, minced
- 2 tablespoons lemon juice
- 1 tablespoon fresh dill, chopped
- 1 tablespoon chives, chopped
- zest of 1 lemon
- 1/4 teaspoon smoked paprika
- 1/4 teaspoon salt
- 1/4 teaspoon ground black pepper

## Instructions
1. Add the mayonnaise, sour cream, lemon juice, lemon zest, smoked paprika, salt and ground black pepper to a small mixing bowl.
2. Whisk to combine, then set aside while you prepare the salad.
3. Chop the crab meat into bite size pieces (flake it and cut each piece into thirds for the ideal size).
4. Add the crab to a large mixing bowl.
5. Add in the chopped celery, red onion, chopped dill and chives.
6. Pour the reserved dressing over the top.
7. Gently toss to combine and it's ready to serve. You can also refrigerate it for 1 hour to allow the flavors to meld before serving.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
Imitation crab meat is used here, but fresh crab meat can be substituted if preferred. Pat the imitation crab meat dry with a paper towel to remove any extra moisture. Use freshly squeezed lemon juice for the best and brightest flavor. Chill the salad for at least 30 minutes to let the flavors meld, and toss gently to avoid breaking up the crab meat too much. Store leftovers in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 3 days (stir before serving); freezing is not recommended since the creamy dressing and crab do not freeze well. Frozen crab meat can be used if fully thawed and drained first. The dressing can be made up to 2 days ahead and stored in an airtight container in the fridge. Serving ideas from the source: crab salad sandwiches, stuffed avocados, lettuce wraps, mixed into a seafood pasta salad, as an appetizer on cucumber rounds or crackers, over a bed of greens, or stuffed into tomatoes.
