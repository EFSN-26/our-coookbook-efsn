# Melon Tomato Salad

## Description
A refreshing summer salad of juicy tomatoes, fragrant cantaloupe, crunchy cucumber and red onion in a zippy Tajín-lime vinaigrette. Part of The Kitchn's "Salad Person" column; pairs well with anything grilled. [Source](https://www.thekitchn.com/melon-tomato-salad-recipe-23790482)

## Ingredients
_(for 6 to 8 servings)_

- 2 tablespoons freshly squeezed lime juice (1 medium lime)
- 2 tablespoons extra-virgin olive oil
- 1 teaspoon honey or agave syrup
- 1 teaspoon Tajín Clásico seasoning, plus more as needed
- ½ teaspoon coriander seeds, coarsely crushed
- ½ medium ripe cantaloupe melon
- 1½ pounds small or medium tomatoes, halved or cut into 1-inch pieces (about 4 cups)
- 2 Persian cucumbers, or ⅓ medium English cucumber, halved lengthwise and thinly sliced crosswise (about 1 cup)
- 1 small red onion, quartered and thinly sliced (about ¾ cup)
- ½ cup fresh basil, mint, or cilantro leaves, or a combination, torn if large

## Instructions
1. Whisk the lime juice, olive oil, honey, Tajín Clásico seasoning, and crushed coriander seeds together in a large bowl.
2. Scoop the seeds out of the cantaloupe half, then cut off the rind. Cut the cantaloupe lengthwise into 1-inch-thick wedges, then thinly slice crosswise on a slight diagonal (about 2 cups).
3. Toss the cantaloupe, chopped tomatoes, sliced cucumbers, and sliced red onion in the dressing. Taste and season with more Tajín Clásico as needed.
4. If you have the time, cover and refrigerate for 1 hour to let the flavors meld. Gently fold in the fresh herb leaves just before serving.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
- Make ahead: the salad can be assembled without the herbs up to 1 day in advance and refrigerated in an airtight container; drain off some liquid if needed, and fold in the herbs just before serving.
- Storing: leftovers keep refrigerated in an airtight container for up to 2 days.
- Swaps: if the cantaloupe isn't great, swap in honeydew or watermelon; add fresh red or white currants for bursts of tart flavor if available; use other fresh herbs like chives, tarragon, dill or fennel fronds; substitute lemon juice for lime; or add 1-2 teaspoons of tangy chamoy to the dressing.
