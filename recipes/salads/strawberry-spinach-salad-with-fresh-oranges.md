# Strawberry Spinach Salad with Fresh Oranges

## Description
Baby spinach piled with juicy strawberries, sweet orange segments, crunchy walnuts, and crumbled goat cheese, tossed with a honey balsamic Dijon dressing. Ready in 10 minutes with zero cooking. [Source](https://confettiandcasseroles.com/strawberry-spinach-salad/)

## Ingredients
_(for 4 servings)_

- 1 cup strawberries, sliced
- 4 cups baby spinach, rinsed and patted dry
- 1/2 red onion, thinly sliced
- 1 large orange, peeled and sliced
- 1/4 cup walnuts, roughly chopped
- 2 ounces goat cheese, crumbled
- Sea salt and black pepper, to taste
- 1/4 cup balsamic vinegar
- 3 tablespoons extra-virgin olive oil
- 2 teaspoons honey
- 2 teaspoons Dijon mustard
- Sea salt and black pepper, to taste (for the dressing)

## Instructions
1. Add the baby spinach, sliced red onion, orange slices, and strawberries to a large salad bowl. Season lightly with sea salt and black pepper.
2. In a small bowl, whisk together the balsamic vinegar, olive oil, honey, and Dijon mustard until smooth. Season with sea salt and black pepper to taste.
3. Pour the dressing over the salad and gently toss until everything is evenly coated.
4. Transfer the salad to a serving platter or individual bowls. Top with chopped walnuts and crumbled goat cheese, and serve immediately.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
- Use ripe, in-season strawberries for the best flavor.
- Dry the spinach well before assembling — wet spinach dilutes the dressing.
- Slice the red onion thinly; soak the slices in cold water for 10 minutes first if you want to mellow the sharpness.
- Toasting the walnuts in a dry pan for about 2 minutes (optional) deepens their flavor and crunch.
- Dress right before serving — dressed spinach wilts quickly. For make-ahead, keep the dressing separate (it keeps up to 5 days in the fridge) and slice the fruit and prep toppings in advance.
- Add grilled chicken or chickpeas to turn this into a full meal.
- Substitutions: candied pecans or sliced almonds for walnuts; feta for goat cheese; arugula for half the spinach; omit the cheese and add sunflower seeds for a dairy-free version; a splash of orange juice in place of some balsamic leans sweeter; a little garlic in the dressing adds depth.
