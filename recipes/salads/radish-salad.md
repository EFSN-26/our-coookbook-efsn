# Radish Salad

## Description
A crispy, refreshing radish and cucumber salad with hints of citrus and nutty Parmesan cheese — light enough for a quick lunch, and versatile enough as a side dish or sandwich filling. [Source](https://www.thekitchn.com/radish-salad-recipe-23779910)

## Ingredients
_(for 4 servings)_

- 1 medium English cucumber or 2 mini seedless cucumbers, thinly sliced
- 1/4 teaspoon kosher salt
- 1 pound loose red radishes (about 16 to 18), trimmed and thinly sliced about 1/8-inch thick
- 2 ounces Parmesan cheese, finely grated (about 1/2 cup)
- 1 teaspoon finely grated lemon zest
- 2 tablespoons freshly squeezed lemon juice (1 medium lemon)
- 1 teaspoon finely chopped fresh dill fronds
- 1/2 teaspoon finely chopped chives (optional)
- 1/4 teaspoon freshly ground black pepper
- Flaky salt, for sprinkling (optional)
- Extra-virgin olive oil, for drizzling (optional)

## Instructions
1. Toss 1 thinly sliced medium English cucumber and 1/4 teaspoon kosher salt together in a fine-mesh strainer. Fit over a medium bowl and let sit for at least 10 minutes to draw out excess moisture.
2. Transfer the cucumbers to a large bowl; discard the liquid. Add 1 pound thinly sliced red radishes, 2 ounces finely grated Parmesan cheese, 1 teaspoon finely grated lemon zest, and 2 tablespoons lemon juice. Toss to combine.
3. Transfer the salad to a serving plate. Sprinkle with 1 teaspoon finely chopped fresh dill fronds, 1/2 teaspoon finely chopped chives if using, 1/4 teaspoon black pepper, and flaky salt if using. Lightly drizzle with extra-virgin olive oil if desired.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
- Ingredient/equipment variation: 2 ounces crumbled feta cheese can be used instead of Parmesan cheese for a creamier, tangier flavor.
- Storage: best eaten right after it's made, as it will wilt and become soggy as it sits. Refrigerate leftovers in an airtight container for up to 1 day.
