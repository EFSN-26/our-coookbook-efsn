# Apple Cranberry Slaw

## Description
A creamy, crunchy slaw of shredded cabbage, carrots, sweet apple, tart dried cranberries, and toasted walnuts in a tangy mayonnaise-sour cream dressing. Works well alongside grilled chicken, holiday ham, or roasted chicken.

## Ingredients
_(for 8 servings)_

- 4 cups cabbage, thinly shredded
- 1 honeycrisp apple, thinly sliced (unpeeled)
- 1 cup carrots, thinly shredded
- 1/2 cup red cabbage, thinly shredded
- 1/3 cup dried cranberries
- 1/3 cup walnuts, toasted and chopped
- 1/2 cup mayonnaise
- 1/3 cup sour cream
- 1/4 cup apple cider vinegar
- 1/2 teaspoon garlic salt
- 1/4 teaspoon black pepper, ground

## Instructions
1. To make the dressing, add the dressing ingredients (mayonnaise, sour cream, apple cider vinegar, garlic salt, black pepper) to a small mixing bowl and whisk until well combined. Set aside.
2. Thinly shred the green cabbage using a sharp knife.
3. Shred the red cabbage.
4. Leaving the apple unpeeled, slice it into thin strips.
5. Add the apple to a bowl along with the green cabbage, red cabbage, carrots, dried cranberries, and toasted chopped walnuts. Pour the dressing over the top.
6. Toss to combine, then chill in the fridge for at least 1-2 hours before serving.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
Use a sharp knife or mandoline to get the cabbage shreds extra thin. Toast the walnuts for a few minutes in a dry skillet to bring out their flavor. Add the apples last so they don't brown while prepping. Store in an airtight container for up to 3 days (do not freeze); give it a stir before serving as cabbage releases liquid over time. Substitutions: swap walnuts for pecans or almonds; add sliced green onions, a tablespoon of Dijon mustard, a bit of Greek yogurt, shredded Brussels sprouts, chopped fresh pineapple or pear, feta or goat cheese, or orange zest.
