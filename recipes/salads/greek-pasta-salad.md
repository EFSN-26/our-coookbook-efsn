# Greek Pasta Salad

## Description
A fresh, vibrant medley of crisp veggies, tangy feta, and a bright herb dressing that transforms simple pasta into a flavor-packed dish. Ready in just 20 minutes and perfect for parties and potlucks.

## Ingredients
_(for 8 servings)_

### Salad
- 16 ounces bowtie pasta (farfalle)
- 3-4 baby cucumbers (Persian cucumbers), sliced
- 12 ounces cherry tomatoes, halved
- 8 ounces feta cheese (block), cut into ¼ inch cubes
- ½ cup Kalamata olives, sliced in half
- ½ medium red onion, thinly sliced

### Dressing
- ½ cup extra virgin olive oil
- ⅓ cup red wine vinegar
- 1 ½ teaspoons dried oregano
- 1 teaspoon garlic powder
- ½ teaspoon salt
- ½ teaspoon black pepper

## Instructions
1. Cook the pasta to al dente according to package directions, then drain and set aside.
2. In a small jar or bowl, combine the olive oil, red wine vinegar, oregano, garlic powder, salt, and black pepper. Shake or whisk vigorously for 1-2 minutes to emulsify.
3. In a large bowl, combine the cooked pasta, cucumbers, tomatoes, feta cheese, and red onion.
4. Pour the dressing over the salad and toss to combine. Serve immediately or refrigerate until ready to serve.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
- Any short pasta shape with nooks and crannies works well — rotini, fusilli, and penne are good alternatives to bowtie.
- Be sure to salt the pasta water for extra flavor.
- Using a block of feta cut into cubes (rather than pre-crumbled) gives bigger, creamier-tangy bites; substitute crumbled feta if preferred.
- Toss the salad with the dressing just before serving so the pasta and veggies stay crisp.
- Store leftovers in an airtight container in the refrigerator for up to 3 days; give the salad a good toss before serving to redistribute the dressing.
- Avoid freezing — the fresh vegetables become soggy when thawed.
- Make ahead: prepare the dressing and chop vegetables a day in advance, storing separately, then combine with freshly cooked pasta when ready to serve.
