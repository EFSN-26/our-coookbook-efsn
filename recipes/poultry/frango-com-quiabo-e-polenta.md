# Frango com Quiabo e Polenta (Chicken with Okra and Polenta)

## Description
A home-style Brazilian chicken and okra stew served over creamy, cheesy polenta — a childhood recipe chef Danilo Parah (of Rio de Janeiro's Rudá and Mäska restaurants) learned from his mother, Dona Sílvia, and later shared on the Brazilian TV show É de Casa. [Source](https://receitas.globo.com/receitas-da-tv/e-de-casa/frango-com-quiabo-e-polenta.ghtml)

## Ingredients
_(servings not stated by source — see §8 for scaling)_

**Roasted tomatoes**
- 800 g plum (Italian) tomatoes
- 20 g dried oregano
- 20 g salt
- 30 mL olive oil
- 2 cloves garlic, grated
- 40 g sugar

**Okra sauté (quiabada)**
- 800 g roasted tomatoes (from above)
- 200 g small okra
- 100 g tomato paste
- 50 mL sunflower oil
- 1/2 bunch thyme
- parsley leaves, to taste
- salt and pepper, to taste

**Chicken**
- 800 g boneless, skinless chicken thighs
- 50 g sugar
- 100 mL sunflower oil
- 100 g white onion
- 1/2 bunch thyme
- parsley leaves, to taste
- 1 leek
- 3 bay leaves
- 1 clove garlic
- okra sauté (quiabada, from above)
- grated ginger, to taste
- finger chile (pimenta dedo-de-moça), to taste

**Chicken stock**
- 1 onion
- 1 carrot
- 4 lemongrass leaves
- ginger, to taste
- finger chile, to taste
- chicken carcass/trimmings
- leek leaves
- parsley stems

**Polenta**
- 1 L chicken stock (from above)
- 200 g fine cornmeal (milharina)
- salt, to taste
- 70 g butter
- 150 g grated parmesan cheese
- 100 g requeijão (Brazilian cream cheese)
- nutmeg, to taste (used in the method; not listed in the source's ingredient block)
- pepper, to taste

## Instructions

**Roasted tomatoes**
1. Cut the tomatoes in half. Mix the oregano, grated garlic, olive oil, salt and sugar with 50 mL of water to make a basting liquid.
2. Brush the liquid onto the tomatoes.
3. Roast at 170°C (340°F) for 15 minutes. Remove from the oven, brush again, and roast for another 15 minutes.
4. Remove again, brush once more, and roast for a final 15 minutes (45 minutes total roasting time, brushed three times). If you don't have a brush, spoon the liquid over instead.
5. Remove from the oven and chop the roasted tomatoes for the okra sauté.

**Okra sauté (quiabada)**
1. Slice the okra.
2. In a very hot skillet, add a little sunflower oil, then the okra, and toast it to cook off its sliminess. A cast-iron skillet or pot is best since it holds heat longer.
3. Once the okra is well toasted, add a whole garlic clove, half a chopped onion and the chopped roasted tomato.
4. Add the herbs and season with salt and pepper to taste.
5. Once the mixture has cooked down into a paste, stir in the tomato paste — this will be folded into the chicken at the end.

**Chicken**
1. Set aside the same vegetables/aromatics used for the stock for the chicken sauté.
2. In a hot pan, sprinkle in a little sugar to form a caramel on the bottom.
3. Add the cleaned, portioned chicken and brown it in the caramel to build a golden crust.
4. Once caramelized, add the sauté: chopped onion, a crushed garlic clove, a little grated ginger, half a chopped chile, and finely chopped leek (reserve the leek leaves for the stock).
5. Cover and let the chicken cook until it starts releasing liquid, using a little of the stock to help it along and add flavor.
6. As the liquid increases, lower the heat and let it simmer gently.
7. After about 20 minutes, the liquid will start to reduce as the chicken finishes cooking.
8. If needed, add a bit more stock to loosen the base of the pan, then stir in the okra sauté (quiabada) at the end.
9. Let everything simmer together to combine.
10. To finish, chop fresh thyme, parsley or other fresh herbs and scatter over the top. Cover the pan and let it steam briefly before serving.

**Chicken stock**
1. Set aside the chicken carcass/trimmings. Roughly cube the onion (reserve the peel) and slice the carrot (reserve the peel).
2. Use the leek leaves and parsley stems for the stock, along with the ginger and chile.
3. Sauté the carcass/trimmings with the onion, carrot, vegetable peels, ginger, lemongrass and aromatic herbs.
4. Add water, bring to a boil, then let it steep covered like a "chicken tea." This stock is the base for the creamy polenta.

**Polenta**
1. Hydrate the cornmeal by bringing 1 liter of the chicken stock to a boil and stirring the cornmeal in to dissolve.
2. Cook the cornmeal over heat, gradually adding more stock to reach the right texture, until fully cooked over low heat.
3. Lower the heat further and stir in the butter, parmesan, requeijão and a pinch of nutmeg.
4. Mix well, then blend with an immersion blender until smooth and creamy. Season with salt and pepper.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
- This recipe was translated into English from the original Portuguese source (receitas.globo.com); the dish's Portuguese name is retained in the title.
- The chef's technique for browning the chicken uses caramelized sugar in the pan (rather than plain oil) to build a deep golden crust.
- This is a multi-component recipe — the tomatoes, okra sauté, chicken stock and polenta can each be started ahead and combined at the end.
