# Grilled Ginger Chicken With Charred Peaches

## Description
Boneless chicken thighs marinated in balsamic vinegar, fresh ginger, garlic and soy sauce, then grilled alongside honey-buttered charred peaches — a summery dinner that pairs sweet fruit with savory, gingery grilled chicken.

## Ingredients
_(for 6 servings — see §8 for scaling)_

**Chicken and Marinade**
- 5 tablespoons balsamic vinegar
- 2 tablespoons finely grated fresh ginger and any ginger juice (2-inch piece)
- 2 1/2 pounds boneless, skinless chicken thighs
- 8 fresh thyme sprigs or 4 fresh rosemary sprigs
- 3 garlic cloves, finely grated or pressed
- 2 tablespoons soy sauce or tamari
- 1 teaspoon fresh lime juice, plus more as needed

**Peach Glaze**
- 2 tablespoons butter, melted
- 1 tablespoon chopped fresh thyme or 2 teaspoons chopped fresh rosemary
- 4 ripe peaches, halved and pitted
- 1 teaspoon honey (optional)
- flaky sea salt (optional)

**For Finishing**
- 3 green onions, white and green parts, thinly sliced (optional)
- chopped fresh basil, for garnish (optional)

## Instructions
1. Make the marinade for the chicken by whisking together the balsamic vinegar, ginger and a good pinch of salt. Reserve 2 tablespoons of the marinade for serving.
2. Salt the chicken and place it into a large container or resealable bag. Drizzle the balsamic mixture over it. Add the thyme or rosemary sprigs, garlic, soy sauce or tamari and lime juice. Cover or seal and place in the fridge for up to 2 hours or overnight.
3. When ready to cook, preheat the grill by brushing with olive oil, or turn on the oven broiler. Get the peaches ready to grill by combining all the glaze ingredients in a small bowl. Brush the halved peaches with the mixture and place on a grilling basket or directly on the grill. Grill for 2 to 4 minutes and transfer to a platter when the skin begins to curl back. Sprinkle with a bit of flaky salt, if desired, and set aside.
4. Cook the chicken at the same time as the peaches if there's enough room, or afterward if not. Cook the chicken for about 5 minutes per side, or until the internal temperature reaches 165 degrees F. Remove from the grill.
5. Serve the chicken with the peach slices and the reserved balsamic drizzled over it. Top with green onions and basil, if desired.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
- Marinating the chicken for 12 to 24 hours (rather than the minimum 2 hours) makes it extra special and worth the wait.
- If fresh peaches aren't available, use fresh nectarines instead.
- Use the best balsamic vinegar you can — it makes a real difference in the finished dish.
- Low-sodium soy sauce can be used; substitute tamari for a gluten-free version.
- No grill? Cook the chicken and peaches under the oven broiler, or in a grill pan or cast iron skillet.
