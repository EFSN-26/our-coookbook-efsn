# Juicy Greek Chicken

## Description
Boneless/skinless chicken thighs marinated in a bright, herby Greek-style marinade of lemon, shallot, garlic, olive oil, oregano and paprika, then grilled or seared until juicy. [Source](https://30seconds.com/food/tip/75952/Easy-Greek-Chicken-Recipe-Is-Juicy-Full-of-Mediterranean-Flavors)

Note: the recipe card lists prep (10 min) and cook (15 min) times separately from a 1-hour marinating time — total time below (85 minutes) includes that hour of inactive marinating time.

## Ingredients
_(for 6 servings — see §8 for scaling)_

- 2 large lemons, zested and juiced
- 1 shallot
- 4 cloves garlic
- 1/4 cup olive oil
- 2 teaspoons dried oregano
- 1 1/2 teaspoons kosher salt
- 1 teaspoon paprika
- 1/2 teaspoon freshly ground black pepper
- 2 pounds boneless/skinless chicken thighs (about 8)
- 6 sprigs fresh parsley or fresh oregano, for garnish (optional)
- sprinkle of crushed red pepper flakes, if desired (optional)

## Instructions
1. Make the marinade by juicing and zesting the lemons into a large mixing bowl.
2. Mince the shallot and garlic into the bowl. Add the olive oil, dried oregano, salt, paprika and pepper. Whisk to combine.
3. Add the chicken and use tongs to coat it in the marinade. Cover and place in the refrigerator for 1 to 2 hours. (Boneless chicken breasts can be used instead of thighs, but this may affect cooking time.)
4. When marinating time is finished, heat the grill or skillet on medium-high. Cook the chicken, flipping halfway through, until internal temperature reaches 165°F. Depending on your stove or grill, this could take 5 to 10 minutes per side. Monitor with a meat thermometer.
5. Remove chicken to a platter and garnish with parsley or oregano, if desired. Serve and enjoy!

## Cook's Notes & Tips
- Grilling is the source's preferred method; cooking on the stovetop works too but takes a bit longer.
- Marinate for 1 to 2 hours for the best flavor.
- Pair with a salad, a favorite vegetable, and a grain like quinoa or rice for a complete meal.
