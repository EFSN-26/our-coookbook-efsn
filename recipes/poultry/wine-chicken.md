# Wine Chicken

## Description
Bone-in, skin-on chicken thighs seared until crisp, then braised in the oven with onion, garlic and a cup of dry white wine — an easy, French-technique-inspired weeknight dinner built on just five ingredients.

## Ingredients
_(for 4 to 6 servings — see §8 for scaling)_

- 1 tablespoon olive oil
- 5 to 6 medium skin-on, bone-in chicken thighs (about 2 1/2 pounds)
- 1 1/2 teaspoons kosher salt, plus more to taste
- 1/2 teaspoon freshly ground black pepper, plus more to taste
- 1 large yellow onion
- 4 garlic cloves
- 2 bay leaves, optional
- 1 cup dry white wine, like a dry riesling
- 2 tablespoons butter, optional

## Instructions
1. Preheat the oven to 400°F.
2. Heat the oil in a large Dutch oven, large braiser, or similar heavy-bottomed pot over medium heat.
3. Pat the skin side of the chicken thighs dry with a paper towel and season with half of the salt and pepper. Add the chicken to the pot, skin side down, in a single layer, squeezing them in so they all fit and get contact with the pan. Season the sides facing up with the rest of the salt and pepper.
4. Cook, without moving, until the skin is well-crisped and browned, about 10 minutes. Remove to a plate, skin side up. The chicken will not be cooked through yet.
5. While the chicken sears, prepare the onion and garlic: slice the onion in half top to root, peel, and slice into 1/4-inch half moons. Smash the garlic well and peel.
6. If there's more than about 2 tablespoons of fat in the pan, carefully remove and discard the extra. Add the onion and sauté until translucent, 3 to 4 minutes. Add the garlic and bay leaves (if using) and sauté until fragrant, about 1 minute.
7. Add the wine and scrape the bottom of the pot to release any browned bits. Add the chicken back in a single layer, skin side up. Bring to a simmer, then transfer to the oven.
8. Bake until the chicken registers 175°F in the thickest parts (without hitting the bone) and is tender, 12 to 17 minutes. Let sit for a few minutes, then serve.
9. Optional — to thicken the sauce: remove the chicken to a platter, return the pot to the stove over medium heat, and simmer until slightly thickened, about 5 minutes. Stir in the butter, taste, and add salt as needed. Serve with the chicken.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
- Use a dry white wine — dry riesling, sauvignon blanc or pinot grigio all work. Avoid red wine (it turns the chicken a grey color) and sweet or oaky wines.
- Don't skip browning the chicken skin — the browned bits left in the pan add flavor and color to the finished dish.
- Pairs well with roasted or mashed potatoes, a bright green salad, couscous, or buttered noodles.
- Store leftovers in an airtight container for up to three days.
