# Apple Cider Chicken Thighs

## Description
Bone-in chicken thighs seared until golden, then braised with apple cider, brown sugar, Dijon mustard, and soy sauce into a sticky sweet-savory glaze, finished in the oven with fresh apple slices. Ready in about 40 minutes. [Source](https://www.troprockin.com/apple-cider-chicken-thighs/)

## Ingredients
_(for 4 servings)_

- 1 apple, sliced
- 4 chicken thighs, bone-in
- 1 teaspoon garlic powder
- 1 teaspoon onion powder
- 1 teaspoon paprika
- 1 teaspoon salt
- 1 teaspoon pepper
- 2-3 tablespoons vegetable oil
- 1/2 medium onion, diced
- 1 1/3 cups apple cider
- 1/3 cup brown sugar
- 1 tablespoon Dijon mustard
- 1 tablespoon Worcestershire sauce
- 1 tablespoon soy sauce
- 2 tablespoons garlic, minced

## Instructions
1. Slice the apple and set aside.
2. In a large bowl, combine the chicken thighs with the salt, pepper, onion powder, garlic powder, and paprika. Stir together until the chicken thighs are coated.
3. In a large oven-safe pan, add the vegetable oil and heat over medium-high heat. Add the chicken thighs skin side down and sear one side for 5-6 minutes. Once golden brown and crispy, flip and sear the other side. Remove from the pan and set aside.
4. Preheat the oven to 375°F.
5. Add the onions to the pan and cook, stirring, until almost translucent.
6. Add the apple cider, brown sugar, soy sauce, Worcestershire sauce, Dijon mustard, and minced garlic. Stir until it comes to a boil, then reduce the heat to low and simmer for about 5 minutes.
7. Add the apple slices and return the chicken thighs to the pan. Spoon the glaze from the bottom of the pan over the chicken.
8. Place the pan in the oven and bake, uncovered, for 15 minutes.
9. Spoon more glaze over the chicken when serving.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
- Use bone-in, skin-on thighs — they stay juicier and add more flavor than boneless, skinless thighs.
- Don't rush the sear; let the chicken cook undisturbed for 5-6 minutes per side for a golden, crispy exterior.
- Deglaze the pan after searing, scraping up the browned bits, for a richer glaze.
- Chicken thighs vary in size, so check for doneness around the 15-minute mark to avoid overcooking.
- Best apples: Honeycrisp (sweet-tart, firm) is the author's go-to; Fuji (sweet, holds up well) is a close second; Granny Smith works if you prefer a tart apple.
- Substitutions: boneless chicken breasts can be used instead of thighs, but adjust the cooking time to avoid dryness; maple syrup or honey can replace the brown sugar.
- Serving suggestion: serve over mashed potatoes, using the glaze as a gravy, alongside a fall salad or green beans; pair with an apple dessert.
- Storage: cool completely, then refrigerate in an airtight container for 3-4 days, or freeze for up to 3 months. Reheat on the stovetop over low heat with a splash of apple cider or broth to keep it moist.
