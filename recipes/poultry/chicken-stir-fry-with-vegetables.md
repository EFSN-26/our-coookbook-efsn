# Chicken Stir Fry With Vegetables

## Description
Bite-sized chicken breast or thighs stir-fried with baby bok choy, sugar snap peas, broccoli and bean sprouts, garlic and fresh ginger, tossed in a chicken-broth, soy sauce and honey sauce. On the table in about 25 minutes.

## Ingredients
_(for 4 servings)_

- 1 pound boneless, skinless chicken breast or boneless, skinless chicken thighs, cut into bite-sized pieces
- 2 tablespoons canola oil, divided
- 1 cup chopped baby bok choy
- 1 cup sugar snap peas or snow peas
- 1 cup chopped broccoli or broccolini
- 1/2 cup bean sprouts
- 3 teaspoons minced fresh ginger
- 3 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 tablespoon cornstarch
- 2 tablespoons water
- 1/4 cup chicken broth
- 4 tablespoons low-sodium soy sauce (for a gluten-free dinner use tamari)
- 1/4 cup honey
- crushed red pepper flakes, optional

## Instructions
1. To make the sauce, combine the cornstarch and water. Mix well. Add the chicken broth, soy sauce and honey. Set aside.
2. Heat 1 tablespoon of the oil in a large skillet or wok over medium-high heat. Season the chicken with salt and pepper. Drop the chicken into the skillet and cook, stirring, until cooked through, about 5 minutes. Remove to a plate.
3. Add the remaining 1 tablespoon of oil to the skillet. Add the vegetables and cook until crisp-tender, about 5 minutes. Add the garlic and ginger and cook for 30 seconds more.
4. Put the chicken back into the skillet. Pour in the sauce and cook until it thickens slightly, about 2 minutes. Serve over rice, if desired.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
- Change up the vegetables to suit your family's tastes.
- For a gluten-free dinner, use tamari in place of the soy sauce.
- Store any leftovers in an airtight container in the fridge and reheat in the microwave.
