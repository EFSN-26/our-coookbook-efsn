# Shortcut French Chicken

## Description
A trending 30Seconds recipe nicknamed "French chicken" for the bottled French salad dressing it uses (not because it's from France). Bone-in chicken is baked in a sweet-tangy sauce of French dressing, dry onion soup mix, and apricot preserves. [Source](https://30seconds.com/food/tip/18509/This-Mouth-Watering-French-Chicken-Recipe-Almost-Broke-the-Internet-4-Ingredients)

## Ingredients
_(for 6 servings)_

- 6 - 8 bone-in chicken pieces or boneless chicken thighs
- 8 ounces (1 bottle) French salad dressing
- 1 ounce (1 packet) dry onion soup mix
- 10 ounces (1 jar) apricot preserves

## Instructions
1. Sprinkle the chicken with salt and pepper and put it into a baking dish. (Bone-in thighs work well; boneless thighs can be used but will need adjusted cooking time.)
2. Combine the salad dressing, soup mix and preserves in a bowl. Mix well. (If you don't have apricot preserves, try peach or orange marmalade instead.)
3. Pour the mixture over the chicken, making sure to cover it completely.
4. Bake in a preheated 350-degree F oven for about 45 minutes to 1 hour, depending on the size of your chicken pieces. Baste occasionally with the sauce.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
- French salad dressing is an American dressing (oil, vinegar, sugar), not French in origin — the name comes from the dressing, not the country.
- Substitute peach preserves or orange marmalade for the apricot preserves if desired.
- Reader tips from the comments: browning the chicken first cuts down on fat in the sauce; jalapeño jelly can replace the apricot preserves for a spicier version; skinless boneless thighs work fine, though skin-on holds up better with all the basting.
