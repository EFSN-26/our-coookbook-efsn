# Homemade Chicken Tikka Masala

## Description
Chicken Tikka Masala is one of the most beloved dishes to cross borders, with bold spices, tender marinated chicken, and a rich tomato-cream sauce. Its origins are debated — often credited to South Asian chefs in Glasgow during the 1960s-70s who added a creamy tomato sauce to grilled chicken tikka for Western diners — but its components trace back to Indian tandoor and Mughlai/Punjabi culinary traditions.

## Ingredients
_(for 4 servings)_

**For the chicken marinade:**
- 1 1/2 pounds boneless, skinless chicken thighs or breasts, cut into bite-sized pieces
- 1 cup plain yogurt
- 2 tablespoons lemon juice
- 2 tablespoons garam masala
- 1 tablespoon ground cumin
- 1 tablespoon ground coriander
- 1 teaspoon turmeric
- 1 teaspoon paprika
- 1 teaspoon cayenne pepper (adjust to taste)
- 4 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 tablespoon fresh ginger, minced
- Salt, to taste

**For the sauce:**
- 2 tablespoons vegetable oil or ghee
- 1 large onion, finely chopped
- 4 cloves garlic, minced
- 1 tablespoon fresh ginger, minced
- 2 teaspoons ground cumin
- 2 teaspoons ground coriander
- 2 teaspoons garam masala
- 1 teaspoon turmeric
- 1 teaspoon paprika
- 1 teaspoon cayenne pepper (adjust to taste)
- 1 (14.5-ounce) can diced tomatoes or tomato puree
- 1 cup heavy cream or coconut milk
- 1/2 cup fresh cilantro, chopped (for garnish)
- Salt, to taste

## Instructions
1. **Marinate the chicken:** In a large bowl, combine the yogurt, lemon juice, garam masala, cumin, coriander, turmeric, paprika, cayenne pepper, garlic, ginger, and salt. Add the chicken pieces and mix well to coat. Cover and refrigerate for at least 1 hour, preferably overnight.
2. **Grill the chicken:** Preheat your grill or broiler. Thread the marinated chicken pieces onto skewers and grill until cooked through and slightly charred, about 5-7 minutes per side. Alternatively, bake at 400°F (200°C) for 20-25 minutes, turning once. Set aside.
3. **Prepare the sauce:** In a large skillet or pot, heat the oil or ghee over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and cook until golden brown, about 5-7 minutes. Add the garlic and ginger and cook for another 2 minutes until fragrant. Stir in the cumin, coriander, garam masala, turmeric, paprika, and cayenne pepper. Cook for 1-2 minutes to toast the spices.
4. **Simmer the sauce:** Add the diced tomatoes or tomato puree and cook for about 10 minutes, stirring occasionally. Reduce the heat to low and stir in the heavy cream or coconut milk. Simmer for another 10 minutes until the sauce thickens.
5. **Combine and serve:** Add the grilled chicken pieces to the sauce and simmer for another 10 minutes until the flavors meld. Garnish with fresh cilantro before serving.
6. Serve hot with basmati rice, naan bread, or a side of vegetables.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
- For the best flavor, marinate the chicken overnight so the spices penetrate the meat thoroughly.
- Grilling adds a smoky flavor; broiling or baking are good alternatives if grilling isn't possible.
- The heat level can be adjusted by varying the amount of cayenne pepper — start with less for a milder flavor.
- Heavy cream gives a rich, creamy texture; coconut milk is a good dairy-free substitute.
- Approximate nutrition per serving (source estimate): 350-400 calories, 25-30g protein, 10-15g carbohydrates, 20-25g fat, 3-4g fiber, 5-6g sugar.
