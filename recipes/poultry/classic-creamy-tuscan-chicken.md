# Classic Creamy Tuscan Chicken

## Description
Few dishes capture the rustic charm of Italian-American cooking quite like Tuscan Chicken, with its rich, creamy sauce packed with sun-dried tomatoes, garlic, spinach, and tender chicken breast. Note from the source: this creamy version is more of an Italian-American creation inspired by Tuscan flavors, rather than a dish traditionally found in a rustic Tuscan trattoria.

## Ingredients
_(for 4 servings)_

- 4 boneless, skinless chicken breasts (about 1 1/2 pounds total)
- 2 tablespoons olive oil
- 2 tablespoons unsalted butter
- 4 garlic cloves, minced
- 1 cup heavy cream
- 1/2 cup chicken broth
- 1/3 cup grated Parmesan cheese
- 1/2 cup sun-dried tomatoes (in oil), drained and chopped
- 2 cups baby spinach, roughly chopped
- 1 teaspoon Italian seasoning (oregano, thyme, and basil)
- 1/2 teaspoon red pepper flakes (optional, for heat)
- Salt and black pepper, to taste
- Fresh parsley or basil, for garnish

## Instructions
1. **Prepare the chicken:** Season the chicken breasts generously on both sides with salt, black pepper, and a pinch of Italian seasoning. For even cooking, pound the chicken to an even thickness using a meat mallet.
2. **Sear the chicken:** Heat 2 tablespoons of olive oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Add the chicken breasts and cook for 5-6 minutes per side, or until golden brown and cooked through (internal temperature of 165°F/74°C). Remove the chicken from the skillet and set aside, covered to keep warm.
3. **Make the creamy Tuscan sauce:** In the same skillet, melt the butter over medium heat. Add the minced garlic and sauté for about 1 minute until fragrant. Stir in the chicken broth, scraping up any browned bits from the bottom of the pan.
4. Reduce the heat to medium-low and add the heavy cream, Parmesan cheese, and red pepper flakes (if using). Stir well until the cheese melts and the sauce is smooth.
5. **Add the vegetables:** Stir in the chopped sun-dried tomatoes and baby spinach. Cook for 2-3 minutes until the spinach wilts and the tomatoes are heated through.
6. **Return the chicken:** Place the cooked chicken breasts back into the skillet, spooning some sauce over the top. Simmer for 3-4 minutes to reheat the chicken and let the flavors meld.
7. **Serve:** Garnish with freshly chopped parsley or basil. Serve hot over pasta, mashed potatoes, or with crusty bread to soak up the creamy sauce.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
- For more even cooking, use thin-sliced chicken breasts or butterfly thicker pieces. Boneless chicken thighs can be used for extra tenderness and flavor.
- Sun-dried tomatoes packed in oil have a richer flavor and softer texture; drain them well before adding to the sauce.
- If the sauce becomes too thick, thin it with a little extra chicken broth; for extra creaminess, add a splash more heavy cream.
- Fresh basil, thyme, or rosemary can elevate the flavor of the sauce beyond dried Italian seasoning.
- Mushrooms, artichoke hearts, or roasted red peppers can be added for extra texture and flavor.
- Cook the sauce on medium-low heat to prevent the cream from separating or curdling.
