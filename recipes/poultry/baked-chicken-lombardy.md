# Baked Chicken Lombardy

## Description
A twist on chicken marsala — seared chicken breasts are simmered in a mushroom marsala wine sauce, topped with mozzarella and baked until the cheese is melted and bubbly. [Source](https://30seconds.com/food/tip/22172/Baked-Chicken-Lombardy-Recipe-Is-What-to-Make-for-Dinner-35-Minutes-9-Ingredients)

## Ingredients
_(for 4 servings)_

- 4 boneless/skinless chicken breasts
- 1/2 teaspoon garlic powder
- 1/4 cup flour (all-purpose or gluten-free)
- 1 tablespoon olive oil
- 2 tablespoons butter
- 8 ounces fresh mushrooms, sliced
- 1/2 cup marsala wine
- 1/4 cup chicken broth
- 1 cup grated mozzarella cheese
- 1 tablespoon minced fresh parsley (optional)
- salt and pepper, to taste

## Instructions
1. Season the chicken with salt, pepper and the garlic powder. Dredge it in the flour. (Use gluten-free flour for a gluten-free version. Boneless/skinless chicken thighs can be used instead of breasts.)
2. Heat the oil in an ovenproof skillet. Add the chicken and cook until golden brown on both sides, about 5 to 6 minutes per side. Remove the chicken from the skillet to a plate.
3. Melt the butter in the skillet. Add the mushrooms and cook until soft, about 5 minutes. Add the marsala and chicken broth. Cook about 2 minutes.
4. Put the chicken back into the skillet and cook about 3 to 4 minutes. Be sure to use an ovenproof skillet; otherwise transfer the chicken to a baking pan.
5. Top the chicken with the cheese and minced parsley. Freshly grated cheese melts better than pre-packaged shredded cheese.
6. Bake in a preheated 400-degree F oven for about 5 to 6 minutes, or until the cheese is melted. Serve. Store leftovers in an airtight container in the refrigerator.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
- Use gluten-free flour to make this recipe gluten-free.
- Boneless/skinless chicken thighs can be used instead of breasts.
- An ovenproof skillet is needed to go from stovetop to oven; if you don't have one, transfer the chicken to a baking pan before the final bake.
- Grate your own cheese if you have time — it melts more smoothly than pre-shredded cheese.
- Serve with pasta, rice or potatoes and a fresh vegetable or side salad.
