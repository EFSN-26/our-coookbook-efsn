# Lemon Pepper Baked Chicken Drumsticks

## Description
These easy Lemon Pepper Baked Chicken Drumsticks are first marinated and then baked to perfection. Tender and juicy, with the perfect balance of seasonings, these drumsticks are simply irresistible.

## Ingredients
_(for 6 servings — see §8 for scaling; plus 1 hour marinating time, see Cook's Notes)_

- 6 chicken drumsticks
- 1/3 cup olive oil
- 1/4 cup fresh lemon juice
- 1 1/2 teaspoons lemon pepper seasoning, divided
- 1 teaspoon lemon zest
- 1 teaspoon dijon mustard
- 2 cloves garlic, grated
- 1/2 teaspoon garlic salt

## Instructions
1. Add the olive oil, lemon juice, lemon zest, 1 teaspoon of the lemon pepper seasoning (reserving 1/2 teaspoon), dijon mustard, garlic, and garlic salt to a small mixing bowl.
2. Whisk until well combined and emulsified.
3. Place the chicken in a food storage bag, then pour most of the marinade over the chicken, reserving about 2 tablespoons.
4. Seal up the bag, massage the marinade into the chicken, making sure all sides are coated. Allow to marinate in the fridge for 1 hour.
5. Preheat your oven to 400°F.
6. Add the chicken to a small baking sheet lined with aluminum foil and a wire rack, making sure there is plenty of room to allow the air to circulate. Sprinkle the reserved 1/2 teaspoon of lemon pepper seasoning over the tops.
7. Cook for 30 minutes.
8. After 30 minutes, brush the remaining 2 tablespoons of marinade over the tops of the chicken.
9. Cook for another 5-8 minutes or until the chicken reaches an internal temperature of at least 165°F, when checked with a meat thermometer, and serve. Cooking times will vary, depending on the size of your drumsticks.

## Cook's Notes & Tips
- Marinating time (1 hour) is in addition to the prep/cook times above.
- Optional: the source's blog text also mentions adding about 1/2 tablespoon of fresh or dried thyme to the marinade, though thyme isn't listed in the printed recipe card's ingredient amounts.
- Use a meat thermometer — chicken can look done on the outside but still be undercooked inside; internal temp should reach 165°F.
- Brushing the reserved marinade on partway through cooking boosts the lemon pepper flavor.
- Cooking on a wire rack keeps the skin crisp by lifting the meat away from rendered grease; without a rack, flip the drumsticks about 20 minutes in.
- Let the chicken rest 5–10 minutes after cooking so the juices redistribute.
- Storage: refrigerate in an airtight container up to 3–4 days, or freeze up to 2–3 months.
- Air fryer option: prepare the same way, then air fry 10 minutes, flip, and continue for 13–15 minutes more, until internal temp reaches 165°F.
